## Learning Objectives
- Understand general procedures for safeguarding prisoners in police custody
- Know the Desk Officer's responsibilities for prisoner intake, detention, meals, and release
- Identify proper prisoner search, transport, and monitoring protocols
- Recognize holdover prisoner procedures and borough court section coordination
- Master transport rules including rear cuffing, transport chain thresholds, and leg restraint requirements
- Know high risk prisoner factors and the additional precautions they trigger
- Understand pregnant prisoner restraint procedures and the sergeant's assessment role
- Know attorney interview and parent/guardian visit rules (location, duration, supervision)
- Understand bedside arraignment procedures, MLD requirements, and bail posting rules
- Know prisoner meal rules ($1.80 max, three meal periods, no hot liquids)
- Master holding pen guidelines (30-minute inspections, 3-hour max, separation rules, item removal)
- Distinguish between voided arrests and decline prosecutions — procedures, documentation, and authority
- Understand warrant check requirements before release and record sealing for voided arrests
- Know inter-agency procedures for hospitalized prisoners and release conflict resolution

## Key Terms
- **Holdover Prisoner** — A prisoner who has entered the court process but has not yet been arraigned and has been returned to police custody for overnight detention
- **Voided Arrest** — Release when reasonable cause to believe prisoner did not commit the offense, or no offense was committed
- **Decline Prosecution** — DA exercises discretion not to prosecute; requires Decline Prosecution Letter from ADA
- **High Risk Prisoner** — Assessed based on 7 factors: seriousness of offense, refusal to identify, forcible resistance, threats, violence history, weapons, escape history
- **Prisoner Security Guidelines** — All prisoners searched on entry/exit from any police, detention, DA, or court facility; searched before transport and upon arrival
- **Mobile Livescan Device (MLD)** — Portable fingerprint scanner for hospitalized prisoners; requires 2 MOS present; prohibited for other purposes
- **Transport Chains** — Required for more than 2 prisoners; cuffs must be double locked

## Sergeant Focus Areas

### Key Supervisor Procedures
- **P.G. 210-01 — General Procedure:** Desk officer must step from behind desk to verify prisoner security before transport; immediate supervisor determines pregnant prisoner restraint method; authorize and monitor attorney/parent visits
- **P.G. 210-04 — Medical Treatment:** Patrol supervisor must be notified when prisoner requires medical treatment; verify documentation and guard assignment
- **P.G. 210-05 — Bedside Arraignment:** Monitor 6-day timeline for borough court section notification; verify 2 MOS present for MLD fingerprinting
- **P.G. 210-06 — Meals:** Desk officer authorizes additional meals for medical/unusual conditions; expense report due by 15th of month
- **P.G. 210-08 — Holding Pens:** Sergeant must personally and periodically inspect prisoners, recording condition and position; verify 30-minute MOS inspections; enforce 3-hour maximum with Command Log entries for extensions
- **P.G. 210-13 — Release:** Patrol supervisor is primary authority for consent to release; verify voided arrest documentation (5 elements on OLBS Arrest Worksheet); ensure BCS intake supervisor seals fingerprint records
- **P.G. 210-15 — Other Agency Release:** Escalate inter-agency conflicts to precinct commander/duty captain
- **P.G. 210-16 — ADA Release:** Verify warrant check before release; process on warrant only if active warrant found
