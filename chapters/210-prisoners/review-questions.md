# Section 210 — Review Questions

15 multiple-choice questions covering key Sergeant exam topics from Section 210.

---

### Question 1
**What is the maximum amount that may be spent per meal per prisoner?**

A. $1.50
B. $1.80
C. $2.00
D. $2.50

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-06, the maximum cost is **$1.80 per meal per prisoner**. The three meal periods are 0500-0600, 1200-1300, and 1800-1900. No hot liquids (coffee, tea, hot chocolate) are permitted.
</details>

---

### Question 2
**How often must a designated MOS inspect prisoners in a holding pen?**

A. Every 15 minutes
B. Every 20 minutes
C. Every 30 minutes
D. Every 60 minutes

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-08, a designated MOS must inspect prisoners every **30 minutes**. In addition, a supervisor must personally and periodically inspect prisoners, recording their condition (sleeping, alert) and position (sitting, lying down).
</details>

---

### Question 3
**What is the standard maximum detention time for a prisoner in a holding pen?**

A. 1 hour
B. 2 hours
C. 3 hours
D. 4 hours

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-08, the standard maximum detention in a holding pen is **3 hours**. Extensions require desk officer or detective squad supervisor approval, and the extension must be recorded in the Command Log.
</details>

---

### Question 4
**When transporting prisoners, at what number must transport chains be used?**

A. 2 or more prisoners
B. More than 2 prisoners
C. 3 or more prisoners — only if high risk
D. More than 4 prisoners

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-01, transport chains MUST be used when transporting **more than 2 prisoners**. Transport chain cuffs must be double locked. Two prisoners may be individually rear cuffed; three or more require transport chains.
</details>

---

### Question 5
**Under what TWO circumstances MUST leg restraints be used on a prisoner?**

A. When the prisoner is combative and when transporting to court
B. When the prisoner refused fingerprinting and for ALL hospital transports
C. When the prisoner is high risk and when transporting more than 2 prisoners
D. When the prisoner threatened violence and when the prisoner has a weapons history

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-01, leg restraints MUST be used when (1) the prisoner **refused fingerprinting** and (2) for **ALL hospital transports**. Leg restraints may NOT be used for punitive purposes and do NOT replace rear handcuffing.
</details>

---

### Question 6
**Where must attorney interviews with prisoners be conducted at the precinct?**

A. The cellblock, under supervision
B. The detective squad room
C. The muster room ONLY
D. Any private room designated by the desk officer

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-01, attorney interviews at the precinct must be conducted in the **muster room ONLY**. At borough court, the interview is conducted at a location designated by the supervisor. The attorney visit must be documented on the Arrest Report Supplement (PD244-157).
</details>

### Question 7
**A prisoner is 19 years old and has been detained for 5 hours. The prisoner's parent requests a visit. What are the rules for this visit?**

A. The visit is not permitted — parent visits are only for juveniles under 18
B. Maximum 15 minutes, in the muster room, in the presence of the desk officer or BCS supervisor
C. Maximum 30 minutes, in any designated area, no supervision required
D. Maximum 15 minutes, in the cellblock, in the presence of the arresting officer

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-01, parent/guardian visits are permitted for prisoners aged **18-21** who have been detained **more than 4 hours**. The visit is limited to **15 minutes**, must take place in the **muster room**, and must be in the presence of the **desk officer or BCS supervisor**. This prisoner (age 19, detained 5 hours) meets all criteria.
</details>

### Question 8
**A pregnant prisoner is brought into the command. What is the initial restraint method, and who determines if a less restrictive method is appropriate?**

A. Front cuff initially; the desk officer determines if less restrictive method is needed
B. Rear cuff initially; the immediate supervisor (sergeant) determines if less restrictive method is appropriate
C. No restraints initially; the commanding officer determines restraint level
D. Rear cuff initially; the arresting officer determines if less restrictive method is needed

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-01, pregnant prisoners are initially **rear cuffed**, absent exceptional circumstances. The **immediate supervisor** (sergeant) determines if a less restrictive method is appropriate (front cuff, or one hand cuffed to a stationary post) based on factors including safety, escape likelihood, crime circumstances, demeanor, late-stage pregnancy/fall risk, and labor.
</details>

### Question 9
**Which of the following is NOT one of the seven high risk prisoner factors?**

A. Seriousness of offense
B. Prior criminal record length
C. Forcible resistance
D. History of escape or attempted escape

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-01, the 7 high risk factors are: (1) seriousness of offense, (2) unwillingness to identify self, (3) forcible resistance, (4) threats of violence or escape, (5) history of violence, (6) weapons possession, and (7) escape/attempted escape history. "Prior criminal record length" is NOT one of the factors.
</details>

### Question 10
**An arresting officer wants to void an arrest. With whom must the officer confer to obtain consent?**

A. The desk officer only
B. The commanding officer
C. The patrol supervisor; if unavailable, the desk officer or BCS supervisor
D. The district attorney

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-13, the arresting officer must confer with the **patrol supervisor** for consent to release. If the patrol supervisor is unavailable, the officer confers with the **desk officer or BCS supervisor**. The authority to release rests solely with the Police Department.
</details>

### Question 11
**An ADA issues a Decline Prosecution Letter. Before releasing the prisoner, the desk officer conducts a warrant check and discovers an active warrant. What is the correct action?**

A. Release the prisoner on the decline prosecution and issue a new summons for the warrant
B. Hold the prisoner on both the original charges and the warrant
C. Process the prisoner solely on the warrant authority, NOT on the original charges
D. Contact the ADA to request prosecution on the original charges

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-16, if a warrant check reveals an active warrant during a decline prosecution release, the prisoner is processed **solely on the warrant authority** — NOT on the original charges. The ADA has already declined prosecution on the original charges.
</details>

### Question 12
**How many MOS must be present when using the Mobile Livescan Device (MLD) to fingerprint a hospitalized prisoner?**

A. 1 MOS
B. At least 2 MOS
C. At least 3 MOS
D. The number depends on the prisoner's risk level

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-05, at least **2 MOS** must be present during MLD fingerprinting of a hospitalized prisoner. The MLD is prohibited for any purpose other than fingerprinting hospitalized prisoners. If bedside arraignment is delayed more than 6 days, the borough court section must be notified.
</details>

**When is the annual Prisoner Security Survey conducted, and when is the report due?**

A. First week of January; report due January 7th
B. Last week of September; report due September 30th
C. Last week of December; report due December 31st
D. First week of June; report due June 7th

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

</details>

### Question 14
**Which of the following prisoners may be placed in a holding pen?**

A. An intoxicated prisoner who is cooperative
B. An emotionally disturbed prisoner under observation
C. A prisoner with a minor laceration who has been treated and cleared by EMS
D. An ill prisoner awaiting transport to the hospital

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-08, ill, intoxicated, and emotionally disturbed prisoners are NOT permitted in holding pens — they must be removed to a hospital under P.G. 210-04. A prisoner who has been **treated and medically cleared** by EMS may be placed in a holding pen, as they are no longer in the ill/injured category requiring hospital treatment.
</details>

### Question 15
**A voided arrest was processed and the prisoner was fingerprinted. Who is responsible for faxing DCJS to seal the fingerprint record?**

A. The arresting officer
B. The desk officer
C. The patrol supervisor
D. The BCS intake supervisor

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**D.** Per P.G. 210-13, when a voided arrest involves a fingerprinted prisoner, the **BCS intake supervisor** faxes DCJS to seal the record. This step is critical to ensure that an invalid arrest does not create a permanent fingerprint record.
</details>

### Question 16
**A hospitalized prisoner's doctor requests that the guarding officer remove the prisoner's handcuffs. What is the correct procedure?**

A. Remove the cuffs immediately as directed by the doctor
B. Explain the circumstances of the arrest; if the doctor insists, request the Patrol Supervisor to respond — do NOT remove without the PS present and authorizing
C. Refuse the request and document it in the Activity Log
D. Contact the desk officer for permission to remove the cuffs

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-04, if a doctor requests cuffs be removed, the guarding officer explains the circumstances of arrest. If the doctor still wants them off, the officer requests the **Patrol Supervisor** to respond. Cuffs are NOT removed without the PS on scene and authorizing. Note: this is different from a doctor asking the officer to **leave the room**, which does NOT require the PS.
</details>

### Question 17
**A prisoner ingests narcotics during arrest. How many times must the Patrol Supervisor and Communications Section be notified?**

A. Once — when the ingestion is observed
B. Twice — when observed and when arriving at the hospital
C. Three times — when observed, when transport initiated, and when arrived at hospital
D. Four times — when observed, when transport initiated, when arrived, and when treated

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-04, the Patrol Supervisor AND Communications Section are notified **three times**: (1) upon observing or suspecting ingestion, (2) when transport to hospital is initiated, and (3) when 84 (arrived) at hospital. The prisoner goes DIRECTLY to the nearest hospital — never to the stationhouse first.
</details>

### Question 18
**In P.G. 210-02, a prisoner's lawyer requests to interview the prisoner at the hospital. Under the Patrol Guide, the lawyer is classified as:**

A. An Interviewer — same as at the precinct under 210-01
B. A Visitor — "if requested" by the prisoner
C. Not permitted to visit hospitalized prisoners
D. An Interviewer only if approved by the Commanding Officer

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Under P.G. 210-02, a lawyer drops from the "Interviewer" list (where they appear in 210-01 at the precinct) to the **"Visitor"** list at the hospital. The lawyer may visit "if requested" by the prisoner. This is one of the key differences between the 210-01 and 210-02 interviewer/visitor lists.
</details>

### Question 19
**A prisoner requires psychiatric admission. Generally, male prisoners are transported to which facility, and by what means?**

A. Elmhurst General by FDNY EMS
B. Bellevue by FDNY EMS
C. Bellevue by private ambulance contract
D. Kings County Hospital by RMP

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-04, male prisoners requiring psychiatric admission are generally transported to **Bellevue** (women to Elmhurst General). Transport is by hospital via **private contract** — NOT FDNY EMS. The escorting officer rides in the private ambulance with the prisoner.
</details>

### Question 20
**How many copies of the typed letterhead (49) report does the Commanding Officer forward to the Chief of Department for an unusual occurrence involving an adult prisoner?**

A. 1 copy
B. 2 copies
C. 3 copies
D. 4 copies

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 210-07, the number of copies sent to the Chief of Department varies: **3 for an adult prisoner**, 2 for a child in custody who is DOA, and 1 for a child in custody (not DOA). One additional copy goes to each: Chief of Patrol, Chief of IAB, Chief of Training, C.O. CJB, C.O. Performance Monitoring Unit, each intermediate command, and C.O. of UMOS if different.
</details>

### Question 21
**A family member brings prescription medication to the precinct for a prisoner who has already been transferred to another facility. What documentation is required?**

A. Single Command Log entry at the receiving facility
B. Dual Command Log entries — both the notifying supervisor and the receiving D.O. make entries
C. Telephone Record only
D. Activity Log entry by the desk officer

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 210-04, when a prisoner is not at the facility where family brought medication, the supervisor contacts the D.O. at the facility where the prisoner is located. **Both** the notifying supervisor AND the receiving D.O. make Command Log entries, including time of notification, prisoner's name, and name of supervisor receiving/making notification.
</details>

### Question 22
**Who decides whether to remove handcuffs entirely from a hospitalized prisoner when restraint is deemed "impractical or inexpedient" (e.g., prisoner is comatose)?**

A. The Patrol Supervisor
B. The Commanding Officer
C. The attending physician
D. The Desk Officer, Precinct of Hospitalization

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**D.** Per P.G. 210-02, the **Desk Officer, Precinct of Hospitalization** decides whether cuffs are removed when deemed "impractical or inexpedient." The Patrol Supervisor confers with the doctor and makes a recommendation, but the D.O. makes the final decision. Do not confuse with pregnant prisoner restraint (where the immediate supervisor decides).
</details>
