# Section 210 — General Procedure, Hospitalized Prisoners, and Other Agency Arrests

Covers P.G. 210-01, 210-02, 210-03

---

## P.G. 210-01: Prisoners General Procedure

### Purpose

Safeguard prisoners in police custody at all stages — from intake through transport, detention, and release.

### Holdover Prisoner

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Holdover Prisoner** | A prisoner who has entered the court process, has NOT yet been arraigned, and has been returned to police custody for overnight detention |

### Prisoner Security Guidelines

> **Exam Alert:** All prisoners will be searched on entry to and exit from ANY police facility, detention facility, DA facility, or court facility. Searches are also required before transport departure and upon arrival at destination.

| Search Point | Required? |
|-------------|-----------|
| Entry to any police/detention/DA/court facility | **Yes** |
| Exit from any police/detention/DA/court facility | **Yes** |
| Before transport departure | **Yes** |
| Upon arrival at destination | **Yes** |

---

### Desk Officer Duties — Prisoner Intake and Detention

| Duty | Details |
|------|---------|
| Cell space assignment | Request borough court section for cell space assignment |
| Police attendant | Assign police attendant to cellblock |
| Isolated prisoner | If prisoner placed alone (threats/fights/disruption), ensure MOS continues to **monitor closely** |
| Custody changes | Record ANY change of custody in Command Log |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer is the central authority for prisoner management at the command level. Sergeants must ensure desk officers are actively managing cellblock assignments, custody records, and attendant assignments — not delegating these responsibilities.

### Permitted Prisoner Interviews

The desk officer will permit prisoner interviews by the following persons:

| Authorized Person | Notes |
|-------------------|-------|
| Supervisory officer | — |
| Detective Bureau member | — |
| District Attorney | — |
| Chief Medical Examiner | — |
| Legal representative (attorney) | See attorney interview rules below |
| City department official | Only if prisoner is an employee of that department |
| Federal law enforcement officer | — |
| Clergy | Only on **prisoner's request** |
| NYS DOCCS representative | — |
| Foreign Consulate representative | — |

### Attorney Interview Rules

| Location | Interview Conducted In |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Precinct** | **Muster room ONLY** |
| **Borough court** | Location designated by supervisor |

> **Exam Alert:** Attorney interviews at the precinct are conducted in the **muster room ONLY** — not the cellblock, not the detective squad room, not the desk area. This is a frequently tested detail.

**Documentation:** Record the following on the **ARREST REPORT SUPPLEMENT**:
- Name, address, and phone number of attorney
- Who retained the attorney
- Whether prisoner was interviewed
- Time attorney arrived and departed

### Parent/Guardian Visits (Prisoner Age 18-21)

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Maximum duration | **15 minutes** |
| Location | Muster room |
| Eligibility trigger | Prisoner detained **more than 4 hours** |
| Supervision | In the presence of desk officer or BCS supervisor |

> **Exam Alert:** Parent/guardian visits apply to prisoners aged **18-21** (not juveniles under 18, who have separate procedures). The visit is limited to **15 minutes**, in the **muster room**, only after **4 hours** of detention, and supervised by the **desk officer or BCS supervisor**.

### Female Prisoners — Special Rules

- Do **NOT** confine a female prisoner with a nursing baby in a cell
- Assign a **female attendant** for female prisoners

### Transport Rules

#### Handcuffing and Restraint

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Standard restraint | **Rear cuff ALL prisoners** OR place on transport chains |
| Transport chain cuffs | **Double lock** transport chain cuffs |
| More than 2 prisoners | Transport chains **MUST** be used |

> **Exam Alert:** The threshold for mandatory transport chains is **more than 2 prisoners**. Two prisoners may be rear cuffed; three or more require transport chains.

#### Desk Officer Transport Verification

> **Sergeant Focus:** Before ANY prisoner is removed from the command, the desk officer must **step from behind the desk** and **personally check** that prisoners are properly secured. This is a physical, hands-on duty — not a visual check from behind the desk.

#### Additional Escort Requirements

An additional escort is required when:

| Condition | Additional Escort? |
|-----------|-------------------|
| More than 2 prisoners without chains | **Yes** |
| More than 9 prisoners on chains | **Yes** |
| Several stops required | **Yes** |
| Multiple destinations | **Yes** |
| **High risk** prisoner | **Yes** |

---

### High Risk Prisoner Factors

A prisoner is considered **high risk** based on ANY of the following:

| Factor | Description |
|--------|------------|
| Seriousness of offense | Violent felonies, serious crimes |
| Refusal to identify | Unwillingness to identify self |
| Forcible resistance | Physical resistance to arrest or custody |
| Threats | Threats of violence or escape |
| Violence history | History of violent behavior |
| Weapons | Possession of weapons at time of arrest |
| Escape history | Prior escape or attempted escape |

> **Exam Alert:** Know ALL 7 high risk factors. Exam questions may present a scenario and ask whether the prisoner qualifies as high risk. Any single factor is sufficient.

---

### Leg Restraints

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **MUST** use | When prisoner **refused fingerprinting** |
| **MUST** use | For **ALL hospital transports** |
| May **NOT** use | For **punitive purposes** |
| Does not replace | **Rear handcuffing** — leg restraints are used IN ADDITION to rear cuffs |

> **Exam Alert:** The two mandatory triggers for leg restraints are (1) refusal to be fingerprinted and (2) ALL hospital transports. Leg restraints are NEVER used as punishment and do NOT replace rear handcuffing.

---

### Pregnant Prisoners

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Initial restraint | **Rear cuff**, absent exceptional circumstances |
| Assessment | **Immediate supervisor** determines if less restrictive method is appropriate |
| Less restrictive options | Front cuff, or one hand cuffed to a stationary post |

#### Factors for Supervisor Assessment

| Factor |
|--------|
| Safety of officers and others |
| Likelihood of escape |
| Circumstances of the crime |
| Demeanor of the prisoner |
| Late-stage pregnancy / fall risk |
| Active labor |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The **immediate supervisor** (sergeant) makes the determination on whether to use less restrictive restraints for pregnant prisoners. This is a direct supervisory decision — not a desk officer or commanding officer decision. Document the assessment and rationale.

## P.G. 210-02: Hospitalized Prisoners

### Guarding Hospitalized Prisoners

When a prisoner requires hospitalization, a member of the service must be assigned to guard the prisoner at the hospital. Key considerations:

- Continuous guard must be maintained
- Relief must be arranged through the command
- Prisoner remains in police custody throughout hospitalization

### Other Police Agencies Defined

| Agency |
|--------|

| Court Officers |
| Port Authority Police |
| MTA Police |
| Amtrak Police |
| Sea Gate Police |
| FDNY Fire Marshals |
| DEP Police |
| Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority (TBTA) Police |

### Guarding Responsibility

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Primary responsibility | **Arresting officer** from other agency must guard prisoner |
| Desk officer action | Notify the agency to provide **24-hour guards** |
| Follow-up | If relief not provided within **1 hour**, make follow-up notification |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer must actively track whether the arresting agency has provided relief guards. If no relief arrives within **1 hour**, a follow-up notification is required. Do not allow NYPD resources to be used as indefinite guard replacements for other agencies.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **Attorney interview location** — Muster room ONLY at precinct; supervisor-designated location at borough court
2. **Transport chain threshold** — More than 2 prisoners requires chains
3. **Desk officer transport check** — Must step from behind desk to personally verify
4. **Leg restraint triggers** — Refused fingerprinting AND all hospital transports
5. **Pregnant prisoner restraint** — Immediate supervisor (sergeant) determines less restrictive method
6. **Parent/guardian visit** — Age 18-21, more than 4 hours, 15 minutes max, muster room, desk officer/BCS supervisor present
7. **High risk factors** — All 7 factors; any single factor qualifies
8. **Security survey** — Last week of September, due September 30th
9. **Other agency hospitals** — 1-hour follow-up if no relief provided
10. **$1.80 meal cap and NO hot liquids**

## Sergeant Study Notes

- The **desk officer** is the central figure in prisoner management: cell assignments, attendant assignments, custody log entries, transport verification, and authorized visitor access
- **Sergeants** make the restraint determination for pregnant prisoners — know the 6 assessment factors
- The desk officer must **physically step from behind the desk** to verify prisoner security before transport — this is a hands-on check
- **High risk** prisoner designation triggers additional escort requirements during transport
- Attorney visits at the precinct are in the **muster room ONLY** — document everything on the Arrest Report Supplement
- The **1-hour follow-up** rule for other agency hospitalized prisoners prevents NYPD from absorbing another agency's guarding responsibilities indefinitely
- **Zero tolerance** policy on prisoner sexual assault/harassment — any allegation must be reported and investigated
- Communicable disease information about prisoners is **confidential** and cannot be released without written consent from the Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters
