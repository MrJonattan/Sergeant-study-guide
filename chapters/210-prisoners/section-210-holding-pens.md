# Section 210 — Unusual Occurrences and Holding Pen Guidelines

Covers P.G. 210-07, 210-08

---

## P.G. 210-07: Unusual Occurrences Involving Prisoners

### Prisoner Unusual — Defined

An unusual occurrence is any of the following involving an **unarraigned adult prisoner** OR a **juvenile under 18** in NYPD custody or Corrections:

| Occurrence | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Assaults MOS | Notify desk officer and patrol supervisor |
| Prisoner sexually assaulted/harassed (or alleges such) | Notify desk officer and patrol supervisor |
| Escapes or attempts escape | Notify desk officer and patrol supervisor; if escape/attempt, notify **IAB Command Center** |
| Other unusual (catchall) | Notify desk officer and patrol supervisor |

> **Exam Alert:** The definition covers **any prisoner, any age** — both unarraigned adults and juveniles under 18 in custody or Corrections. The four categories are: assaults MOS, sexual assault/harassment (or allegation), escape/attempt, and other unusual.

### Desk Officer — Notifications

| Notify | Details |
|--------|---------|
| C.O./Duty Captain **AND** Operations Unit | Provide prisoner's name, sex, and charge |
| If escape or attempt | Also notify **IAB Command Center** |

### Commanding Officer/Duty Captain — Investigation and Report

| Duty | Details |
|------|---------|
| Conduct investigation | — |
| Prepare report | **Typed letterhead (form 49)** |
| Forward to Chief of Department | Number of copies varies (see below) |
| One additional copy to each | Chief of Patrol, Chief of IAB, Chief of Training, C.O. Criminal Justice Bureau (CJB), C.O. Performance Monitoring Unit, each intermediate command, C.O. of UMOS if different |

### Copies to Chief of Department

| Situation | Number of Copies |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Adult prisoner** | **3** copies |
| **Child in custody is DOA** | **2** copies |
| **Child in custody** (not DOA) | **1** copy |

> **Exam Alert:** The number of copies of the typed letterhead (49) report forwarded to the Chief of Department depends on the prisoner: **3 for adult, 2 for child DOA, 1 for child in custody**. This is a frequently tested numerical detail.

> **See Also:** PG 221-03 — Suicide/injury of prisoner. PG 221-05 — DOA SILD.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor must respond to ALL unusual occurrences involving prisoners. Document the incident thoroughly in the Command Log, prepare required reports, and notify the commanding officer for serious incidents (death, escape, serious injury).

## P.G. 210-08: Guidelines for Prisoner Holding Pens

### Prisoner Holding Pen Roster

Maintain **PRISONER HOLDING PEN ROSTER (PD244-1410)** for all prisoners placed in holding pens.

### Inspection Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Designated MOS | Designate a MOS to inspect prisoners every **30 minutes** |
| Unattended prohibition | Do **NOT** leave prisoners unattended at any time |
| Supervisor inspection | Assign supervisor to **personally and periodically** inspect prisoners |
| Supervisor documentation | Record prisoner condition (sleeping, alert) and position (sitting, lying down) |

> **Exam Alert:** The **30-minute inspection** interval is one of the most tested numbers in Section 210. A designated MOS must check prisoners every 30 minutes, and a supervisor must also personally and periodically inspect, documenting condition and position.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sergeants have a dual responsibility: (1) ensure the designated MOS is conducting 30-minute inspections, and (2) personally and periodically inspect prisoners, recording their condition (sleeping, alert) and physical position (sitting, lying down). This documentation can be critical in liability situations.

### Maximum Detention Time

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Standard maximum | **3 hours** in a holding pen |
| Extension authority | **Desk officer** or **detective squad supervisor** may approve extension |
| Documentation | Extension must be recorded in **Command Log** |

> **Exam Alert:** The 3-hour maximum for holding pen detention is frequently tested. Know that extensions require desk officer or detective squad supervisor approval AND a Command Log entry.

### Prisoners NOT Permitted in Holding Pens

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Ill | Remove to hospital (see P.G. 210-04) |
| Intoxicated | Remove to hospital (see P.G. 210-04) |
| Emotionally disturbed | Remove to hospital (see P.G. 210-04) |

> **Exam Alert:** Ill, intoxicated, and emotionally disturbed prisoners must NEVER be placed in holding pens. They must be removed to a hospital under P.G. 210-04.

### Separation Requirements

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Male and female | **NEVER** in the same holding pen |
| Adult and juvenile | **NEVER** in the same holding pen |
| Juveniles | Must be placed in a **designated juvenile area** |

> **Exam Alert:** The separation rules are absolute — there are no exceptions. Male/female separation and adult/juvenile separation are mandatory. Juveniles must be placed in a designated juvenile area, not simply a separate cell in the adult holding area.

### Suicide and Assault Prevention — Item Removal

The following items must be removed from prisoners upon placement in a holding pen:

| Item | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Belts | Suicide/assault prevention |
| Neckties | Suicide/assault prevention |
| Shoelaces | Suicide/assault prevention |
| Drawstrings | Suicide/assault prevention |
| Jackets | Suicide/assault prevention |
| Overcoats | Suicide/assault prevention |

> **Exam Alert:** Know all 6 items that must be removed: **belts, neckties, shoelaces, drawstrings, jackets, and overcoats**. A common exam question lists items and asks which must be removed. Remember the mnemonic: **B-N-S-D-J-O** (Belts, Neckties, Shoelaces, Drawstrings, Jackets, Overcoats).

### Menstrual Hygiene

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Products | Issue menstrual hygiene products **on request** |
| Restroom access | Assign a **female MOS** to escort prisoner to restroom |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Menstrual hygiene products must be provided upon request — do not wait for the prisoner to ask repeatedly. A female MOS must be assigned to escort the prisoner to the restroom. Failure to provide these basic accommodations is a supervisory failure.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **30-minute inspections** — Designated MOS checks every 30 minutes; supervisor also inspects personally
2. **3-hour maximum** — Standard max detention in holding pen; extension requires desk officer/detective squad supervisor + Command Log
3. **Separation rules** — Male/female NEVER together; adult/juvenile NEVER together; juveniles in designated area
4. **6 items removed** — Belts, neckties, shoelaces, drawstrings, jackets, overcoats
5. **No ill/intoxicated/EDP** — These prisoners cannot be placed in holding pens
6. **Supervisor documentation** — Record condition (sleeping, alert) AND position (sitting, lying down)
7. **Menstrual hygiene** — Products on request; female MOS escort to restroom
8. **PD244-1410** — Prisoner Holding Pen Roster must be maintained
9. **Unusual occurrence definition** — Unarraigned adult or juvenile under 18: assaults MOS, sexual assault/harassment, escape/attempt, other unusual
10. **Typed letterhead (49) copies** — 3 for adult prisoner, 2 for child DOA, 1 for child in custody
11. **Escape/attempt** — Notify IAB Command Center in addition to standard notifications
12. **Prisoner DOA or seriously injured by police action** — UMOS: request PS response, safeguard scene, obtain medical attention; PS comply with PG 221-05

### Prisoner DOA or Seriously Injured by Police Action / Police Firearms Discharge

| Role | Duty |
|------|------|
| UMOS | Immediately request response of Patrol Supervisor; safeguard crime scene; obtain medical attention |
| Patrol Supervisor | Respond and comply with **PG 221-05** |

## Sergeant Study Notes

- The sergeant must **personally inspect** prisoners in holding pens — this is not delegable to a police officer
- Document prisoner **condition** (sleeping, alert) and **position** (sitting, lying down) during each inspection
- The **3-hour maximum** is the standard; extensions are the exception and require both supervisory approval AND Command Log documentation
- **Ill, intoxicated, and emotionally disturbed** prisoners are categorically excluded from holding pens — immediate hospital removal is required
- The **6 items** removed for suicide/assault prevention are frequently the subject of exam questions — know the complete list
- **Juveniles** must be placed in a designated juvenile area — mere separation from adults in the same holding area is insufficient
- Prisoner holding pen management is a high-liability area — thorough documentation of inspections protects both the prisoner and the Department
- Any unusual occurrence (injury, illness, escape, death, altercation) requires immediate notification of the desk officer and patrol supervisor
