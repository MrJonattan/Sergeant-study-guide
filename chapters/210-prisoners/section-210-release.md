# Section 210 — Release of Prisoners

Covers P.G. 210-13, 210-15, 210-16, 210-17

---

## P.G. 210-13: Release of Prisoners

### Two Types of Release

| Type | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Voided Arrest** | Reasonable cause to believe prisoner did NOT commit the offense, or that no offense was committed | P.G. 210-13 |
| **Decline Prosecution** | DA exercises discretion not to prosecute; requires a **Decline Prosecution Letter** from an ADA | P.G. 210-13 |

> **Exam Alert:** A voided arrest and a decline prosecution are fundamentally different. A **voided arrest** means the arrest itself was improper (wrong person or no crime). A **decline prosecution** means the arrest was valid but the DA chooses not to prosecute. Know the distinction — exam questions frequently test this.

### Supervisor Conferral Requirement

| Step | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Primary authority | Arresting officer must confer with **patrol supervisor** for consent to release |
| If unavailable | Confer with **desk officer** or **BCS supervisor** |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor is the first-line authority for consent to release a prisoner. This conferral is mandatory — the arresting officer cannot unilaterally void an arrest or release a prisoner. If the patrol supervisor is unavailable, the desk officer or BCS supervisor serves as the alternate authority.

### Release Timing

Release may occur at any time from **initial custody** until **arraignment**.

### Notification Requirements — Voided Arrest

After a voided arrest, the following must be notified:

| Notify | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Desk officer of command of occurrence** | Must be informed in ALL cases |
| **Patrol borough commander** | Notification required |
| **Identification Unit** | Notification required |
| **Borough Court Section (BCS)** | Notification required |
| **Commanding officer of arresting officer** | Notification required |

### Voided Arrest Documentation

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Omniform System | Void arrest through Omniform — note: this **still generates an arrest number** |
| OLBS ARREST WORKSHEET | Must include: reason for void, property returned, witnesses, authorizing supervisor, time of release |

> **Exam Alert:** Even a voided arrest generates an arrest number through the Omniform System. The OLBS Arrest Worksheet must document 5 key elements: (1) reason for void, (2) property returned, (3) witnesses, (4) authorizing supervisor, and (5) time of release.

### Sealing Fingerprint Records

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Voided arrest + prisoner was fingerprinted | **BCS intake supervisor** faxes DCJS to seal the record |

> **Sergeant Focus:** When a voided arrest involves a fingerprinted prisoner, the BCS intake supervisor must fax DCJS to seal the record. Sergeants should verify this step is completed — failure to seal creates a permanent record from an invalid arrest.

### When ADA Requests a Void (Not Decline Prosecution)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Desk officer confers with **Legal Bureau** |
| 2 | Legal Bureau confers with **ADA** |
| 3 | Legal Bureau attempts to **resolve** the issue |

> **Exam Alert:** When an ADA requests that an arrest be voided (as opposed to simply declining prosecution), the desk officer does NOT void the arrest directly. The desk officer contacts the **Legal Bureau**, which then confers with the ADA and attempts to resolve the matter. The authority to release rests solely with the **Police Department**.

### Authority to Release

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Authority** | Rests **solely with the Police Department** |
| ADA role | DA has discretion to decline prosecution but does NOT have authority to direct release |

## P.G. 210-15: Release of Prisoners Arrested by Other Agencies

### Conflict Resolution

| Situation | Resolution |
|-----------|-----------|
| Conflict between desk officer/BCS supervisor and arresting officer | **Precinct commander** or **duty captain** makes the final decision |

> **Sergeant Focus:** When there is a disagreement between the desk officer or BCS supervisor and an arresting officer from another agency regarding prisoner release, the matter is escalated to the precinct commander or duty captain. Sergeants should not attempt to resolve inter-agency disputes at their level — escalate promptly.

## P.G. 210-16: Release at Complaint Room by Direction of ADA

### Decline Prosecution Process

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| ADA designation | ADA designates case as "Decline Prosecution" |
| Documentation | Arresting officer obtains **Decline Prosecution Letter** from ADA |
| Warrant check | Desk officer conducts **warrant check** before release |
| Release | Prisoner released after warrant check is clear |

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Rapsheet delay | Release is **NOT delayed** solely because rapsheet has not been received |
| Active warrant found | Process prisoner **solely on warrant authority** (NOT on original charges) |
| Hospitalized prisoner | Desk officer directs release after confirming **no active warrant** |

> **Exam Alert:** If a prisoner is being released on a decline prosecution and a warrant check reveals an active warrant, the prisoner is processed **solely on the warrant** — the original charges are dropped per the ADA's decline. The prisoner is NOT processed on both.

> **Exam Alert:** Release cannot be delayed just because the rapsheet has not arrived. If the ADA has issued a Decline Prosecution Letter and the warrant check is clear, the prisoner is released.

## P.G. 210-17: Special Category Prisoners

### Special Categories

Certain prisoner categories require additional notifications, procedures, or handling considerations. These include but are not limited to:

| Category | Additional Considerations |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Foreign nationals | Consular notification requirements |
| Diplomatic/consular immunity | Verify credentials; contact Operations Unit |
| Military personnel | Notify military authorities |
| Government officials | Additional notification chain |
| Juveniles | Separate handling under P.G. 215 |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Special category prisoners require additional notifications beyond standard procedures. Sergeants must verify that all required notifications are made and documented. Consular notification for foreign nationals is a legal requirement, not discretionary.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **Voided arrest vs. decline prosecution** — Know the fundamental difference: invalid arrest vs. valid arrest that DA won't prosecute
2. **Supervisor conferral** — Arresting officer MUST confer with patrol supervisor (or desk officer/BCS supervisor if unavailable) before release
3. **Authority to release** — Rests solely with the Police Department, not the DA
4. **ADA requests void** — Desk officer contacts Legal Bureau, NOT direct action
5. **Warrant check before release** — Mandatory for decline prosecution releases
6. **Active warrant found** — Process on warrant ONLY, not original charges
7. **Rapsheet delay** — Does NOT delay release
8. **Voided arrest documentation** — 5 elements on OLBS Arrest Worksheet
9. **Sealing records** — BCS intake supervisor faxes DCJS for fingerprinted voided arrests
10. **Other agency conflicts** — Precinct commander/duty captain resolves

## Sergeant Study Notes

- The **patrol supervisor** is the primary authority for consent to release — this is a direct supervisory responsibility
- **Authority to release** rests solely with the Police Department — even when an ADA requests a void, the process goes through the Legal Bureau
- When an ADA requests a void (not decline prosecution), the desk officer contacts the **Legal Bureau** first — never void directly at ADA request
- The **5 elements** of the OLBS Arrest Worksheet for voided arrests must be complete: reason, property returned, witnesses, authorizing supervisor, time of release
- **Sealing fingerprint records** after a voided arrest is mandatory — the BCS intake supervisor handles this by faxing DCJS
- For decline prosecution releases, the **warrant check** is mandatory before release — but do not delay release solely for a rapsheet
- If a warrant is found during release processing, the prisoner is held on the **warrant only** — original charges are dropped per the decline
- Inter-agency release conflicts are resolved at the **precinct commander/duty captain** level — do not attempt to resolve at the sergeant level
- Notification requirements after a voided arrest are extensive — patrol borough commander, Identification Unit, BCS, CO of arresting officer, and desk officer of command of occurrence
