## P.G. 210-04: Prisoners Requiring Medical/Psychiatric Treatment

> **Updated:** 01/14/26 | R.O. 8 | 10 pages

### When to Remove a Prisoner to the Hospital

A prisoner must be removed to the hospital when any of the following conditions exist:

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Prisoner is ill | Remove to hospital |
| Prisoner is injured | Remove to hospital |
| Prisoner is intoxicated | Remove to hospital |
| Prisoner is emotionally disturbed | Remove to hospital |
| Prisoner requests medical attention | Remove to hospital |

> **Exam Alert:** Ill, intoxicated, and emotionally disturbed prisoners are NOT to be placed in holding pens (see P.G. 210-08). They must be removed to a hospital for evaluation and treatment.

### Which Hospital?

| Situation | Hospital |
|-----------|----------|
| Non-emergency | **Local** hospital |
| Life-threatening | **Nearest** hospital |
| Narcotics/dangerous substance ingestion | Go **DIRECTLY** to **nearest** hospital (NEVER to stationhouse) |

> **Exam Alert:** A perp who ingests narcotics or a dangerous substance goes directly to the **nearest** hospital — NEVER to the stationhouse first. The exam may present a scenario asking whether to process the arrest first; the answer is always go directly to the hospital.

### Narcotics Ingestion — Triple Notification

When a prisoner ingests narcotics or a dangerous substance, notify **Patrol Supervisor AND Communications Section** three times:

| Notification | When |
|-------------|------|
| 1st | Upon observing or suspecting ingestion |
| 2nd | When transport to hospital is initiated |
| 3rd | When 84 (arrived) at hospital |

Additionally, inform **EMS and hospital personnel** of the type and quantity of drugs ingested.

### UMOS — Initial Medical Treatment Duties

| Duty | Details |
|------|---------|
| Request ambulance | Accompany prisoner to hospital from place of arrest if necessary |
| Notify | Desk Officer |
| Medical Treatment of Prisoner Form | Prepare when prisoner: receives treatment, refuses treatment after claiming injury/illness, has apparent need for treatment, has previously treated injuries, has prescribed medication, has communicable disease status |

> **Exam Alert:** A prisoner who **volunteers** their positive communicable disease status triggers MTP preparation **only** if not already on the Prisoner Movement Slip.

### Spit Hood Procedures (NEW in R.O. 8)

| Role | Duty |
|------|------|
| **ESU Member** | Apply spit hood if prisoner is spitting at or threatening to spit at any person |
| ESU Member | Monitor and remain with subject wearing spit hood until relieved by a medical professional with minimum EMT certification, unless hood is removed |
| ESU Member | If disagreement between EMS and ESU about application/removal, request **ESU Supervisor** to respond |
| **ESU Supervisor** | Confer with EMS supervisor to resolve disagreement about spit hood application or removal |

> **Exam Alert:** Only **ESU personnel** may apply a spit hood — NOT regular MOS. If there is a disagreement between EMS and ESU about applying or removing the spit hood, the **ESU Supervisor** resolves it by conferring with the EMS supervisor.

### MTP Triggers — When to Prepare

Prepare **MEDICAL TREATMENT OF PRISONER (PD244-150)** for each prisoner who:

| Trigger |
|---------|
| a. Receives medical/psychiatric treatment |
| b. Refuses treatment after claiming injury or illness |
| c. Is in apparent need of treatment |
| d. May require prescribed medication |
| e. Volunteers communicable disease status (if not on Prisoner Movement Slip) |
| f. Already has previously treated injuries |
| g. **Has a spit hood placed on them** (NEW) |

### Medical Treatment of Prisoner Form (MTP) — Key Details

| Detail | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Reserve function | Enter info from Section I of MTP into MTP app in FORMS by selecting the "Reserve" function; number generated by FORMS entered on the MTP |
| Copy of MTP | **MUST** accompany prisoner until no longer in NYPD custody |
| HHC entitled to copy | Allow hospital personnel to make a **copy** of the MTP upon completion of treatment |
| Signing | If perp, doctor, or hospital staff refuses to sign, note on MTP and in Digital Activity Log |
| MTP copies to DOC | Forward **three copies** (white, blue, pink) with prisoner to DOC |
| Buff copy retained | BCS retains **fourth (buff) copy** at Borough Court Section facility |

### Section I of MTP — Transport Numbers

| Transport Method | Number Recorded |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Taken by **EMS** (ambulance) | **ACR** # (Ambulance Call Report) |
| Taken by **other means** (RMP, etc.) | **PCR** # (Patient Care Report) |

> **Memory Aid — ACR vs PCR:** **A** = Ambulance (ACR), **P** = Police/other (PCR). The ACR number comes from the ambulance attendant. The PCR number also comes from the ambulance attendant (received from them).

### Remarks Section of MTP

| Content | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Specific treatment instructions from EMS | — |
| Whether EMS took perp to hospital room for further treatment | — |
| **RMA** | Check-box for RMA; does **NOT** go in remarks |

### Supervisor at Scene

| Duty | Details |
|------|---------|
| If perp removed from scene direct to hospital | Ensure MTP is prepared per steps above and ADDITIONAL DATA (Preparation of MTP) |
| Review and sign | **Section I** of MTP |

> **See Also:** PG 210-01 — Release of confidential status information (communicable disease): Only **DCLM** (Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters) can authorize release. Isolate prisoner if isolated cell is available.

### Arresting/Escorting Officer — Transport to Hospital

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 15 | **Rear cuff** AND place **leg restraints** before transporting to hospital |
| 15a | Handcuffs and leg restraints will be **DOUBLE LOCKED** |
| 16 | **Remain with prisoner at all times** in hospital |
| 17 | Notify D.O. of prisoner status **prior to transport** from local hospital to Bellevue/Elmhurst |

| 20 | Notify D.O. precinct of arrest/BCS re: Medical Alert Emblem and PIN |

### Prisoners Requiring Psychiatric Admission

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Notify D.O. of psych status | Before leaving local hospital for Bellevue/Elmhurst |
| Transport | Ride in **(private) ambulance** with prisoner to Bellevue/Elmhurst |
| Digital Activity Log | Enter **PIN** # (Patient Identification Number) obtained from hospital intake |

> **Exam Alert:** The **PIN** (Patient Identification Number) is different from the ACR or PCR number. The PIN is obtained from hospital intake people — NOT from EMS. The exam tests: ACR = initial EMS transport, PCR = initial RMP/other transport, PIN = secondary transport to Bellevue/Elmhurst.

### Psych Admission — Where Transported?

| Gender | Hospital |
|--------|----------|
| **Men** | **Bellevue** |
| **Women** | **Elmhurst General** |

> **Exam Alert:** Generally, men go to **Bellevue** and women go to **Elmhurst** for psychiatric admission. Transport is done by hospital via **private contract** — NOT FDNY EMS.

### Psych Admission — D.O. Pct of Arrest/Boro Court Section

| Duty | Details |
|------|---------|
| Confer with medical staff | Medical staff determines appropriate transfer method and hospital |
| Determine additional resources | D.O. determines if additional resources needed for the transfer |
| Additional resources decision | **Patrol Supervisor** decides if extra personnel or equipment (like ESU) are needed |

### Desk Officer — Psych Treatment Documentation

| Duty | Details |
|------|---------|
| Command Log entry | **PIN** # (NOT ACR or PCR), Medic Alert information, new location of prisoner |

> **Exam Alert:** For psych transfers, the Desk Officer logs the **PIN number** in the Command Log — NOT the ACR or PCR. The exam may present choices mixing up these numbers.

### Escorting Officer at Hospital

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 23 | Request **room change** if security is inadequate (Escorting officer does this — NOT Patrol Supervisor!) |
| 24 | Do NOT remove handcuffs or leg restraints unless requested by attending physician |
| 24a | If physician requests removal, **inform physician of circumstances of arrest** |
| 24b | If physician **still** requests removal, request response of **patrol supervisor** to evaluate need for additional personnel and equipment (including ESU) to ensure safety during treatment prior to removal |
| 25 | If asked to leave exam room: remain **immediately outside room**, attempt to maintain **visual contact** |
| 26 | Make digital Activity Log entries; request attending physician to sign entries |
| 26a | If physician refuses to sign, indicate refusal on Activity Log |
| 27 | Complete entries on **MTP** form |
| 28 | Notify D.O. precinct of arrest (who notifies BCS immediately) if prisoner admitted to any hospital |

> **Prior Test:** Doctor asks the guarding officer to remove cuffs — what is the correct response? Explain circumstances of arrest. If doctor still wants them off, request **Patrol Supervisor** to respond. Do NOT remove without PS present and authorizing. BUT if doctor asks you to **leave the room**, you do NOT need the PS — just step outside and maintain visual contact.

> **Exam Alert:** There is a critical distinction between a doctor asking to **remove restraints** (requires PS response and authorization) versus asking the officer to **leave the examination room** (officer complies, steps outside, maintains visual contact — no PS needed). The exam frequently tests this difference.

> **Exam Alert:** The command **MUST** take custody within **4 hours** from notification by DOC. If unable, a notification must be made to the **Borough Court Section supervisor**, who will coordinate with DOC Custody Management Unit.

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|

| Doctor requests medication delivery | D.O. shall: (a) Check medication name against Property Clerk Invoice, (b) Complete "R.T.O." function in FORMS Property, sign rank and name, include pertinent details in Remarks, (c) Release medication to UMOS for delivery, (d) Make Command Log entry |
| UMOS delivering medication | Request physician to **sign** digital Activity Log; if refuses, note refusal with full title and name |

### Doctor Gives Prisoner Medicine To Go

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Take custody of meds | **No voucher** needed |
| Give meds to | **Nurse Practitioner (NP)** at BCS until prisoner needs to self-administer |
| NP not authorized to administer | If NP cannot administer that form of medication, prisoner must be brought to hospital |

> **Exam Alert:** When a doctor provides medicine for a prisoner to take, the medication goes to the **Nurse Practitioner at BCS** — no voucher is needed. But if the NP is not authorized to administer that specific form of medication, the prisoner must go back to the hospital.

### Medication Brought to Precinct by Family/Household Member

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Acceptance | UMOS are **NOT required to accept** medication from relatives/household members |
| Invoicing | UMOS are **NOT required to invoice** prisoner medications brought to command by relatives/household members |
| Conditions for MTP | Prepare MTP when properly identified relative appears at Dept facility where prisoner is located AND: (a) indicates prisoner has medical condition/needs medication, AND/OR (b) brings medication in pharmacy container with prisoner's name |

> **Exam Alert:** UMOS are **NOT required to accept** AND are **NOT required to invoice** medication from family. Both points are testable — the updated procedure now explicitly states both.

### Prisoner Not at Facility Where Family Brought Medication

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| UMOS | Determine if prisoner still in Dept custody; ascertain location; inform supervisor |
| Supervisor notified | Contact D.O./supervisor at facility where prisoner located — **DUAL Command Log entry** (both sides make entries) |
| Command Log entries include | Time of notification, prisoner's name, name of desk officer/supervisor receiving/making notification |
| D.O./Supervisor where prisoner located | Prepare Medical Treatment of Prisoner Form |

### Prisoner Security — Additional Personnel for Hospital Transport

Patrol supervisors and desk officers shall assign additional personnel to accompany arresting officers based on:

| Factor |
|--------|
| a. Type of offense (violent crime, resisting arrest, etc.) |
| b. Physical **size** of prisoner |
| c. Prisoner's **past history** (history of violence, escape attempts) |
| d. Suspected **escape opportunity** |
| e. **Lack of physical signs of injury** (may be faking injury to attempt escape) |

> **Exam Alert:** One of the factors for assigning additional personnel is **lack of physical signs of injury** — meaning the prisoner might be faking to create an escape opportunity. This is a counterintuitive factor that the exam may test.

### Prisoner on Hospital Bed or Gurney

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Handcuff to bed/gurney | **Double locked** to the bed or gurney |
| Second pair of cuffs | Use second pair to handcuff prisoner's free wrist to bed/gurney **before** removing first pair for treatment |
| Constant observation | Keep prisoner under constant observation even when handcuffed to bed/gurney |

### Long Chain Handcuffs and Leg Restraints

Patrol supervisors and desk officers shall direct that **long chain handcuffs and leg restraints** be used if available and appropriate to secure prisoners.

### ESU Consideration

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| ESU | Supervisors may consider the assignment of ESU for **extraordinary circumstances** |

### Leg Restraints — Mandatory Use

| Situation | Leg Restraints Required? |
|-----------|------------------------|
| ALL prisoners transported to hospital for medical treatment | **YES — MUST** be used |
| Prisoner refused to be fingerprinted | **YES — MUST** be used |
| Potential escape risk | YES |
| Punitive purposes | **NO — PROHIBITED** |

> **Exam Alert:** Leg restraints **MUST** be used for ALL prisoners transported to a hospital for medical treatment AND for prisoners who refused fingerprinting. If D.O. determines extenuating circumstances preclude placing leg restraints, a **Command Log entry** must be made detailing the reasons.

> **Sergeant Focus:** When a prisoner requires medical treatment, the patrol supervisor must be notified. The sergeant should verify that the desk officer has properly documented the condition, arranged transport, and assigned a guard. Prisoners with medical conditions cannot simply be held in the cellblock. The supervisor must also evaluate the need for additional personnel, ESU, and ensure leg restraints are applied for hospital transport.

## P.G. 210-05: Bedside Arraignment

### Purpose

Reduce manpower required to guard hospitalized prisoners by expediting the arraignment process at the hospital.

### Mobile Livescan Device (MLD)

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| What it is | Portable fingerprint scanner that transmits via cellular to DCJS |
| Staffing requirement | At least **2 MOS present** during MLD fingerprinting |
| Prohibited use | MLD may NOT be used for any purpose other than hospitalized prisoner fingerprints |

> **Exam Alert:** The MLD requires a minimum of **2 MOS present** during fingerprinting. It is strictly limited to hospitalized prisoner fingerprints — using it for any other purpose is prohibited.

### Bedside Arraignment Procedures

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Legal assistance | Guarding member asks prisoner about need for legal assistance and/or interpreter |
| Fingerprinting | Use MLD with 2 MOS present |
| Delay notification | If bedside arraignment delayed **more than 6 days**, notify borough court section |
| Bail posting | Bail is posted at **borough criminal court**, NOT at the stationhouse |

> **Exam Alert:** Bail for a hospitalized prisoner is posted at the **borough criminal court** — never at the stationhouse. The 6-day notification threshold is also frequently tested.

### Prisoner Released from Hospital Before Arraignment

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Medical release | Guarding member obtains **signed medical release** from hospital |
| Restraint | Escort prisoner **rear cuffed** to court |
| Transport | Standard prisoner transport procedures apply |

> **Sergeant Focus:** If a hospitalized prisoner is discharged before arraignment, the guarding member must obtain a signed medical release and escort the prisoner to court in rear cuffs. The sergeant should verify this process is followed — prisoners should not be released from the hospital without proper documentation and escort.

| **Meal Period 3** | 1800-1900 |

> **Exam Alert:** The $1.80 maximum meal cost and the prohibition on hot liquids are high-frequency exam items. Know all three meal periods and the exact dollar amount.

### Additional Meal Authorization

| Situation | Authority |
|-----------|-----------|
| Medical condition requiring additional meals | **Desk officer** authorizes |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer has authority to authorize additional meals beyond the standard three periods. Sergeants should ensure meal requests are handled promptly — denying food to a prisoner who missed a meal period is not acceptable.

## P.G. 210-07: Prisoners — Unusual Occurrence

> **Updated:** 10/27/25 | R.O. 112 | 2 pages

### Purpose

To make notifications and investigate certain unusual occurrences related to prisoners.

### Definition — Unusual Occurrence

A prisoner in NYPD custody, or an unarraigned prisoner in NYC DOC custody, who:

| Type |
|------|
| a. **Assaults** a member of the service |
| b. **Escapes** or attempts to escape |
| c. Is **sexually assaulted/harassed** or alleges they were sexually assaulted/harassed by another prisoner or any other person |
| d. Is involved in any other occurrence of an **unusual nature** |

### Desk Officer

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Notify **commanding officer/duty captain** AND **borough court section supervisor** concerned |
| 2 | Notify **Operations Unit**, including: (a) Name and sex of prisoner/child in custody, (b) Charge for which prisoner/child is held |
| 3 | Notify **Internal Affairs Bureau Command Center** if prisoner **escapes or attempts to escape** |

> **Exam Alert:** IAB is notified ONLY for escape/attempted escape — NOT for all unusual occurrences. The D.O. notifies CO/Duty Captain + BCS Supervisor + Operations Unit for ALL unusual occurrences, but IAB only for escapes.

### Commanding Officer / Duty Captain

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 4 | Conduct **investigation** of incident |
| 5 | Prepare report on **Typed Letterhead** |
| 6 | Forward **three copies** of report to **Chief of Department, DIRECT** (adult prisoners); **two copies** if child in custody dies |
| 7 | Forward **one additional copy** to EACH of the following: |

| Report Distribution |
|---------------------|
| a. Chief of Patrol (DIRECT) |
| b. Chief of Internal Affairs |
| c. Chief of Training |
| d. Commanding Officer, Criminal Justice Bureau |
| e. Commanding Officer, Performance Monitoring Unit |
| f. Each intermediate command |
| g. CO of member's command (if member not assigned to reporting command) |

> **Exam Alert:** The report goes to **Chief of Department DIRECT** (3 copies) — not through channels. One additional copy goes to each of 7 recipients listed above.

### Prisoner Escape

In the event of a prisoner escape, arresting/escorting officers will **immediately**:

| Action |
|--------|
| Notify **radio dispatcher** |
| Notify **immediate supervisor** (e.g., desk officer of member's command) |
| Conduct **search** to apprehend the prisoner |

> **Sergeant Focus:** For unusual occurrences, the CO/Duty Captain must personally investigate and prepare typed letterhead reports. The distribution is extensive — 3 copies DIRECT to Chief of Dept, plus 7 additional individual copies. For escapes, IAB must be notified in addition to Operations Unit.

### Notes on Related Procedures

| If... | Then comply with... |
|-------|-------------------|
| Prisoner sustains physical injury or attempts suicide | P.G. 221-03, "Reporting and Investigation of Force Incident or Injury to Persons During Police Action" |
| Prisoner dies or is likely to die | P.G. 221-05, "Person Dies or Sustains a Serious Injury and Is Likely to Die in Police Custody or in Connection With Police Action" |

## Common Exam Angles

1. **$1.80 per meal** — Know the exact dollar figure
2. **No hot liquids** — Coffee, tea, and hot chocolate are all prohibited
3. **Three meal periods** — 0500-0600, 1200-1300, 1800-1900
4. **MLD requirements** — 2 MOS present, hospitalized prisoners ONLY
5. **6-day delay notification** — Notify borough court section if bedside arraignment delayed more than 6 days
6. **Bail location** — Borough criminal court, NOT the stationhouse
7. **Desk officer meal authority** — Can authorize additional meals for medical/unusual conditions or missed meal periods
8. **Expense report** — Due by the 15th of each month
9. **ACR vs PCR vs PIN** — ACR = ambulance transport, PCR = RMP/other transport, PIN = secondary transport to psych facility
10. **Which hospital** — Non-emergency = local; life-threatening = nearest; narcotics ingestion = DIRECTLY to nearest (NEVER stationhouse)
11. **Psych admission destination** — Men to Bellevue, Women to Elmhurst; transport by private ambulance (not FDNY EMS)
12. **Cuffs off vs. leave room** — Doctor asks to remove restraints = request PS response (evaluate additional personnel/ESU); doctor asks to leave room = comply, step outside, maintain visual contact (no PS needed)
13. **UMOS not required to accept OR invoice family medication** — Both acceptance and invoicing are discretionary (R.O. 8 explicitly states both)
14. **Medicine to go from doctor** — Goes to Nurse Practitioner at BCS; no voucher; if NP cannot administer, prisoner goes to hospital
15. **DOC custody** — D.O. Command of Arrest requests DOC take custody; if refused, BCS Supervisor resolves with DOC Custody Management Unit
16. **4-hour deadline** — MUST take custody within 4 hours from DOC notification; if unable, notify BCS Supervisor
17. **Lack of physical signs of injury** — Factor for additional personnel (prisoner may be faking)
18. **Triple notification for narcotics ingestion** — PS and Communications Section notified 3 times (observation, transport initiated, arrived at hospital)
19. **Spit hood — ESU only** — Only ESU personnel may apply a spit hood; ESU Supervisor resolves disagreements with EMS
20. **Leg restraints mandatory for hospital transport** — ALL prisoners transported to hospital MUST have leg restraints; also required for refusal to be fingerprinted
21. **MTP copies** — 3 copies (white, blue, pink) to DOC; buff copy retained at BCS; MTP MUST accompany prisoner at all times
22. **Operations Coordinator** — Responsible for ensuring all MTPs are finalized in FORMS and filing buff copy
23. **Unusual Occurrence — IAB notification** — IAB notified ONLY for escape/attempted escape; Operations Unit notified for ALL unusual occurrences
24. **Unusual Occurrence report distribution** — 3 copies DIRECT to Chief of Dept, plus 1 copy each to 7 additional recipients

## Sergeant Study Notes

- The **desk officer** controls meal authorization — both standard meals and additional meals for medical or unusual conditions
- Expense reports for prisoner meals must be submitted by the **15th of each month** — track this deadline
- The **MLD** is a restricted-use device: hospitalized prisoner fingerprints only, with 2 MOS present
- When bedside arraignment is delayed more than **6 days**, the borough court section must be notified — the sergeant should monitor this timeline
- Prisoners released from the hospital before arraignment must be escorted **rear cuffed** to court with a **signed medical release**
- Ill, intoxicated, and emotionally disturbed prisoners must be removed to hospital — they cannot be held in standard cellblock or holding pen conditions
- The patrol supervisor must be notified whenever a prisoner requires medical treatment
- Know the **three transport numbers**: ACR (ambulance), PCR (RMP/other), PIN (secondary psych transport) — each has a different source and context
- The **escorting officer** requests room changes when hospital security is inadequate — NOT the Patrol Supervisor
- Removing restraints at a doctor's request: PS evaluates need for **additional personnel and equipment (including ESU)** to ensure safety prior to removal — but leaving the exam room does NOT require a PS
- For narcotics ingestion, the **triple notification** to PS and Communications is a high-frequency exam item
- **Medication returned to command** after delivery will be "invoiced again" — know this procedural detail
- When DOC refuses custody, the BCS Supervisor resolves the issue with DOC's Custody Management Unit
- **Spit hoods** are applied ONLY by ESU; ESU Supervisor resolves any EMS disagreement — this is a new testable procedure
- **Leg restraints are mandatory** for ALL hospital transport and for prisoners who refused fingerprinting — extenuating circumstances require Command Log entry
- The **Operations Coordinator** is responsible for ensuring all MTPs are finalized within FORMS — a sergeant-level responsibility
- For **unusual occurrences**, IAB is notified only for escape/attempt — the report goes DIRECT to Chief of Dept (3 copies)
- **Prisoner on hospital bed/gurney**: use second pair of cuffs to secure free wrist to bed BEFORE removing first pair for treatment; double lock; constant observation even when cuffed
- MTP must accompany prisoner at ALL times until no longer in NYPD custody — HHC is entitled to a photocopy
