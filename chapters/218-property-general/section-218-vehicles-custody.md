# Section 218 — Vehicles in Police Custody

## P.G. 218-12: Safeguarding Vehicles in Police Custody

**Date Effective:** 07/25/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 65 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To safeguard vehicles in police custody at the stationhouse.

### Immobilization Methods

The recovering member must immobilize the vehicle without causing damage by:

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| a | Remove rotor or coil wire and attach to printout of INVOICE |
| b | Physically block vehicle with another vehicle or stationary structures |
| c | Utilize wheel boots |
| d | Locate starter relay/fuse or fuel pump relay/fuse; photograph location, remove, and attach to INVOICE |

> **Exam Alert:** Members should **NOT disconnect the battery** due to potential risk of damaging some vehicles. Contact **Auto Crime Unit** (0800–2300) with questions.

- Prepare WARNING NOTICE — VEHICLE IN POLICE CUSTODY (PD571-1211) and attach to windshield **only after** fingerprint processing is completed
- Ensure vehicle is locked
- Inform relieving desk officer of vehicle location

> **Exam Alert:** A vehicle may be safeguarded at the command for **48 hours** when not required as evidence or forfeiture. After 48 hours → removed to Property Clerk storage facility.

## P.G. 218-13: Inventory Searches of Automobiles and Other Property

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To protect property, ensure against unwarranted claims of theft, and protect members and others against dangerous instrumentalities.

### Automobile Inventory Search Areas

Search includes: glove compartment, console, map pockets, under seats, under floor mats, under/behind dashboard, ashtrays, air vents, under hood, and trunk.

### Forcing Open Locked Items

Force open trunk/glove compartment only if it can be done with **minimal damage**, UNLESS:

| Exception | Condition |
|-----------|-----------|
| a | Reasonably suspect weapons, explosives, hazardous materials or contraband |
| b | Contents in plain view |
| c | Contents can be inferred from outward appearance (gun cases, toolboxes) |
| d | Consent of owner to break open |

### Consent Requirements (Step 2d)

1. Ask for consent in a manner that elicits a clear "yes" or "no"
2. Follow up: *"I can only conduct this inventory if you consent"*
3. Inform owner significant damage may occur
4. Ask: *"Do you understand?"*
5. If conducted, offer RIGHT TO KNOW BUSINESS CARD (PD142-012 or PD142-013)
6. **If owner does NOT consent, cannot inventory unless search warrant obtained**

> **Exam Alert:** No consent = no inventory of locked items (absent exigent circumstances). A **search warrant** is required.

- Remove all valuables from vehicle and invoice on a **separate** PROPERTY CLERK INVOICE

## P.G. 218-14: Vehicle Stolen and Recovered Within New York City

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To process and dispose of vehicles stolen and recovered within NYC.

### Key Steps

| Role | Key Actions |
|------|------------|
| Recovering Member | Obtain NYSPIN info, prepare WORKSHEET, request patrol supervisor |
| Patrol Supervisor | Verify accuracy and completeness of WORKSHEET |
| Recovering Member | Notify registered owner ASAP; at least one telephone attempt |
| Recovering Member | Prepare ROTATION TOW-OWNER NOTIFICATION (PD571-1210) regardless of phone notification |
| Desk Officer | Release vehicle to owner AFTER cancellation of alarm |
| Desk Officer | Determine if patterns of theft/dumping exist; initiate appropriate action |
| Commanding Officer | Review to ensure alarm cancelled and owner notified |

> **Exam Alert:** If final insurance settlement was received, enter name of insurance company on INVOICE under "Lien Holder or True Owner" and notify the insurance company.

## P.G. 218-18: Photographing Stolen Evidence Vehicles When an Arrest Is Made

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To expeditiously release stolen recovered vehicles where arrest is made and vehicle was NOT used in conjunction with any other crime.

### Key Rule

**THE VEHICLE MAY BE RELEASED TO THE OWNER/REPRESENTATIVE AT THE STATIONHOUSE WITHOUT A RELEASE FROM THE DISTRICT ATTORNEY/CORPORATION COUNSEL** — provided evidence photographs are taken.

### Required Photographs

VIN, registration sticker, license plates, each side of vehicle (including vent windows, door locks, handles), front/back, interior (ignition lock, console, radio, dashboard), motor, and all damage.

> **Exam Alert:** Operable vehicles remain at command for **48 hours** pending owner arrival. If unclaimed → Rotation Tow Program → tow facility for **30 days** → pound.

## P.G. 218-19: Invoicing Vehicles/Property as Arrest/Investigatory Evidence or for Forfeiture Proceedings or to Determine True Owner

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Pages:** 4

### Vehicles MUST Be Seized as Arrest Evidence When Arrests Made For:

| Charge | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Larceny of vehicle | Grand Larceny Auto (unless value ≤$100 for auto or ≤$1,000 for motorcycle) |
| Unauthorized use of vehicle | — |
| Criminal possession of stolen property (vehicle) | — |
| Possession of vehicle with altered VIN | — |
| Leaving scene of collision — serious/critical injury | — |
| Assault or homicide — vehicle used as weapon | — |
| Illegal Registration — VTL Section 401(1)(a) | Operating an Unregistered Vehicle |

### Vehicles MAY Be Seized for Forfeiture When Used to Transport:

- Controlled Substances (Article 220, Penal Law)
- Gambling Records (Sections 225.20 and 415, PL)
- Untaxed Cigarettes (State Tax Law/Admin Code)
- Equipment used in promoting pornography (Article 410, PL)
- Equipment used in unauthorized recording of sound (Section 420.05, PL)
- Illegally possessed firearms/rifles/shotguns (Section 265, PL)

> **Exam Alert:** In ALL cases of vehicle seizure (arrest evidence or forfeiture), a **VEHICLE SEIZURE FORM (PD571-1218)** must be completed and served on the arrestee per P.G. 218-48.

## P.G. 218-21: Rotation Tow

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 9

### Purpose

To process non-evidence stolen or apparently abandoned vehicles, including motorcycles, recovered within NYC.

### Key Definition

**APPARENTLY ABANDONED VEHICLE** — Unoccupied vehicle abandoned per VTL Section 1224 OR observed under circumstances indicating it may have been stolen but no alarm transmitted.

**DOUBLE TOW** — Additional tow truck needed for unusual recovery (embankment, overturned, wedged). NOT authorized solely because vehicle has no wheels.

> **Exam Alert:** Double Tow is approved by selecting "Yes" in the "Double Tow" caption in FORMS-P when preparing the PCI, and entering the TAX # of the authorizing officer. A vehicle with **no wheels** does NOT automatically qualify for a double tow — a tow should handle that with one truck. *(PG 218-21)*

### VTL 1224 — Abandoned Vehicle Time Chart

| Time Left Unattended | Condition |
|---------------------|-----------|
| More than 6 hours | No plates |
| More than 24 hours | Illegally parked |
| More than 48 hours | Legally parked initially, then becomes illegally parked (e.g., alternate side); 48 hours later it is considered abandoned |
| More than 96 hours | On private property without permission of owner |

> **Exam Alert:** Know these VTL 1224 time thresholds cold — they appear frequently in test scenarios involving whether a vehicle qualifies for Ro-Tow. *(PG 218-21)*

### What is NOT Ro-Tow (Memory Aid)

> **Memory Aid — CHASED FIT DOM:** The situations that disqualify a vehicle from Ro-Tow are remembered as: **C**rime (Dept. Tow), **H**ighway (Authorized or Dept. Tow), **A**rrest made (except photo release), **S**afeguard (Aided/Collision), **E**vidence (Dept. Tow), **D**etermine True Owner — **F**orfeiture, **I**nvestigation, **T**ruck (large) — **D**erelict (Sanitation), **O**wner on scene AND recovered prior to police, **M**oped (limited use motorcycle). *(PG 218-21)*

### Vehicle Is NOT Qualified for Rotation Tow If:

1. Arrest made in conjunction with recovery (except photo-release cases)
2. Required as evidence or held for investigation
3. Used in commission of a crime
4. Subject to forfeiture proceedings
5. Meets derelict guidelines
6. Large truck or limited-use motorcycle (moped)
7. Owner on scene and recovers own vehicle
8. Must be safeguarded due to owner inability to remove (prisoner, aided, collision)

> **Exam Alert:** A DSNY (Sanitation Department) sticker or notification does **NOT** automatically make a vehicle eligible for Ro-Tow. The **final determination is made by the UMOS** based on a visual inspection using criteria on the rear inside cover of the Ro-Tow Log. *(PG 218-21)*

### Desk Officer Responsibilities

- Notify next participating tow operator in rotation **IMMEDIATELY**
- Determine availability **PRIOR TO** furnishing vehicle information
- Tow operators **DO NOT** have option of deciding availability **AFTER** being told vehicle type/condition
- Prepare REPORT OF VIOLATION if tow operator refuses AFTER being informed of vehicle
- On limited access highways: **ONLY** franchise tow operator may tow

> **Exam Alert:** If a tow operator refuses an assignment AFTER being informed of vehicle type and condition, prepare a **Report of Violation (LD 6)** AND forward to the **Dept. of Consumer/Worker Protection (DCWP)**. The next tow operator in sequence is used whenever a tow operator is not available for any reason. *(PG 218-21)*

> **Exam Alert:** Do **NOT** cancel the alarm or notify the owner until the tow operator advises that the vehicle has been removed to the storage facility. *(PG 218-21)*

### Commanding Officer/Designee Responsibilities

- **Daily** inspection of Ro-Tow Log for previous 24 hours (sign in remarks column with corrective action)
- **Daily** inspection of Ro-Tow 30-day file to ensure no vehicle is held more than 30 days
- Complaints involving license violations/overcharges by tow operators: **LD6** forwarded to DCWP
- Complaints of theft/other unlawful acts by tow company: **Complaint Report (61)** forwarded to DCWP (for information only)

> **Exam Alert:** Overcharges by tow operators = **LD6 to DCWP**. Theft or other unlawful acts by tow company = **61 to DCWP** (duplicate, informational only). Know which form applies to which complaint type. *(PG 218-21)*

### Tow Operator Rights

Participating tow operators are permitted to view the **Rotation Towing Logs** of precincts within their assigned zone to verify their rotational sequence.

> **See Also:** PG 218-20 — Delivery of vehicle to Property Clerk (48-hour deadline, Traffic Safety Sergeant responsibility).

### Tow Operator Timelines

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Day 1 | Vehicle towed to operator's facility |
| Day 8–30 | Vehicle may be delivered to Property Clerk Auto Pound (weekdays) |
| Day 30 | Vehicles remaining **MUST BE DELIVERED** to Auto Pound by tow operator |
| After 15 days at Auto Pound | Vehicle/motorcycle disposed of according to law |

> **Exam Alert:** An **owner WILL NOT be notified** nor alarm cancelled until tow operator advises vehicle has been removed to storage facility.

> **Exam Alert:** **Final determination** of Rotation Tow eligibility must be made by a uniformed member based on visual inspection — Sanitation Department notification does NOT confer automatic eligibility.

### Other Agencies Authorized to Use Rotation Tow

- NYC Parks Department
- Seagate PD (60th Precinct)

## P.G. 218-48: Vehicle Seizure at Time of Arrest

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To ensure opportunity for a post-seizure retention hearing for individuals from whom a vehicle is seized.

### Procedure

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Arresting Officer | Prepare INVOICE per P.G. 218-19 |
| 2 | Arresting Officer | Complete VEHICLE SEIZURE FORM (PD571-1218) for "Forfeiture" or "Arrest Evidence" |
| 3 | Arresting Officer | Insert precinct vehicle seizure number from COMMAND VEHICLE SEIZURE INDEX (PD571-091) |
| 5 | Arresting Officer | **Activate Body-Worn Camera** when serving VEHICLE SEIZURE FORM |
| 6 | Arresting Officer | Have defendant/operator sign form (note refusal if applicable) |
| 10 | Desk Officer | Ensure four photocopies distributed: defendant, ADA, invoicing officer, arresting officer |

> **Exam Alert:** Failure to serve VEHICLE SEIZURE FORM may result in the **inability to prosecute forfeiture cases**.

> **Exam Alert:** Vehicles registered to third parties are generally **NOT seized for forfeiture** unless beneficial ownership or specific knowledge by the third party can be proven.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Commanding officers must ensure compliance is part of the command's **monthly self-inspection program** monitored by Quality Assurance Section.
