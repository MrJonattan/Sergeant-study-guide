# Section 218 — Property: General

## Overview

Section 218 governs all aspects of invoicing, processing, safeguarding, and disposing of property coming into police custody. This is one of the largest sections in the Patrol Guide, covering 44 procedures that address everything from general invoicing to specialized evidence handling (firearms, DNA, computers, fireworks), stolen vehicle processing, currency handling, vendor/peddler enforcement, and handgun license surrenders.

## Source

NYPD Patrol Guide, Section 218 (Property — General)
Source PDF: `public-pguide4-ocr.pdf`

## Learning Objectives

1. Understand general invoicing procedures and property categories (P.G. 218-01)
2. Know the process for returning property at command and voiding invoices (P.G. 218-02)
3. Understand vehicle safeguarding, inventory search rules, and the 48-hour limit (P.G. 218-12, 218-13)
4. Know stolen vehicle processing for NYC-recovered, out-of-city, and Port Authority scenarios (P.G. 218-14, 218-15, 218-16)
5. Master Rotation Tow Program procedures and the 30-day tow operator timeline (P.G. 218-21)
6. Understand firearms evidence marking, packaging, and chain of custody (P.G. 218-23)
7. Know found property, decedent's property, and currency processing rules (P.G. 218-26, 218-28, 218-35, 218-36)
8. Understand vendor/peddler property seizure and forfeiture procedures (P.G. 218-39, 218-40, 218-41)
9. Know handgun license surrender investigation requirements (P.G. 218-45)
10. Understand vehicle seizure at arrest and the VEHICLE SEIZURE FORM requirement (P.G. 218-48)
11. Know DNA evidence immediate delivery chain: Command → Police Lab → OCME (P.G. 218-49)
12. Understand property retention timelines — 1 year for property, 15 days for vehicles (P.G. 218-55)

## Study Tips

- **Property Categories** — Memorize all categories: Arrest Evidence, DNA Arrest Evidence, Investigatory, DNA Investigatory, Forfeiture, Decedent's Property, Found Property, Safekeeping, Peddler Property, Amnesty, Determine True Owner, Parking Enforcement, Photo Release, Rotation Tow, Other
- **48-hour rule** appears twice: vehicles at command (218-12/218-20) and VIN check delivery to Auto Pound (218-21)
- **Retention deadlines**: 1 year for property, 15 days for vehicles — failure to submit retention request = disposal
- **Currency handling** differs based on type: evidence vs. non-evidence vs. numismatic/sentimental vs. forfeiture vs. red dye
- **Inventory search consent**: If owner doesn't consent to opening locked items, a search warrant is needed (218-13)
- **FORMS Property** is the central system — know the FORMS failure procedures (218-57)
