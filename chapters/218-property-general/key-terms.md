# Section 218 — Key Terms, Timeframes, Fees, and Forms

## Property Categories

| Category | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| **Arrest Evidence** | Property seized for court presentation |
| **DNA Arrest Evidence** | Property seized for court presentation containing potentially probative DNA evidence |
| **Investigatory** | Property seized for investigation with COMPLAINT REPORT and no ARREST WORKSHEET |
| **DNA Investigatory** | Investigatory property containing potentially probative DNA evidence |
| **Forfeiture** | Property used to facilitate a crime or representing proceeds/substituted proceeds |
| **Decedent's Property** | Personal property of a deceased person |
| **Found Property** | Non-contraband property found by a member or civilian |
| **Peddler's Property** | Items removed from vendors (stands, carts) |
| **Amnesty** | Firearms surrendered via Cash for Guns Program |
| **Safekeeping** | Property to be returned to owner |
| **Determine True Owner** | Property where true owner cannot be determined and no immediate arrest warranted |
| **Photo Release** | Recovered stolen vehicles where arrest made and vehicle not used in other crime |
| **Rotation Tow** | Non-evidence stolen or apparently abandoned vehicles/motorcycles recovered in NYC |

## Firearms Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Firearm** | Includes rifles, shotguns, pistols, revolvers, starter's pistols, zip guns, air/CO2 guns, homemade/modified guns, simulated/toy firearms, ghost guns, 3D-printed firearms, stabilizing brace firearms |
| **Cartridge** | Live ammunition — bullet + shell casing as one unit |
| **Fired Bullet** | Part of cartridge fired through barrel |
| **Shell Casing** | Metal casing remaining in/ejecting from firearm after bullet leaves |

## Vehicle Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Apparently Abandoned Vehicle** | Unoccupied vehicle abandoned per VTL 1224 OR observed under circumstances indicating it may be stolen but no alarm transmitted |
| **Double Tow** | Additional tow truck for unusual recovery (embankment, overturned, wedged) — NOT authorized solely because vehicle has no wheels |

## Other Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Regulated Waste** | Blood/infectious materials in liquid/semi-liquid state; contaminated sharps; pathological wastes |
| **Contraband** | Property whose mere possession is prohibited by law (not property merely suspected stolen) |
| **Numismatic/Sentimental Value** | All gold coins, all U.S. silver coins dated 1964 or prior, extremely old bills |
| **Negotiable Instrument** | Cash equivalents: money orders, traveler's checks, bank checks |
| **Co-Occupant** | Person residing on a permanent basis in same premises with deceased |
| **General Vendor** | Person selling goods other than food at retail in a public area |
| **Food Vendor** | Person selling food at retail in any public place |
| **Distributor** | Person/organization engaged in sale/distribution of goods for resale by a general vendor |
| **Handgun** | Any pistol or revolver listed on a Handgun License |

## Critical Timeframes

| Timeframe | Rule | Source |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **48 hours** | Maximum time vehicle may be safeguarded at command (non-evidence) | 218-12 |
| **48 hours** | Maximum time for delivery of seized vehicle to Property Clerk Auto Pound | 218-20 |
| **30 days** | Tow operator MUST deliver vehicle to Auto Pound | 218-21 |
| **15 days** | After 15 days at Auto Pound, vehicle disposed of according to law | 218-21 |
| **15 days** | Retention deadline for investigatory motor vehicles/boats | 218-55 |
| **1 year** | Retention deadline for investigatory property (non-vehicle) | 218-55 |
| **6 years** | Filed INVOICES maintained (each July, previous year boxed to Property Clerk warehouse) | 218-02 |
| **10 days** | Desk officer must authorize release or denial of investigatory evidence | 218-02 |
| **5 days** | Claimant may request CO/designee review after denial of investigatory evidence | 218-02 |
| **30 days** | Handgun license surrender investigation must be completed | 218-45 |
| **60 days** | Investigation if found property not returned by interstate company/airline/bank | 218-27 |
| **24 hours** | Report prepared after disposal of perishable vendor food | 218-39 |
| **120 hours** | Sexual Offense Evidence Collection Kit used within this period (or medical discretion) | 218-33 |
| **0100 hours** | Daily deadline for firearms requiring lab analysis to be delivered to patrol borough office | 218-23 |
| **1 minute** | Time for vendor to begin moving in exigent circumstances | 218-39 |
| **2 hours** | Minimum time vendor must stay away from location in exigent circumstances | 218-39 |
| **3 business days** | Property Clerk issues check for federal/state forfeiture upon written request | 218-38 |
| **6 hours** | FORMS outage threshold — under 6 hours: prepare WORKSHEET and wait | 218-57 |
