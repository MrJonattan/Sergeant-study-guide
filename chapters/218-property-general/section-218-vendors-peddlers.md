# Section 218 — Vendor and Peddler Property

## P.G. 218-39: Seizure, Removal, and Disposition of Property from General and Food Vendors

**Date Effective:** 12/31/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 78 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To remove property from unlicensed food/general vendors or licensed general vendors peddling in violation of law.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **General Vendor** | Person who peddles/sells/leases goods or services, other than food, at retail in a public area |
| **Food Vendor** | Person who peddles/sells food at retail in any public place |
| **Pushcart** | Wheeled vehicle used by vendor, movable with or without motor, no DMV registration required |
| **Stand** | Movable/portable/collapsible structure used to display or store merchandise |
| **Seizure** | Removal pending forfeiture by City's Law Department — NOT returned without written authorization |
| **Removal** | Confiscation — may be returned under certain circumstances even if OATH summons not adjudicated |
| **Property Clerk Division Seal** | Non-releasable, barcoded, pre-numbered security seal for vendor property bags |

### Procedure

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1a | Member | Serve Civil Summons Returnable to OATH |
| 1b | Member | Place seized property in large plastic bags; twist top closed |
| 1c | Member | Affix Property Clerk Division seal with prongs facing inward |
| 4 | Member | Deliver pushcart, stand, goods to command of occurrence |
| 5 | Member | Do NOT permit vendor to assist in removal or ride in Department vehicle (except arrest) |
| 6 | Member | Return vendor's license after issuing summons |
| 7 | Member | Prepare WORKSHEET marked "Removed/Safeguard" |
| 9 | Member | Notify food rescue organization when DOH employee determines food meets sanitary requirements |

### Desk Officer Responsibilities

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 12 | Examine sealed bag — verify seal number matches WORKSHEET and summons |
| 16 | Notify Property Clerk Division if large amount of property |
| 17 | Notify Department of Sanitation for expeditious disposal of spoiled food |
| 18 | Have OFFICIAL LETTERHEAD report prepared within **24 hours** after perishable food disposed |
| 19 | Mail copy of report to vendor via U.S. Mail |

### Exigent Circumstances

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 23 | Notify vendor to stop selling and begin to move within **one minute** |
| 24 | Inform vendor not to return for at least **two hours** (vendor must be notified of time period) |

> **Exam Alert:** Supervisory span of control for peddler enforcement: **one supervisor for five police officers**. Dedicated supervisors present at all property seizures when practical.

> **Exam Alert:** If a sealed bag breaks, a supervisor will **re-bag** using the FORMS "repack" function. Do NOT remove property or the original seal — place entire bag with seal into new bag with new seal.

> **Sergeant Focus:** CO must designate sufficient supervisors to frequently inspect perishable foods so items can be condemned and disposed of when necessary.

## P.G. 218-40: General Vendor Distributor Forfeiture Procedure

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To confiscate vehicles of unlicensed general vendor distributors and institute forfeiture proceedings.

### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Distributor** | Person/organization engaged in sale, consignment, or distribution of goods for sale/resale by a general vendor (excluding owners who personally transport and sell their own goods in compliance with law) |
| **Public Space** | All publicly-owned property between property lines on a street, including parks, plazas, roadways, sidewalks, piers, stadiums, terminals |

### Key Steps

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Member | Serve Civil Summons Returnable to OATH for NYC Admin Code 20-474.1 |
| 2 | Member | Confiscate vehicle and contents for forfeiture |
| 3 | Member | Remove vehicle to precinct; comply with P.G. 218-12 |
| 4 | Member | Prepare WORKSHEET — select "FORFEITURE" |
| 6 | Desk Officer | Have vehicle removed to Property Clerk storage facility |
| 9 | Desk Officer | Prepare PEDDLER FORFEITURE QUESTIONNAIRE (PD260-155) when claimant arrives |
| 10 | Desk Officer | Have claimant sign "Peddler Property — Acknowledgment of Demand" tear-off portion |
| 11 | Desk Officer | Forward documents within **one day** of demand to NYC Law Department |

### Required Vehicle Signage

Distributor vehicles must display a sign with:
- Licensee's name and address
- Business telephone numbers
- Words "General Vending Distributor"
- Department of Consumer and Worker Protection telephone complaint number

> Letters/numerals must be at least **1.5 inches** in height, **0.25 inches** in width, black and white.

## P.G. 218-41: Unlicensed Peddler Forfeiture Program

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To institute forfeiture proceedings against property seized from unlicensed street vendors.

### Scope

ONLY property of:
- An **unlicensed** food or general vendor, OR
- A **licensed** general vendor violating restricted streets provision (Admin Code 20-465, subd. 1)

is subject to forfeiture. Confiscate only property on display and any cart, table, or vehicle used to display it.

### Key Steps

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Member | Comply with P.G. 218-39 |
| 2 | Member | Select "FORFEITURE" as Property Category |
| 4 | Desk Officer | Prepare PEDDLER FORFEITURE QUESTIONNAIRE upon claimant arrival |
| 6 | Desk Officer | Forward documents within **one day** to NYC Law Department |
| 9 | Desk Officer | Comply with steps 4–8 for EACH SUBSEQUENT claimant |

> **Exam Alert:** Corporation Counsel is responsible for serving the claimant with the summons and complaint — but documents must be forwarded within **one day** of claimant's appearance.

## P.G. 218-42: Obtaining and Returning Property Clerk Division Seals

**Date Effective:** 12/31/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 78 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To record distribution of Property Clerk Division seals and ensure integrity of returned seals.

### Key Points

- Members below rank of captain on peddler enforcement must carry sufficient seals
- Seals are issued in sets of **20, consecutively numbered**
- Member signs PROPERTY CLERK DIVISION SEAL DISTRIBUTION CARD (PD521-1411)
- Completed CERTIFICATION OF PROPERTY CLERK DIVISION SEALS (PD521-061) delivered to immediate supervisor
- Unused/damaged seals are **destroyed** — NOT re-issued
- **Integrity Control Officer** makes random inspections

> **Exam Alert:** Member must notify CO **without delay** when a Property Clerk Division seal is damaged or missing.

## P.G. 218-43: Removal and Storage Fees for Licensed Food Vendors

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To collect removal and storage fees for licensed food vendor property.

### Fee Schedule

| Item | Fee |
|------|-----|
| Vehicle, cart, stand removed by Department vehicle | **$65.00** |
| Vehicle, cart, stand NOT removed by Department vehicle | **$20.00** |
| Goods seized with vendor's vehicle/cart/stand | **$10.00** |
| Goods only seized | **$20.00** |
| Storage fee | **$5.00 per day** (or part of day) |

> **Exam Alert:** No storage fee for the day property is **initially delivered** to the command.

> **Exam Alert:** Goods/foods and vehicle/cart/stand stored separately are charged **separate** storage fees.

### Special Rules

- Licensed vendor with valid license: property returned upon demand **without payment of any fee**
- Unlicensed peddler property: NOT returned upon payment — subject to forfeiture per P.G. 218-41
- DO NOT release to owner who alleges property was stolen and refuses to pay
- Fees forwarded to **Audits and Accounts Unit** on next business day

## P.G. 218-44: Removal and Storage Charges (Vehicles/Boats)

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To collect and forward fees for vehicles or boats in police custody (abandoned, collision, unoccupied boat found adrift — NOT evidence/impounded vehicles).

### Fee Schedule

| Item | Fee |
|------|-----|
| Towing fee (Department tow/launch) | **$25.00** |
| Storage fee (abandoned or collision vehicle/boat) | **$5.00 per day** (no fee for delivery day) |
| Storage fee (stolen vehicle/boat or boat found adrift) | **$5.00 per day** commencing **3 days** after notice to owner by registered mail |

- Prepare FEE RECEIPT (PD122-017) in numerical sequence
- Forward fees to **Audits and Accounts Unit** each day (excluding weekends/holidays)

> **Exam Alert:** DO NOT release vehicle/boat to an owner who alleges it was stolen and **refuses to pay** charges due.
