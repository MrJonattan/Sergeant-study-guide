# Section 219 — Fuel, Heating, and Facility Operations

## P.G. 219-11: Computerized Fuel Dispensing

**Date Effective:** 12/31/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 78 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To provide for the proper use of the computerized fuel dispensing system.

### Vehicle Identification Box (VIB)

The VIB is the in-vehicle transponder for automated fuel dispensing. Fuel is dispensed using card-based authorization.

### Card Types

| Card | Color | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Master | **Green** | Administrative control |
| CRALO | **Yellow** | Command Radio Auto Liaison Officer |
| Non-Fleet | **Orange** | Non-fleet vehicles |
| Private | **Red** | Private vehicles authorized for official use |
| Operator | **White** | Individual operator cards |

### Electric Vehicles

- **ChargePoint cards** issued for electric vehicle charging
- **Strictly prohibited** to charge personal vehicles using Department ChargePoint cards or stations

> **Exam Alert:** It is **strictly prohibited** to charge personal vehicles at Department charging stations.

> **Exam Alert:** Know the fuel card colors: Green = Master, Yellow = CRALO, Orange = Non-Fleet, Red = Private, White = Operator.

## P.G. 219-12: Fuel Delivery Supervision

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To ensure proper supervision of fuel deliveries to Department facilities.

### Key Points

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Tank fill level | **90% ullage amount** (maximum fill capacity) |
| Monitoring | Electronic tank monitor system |
| Supervision | Delivery must be supervised by designated member |

> **Exam Alert:** Fuel tanks are filled to **90% ullage** — never completely full.

## P.G. 219-13: Heating Oil Delivery

**Date Effective:** 05/20/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 31 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To ensure proper procedures for receiving heating oil deliveries.

## P.G. 219-28: Emergency Power Outages

**Date Effective:** 01/18/22 | **Last Revision:** 1.0.4 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To address emergency power outages and ensure non-essential equipment is removed from emergency electrical receptacles.

### Procedure

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Desk Officer/Supervisory Officer | **Immediately** notify Building Maintenance Section (24/7) |
| 1a | Same | Record notification in Telephone Record |
| 2 | Same (if generator activated) | Survey command — ensure only **essential emergency equipment and critical computer systems** operate on emergency power |
| 3 | Same | Have all **non-essential equipment disconnected** from emergency electrical receptacles |

### Non-Essential Equipment (Disconnect Immediately)

- Air conditioners
- Vending machines
- Refrigerators
- Microwaves

### Emergency Receptacle Identification

- **Red cover plate** over the receptacle, OR
- **Raised lettering** on the outlet receptacle

### Fuel Tank Responsibilities

| Configuration | Responsibility |
|--------------|----------------|
| **Single fuel tank** (heating + generator) | Command maintains fuel level **above 50%** |
| **Two fuel tanks** (separate) | Command maintains heating tank; Building Maintenance Section maintains generator tank |

### CO Quarterly Survey

- Commanding officers conduct **quarterly survey** to ensure generator rooms, mechanical rooms, and electrical rooms are used **only for their intended purpose** — not as locker rooms, storage rooms, etc.
- Items in these rooms may create a **fire hazard**

> **Exam Alert:** Commands with a **single fuel tank** must maintain the level **above 50%**.

> **Exam Alert:** Only qualified **Building Maintenance Section** personnel should start or repair the emergency generator. No outside agencies without BMS supervisor approval (absent exigent circumstances).

> **Sergeant Focus:** CO must conduct a **quarterly survey** of generator/mechanical/electrical rooms to ensure proper use only.
