# Section 219 — Information Technology and Communications Equipment

## P.G. 219-14: Computer Systems — Security and Integrity

**Date Effective:** 12/31/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 78 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To establish security and integrity procedures for Department computer systems.

### Integrity Control Officer (ICO) Responsibilities

| Duty | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Monthly audit | Review **Domain Awareness System (DAS)** usage for **5 random members** per month |
| Password management | Ensure compliance with password policies |
| Access control | Monitor authorized access |

### Key Rules

- Department reserves the right to **review all computer activity** at any time
- Members have **no expectation of privacy** on Department computer systems
- Unauthorized access or use subject to disciplinary action

> **Exam Alert:** The ICO must conduct a **monthly DAS audit of 5 random members**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Department reserves the right to review **all** computer activity — members have no privacy expectation on Department systems.

## P.G. 219-15: Portable Radios

**Date Effective:** 12/31/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 78 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To provide for the issuance, use, and accountability of portable radios.

### Key Points

- Maintain RADIO ASSIGNMENT LOG at each command
- Radio must be carried in an **authorized carrying case**
- Lost radio = **negligence finding** if not in authorized carrying case at time of loss

### Notifications for Lost/Stolen Radio

| Notify |
|--------|
| Operations Unit |
| Electronics Section |
| Internal Affairs Bureau (IAB) |
| Stolen Property Inquiry Unit |

> **Exam Alert:** If a portable radio is lost and was **not in an authorized carrying case**, the member is deemed **negligent**.

> **Exam Alert:** Four notifications required for lost/stolen radio: **Operations Unit, Electronics Section, IAB, and Stolen Property Inquiry Unit**.

## P.G. 219-31: License Plate Reader Devices

**Date Effective:** 11/16/22 | **Last Revision:** 1.0.112 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To provide for control, use, maintenance, and accountability of License Plate Reader (LPR) devices.

### Definition

**LICENSE PLATE READER (LPR)** — A specialized camera and computer that captures license plate numbers, converts them to text, and compares against a "hot list" (stolen, wanted, suspended registration/insurance, etc.).

### Critical Operational Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| NYSPIN verification | **Absolutely essential** — verify through NYSPIN before any law enforcement action |
| Not connected to NYSPIN | LPR is NOT automatically connected to NYSPIN |
| State of registration | LPR does NOT scan state of registration |
| Partial matches | LPR will activate on partial scan match |
| Hot list currency | Do NOT use if hot list not updated within **24 hours** |

### Procedure

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Member | Inspect LPR for operability at beginning of tour; report to desk officer |
| 2 | Member | Activate LPR; record in Activity Log last hot list update time |
| 4 | Desk Officer | Account for each LPR device at beginning of each tour; record in Command Log |
| 5 | Desk Officer | Enter vehicle LPR assignment on CPR Roll Call System |
| 6 | Desk Officer | Contact ITB Service Desk for LPR repairs |
| 7 | Member (on alarm) | **Verify through NYSPIN** before any law enforcement action |
| 8 | Member | Take law enforcement action (arrest, summons, rotation tow) |

### Restrictions

- LPR used **only** for stolen/wanted files and furtherance of criminal investigations
- Any other use is **strictly prohibited**
- Camera lens cleaned only with glass cleaner applied with soft cloth
- LPR equipment must **not be moved** from mounting location
- Vehicle does not go out of service for LPR repair

> **Exam Alert:** **ALWAYS verify through NYSPIN** before any law enforcement action based on an LPR alarm — the device activates on **partial matches** and is NOT connected to NYSPIN.

## P.G. 219-32: Department Mobile Digital Devices

**Date Effective:** 03/26/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 15 | **Pages:** 4

### Definition

**MOBILE DIGITAL DEVICE** — Any smartphone, tablet, or portable computer issued by the Department.

### Key Rules for Members

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Official use only | Use Department devices for **official business exclusively** |
| Personal devices | Do NOT use personal devices for official business |
| On-duty personal phone | NOT permitted during tour (except authorized meal or emergency) |
| Carry requirement | Carry Department smartphone while on duty |
| NYPD Phone Sticker | Affix NYPD PHONE STICKER (PD589-090) to phone case |
| No app installation | Do NOT install any applications unless approved by Deputy Commissioner, IT |
| No content deletion | Do NOT delete any content (texts, photos, videos, notes, voicemail) |
| Foreign travel | Request ITB Information Security Desk permission **5 business days** prior |

### Lost/Stolen/Damaged

- Notify supervisor and ITB Telecommunications Unit **immediately**
- Process per P.G. 219-20, "Loss or Theft of Department Property"
- Complete DEPARTMENT MOBILE DIGITAL DEVICE REPAIR/REPLACEMENT/RETURN REQUEST (PD589-163)

### Off-Duty Considerations

- Members may secure Department smartphone at command when off-duty
- Members may carry phone off-duty and remove phone case (to not identify as MOS)

### Privacy Notice

- Members have **no right to privacy** in any feature of Department mobile devices
- All communications, photos, video may be accessed by Department **at any time** without prior knowledge
- Content may be Rosario material and/or discoverable in criminal prosecutions, civil lawsuits, CCRB/IAB investigations

### Operational Considerations

- Members are **permitted but not required** to photograph/video record encounters with public
- Shall NOT record situations where BWC use would be prohibited
- Shall NOT document demonstrations or political activity (unless per P.G. 212-71, 212-72)
- Crime Scene Unit remains solely responsible for documenting crime scenes

> **Exam Alert:** Members must **not delete any content** from Department mobile devices. All content is potentially discoverable.

> **Exam Alert:** Permission from ITB Information Security Desk required **5 business days** before taking a Department phone to a foreign country.

> **Sergeant Focus:** CO/designated supervisor must return Department smartphone to ITB when member has change in duty status (retired, suspended, modified).
