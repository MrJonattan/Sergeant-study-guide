# Section 303 — Review Questions

## Practice Questions

**1. How often must a Borough Commander confer with the Internal Affairs Bureau, Community Affairs Bureau, Quality Assurance Section, Office of Equal Employment Opportunity, and Personnel Orders Division?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Semi-annually** (after the initial briefing upon assignment to command). (A.G. 303-01, duty #5a)

</details>

---

**2. How often must a Borough Commander inspect each subordinate command's "Confidential Performance Profile"?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Annually.** (A.G. 303-01, duty #6)

</details>

---

**3. The Borough Executive Officer, Operations supervises which of the following?**
- A. Borough Administrator and Borough Adjutant
- B. Borough Operations Commander, Borough Special Projects Coordinator, and Borough specialty units
- C. Borough Adjutant and Borough Operations Commander
- D. Borough Administrator and Borough Operations Commander

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The Borough Executive Officer, Operations supervises the Borough Operations Commander, Borough Special Projects Coordinator, and Borough specialty units. (A.G. 303-02, duty #1)

</details>

---

**4. Which borough executive officer is responsible for oversight of discipline and the evaluation process?**
- A. Borough Executive Officer, Operations
- B. Borough Commander
- C. Borough Executive Officer, Administration
- D. Borough Adjutant

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** The Borough Executive Officer, Administration provides operational and administrative oversight for discipline and personnel issues including the evaluation process. (A.G. 303-03, duty #2c, #2e(2))

</details>

---

**5. The Borough Adjutant reports to which position?**
- A. Borough Commander
- B. Borough Executive Officer, Operations
- C. Borough Executive Officer, Administration
- D. Borough Operations Commander

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** The Borough Adjutant reports to the Borough Executive Officer, Administration. (A.G. 303-04, duty #1)

</details>

---

**6. Which of the following is NOT a responsibility of the Borough Adjutant?**
- A. Participate in Early Intervention Committee
- B. Monitor sick records with attention to Chronic Absent members
- C. Supervise special event preparation
- D. Review CCRB conferral reports for accuracy and completeness

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Supervising special event preparation is a duty of the Borough Operations Commander (303-05), not the Borough Adjutant. (A.G. 303-04 and 303-05)

</details>

---

**7. The Borough Operations Commander develops crime reduction strategies for how many specific crime categories?**
- A. 5
- B. 7
- C. 9
- D. 11

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Nine categories: sex crimes, robberies, assaults, burglaries, larcenies/thefts, vehicle theft, domestic violence, firearms violence, and other crimes/conditions as they emerge. (A.G. 303-05, duty #3)

</details>

---

**8. The Borough Executive Officer, Operations must liaison with which coordinator regarding emergency preparedness and mobilizations?**
- A. Borough Special Projects Coordinator
- B. Borough Counterterrorism Coordinator
- C. Borough Administrator
- D. Borough Adjutant

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The Borough Executive Officer, Operations must liaison with the Borough Counterterrorism Coordinator to ensure operational readiness of emergency preparedness and mobilizations. (A.G. 303-02, duty #4)

</details>

---

**9. How often must the Borough Commander convene meetings with members of the borough's executive staff and the Department's line organizations?**
- A. Monthly
- B. Semi-annually
- C. Quarterly (or as needed)
- D. Annually

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Quarterly (or as needed) to discuss command level management issues and resolve challenges faced by members on patrol. (A.G. 303-01, duty #9)

</details>

---

**10. The Borough Operations Commander oversees the selection of command youth coordination officers after conferral with whom?**
- A. Borough Commander
- B. Borough Executive Officer, Operations
- C. Precinct/PSA commanding officers
- D. Community Affairs Bureau

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** The Borough Operations Commander oversees selection of command youth coordination officers after conferral with precinct/PSA commanding officers. (A.G. 303-05, duty #5)

</details>
