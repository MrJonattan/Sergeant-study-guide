# Section 202 — Supervisor-Level Duties & Responsibilities

> **Source:** NYPD Patrol Guide, Section 202 (P.G. 202-18 through 202-39)
> **Exam Relevance:** THIS IS THE MOST CRITICAL SECTION for the Sergeant Promotional Exam.
> Sergeants must master every duty listed for the Desk Officer and Patrol Supervisor roles.

---

## P.G. 202-18: Desk Officer (CRITICAL — 5 pages)

> **Rank Requirement:** Must be a SERGEANT or above.
> **Core Responsibility:** Responsible for ALL police operations within the command during the tour.

### Before Assuming Duties

- **SHALL** inspect the arrest processing area and prisoner detention areas BEFORE assuming desk officer duties
- **SHALL** sign name and time in the Command Log when assuming duties
- **SHALL** inspect the property locker at the commencement of each tour
- **SHALL** query the Medical Division Sick Reporting System at the start of the tour
- **SHALL** check Department email at the start of the tour

### Personnel Accountability

- Conduct an immediate investigation when any MOS is unaccounted for at the start of the tour
- If the member is STILL unaccounted for after **2 hours**, comply with A.G. 324-17 ("AWOL" procedures)
- This is a mandatory escalation — the 2-hour threshold triggers formal AWOL reporting

### Access Control — Behind the Desk

- Do NOT permit unauthorized persons behind the desk
- **Authorized persons ONLY:**
  - Members of the Service (MOS)
  - Judicial officers
  - Medical Examiner (ME)
  - Corporation Counsel
  - Comptroller
  - District Attorney (DA)
  - Non-local law enforcement officers with a judicial warrant

### Facility Management

- **SHALL** visit ALL areas of the command facility at least ONCE each tour
- **SHALL** check restrooms and locker rooms for structural defects permitting visual access (privacy violations)
- **SHALL** inspect parking facilities around the command (no illegal parking by MOS)
- **SHALL** check Department computer systems frequently
- **SHALL** check Department email at start of tour AND at the **4th hour** of the tour

### Direct Supervision

The desk officer directly supervises the following positions:

| Position | Procedure |
|----------|-----------|
| Switchboard Operator | P.G. 202-14 |
| Police Attendant | P.G. 202-15 |
| Command Clerk | (administrative) |
| Patrolwagon Operator | P.G. 202-17 |
| Arrest Processing Officer | P.G. 202-09 |
| Community Guide | (community liaison) |

### When Leaving the Desk

- **SHALL** sign name and time in the Command Log when leaving desk officer duties
- Ensure proper relief before departing

### Key Exam Points — Desk Officer

1. The desk officer is responsible for ALL operations within the command during the tour
2. Property locker inspection happens at the START of every tour
3. AWOL threshold is 2 hours
4. Email must be checked at start of tour AND at the 4th hour
5. Memorize the list of persons authorized behind the desk
6. Facility walk-through required at least ONCE per tour
7. Restroom/locker room checks are for structural privacy defects

## P.G. 202-19: Patrol Supervisor (CRITICAL)

> **Rank Requirement:** Must be a SERGEANT or above.
> **Core Responsibility:** Supervise all police FIELD operations during the tour.

### Roll Call

- **SHALL** conduct roll call **5 minutes** after the start of the tour
- Verify attendance, uniform compliance, equipment readiness

### Field Supervision — Mandatory Responses

- **SHALL** immediately respond to:
  - Weapons calls
  - Serious crimes in progress
  - Burglaries
  - Emergencies
- Response is MANDATORY, not discretionary

### Patrol Visits

- **SHALL** visit foot and RMP patrol posts **FREQUENTLY** at **IRREGULAR** intervals
- **SHALL** visit hospital and special posts at least **ONCE** each tour
- **SHALL** ensure members do NOT congregate or engage in unnecessary conversation
- **SHALL** use AVL (Automatic Vehicle Locator) in DAS (Domain Awareness System) to monitor RMP locations

### Vehicle and Safety Oversight

- **SHALL** report serious seatbelt deficiencies to the platoon commander
- **SHALL** report seatbelt vandalism to the Internal Affairs Bureau (IAB)
- **SHALL** evaluate driving habits of subordinates and take corrective action when needed

### Administrative Duties

- **SHALL** sign OFFICER PROFILE REPORTS monthly
- **SHALL** deliver signed Officer Profile Reports by the **5th of the month**

### Key Exam Points — Patrol Supervisor

1. Roll call is 5 minutes after tour start
2. Must respond to weapons/serious crimes/burglaries/emergencies
3. Patrol visits must be FREQUENT and IRREGULAR (predictable patterns defeat the purpose)
4. Hospital/special posts visited at least ONCE per tour
5. Seatbelt deficiencies go to platoon commander; seatbelt VANDALISM goes to IAB
6. Officer Profile Reports due by the 5th of each month
7. AVL/DAS is the tool for monitoring RMP locations remotely

## P.G. 202-20: Supervisor of Neighborhood Safety Team (NST)

> **Rank Requirement:** Sergeant or above, SOLELY assigned to NST supervision.
> **Core Rule:** NSTs may NOT be deployed without a sergeant or above solely assigned.

### Deployment and Supervision

- **SHALL** be solely assigned to NST supervision — cannot be split between NST and other duties
- **SHALL** supervise no more than **5 NST members** per tour
- **SHALL** report to the special operations lieutenant
- **SHALL** ensure all NST members have completed required NST training before deployment

### Accountability Tools

- **SHALL** systematically review Body Worn Camera (BWC) video of NST officers
- **SHALL** use the CRAFT SUPERVISOR FEEDBACK FORM for commendable actions observed
- CRAFT form is a positive reinforcement/accountability tool

### Key Exam Points — NST Supervisor

1. NSTs CANNOT deploy without a solely assigned sergeant
2. Maximum 5 NST members per supervisor per tour
3. Maximum 3 NST members (including supervisor) per vehicle (from P.G. 202-05)
4. BWC review is SYSTEMATIC, not random or optional
5. CRAFT form is for commendable actions

## P.G. 202-21: Training Sergeant

> **Rank Requirement:** Sergeant

### Key Duties

- Coordinate and deliver in-service training within the command
- Ensure all members complete required training on schedule
- Maintain training records and track compliance
- Identify training deficiencies and recommend corrective training
- Coordinate with the Police Academy and borough training units

### Sergeant Relevance

The Training Sergeant role is a common assignment for newly promoted sergeants. Accountability for training compliance (firearms requalification, CPR recertification, legal updates) falls on this position.

---

## P.G. 202-25: Field Intelligence Sergeant

> **Rank Requirement:** Sergeant

### Key Duties

- Supervise the Field Intelligence Officer (P.G. 202-16)
- Coordinate intelligence-gathering activities within the command
- Ensure intelligence activities comply with Department guidelines and legal standards
- Brief command staff on intelligence findings and crime trends
- Coordinate with the Intelligence and Counterterrorism Bureau as needed

### Sergeant Relevance

This role requires balancing proactive intelligence gathering with legal and policy constraints. The FI Sergeant ensures that field intelligence feeds into the command's crime-fighting strategy and CompStat preparations.

---

## P.G. 202-26: Lieutenant — Platoon Commander

> **Rank Requirement:** Lieutenant
> **Core Responsibility:** Responsible for ALL command operations during the assigned tour (platoon).

### General Duties

- **SHALL** cause necessary changes and adjustments in roll call based on conditions
- **SHALL** schedule meals so that a supervisor is on duty at ALL times (no gap in supervision)
- **SHALL** query the NYPD 360 CCRB Dashboard regularly to monitor complaint trends
- **SHALL** respond to radio runs unannounced to observe subordinate performance firsthand

### Platoon-Specific Duties

| Platoon | Special Responsibility |
|---------|----------------------|
| **First Platoon** (midnight tour) | Review reports from the previous 24 hours |
| **Second Platoon** (day tour) | Responsible for vehicle maintenance and weekly vehicle inspection |

### Sergeant Relevance

The platoon commander is the sergeant's immediate supervisor in many commands. Understanding the LT's responsibilities helps sergeants anticipate what information will be requested and what standards will be enforced. The meal-scheduling rule (always maintain supervisor coverage) directly affects sergeant assignments.

---

## P.G. 202-27: Special Operations Lieutenant

> **Rank Requirement:** Lieutenant

### Key Duties

- Oversee specialized units within the command (NST, anti-crime, conditions teams)
- Coordinate with the Community Affairs Officer (P.G. 202-10)
- Manage deployment of specialized resources based on crime data
- Supervise the NST Supervisor (P.G. 202-20) and related personnel

### Sergeant Relevance

NST supervisors and CRT supervisors report through the special operations lieutenant. Understanding this chain of command is essential for sergeants assigned to specialized units.

---

## P.G. 202-28: Operations Coordinator

> **Rank Requirement:** As designated by the commanding officer

### Key Duties

- Coordinate daily operations and resource allocation within the command
- Prepare and distribute operational orders and directives
- Maintain operational readiness of command resources
- Serve as a liaison between the commanding officer and operational units

### Sergeant Relevance

The operations coordinator is a key administrative contact for sergeants needing resource allocation, scheduling changes, or operational guidance.

---

## P.G. 202-29: Command Integrity Control Officer (ICO)

> **Rank Requirement:** As designated by the commanding officer

### Key Duties

- Monitor command integrity and compliance with Department policies
- Conduct random integrity checks and audits
- Review use-of-force reports, arrest documentation, and other accountability records
- Report integrity concerns to the commanding officer
- Coordinate with the Internal Affairs Bureau on integrity matters

### Sergeant Relevance

Sergeants should be aware that the ICO monitors compliance within the command. Supervisory lapses (failure to review BWC, inadequate use-of-force documentation, etc.) may be flagged by the ICO. Understanding this role reinforces the importance of thorough supervisory documentation.

---

## P.G. 202-30: Executive Officer (XO)

> **Rank Requirement:** Captain (typically)

### Key Duties

- Serve as second-in-command of the precinct/command
- Oversee administrative operations and personnel matters
- Act as commanding officer in the CO's absence
- Coordinate with the operations coordinator and platoon commanders
- Review and approve administrative reports and requests

### Sergeant Relevance

The XO is typically the captain who handles day-to-day administrative oversight. Sergeants interact with the XO on personnel issues, scheduling, and administrative approvals.

---

## P.G. 202-31: Supervisor of Patrol (Captain)

> **Rank Requirement:** Captain

### Key Duties

- Overall supervision of patrol operations across multiple platoons
- Review patrol deployment and staffing levels
- Evaluate patrol supervisor and platoon commander performance
- Ensure compliance with patrol standards and directives

---

## P.G. 202-32: Duty Captain

> **Rank Requirement:** Captain

### Key Duties

- Serve as the borough/citywide captain on duty during the assigned tour
- Respond to major incidents within the borough/area of responsibility
- Coordinate resources across multiple commands when needed
- Act as the link between precinct-level supervisors and borough/citywide command

---

## P.G. 202-33: Commanding Officer

> **Rank Requirement:** Captain or above (Inspector for certain commands)

### Key Duties

- Ultimate responsibility for all operations, personnel, and administration within the command
- Set command priorities and enforcement strategies
- Conduct CompStat preparation and presentations
- Approve personnel assignments, transfers, and evaluations
- Maintain discipline and accountability throughout the command

### Sergeant Relevance

The commanding officer sets the tone and priorities that sergeants enforce. Understanding the CO's responsibilities helps sergeants align their supervision with command objectives.

---

## P.G. 202-34: Duty Inspector

> **Rank Requirement:** Inspector (or Deputy Inspector)

### Key Duties

- Serve as the borough/area inspector on duty
- Respond to significant incidents requiring inspector-level oversight
- Coordinate with duty captains and precinct commanding officers

---

## P.G. 202-35: Duty Chief

> **Rank Requirement:** Chief (Assistant Chief or above)

### Key Duties

- Serve as the citywide chief on duty
- Make command decisions for major incidents requiring chief-level authority
- Coordinate citywide resources and responses

---

## P.G. 202-36: Community Response Team (CRT) Supervisor (HIGH PRIORITY)

> **Rank Requirement:** Sergeant or above, SOLELY assigned to CRT supervision.
> **Core Rule:** CRTs may NOT deploy without a sergeant or above assigned solely to supervision.

### Key Duties

- **SHALL** attend requisite training BEFORE performing CRT supervisory duties
- **SHALL** be solely assigned to CRT supervision during deployment
- **SHALL** systematically review Body Worn Camera (BWC) video of CRT officers
- **SHALL** use the CRAFT SUPERVISOR FEEDBACK FORM for commendable actions

### Key Exam Points — CRT Supervisor

1. CRTs CANNOT deploy without a solely assigned sergeant (same rule as NST)
2. Supervisor must complete training BEFORE performing CRT duties
3. BWC review is systematic (same standard as NST)
4. CRAFT form used for commendable actions (same tool as NST)
5. The NST and CRT supervisor requirements are nearly identical — exam may test whether you can distinguish them or recognize the parallel structure

---

## P.G. 202-37: Community Response Team Officer

### Key Duties

- Perform community response duties as directed by the CRT supervisor
- Engage with community members in designated areas
- Address quality-of-life conditions and community concerns
- Maintain required documentation of community interactions

### Sergeant Relevance

CRT officers operate under direct sergeant supervision. The supervisor is accountable for their deployment, conduct, and documentation.

---

## P.G. 202-38: Quality of Life Officer

### Key Duties

- Address quality-of-life conditions within the command
- Issue summonses for quality-of-life violations
- Coordinate with the Quality of Life Sergeant on enforcement priorities
- Document conditions and enforcement actions taken

---

## P.G. 202-39: Quality of Life Sergeant (HIGH PRIORITY)

> **Rank Requirement:** Sergeant

### Key Duties

- Supervise Quality of Life Officers (P.G. 202-38) and Administrative QOL Officers (P.G. 202-06)
- Set quality-of-life enforcement priorities based on community complaints and command direction
- Coordinate QOL enforcement strategy with the platoon commander and special operations lieutenant
- Review and approve QOL-related documentation and summons activity
- Ensure QOL enforcement is consistent, fair, and aligned with community needs

### Sergeant Relevance

This is a direct sergeant assignment. The QOL Sergeant balances enforcement with community relations and must coordinate across multiple officer-level positions.

## Supervisor Roles — Comparison Matrix

| Feature | Desk Officer (202-18) | Patrol Supervisor (202-19) | NST Supervisor (202-20) | CRT Supervisor (202-36) |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Minimum Rank** | Sergeant | Sergeant | Sergeant | Sergeant |
| **Scope** | ALL command operations | ALL field operations | NST unit only | CRT unit only |
| **Location** | Inside command facility | In the field | In the field | In the field |
| **Solely Assigned** | Yes (at desk) | Yes (to field) | YES (mandatory) | YES (mandatory) |
| **BWC Review** | N/A | As needed | Systematic | Systematic |
| **CRAFT Form** | N/A | N/A | Yes | Yes |
| **Max Subordinates** | N/A (facility staff) | N/A (all field MOS) | 5 per tour | Per assignment |
| **Reports To** | Platoon Commander | Platoon Commander | Special Ops Lt | Special Ops Lt |
