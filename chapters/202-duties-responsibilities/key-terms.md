# Section 202 — Key Terms, Abbreviations & Acronyms

> **Source:** NYPD Patrol Guide, Section 202

---

## Abbreviations & Acronyms

| Abbreviation | Full Term | Context |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **RMP** | Radio Motor Patrol | Standard patrol vehicle; operator and recorder roles defined in P.G. 202-02 and 202-03 |
| **AVL** | Automatic Vehicle Locator | GPS-based system for tracking RMP locations; used by patrol supervisors via DAS (P.G. 202-19) |
| **DAS** | Domain Awareness System | NYPD technology platform integrating surveillance, sensor data, and AVL; patrol supervisors use AVL within DAS |
| **BWC** | Body Worn Camera | Required equipment; NST and CRT supervisors must systematically review BWC video (P.G. 202-20, 202-36) |
| **CRAFT** | (Supervisor Feedback Form) | CRAFT SUPERVISOR FEEDBACK FORM used by NST and CRT supervisors to document commendable actions |
| **CCRB** | Civilian Complaint Review Board | Independent oversight agency; platoon commanders must query the NYPD 360 CCRB Dashboard regularly (P.G. 202-26) |
| **NST** | Neighborhood Safety Team | Specialized patrol unit deployed based on crime data; strict supervision requirements (P.G. 202-05, 202-20) |
| **CRT** | Community Response Team | Community-focused patrol unit; supervision requirements parallel NST (P.G. 202-36, 202-37) |
| **ECMS** | Evidence Collection and Management System | System for tracking invoiced property and evidence; relates to Evidence/Property Control Specialist (P.G. 202-13) |
| **EDP** | Emotionally Disturbed Person | Relevant to RMP recorder seating rules — recorder moves to rear seat when transporting EDP in vehicle without partition (P.G. 202-03) |
| **FORMS** | Field Online Reporting and Management System | Digital system for maintaining reports and activity logs |
| **CPR System** | Complaint, Personnel, and Report System | Department system for tracking complaints, personnel records, and reports |
| **IAB** | Internal Affairs Bureau | Receives reports of seatbelt vandalism from patrol supervisors (P.G. 202-19) |
| **ICO** | Integrity Control Officer | Command-level position monitoring policy compliance and integrity (P.G. 202-29) |
| **MOS** | Member of the Service | Any sworn or civilian employee of the NYPD |
| **QOL** | Quality of Life | Refers to quality-of-life enforcement, officers (P.G. 202-38), and sergeants (P.G. 202-39) |
| **XO** | Executive Officer | Second-in-command of a precinct/command (P.G. 202-30) |
| **CO** | Commanding Officer | Head of a precinct/command (P.G. 202-33) |
| **FIO** | Field Intelligence Officer | Gathers and analyzes field intelligence; supervised by Field Intelligence Sergeant (P.G. 202-16) |
| **A.G.** | Administrative Guide | Internal NYPD administrative procedures; A.G. 324-17 governs AWOL reporting |

## Key Roles

| Role | Procedure | Minimum Rank | Key Responsibility |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Desk Officer** | P.G. 202-18 | Sergeant | ALL command operations during tour; facility management; direct supervision of station house personnel |
| **Patrol Supervisor** | P.G. 202-19 | Sergeant | ALL field operations during tour; roll call; patrol visits; responding to serious incidents |
| **Platoon Commander** | P.G. 202-26 | Lieutenant | ALL command operations during assigned platoon; roll call adjustments; meal scheduling; vehicle maintenance (2nd platoon) |
| **Special Operations Lieutenant** | P.G. 202-27 | Lieutenant | Oversees specialized units (NST, CRT, conditions teams); receives reports from NST/CRT supervisors |
| **Integrity Control Officer (ICO)** | P.G. 202-29 | As designated | Monitors command integrity and policy compliance; conducts audits |
| **Operations Coordinator** | P.G. 202-28 | As designated | Coordinates daily operations and resource allocation |
| **NST Supervisor** | P.G. 202-20 | Sergeant | Solely assigned supervision of up to 5 NST members; BWC review; CRAFT forms |
| **CRT Supervisor** | P.G. 202-36 | Sergeant | Solely assigned supervision of CRT; BWC review; CRAFT forms |
| **QOL Sergeant** | P.G. 202-39 | Sergeant | Supervises QOL enforcement strategy and personnel |
| **Training Sergeant** | P.G. 202-21 | Sergeant | Coordinates in-service training; tracks compliance |

## Key Concepts

### Command Log
- Official record maintained at the desk
- Desk officer signs name and time when assuming AND leaving duties (P.G. 202-18)
- Permanent record of command operations

### Activity Log (Digital)
- Maintained by each police officer during the tour (P.G. 202-01)
- Records patrol activity, observations, and actions taken
- Digital format maintained through Department systems

### Roll Call
- Conducted by the patrol supervisor **5 minutes** after start of tour (P.G. 202-19)
- Verifies attendance, uniform compliance, and equipment readiness
- Platoon commander may cause necessary changes/adjustments (P.G. 202-26)

### Interrupted Patrol Log
- Record of interruptions to normal patrol activity
- Documents reasons for leaving assigned post/sector
- Supports accountability for time spent off normal patrol

### Officer Profile Report
- Monthly supervisory document signed by the patrol supervisor
- Must be delivered by the **5th of each month** (P.G. 202-19)
- Documents officer performance and supervisory observations

### CRAFT Supervisor Feedback Form
- Used by NST supervisors (P.G. 202-20) and CRT supervisors (P.G. 202-36)
- Documents commendable actions by subordinates
- Positive accountability tool — reinforces good performance

### NYPD 360 CCRB Dashboard
- Queried regularly by the platoon commander (P.G. 202-26)
- Tracks civilian complaint trends within the command
- Used for early identification of complaint patterns

## "Shall" vs. "May" — Mandatory vs. Discretionary

Understanding the distinction is critical for the Sergeant exam:

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Shall** | Mandatory — MUST be done | "The patrol supervisor SHALL conduct roll call 5 minutes after the start of tour" |
| **Must** | Mandatory — same force as "shall" | "Must be a sergeant or above" |
| **Ensure** | Supervisory mandate — the supervisor is accountable for the outcome | "Ensure members do not congregate" |
| **May** | Discretionary — permitted but not required | Seatbelts "may be removed ONLY in dangerous tactical situations" |
| **Should** | Recommended — expected but with some flexibility | Less common in Section 202 |

> **Exam Tip:** When an answer choice includes "shall" or "must" language from the Patrol Guide, it is almost always the correct answer over choices using softer language.
