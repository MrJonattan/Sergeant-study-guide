# Section 202 — Review Questions

> **Source:** NYPD Patrol Guide, Section 202
> **Focus:** Sergeant-relevant duties and responsibilities
> **Format:** Multiple-choice with hidden answers

---

### Question 1
**The desk officer is required to check Department email at which of the following times during the tour?**

A. At the start of the tour only
B. At the start of the tour and at the 2nd hour
C. At the start of the tour and at the 4th hour
D. Every 2 hours throughout the tour

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-18, the desk officer shall check Department email at the start of the tour AND at the 4th hour of the tour. These are the two mandatory email check times.
</details>

### Question 2
**A member of the service is unaccounted for at the start of the tour. The desk officer conducts an immediate investigation but the member remains unaccounted for. At what point must the desk officer initiate AWOL procedures in accordance with A.G. 324-17?**

A. After 1 hour
B. After 2 hours
C. After 3 hours
D. After 4 hours

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-18, if a member is still unaccounted for after 2 hours, the desk officer must comply with A.G. 324-17 (AWOL procedures). The desk officer should conduct an immediate investigation first, but the 2-hour mark triggers the formal AWOL process.
</details>

### Question 3
**Which of the following persons is NOT authorized to be behind the desk at a precinct station house?**

A. A District Attorney
B. A representative from the Comptroller's office
C. A local community council president
D. A non-local law enforcement officer with a judicial warrant

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-18, only the following are authorized behind the desk: MOS, judicial officers, Medical Examiner, Corporation Counsel, Comptroller, District Attorney, and non-local law enforcement officers with a judicial warrant. A community council president, regardless of their relationship with the command, is NOT authorized behind the desk.
</details>

### Question 4
**The patrol supervisor shall conduct roll call at what time relative to the start of the tour?**

A. At the exact start of the tour
B. 5 minutes after the start of the tour
C. 10 minutes after the start of the tour
D. 15 minutes after the start of the tour

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-19, the patrol supervisor shall conduct roll call 5 minutes after the start of the tour. This allows time for members to assemble and prepare.
</details>

### Question 5
**A patrol supervisor discovers that a seatbelt in an RMP has been intentionally vandalized. To whom must this be reported?**

A. The platoon commander
B. The desk officer
C. The Internal Affairs Bureau
D. The operations coordinator

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-19, seatbelt vandalism must be reported to the Internal Affairs Bureau (IAB). This is distinct from seatbelt deficiencies (mechanical failures or wear), which are reported to the platoon commander. Vandalism implies intentional damage, which is an integrity issue warranting IAB involvement.
</details>

### Question 7
**Which of the following best describes how the patrol supervisor should visit foot and RMP patrol posts?**

A. At scheduled intervals announced during roll call
B. Once at the beginning and once at the end of the tour
C. Frequently at irregular intervals
D. Only when responding to a radio run in the area

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-19, the patrol supervisor shall visit foot and RMP patrol posts FREQUENTLY at IRREGULAR intervals. The key words are "frequently" and "irregular" — predictable patterns of supervision defeat the purpose and allow officers to anticipate when the supervisor will check.
</details>

### Question 8
**Neighborhood Safety Teams (NSTs) may NOT be deployed without which of the following?**

A. Approval from the borough commander
B. A lieutenant solely assigned to NST supervision
C. A sergeant or above solely assigned to NST supervision
D. At least 6 NST members available for the tour

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-20, NSTs may NOT be deployed without a sergeant or above solely assigned to NST supervision. The key word is "solely" — the supervisor cannot be splitting duties between NST and other assignments. The minimum rank is sergeant, not lieutenant.
</details>

### Question 9
**What is the maximum number of NST members a single NST supervisor may supervise per tour?**

A. 3
B. 4
C. 5
D. 8

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-20, the NST supervisor shall supervise no more than 5 NST members per tour. Note that this is a separate limit from the 3-per-vehicle rule (P.G. 202-05), which limits the number of NST members including the supervisor in any single vehicle.
</details>

### Question 10
**An RMP operator observes a serious emergency 4 blocks from their current location. The operator has been directed by the dispatcher to respond to a different assignment. What should the operator do?**

A. Continue to the assigned call as directed by the dispatcher
B. Respond to the serious emergency since it is within 5 blocks
C. Radio the patrol supervisor for guidance before taking action
D. Respond only if no other units are available

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-02, the RMP operator shall respond to a serious emergency within 5 blocks, even if directed to another assignment. The 5-block rule for serious emergencies takes priority over the dispatcher's assignment. At 4 blocks, the emergency is within the mandatory response range.
</details>

### Question 11
**The desk officer is required to inspect which of the following BEFORE assuming desk officer duties?**

A. The property locker and parking facilities
B. The arrest processing area and prisoner detention areas
C. All restrooms and locker rooms
D. The command's vehicle fleet

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-18, the desk officer shall inspect the arrest processing area and prisoner detention areas BEFORE assuming duties. The property locker is inspected at the commencement of each tour (after assuming duties). Restrooms/locker rooms and parking facilities are checked during the tour but are not pre-assumption requirements.
</details>

### Question 12
**Regarding RMP seatbelts, under what circumstance may a member of the service remove their safety belt?**

A. When conducting a vehicle stop
B. When approaching a scene of a reported crime
C. Only in dangerous tactical situations
D. When transporting a prisoner

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-02, safety belts shall be worn at all times and may be removed ONLY in dangerous tactical situations. None of the other listed activities — vehicle stops, approaching crime scenes, or transporting prisoners — authorize removal of the safety belt.
</details>

### Question 13
**The CRT Supervisor shares several requirements in common with the NST Supervisor. Which of the following is required of the CRT Supervisor that is NOT explicitly required of the NST Supervisor?**

A. Systematic BWC video review
B. Use of the CRAFT Supervisor Feedback Form
C. Completing requisite training BEFORE performing CRT supervisory duties
D. Being solely assigned to supervision during deployment

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-36, the CRT supervisor must attend requisite training BEFORE performing CRT supervisory duties. While both roles require BWC review, CRAFT forms, and sole assignment, the explicit pre-duty training requirement is specifically highlighted for the CRT supervisor. Both roles are functionally similar, but the exam may test this distinction.
</details>

### Question 14
**Which platoon commander is specifically responsible for vehicle maintenance and weekly vehicle inspection?**

A. First Platoon (midnight tour)
B. Second Platoon (day tour)
C. Third Platoon (evening tour)
D. All platoon commanders share this duty equally

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-26, the Second Platoon (day tour) lieutenant is responsible for vehicle maintenance and weekly vehicle inspection. The First Platoon (midnight tour) lieutenant is responsible for reviewing reports from the previous 24 hours.
</details>

### Question 15
**A police officer assigned to a Neighborhood Safety Team is asked to conduct a decoy operation targeting robbery suspects. Which of the following is correct?**

A. The officer may conduct the operation with NST supervisor approval
B. The officer may conduct the operation if wearing the prescribed NST uniform
C. The officer must NOT conduct decoy operations as an NST member
D. The officer may conduct the operation only during the first platoon

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-05, NST officers must NOT perform duty in plainclothes or conduct decoy operations. This is an absolute prohibition regardless of supervisory approval or uniform status. Decoy operations are outside the scope of NST duties.
</details>

### Question 16
**Which of the following persons is authorized behind the desk at a precinct station house?**

A. The Mayor of New York City
B. An assistant to the District Attorney performing official duties
C. An assistant to a Judicial Officer performing official duties
D. A City Council member representing the precinct's district

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-18, the DA and their assistant are authorized behind the desk. The Mayor, City Council members, and assistants to Judicial Officers are NOT authorized. DA, ME, Corporation Counsel, and Comptroller each include their assistants; Judicial Officers do NOT include assistants.
</details>

### Question 17
**The US Flag at the command is required to be displayed during which hours?**

A. Sunset to sunrise
B. Sunrise to sunset
C. 0600 to 2400 hours
D. At all times, 24 hours a day

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-18, the desk officer shall ensure the US Flag is properly displayed from sunrise to sunset each day. A prior exam reversed the order to "sunset to sunrise" — the flag is NOT up in the dark. Watch for this reversal in answer choices.
</details>

### Question 18
**The desk officer discovers that invoiced property has been stored in the property locker for 35 days. What action is required?**

A. Notify the Commanding Officer and make a notation in the Command Log
B. Make a separate Command Log entry and notify the Operations Coordinator
C. Contact the Property Clerk Division and request immediate pickup
D. Notify the Integrity Control Officer and make a Command Log entry

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-18, when property is stored in excess of 30 days, the desk officer must make a SEPARATE Command Log entry indicating invoices stored in excess of 30 days AND notify the Operations Coordinator of the number of vouchers. Additionally, expedite delivery to the final storage location.
</details>

### Question 19
**Which of the following BEST describes the core responsibility of the Patrol Supervisor?**

A. Responsible for all police operations in the command during the tour
B. Responsible for all command operations during the assigned tour
C. Supervise all police field operations in the command during the tour
D. Direct all police operations in the field during the tour

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-19, the Patrol Supervisor "supervise[s] all police field operations in command during tour." Choice A describes the Desk Officer ("responsible for police operations"), and choice B describes the Lt. Platoon Commander ("responsible for command operations"). The exact wording — "supervise" vs. "responsible for," and "field" vs. "police" vs. "command" — is critical.
</details>

### Question 20
**The Patrol Supervisor must immediately respond to and direct activity at radio runs involving which of the following categories?**

A. Weapons, Emotionally Disturbed Persons, Burglaries, Serious crimes
B. Weapons, Emergencies, Break-ins, Suspicious persons
C. Weapons, Emergencies, Burglaries, Serious crimes
D. Weapons, Emergencies, Burglaries, Shots fired

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 202-19, the Patrol Supervisor must immediately respond to WEBS radio runs: Weapon, Emergency, Burglary, Serious crime. The mnemonic WEBS captures all four categories. EDPs, suspicious persons, and shots fired are not part of this mandatory response list.
</details>

### Question 21
**Regarding the Patrol Supervisor's meal, which of the following is correct?**

A. The P/S notifies the Desk Officer of meal location and relieves the Platoon Commander
B. The P/S notifies the Telephone Switchboard Operator of meal location and relieves the LPC or D.O. when designated
C. The P/S notifies the Communications Dispatcher of meal location
D. The P/S takes meal at the command facility without notification

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 202-19, the Patrol Supervisor notifies the Telephone Switchboard Operator of meal location AND relieves the Lt. Platoon Commander or D.O. for meal when designated. The notification goes to the TSO, not the D.O. or dispatcher.
</details>

### Question 22
**P.G. 202-18 states the desk officer should accept service of civil process "when the principal administrative associate is not performing duty." P.G. 211-21 addresses the same topic differently. Which of the following is the BEST approach for the exam?**

A. Follow P.G. 211-21 exclusively as it is the more specific procedure
B. Follow P.G. 202-18 exclusively as it governs desk officer duties
C. Know that a conflict exists between the two procedures and be prepared for either version
D. The procedures do not conflict — they address different situations

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** P.G. 211-21 says the D.O. accepts civil process regardless (no mention of the PRAA condition), while P.G. 202-18 includes the condition "when principal administrative associate not performing duty." This is a known PG conflict. The exam may reference either procedure number, so know both versions exist.
</details>
