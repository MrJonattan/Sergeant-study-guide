## P.G. 212-84 — Verification of Pistol License

### Verification Steps
1. Request documentary ID **in addition to** the pistol license.
2. Check for valid license features.

### Valid License Features

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Seal | Embossed Police Department seal |
| Validity period | **2 years** |
| Expiration | On licensee's **birth date** |

### Verification Resources

| Resource | Availability |
|----------|-------------|
| License Division (telephone) | **0900-1700, Monday-Friday** |
| ALPS (Automated License and Permit System) | Electronic verification |

### If License Division Closed / ALPS Unavailable
- Desk officer contacts License Division at **0900 next business day** for verification.

### Questionable Validity
1. Retain the license and firearms.
2. Have a Complaint Report prepared.
3. Forward Typed Letterhead to CO, License Division.

> **Exam Alert:** Pistol licenses are valid for **2 years** and expire on the licensee's **birth date**. Two features to remember.

## P.G. 212-85 — Rifle/Shotgun Permit Suspended/Revoked/Disapproved

### Assignment
- CO assigns MOS (**preferably a sergeant**) for immediate investigation.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sergeants are the preferred rank for conducting rifle/shotgun permit surrender investigations.

### Procedure at Licensee Address
1. Respond to the licensee's address.
2. Request surrender of firearms, permit, and registration.
3. Prepare 3 copies of Surrender Notice:

| Copy | Destination |
|------|------------|
| Original | Rifle/Shotgun Section |
| Copy | Precinct file |
| Copy | Receipt for permittee |

### Investigation Deadline

> **Exam Alert:** Investigations must be completed and returned within **60 days**.

### Refusal to Surrender
- If permit holder refuses or fails to surrender: serve summons for Admin Code **section 10-303** (Criminal Court).
- Do **NOT** serve summons for unrenewed permits (administrative matter).

### Priority Cases
- Serious crime conviction
- Mental incompetence
- Consult **Legal Bureau** regarding search warrant for priority cases.

## P.G. 212-128 — Extreme Risk Protection Order (ERPO)

### Definition
A court-issued Order of Protection prohibiting the purchasing or possessing of **any** firearm.

### Triggering Factors
- Threat, violence, or stalking
- Order of Protection violation
- Pending weapons charge
- Reckless firearm display
- History of ERPO violations
- Substance or alcohol use
- Recent firearm acquisition combined with one of the above factors (a-f)

### Evaluation Process

| Step | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Initial evaluation and confirmation | Immediate supervisor |
| Record in Activity Log | Immediate supervisor |
| Determine probable cause (self-harm/harm to others) | Immediate supervisor |
| Final determination on filing | Legal Bureau ERPO Unit |

### Legal Bureau ERPO Unit Availability

| Time | Contact |
|------|---------|
| Monday-Friday, 0900-1700 | ERPO Unit |
| 1700-2300 and weekends | Duty attorney |

### Service and Execution
- **Temporary ERPO** served by **NYC Sheriff's Office** — NOT NYPD.
- Intelligence Division assists if a search provision is granted.
- Department attorney appears with MOS at all hearings.

> **Exam Alert:** Temporary ERPOs are served by the **NYC Sheriff's Office**, not NYPD. This is a commonly tested distinction.

### License Division Role
- Conducts background check for final hearing:
  - Prior convictions and pending charges
  - Parole/probation status
  - Registered firearms
  - Any Orders of Protection

### Firearm Retention

> **Exam Alert:** Firearms seized under ERPO are retained for a minimum of **2 years**. No release without written authorization from CO, License Division (plus CO, Medical Division if the subject is a MOS).

### ERPO Renewal
- ERPO Unit reviews **30 days before expiration** to determine if renewal is warranted.

### District Attorney ERPOs
- DA Offices can file ERPOs for arrests/open complaints **independently** of NYPD.

### Exigent Circumstances Exception
- ERPO is **NOT** required when:
  - Imminent harm exists, AND
  - Firearm is on person or readily accessible

### MOS as Subject of ERPO
- Notify CO **without delay**.
- Modified Assignment per A.G. 318-08.

> **Sergeant Focus:** When a subordinate MOS becomes the subject of an ERPO, the supervisor must ensure immediate CO notification and initiation of the Modified Assignment process.
