# Section 212 — Media and Information

## P.G. 212-49 — Incidents Involving Media Representatives

### Purpose
Cooperate with the press and do not interfere with news gathering.

### MOME Press Card Holders

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Cross police/fire lines | Access **WILL NOT** be denied |
| Interior crime scenes | Access **NOT** included |
| Security-restricted areas | Access **NOT** included |
| Proximity | As close as possible with clear line of sight and hearing range (unaided by electronic device) |

> **Exam Alert:** MOME press card holders are entitled to cross police and fire lines. However, this does **not** include access to interior crime scenes or security-restricted areas.

### Press and Criminal Trespass
- Press shall **NOT** be arrested for criminal trespass on private property **unless** the owner expressly indicates the press is not permitted.

### First Amendment Activity Rules

> **Exam Alert:** During First Amendment Activity, MOME press are **NOT** required to leave the area even if NYPD orders persons to leave. They shall be directed to move to a **safe location** instead.

### Recording and Photography Rules
- Members **WILL NOT** interfere with videotaping or photographing in public places.
- Blocking or obstructing cameras constitutes **censorship**.
- Members may **NOT** set up police lines or frozen zones to prevent press from viewing or recording.

### Credential Seizure

> **Exam Alert:** Members may **NO LONGER** seize press credentials under **any** circumstances, except during a normal arrest for safekeeping purposes.

### Prohibited Retaliatory Actions
- May **NOT** take police action in retaliation for:
  - Expressing viewpoints
  - Exercising the right to observe, record, or protest police activity

### "Red Light" Offense Arrest of Press
- Arrest must be approved by the **incident commander** or ranking **DCPI** member.
- **Presumption:** summons issued **on scene** if eligible.

### DCPI Notification
- Notify DCPI **immediately** for **ANY** incident involving the press, regardless of outcome.
- DCPI available **24/7**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure members understand that press access cannot be restricted through police lines or frozen zones designed to block recording. Any incident involving the press requires immediate DCPI notification.

## P.G. 212-76 — Information Concerning Official Business of Department

### General Principles
- Treat official business as **CONFIDENTIAL**.
- Understand the public's right to be informed.
- Exercise discretion when divulging authorized information.

### Authorized Releases
- Specific agencies may receive specific categories of information per Department guidelines.

### Requests for Statistics/Surveys
- Forward to **Office of Management Analysis and Planning**.

### Medical Records

| Requestor | Requirements | Fee |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| MOS (own records) | Written request to CO, Medical Division | **No fee** |
| Outside parties | Written request with notarized authorization | **$5 fee** |

### Grants and Equipment Offers

> **Exam Alert:** Members may **NOT** accept offers of grants, equipment, or funding from elected officials, agencies, or private entities. All such offers must be directed to the **Police Commissioner** in writing.

### Sensitive Information from Elected Officials
- Submit the request in writing to the **Police Commissioner**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure subordinates understand that all grant/equipment/funding offers must be forwarded to the Police Commissioner — members have no authority to accept directly.
