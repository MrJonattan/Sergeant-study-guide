# Section 212 — Media and Information

## P.G. 212-49 — Incidents Involving Media Representatives

*(Updated R.O. 93, effective 10/03/25)*

### Purpose
To cooperate with members of the press by not interfering or allowing others to interfere with media personnel acting in their news gathering capacity.

### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Member of the Press** | An individual who gathers, reports, or licenses news or content for publishing, broadcasting, or cablecasting (radio, TV, newspapers, magazines, wire, books, Internet). Includes employees of news-gathering organizations, independent contractors, freelancers, and self-employed persons. |
| **MOME Press Credential** | City of New York Press Card issued by the Mayor's Office of Media and Entertainment (MOME) Press Credentials Office allowing bearer to pass NYPD/city official lines and attend official city-government sponsored events open to the press. |

### MOME Press Card Holders

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Cross police/fire lines | Access **WILL NOT** be denied |
| Interior crime scenes | Access **NOT** included |
| Security-restricted areas | Access **NOT** included |
| Proximity | As close as possible with clear line of sight and hearing range (unaided by electronic device) |

> **Exam Alert:** **Only** MOME-issued Press Card holders are entitled to cross police and fire lines. However, this does **not** include access to interior crime scenes or areas designated off-limits for security reasons.

### Press Access Rules (UMOS)

1. Adhere to the following guidelines unless safety or police duties require otherwise:
   a. Press given access as close as possible with clear line of sight and hearing range (unaided by electronic device)
   b. Press access to demonstrations on private property shall not be impeded
   c. Press shall **NOT** be arrested for criminal trespass on private property **unless** owner expressly indicates the press is not to enter or remain
   d. If ranking officer determines press access must be restricted, they retain the discretion to do so
2. Determine if any threat to safety of press exists; take appropriate action.
3. Request response of patrol supervisor.
4. Take enforcement action as necessary.

### First Amendment Activity Rules

> **Exam Alert:** During First Amendment Activity, MOME press are **NOT** required to leave the area even if NYPD orders persons to leave. They shall move to a **safe location** instead. At the discretion of the incident commander, press may be directed to a different location where they may still see and hear ongoing newsworthy events and will **not** be subject to arrest for documenting police activity or for not leaving the general area. See P.G. 213-21.

### Patrol Supervisor Duties

5. Cooperate with and assist members of the press; provide safe access to the scene.
6. Conduct **immediate investigation** if a member of the press is assaulted, harassed, or their vehicle/equipment is vandalized/damaged.
7. Attempt to obtain third party witnesses if confrontation with MOS or others.
8. Request all parties, including witnesses, to report to precinct of occurrence for further investigation when necessary.
9. Notify **without delay**:
   a. Office of the Deputy Commissioner, Public Information (DCPI)
   b. Desk officer
   c. Lieutenant platoon commander

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor must investigate immediately when a press member is assaulted or harassed. Notification to DCPI must be made without delay. DCPI is available via phone **24/7**.

### Desk Officer

10. Notify:
    a. CO/XO/duty captain
    b. Operations Unit

### Lieutenant Platoon Commander

11. Respond to command (if in the field) and under supervision of CO/XO/duty captain, conduct investigation.
12. Prepare report on **Typed Letterhead** containing details of incident and results of investigation. Forward to:
    a. Chief of Patrol/bureau chief concerned
    b. Borough commander concerned
    c. CO, Public Information Division
    d. Command file

> *NOTE: The CO/XO/duty captain will perform the duties of the lieutenant platoon commander if the LPC is unavailable.*

### Commanding Officer/Duty Captain

13. Forward supplementary report, if necessary.
14. Respond to command and supervise the investigation and preparation of the report.

### Recording and Photography Rules
- Members **WILL NOT** interfere with videotaping or photographing in public places.
- Blocking or obstructing cameras or harassing the photographer constitutes **censorship**.
- Members may **NOT** set up police lines or frozen zones to prevent press from viewing or recording the scene from a public place.

### Credential Seizure

> **Exam Alert:** Members may **NO LONGER** seize press credentials under **any** circumstances, except when seized in the normal course of an arrest and held for safekeeping like other types of non-evidentiary, non-investigatory personal property.

### Prohibited Retaliatory Actions
- May **NOT** take police action in retaliation for:
  - Expressing a viewpoint
  - Engaging in First Amendment Activities
  - Exercising the right to witness, observe, record, comment on, or protest police activity

### "Red Light" Offense Arrest of Press

- If arrested for a "Red Light" offense (per P.G. 213-21) and presents a MOME-issued credential or official government-issued press credential: processing **shall** be approved by the **incident commander** or ranking member from **DCPI**.
- **Presumption:** summons issued **on scene**, if eligible.
- Press member may be removed to arrest processing facility **only** if incident commander determines issuing summons on scene would create a health/safety risk.
- Applies to all "Red Light" arrests of press regardless of whether it occurs at a First Amendment Activity event or not.

### Government-Issued Press Credentials from Other Jurisdictions

- Shall be verified to the extent possible at the time of encounter.
- If unable to verify on scene, officers **shall** consider the following indicia of validity:
  - Name matches another form of ID
  - Contains a photograph
  - Details the issuing city or agency
  - Includes the associated media outlet
  - Indicates whether the individual is independent
  - Includes an expiration date, serial, or ID number
- Members **should** confer with DCPI if necessary.

### Press Card Age Requirements

| Type | Age Requirement |
|------|----------------|
| Standard/Reserve Press Card | At least **18 years old** |
| Single Event Press Card | Under **18** may apply |
| Under 16 with Single Event card | Must be accompanied by an **adult** (1 adult per child in restricted area) |

### DCPI Notification
- Notify DCPI **immediately** for **ANY** incident involving the press, regardless of outcome.
- DCPI available **24/7**.
- DCPI will maintain a central repository of all reports received of incidents involving members of the press.

### MOME Verification
- MOME is available to confirm validity of press credentials.
- Contact the **Operations Unit** for the MOME phone number and contact information.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure members understand that press access cannot be restricted through police lines or frozen zones designed to block recording. Any incident involving the press requires immediate DCPI notification. The patrol supervisor must conduct an immediate investigation if press members are assaulted or harassed.

## P.G. 212-77 — Release of Information to News Media

### General Rule

- **Media inquiries should be directed to DCPI**
- MOS provide assistance ONLY when:
  - It does not pose a threat to personal safety of MOS, the public, or the news media
  - It does not interfere with police operations, crime scene investigations, or other emergency service providers
  - It does not infringe on the rights of the accused or privacy/security of crime victims
- Even if other agencies are involved, ALL media requests go through DCPI
- Unauthorized communication with news media without conferral with DCPI is **PROHIBITED**

> **Exam Alert:** MOS are required to **immediately** notify DCPI of any interaction with the media. This is an absolute requirement.

### Highest Ranking UMOS Addressing Media

- Prior to addressing media, confer with DCPI regarding info to be released
- Briefly describe incident: how called to scene, type of job, location, # of aideds, etc.

### Crime Scenes or Frozen Areas

> **Exam Alert:** Media access to an incident does NOT extend to interior crime scenes or frozen areas. This applies to all media, including MOME press card holders (per PG 212-49).

### Information NOT to Be Released

- **Juvenile under 18** taken into custody — EXCEPT Juvenile Offender or Adolescent Offender (can be released)
  - If case drops from Criminal Court to Family Court: offender becomes Juvenile Delinquent and ID can NOT be released from that point forward
- **Abused child** — neglected or abused child or child complainant under 18
- **Sex crime** victim ID or location of sex assault if victim's residence or perp's residence (if not apprehended)
- **Communicable disease** info indicating a person has one
- **Arrest/booking photo** unless it would serve a law enforcement purpose
- **Witness or confidential informant** information
- **MOS, complainant, or witness** — address or phone # of any of these three; info that may hinder prosecution or jeopardize safety

### Civil Rights Law — Sex Crime Victim ID

- Allows victims to bring private action if their ID is disclosed
- MOS releasing records **must** ensure sex offense victim identity is NOT divulged
- Proper release of sex crime victim ID is limited to:
  - Defendant and their counsel or guardian
  - Public officers/employees investigating, prosecuting, and keeping records relating to offense

### Communicable Disease — Confidentiality

- Prisoner or victim status regarding communicable disease (HIV, AIDS, etc.) is confidential
- Generally do NOT release to public, media, family, friends, or other prisoners
- Any release requires written consent of the **DCLM** (Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters)

### Prisoner Volunteers Medical Status

- If prisoner volunteers positive medical status or is treated medically: use isolation cells if available
- Document on Prisoner Movement Slip **or** Medical Treatment of Prisoner (either form)

### Releasable Information Post Arrest

- **Pedigree:** name, age, residence, employment, marital status, similar background (after ID confirmed)
- **Charge:** substance or text of charge
- **Complainant info:** only when appropriate, ID of complainant
- **Agency involved:** identity of investigating/arresting agency and length of investigation
- **Arrest information:** circumstances surrounding arrest (time, place, resistance, pursuit, possession/use of weapons, description of items seized)

### Release ONLY After Conferral with DCPI AND Chief of Detectives — "A RAT COP"

> **Memory Aid — RATCOP:** Information released only after conferral with DCPI AND Chief of Detectives:
> - **R**eputation — statements concerning reputation/character of accused or witness
> - **A**nticipated testimony of prospective witness
> - **T**est — performance of tests, results of tests, OR refusal by accused to take a test
> - **C**redibility of prospective witness
> - **O**pinion/speculation concerning evidence or argument in the case
> - **P**hotos, films, videotape and/or mug shot

> **Exam Alert:** RATCOP items require conferral with **both** DCPI and Chief of Detectives before release. This is a commonly tested mnemonic.

## P.G. 212-76 — Information Concerning Official Business of Department

### General Principles
- Treat official business as **CONFIDENTIAL**.
- Understand the public's right to be informed.
- Exercise discretion when divulging authorized information.

### Authorized Releases
- Specific agencies may receive specific categories of information per Department guidelines.

### Requests for Statistics/Surveys
- Forward to **Office of Management Analysis and Planning**.

### Medical Records

| Requestor | Requirements | Fee |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| MOS (own records) | Written request to CO, Medical Division | **No fee** |
| Outside parties | Written request with notarized authorization | **$5 fee** |

### Grants and Equipment Offers

> **Exam Alert:** Members may **NOT** accept offers of grants, equipment, or funding from elected officials, agencies, or private entities. All such offers must be directed to the **Police Commissioner** in writing.

### Sensitive Information from Elected Officials
- Submit the request in writing to the **Police Commissioner**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure subordinates understand that all grant/equipment/funding offers must be forwarded to the Police Commissioner — members have no authority to accept directly.
