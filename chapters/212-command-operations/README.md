# Chapter 212 — Command Operations

## Source
- **Patrol Guide Section:** 212
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide2.pdf
- **Priority:** High

## Overview

Section 212 is the **largest section** in the Patrol Guide, covering 56 procedures (212-03 through 212-129) that govern day-to-day operational procedures for command-level activities. This chapter spans tour management, transit and housing operations, special incidents, media relations, firearms licensing, election duties, technology systems, and interaction procedures.

## Learning Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Explain tour expiration procedures, Activity Log requirements, and the 4th platoon rule
2. Describe transit evacuation types (reach train vs. rescue train) and power removal criteria
3. Apply proper procedures for critical transit situations and subway power removal
4. Identify rules governing service animals, photography, and radio malfunction on transit
5. Conduct proper inspections of NYCHA facilities and transit stations
6. Handle incidents involving former/retired MOS, diplomats, and off-duty law enforcement
7. Apply media access rules including MOME press card privileges and First Amendment protections
8. Verify pistol licenses and process rifle/shotgun permit surrenders
9. Evaluate and initiate Extreme Risk Protection Orders (ERPOs)
10. Execute election-day duties before, during, and after polls
11. Activate and cancel AMBER Alerts with proper authority
12. Understand facial recognition limitations and VGTOF handling codes
13. Ensure effective communication with persons who are deaf or hard of hearing
14. Manage auxiliary police deployments and coordinator requirements
