# Section 212 — Special Incidents

## P.G. 212-36 — Incidents Involving Former/Retired MOS or Off-Duty Law Enforcement

### Initial Response
1. Ascertain identity of the individual.
2. Determine if the individual is armed.
3. Request personal ID and pistol license.
4. Request to see NYPD ID Card (PD416-091) or other official law enforcement ID.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor shall respond and approve resolution (arrest, summons, court referral, or no action).

> **Exam Alert:** These individuals shall **NOT** receive preferential treatment based on former or present law enforcement status. This is a critical principle tested on exams.

### Notifications
- Desk officer notifies Operations Unit for incidents involving agents from **other agencies**.

## P.G. 212-43 — Vacate Orders for Residential Buildings

### Authority
- Issuing agency (Buildings, Health, Fire, HPD) serves the order.
- NYPD **assists** — does not serve the order.

### Issuing Agencies
- Department of Buildings
- Department of Health
- Fire Department
- HPD (Housing Preservation and Development)

### Procedure
1. Verify credentials and authority of agency representative.
2. NYPD assists in execution of the vacate order.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor shall consult the duty captain when:
> - Doubt exists about the authority of the agency representative
> - Large-scale relocations are involved
> - Occupants refuse to vacate
> - Potential for violence exists

### Key Timelines

| Responsibility | Deadline |
|---------------|----------|
| HPD seals building | Within **72 hours** |
| Investigation and Review Section contacted | **24 hours** in advance |

> **Exam Alert:** HPD is responsible for sealing the building within **72 hours**. Investigation and Review Section must be contacted **24 hours in advance**.

## P.G. 212-54 — Person Threatening to Jump from Structure

### Immediate Actions
1. Notify radio dispatcher.
2. Request patrol supervisor, ESU, and ambulance.
3. Request Harbor Unit if location is over navigable waters.

### Intervention Techniques
- Attempt to persuade or prevent person from jumping.
- Seek assistance of relatives, friends, or clergy.
- Confine person to the **side facing the street**, if possible.
- Rope off area below.

### Cross-Reference
- Follow **P.G. 221-13** (Mentally Ill or Emotionally Disturbed Persons) as appropriate.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor shall take command of the scene, coordinate with ESU, and ensure the area below is secured. Consider requesting additional resources (negotiators, mental health professionals) early.

## P.G. 212-55 — Investigation of Aided Cases — Illegal Fireworks Injuries

### Procedure
1. Request patrol supervisor and precinct detective squad.
2. Issue summons or arrest for illegal fireworks possession.

### Aided Under 18 Years Old
- Determine extent of adult involvement.
- Arrest for **Endangering Welfare of Child** if probable cause exists.

### Database and Notifications
- **Intelligence Division** maintains database of all fireworks injury incidents.
- **Detective Bureau Wheel** notified 24/7 for ongoing investigations.

> **Exam Alert:** When the aided person is under 18, determine adult involvement and consider charges of Endangering Welfare of Child.

## P.G. 212-56 — Diplomatic Incidents

### Diplomatic Immunity — Core Rules

| Action | Permitted? |
|--------|-----------|
| **Arrest** | **NO** — shall not be arrested |
| **Issue summons** | **YES** — may be issued summons |
| **Enter UN HQ** | **NO** — not without Secretary General consent |
| **Detain** | **YES** — pending verification, if danger to self/public |

> **Exam Alert:** Diplomats with immunity shall **NOT** be arrested but **MAY** be issued a summons. This is a frequently tested distinction.

### Notifications (Immediate, by Telephone)
1. Operations Unit
2. Intelligence Division

### Verification Process
- Intelligence Division verifies diplomatic status through:
  - Mayor's Office for International Affairs
  - US Mission to the United Nations
  - US Department of State

### DWI and Diplomatic Immunity

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Sobriety test/breathalyzer | May **ASK** — diplomat entitled to **REFUSE** |
| Documentation | Document request and response in Activity Log and Unusual Occurrence Report |

> **Exam Alert:** MOS **may ask** a diplomat to take a breathalyzer test, but the diplomat is entitled to **refuse**. Always document the request and response.

### Even with Full Immunity
- Prepare **ALL** normally required reports.

### Parking Summons
- Issuing a parking summons on diplomat/consul plates for a safety hazard is **NOT** a diplomatic incident.

### Emergency Child Removal
- With diplomatic immunity: directly supervised by **CO or duty captain**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Do NOT detain a properly identified and verified diplomat. All reports must still be prepared even when immunity applies.
