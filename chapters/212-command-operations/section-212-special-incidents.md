# Section 212 — Special Incidents

## P.G. 212-36 — Incidents Involving Former/Retired MOS or Off-Duty Law Enforcement

### Initial Response
1. Ascertain identity of the individual.
2. Determine if the individual is armed.
3. Request personal ID and pistol license.
4. Request to see NYPD ID Card (PD416-091) or other official law enforcement ID.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor shall respond and approve resolution (arrest, summons, court referral, or no action).

> **Exam Alert:** These individuals shall **NOT** receive preferential treatment based on former or present law enforcement status. This is a critical principle tested on exams.

### Notifications
- Desk officer notifies Operations Unit for incidents involving agents from **other agencies**.

## P.G. 212-43 — Vacate Orders for Residential Buildings

### Authority
- Issuing agency (Buildings, Health, Fire, HPD) serves the order.
- NYPD **assists** — does not serve the order.

### Issuing Agencies
- Department of Buildings
- Department of Health
- Fire Department
- HPD (Housing Preservation and Development)

### Procedure
1. Verify credentials and authority of agency representative.
2. NYPD assists in execution of the vacate order.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor shall consult the duty captain when:
> - Doubt exists about the authority of the agency representative
> - Large-scale relocations are involved
> - Occupants refuse to vacate
> - Potential for violence exists

### Key Timelines

| Responsibility | Deadline |
|---------------|----------|
| HPD seals building | Within **72 hours** |
| Investigation and Review Section contacted | **24 hours** in advance |

> **Exam Alert:** HPD is responsible for sealing the building within **72 hours**. Investigation and Review Section must be contacted **24 hours in advance**.

## P.G. 212-47 — Counterfeit Money

### When Counterfeit Money Is Detected

- Determined that the passer is an innocent victim, OR
- No indication who passed it

### Investigating Officer

- Document: have person **last in possession** of the bill:
  - Write name/date across the **face of the bill** or scratch initials **on** a coin
- Sign: rank, name, shield, date on bill or scratch initials on coin

### Desk Officer

- Document: prepare **4 copies** of report on Typed Letterhead (49x4) to **Chief of Patrol**
- Deliver:
  - Have messenger sign itemized receipt in **Property Receipt Book**
  - Deliver 49x4 and counterfeit bill to **Patrol Borough** office
  - Messenger brings back 1 copy of the 49 as a receipt from Borough
- Receipt: attach receipted copy of 49 to the entry in the Property Receipt Book

> **Exam Alert:** No voucher is prepared for counterfeit money. The bill goes to the Patrol Borough office with the 49x4, not to the Property Clerk.

### Cross-References for Counterfeiting

> **See Also:** PG 208-47 — Arrests for counterfeiting: D/O or counterpart notifies Dept. of Treasury, US Secret Service **by phone direct**.

> **See Also:** PG 207-06 — Complaints of counterfeiting: D/O notifies Operations; Operations notifies US Treasury Dept., Secret Service Bureau.

## P.G. 212-54 — Person Threatening to Jump from Structure

### Immediate Actions
1. Notify radio dispatcher.
2. Request patrol supervisor, ESU, and ambulance.
3. Request Harbor Unit if location is over navigable waters.

### Intervention Techniques
- Attempt to persuade or prevent person from jumping.
- Seek assistance of relatives, friends, or clergy.
- Confine person to the **side facing the street**, if possible.
- Rope off area below.

### Cross-Reference
- Follow **P.G. 221-13** (Mentally Ill or Emotionally Disturbed Persons) as appropriate.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor shall take command of the scene, coordinate with ESU, and ensure the area below is secured. Consider requesting additional resources (negotiators, mental health professionals) early.

## P.G. 212-55 — Investigation of Aided Cases — Illegal Fireworks Injuries

### Procedure
1. Request patrol supervisor and precinct detective squad.
2. Issue summons or arrest for illegal fireworks possession.

### Aided Under 18 Years Old
- Determine extent of adult involvement.
- Arrest for **Endangering Welfare of Child** if probable cause exists.

### Database and Notifications
- **Intelligence Division** maintains database of all fireworks injury incidents.
- **Detective Bureau Wheel** notified 24/7 for ongoing investigations.

> **Exam Alert:** When the aided person is under 18, determine adult involvement and consider charges of Endangering Welfare of Child.

## P.G. 212-57 — Service of Family Court/Supreme Court Orders of Protection

*(Updated R.O. 104, effective 10/20/25)*

### Purpose
To assist in the service of all orders of protection and any associated papers issued by Family Court or Supreme Court.

### Desk Officer — When Order of Protection Is Presented at a Department Facility

1. Accept the order of protection from the member of the public or Court.

> *NOTE: Courts and advocacy organizations encourage petitioners to contact the NYC Sheriff in the borough of issuance so that the NYC Sheriff may serve the order. However, if a member of the public appears at a Department facility seeking assistance, the member of the service must accept the order.*

2. Have the petitioner complete the **NYC Dept. of Finance, Office of the Sheriff, Service of Process Intake (SH-0609)** form.

### Monday Through Thursday (0300-2300) and Friday (0500-2300)

3. Fax a copy of the order and the SH-0609 form directly to the NYC Sheriff's Operations Desk.
4. Confirm receipt via telephone.
5. Make an entry in the **Order of Protection Log**: petitioner's name, respondent's name, name of court, docket number, and confirmation of NYC Sheriff's receipt.

### Order of Protection Log Format

| Left Page | Right Page |
|-----------|------------|
| Pct. Serial #, Petitioner's Name, Respondent's Name, Docket #, 1st Tour UMOS Assigned/D.O., Time/Date of Attempt(s) | Date & Time of Attempt(s), (Through), 6th Tour UMOS Assigned/D.O., Date & Time of Attempt(s), Final Disposition, Time/Date NYC Sheriff Office Received, Time/Date DVP Officer Entered In Registry |

### All Other Times

6. Make appropriate entries in the Order of Protection Log.
7. Assign a uniformed member to serve the order of protection.
   a. Indicate name under caption "1st TOUR UMOS."

### If Order of Protection Is Served

8. (UMOS) Attempt to serve the order.
9. Prepare **STATEMENT OF PERSONAL SERVICE (PD260-152)** and a photocopy.
   a. Examine and account for all documents served; ensure appropriate box is checked.
10. Make a digital **Activity Log** entry.
11. Deliver form to desk officer.
12. Forward photocopy of the Statement of Personal Service to **domestic violence prevention officer**.

> *NOTE: When preparing Statement of Personal Service forms for orders issued by Supreme Court, strike out "Family Court" and write in "Supreme Court."*

### Desk Officer

13. Enter into the Order of Protection Log that the order was served.

### Domestic Violence Investigator

14. Coordinate all tasks relating to service and recording of orders of protection (filing, forwarding, etc.).
15. Enter applicable information for any order of protection served by the Department into the **State Order of Protection Registry**, via the FINEST system, upon receipt of each photocopy of a Statement of Personal Service.
16. Enter the date and time entry into the Registry was made under the appropriate caption in the Order of Protection Log.
    a. Place initials inside same caption.
17. Forward Statement of Personal Service to Family Court or Supreme Court clerk for any order of protection served by the Department.

### If Unable to Serve Order of Protection

18. (UMOS) Make a digital Activity Log entry of **each attempt** to serve the order.
19. Return the order and related forms at end of tour to the desk officer.

### Desk Officer

20. Repeat steps "6" and "7" at least once **each consecutive tour** until the NYC Sheriff's Office reopens or the order is served.
    a. Indicate name under caption "2nd Tour UMOS Assigned/Desk Officer."
21. Repeat steps "3" and "4" for further processing when assigned member is unable to serve and the NYC Sheriff's Office has reopened.
22. Enter into the Order of Protection Log that the order was forwarded to NYC Sheriff.

### Key Rules

- Each attempt to serve **shall** be documented in the member's digital Activity Log (respondent's name, docket number, pertinent information).
- Service must be attempted **at least once each tour** until effected or forwarded to the NYC Sheriff.
- Assigned UMOS will inquire of neighbors, superintendent, etc. as to respondent's whereabouts.
- If respondent has moved, attempt to obtain new address; if within precinct, attempt service at new location.
- If new address is outside the precinct, indicate in digital Activity Log and return order to desk officer for forwarding to appropriate command.

### Exclusionary Order of Protection

> **Exam Alert:** When an exclusionary order has been served (prohibits contact and/or excludes respondent from a location), uniformed members may **NOT** assist respondents who wish to remove clothing/property ("clothes jobs"). The respondent should be advised to return to court to have the order amended. However, this does **NOT** prevent a member from accompanying the **PETITIONER** if the petitioner has safety concerns about returning to a shared location to retrieve clothing/personal property.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure desk officers are maintaining the Order of Protection Log properly and that service is attempted at least once per tour. The Domestic Violence Investigator is responsible for entering service information into the State Order of Protection Registry via FINEST.

## P.G. 212-56 — Diplomatic Incidents

### Diplomatic Immunity — Core Rules

| Action | Permitted? |
|--------|-----------|
| **Arrest** | **NO** — shall not be arrested |
| **Issue summons** | **YES** — may be issued summons |
| **Enter UN HQ** | **NO** — not without Secretary General consent |
| **Detain** | **YES** — pending verification, if danger to self/public |

> **Exam Alert:** Diplomats with immunity shall **NOT** be arrested but **MAY** be issued a summons. This is a frequently tested distinction.

### Notifications (Immediate, by Telephone)
1. Operations Unit
2. Intelligence Division

### Verification Process
- Intelligence Division verifies diplomatic status through:
  - Mayor's Office for International Affairs
  - US Mission to the United Nations
  - US Department of State

### DWI and Diplomatic Immunity

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Sobriety test/breathalyzer | May **ASK** — diplomat entitled to **REFUSE** |
| Documentation | Document request and response in Activity Log and Unusual Occurrence Report |

> **Exam Alert:** MOS **may ask** a diplomat to take a breathalyzer test, but the diplomat is entitled to **refuse**. Always document the request and response.

### Even with Full Immunity
- Prepare **ALL** normally required reports.

### Parking Summons
- Issuing a parking summons on diplomat/consul plates for a safety hazard is **NOT** a diplomatic incident.

### Emergency Child Removal
- With diplomatic immunity: directly supervised by **CO or duty captain**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Do NOT detain a properly identified and verified diplomat. All reports must still be prepared even when immunity applies.

## P.G. 212-61 — Burglary/Robbery Apprehension Module (BRAM)

*(Updated R.O. 27, effective 05/01/25)*

### Purpose
To reduce the occurrence of burglaries/robberies by identifying and apprehending those individuals committing burglaries/robberies.

### Scope
The Precinct Detective Squad/Burglary Robbery Apprehension Module (PDS/BRAM) will maintain a dedicated focus on burglary/robbery investigations and shall be under the direct organizational control and supervision of the Precinct Detective Squad Commander.

### Uniformed Member of the Service — Upon Receiving a Burglary or Robbery Complaint

1. Determine whether a burglary or robbery has in fact been committed by conducting preliminary investigation.
2. Broadcast description of perpetrator(s) and conduct search, if appropriate.
3. Safeguard evidence; follow crime scene procedures per P.G. 212-04.
4. Interview complainant and initiate investigation.
   a. If perpetrator(s) can be identified, transport complainant to station house to view photos.
5. Prepare **COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A)** for robbery.
   a. Prepare for burglary if Burglary Apprehension Team member is not working.
   b. Comply with P.G. 207-01 (Complaint Reporting System).
6. Notify **Detective Borough Evidence Collection Team (ECT)** to respond to **all** burglary incidents, including situations where an arrest is made at the scene.
7. Notify Precinct Detective Squad who will immediately notify the designated PDS/BRAM investigator, when available.
   a. Provide all available information necessary to continue investigation.
   b. Notify Detective Borough Night Watch if PDS/BRAM office is closed (0100-0800 hours).

> *NOTE: Copies of robbery/burglary complaints recorded on COMPLAINT REPORTS in the precinct on late tours will be picked up at 0800 hours by a PDS/BRAM member.*

### Desk Officer

8. Review and finalize the completed **COMPLAINT REPORT** in OMNIFORM System.
9. Have a copy forwarded to the Precinct Detective Squad/BRAM.
10. Ensure additional copies are sent to other units/agencies, as required.

### PDS/BRAM Investigator

11. Review burglary and robbery complaint reports and assist in identifying patterns or trends.
12. Confer with the Precinct Field Intelligence Officer, when assigned, prior to and following the issuance of a pattern.
13. Compile a pattern case folder for all identified patterns, documenting all investigative steps taken.
14. Maintain photo books in accordance with established Legal Bureau guidelines.
15. Confer with precinct and borough patrol and investigative units and disseminate all information regarding burglary and robbery trends, patterns, and wanted perpetrators.
16. Update and maintain the Precinct Crime Information Center with active **INVESTIGATION CARD (PD373-163)**, Field Information posters, serial burglar lists, and photos.
17. Conduct an investigation when the subject of a "Home Visit" cannot be located by the Precinct Burglary Apprehension Team (BAT).
18. Cultivate and maintain sources of information within command (confidential informants, building superintendents, landlords, block watchers, etc.).
19. Debrief all suspects arrested for burglary/robbery to:
    a. Identify accomplices
    b. Identify fencing locations
    c. Identify other victims
20. Be responsible for reviewing the list of persons selling items to pawnshops or secondhand dealers.
    a. Conduct INVESTIGATION CARD check, warrant investigation, criminal record check.
21. Assist the Burglary Apprehension Team with "Fencing Sting Operations."
22. Take major case prints for every person arrested for the crime of burglary.

### PDS/BRAM Supervisor

23. Be responsible for the review of all burglary/robbery complaints received at the office to determine case status.
24. Review all **COMPLAINT REPORTS (PD313-152)**, Omniform Complaint Revisions, and associated Enterprise Case Management (ECMS) entries to determine whether cases should remain ACTIVE or CLOSED, and to ensure all necessary investigative steps have been taken.
25. Assign robbery and burglary cases amongst all detectives in the unit.
26. Ensure the crime information centers are updated accordingly.
27. Ensure pawnshop/secondhand dealer inspections are properly investigated.
28. Verify major case prints are taken for every person arrested for burglary.

### Key Elements of BRAM Program

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Specialized BRAM unit | Created within **every** Precinct Detective Squad |
| Command structure | Under direct control of PDS Commander |
| Personnel allocation | Commensurate with reported burglary/robbery cases |
| Liaison | One PDS/BRAM member designated liaison with Borough Robbery Squad and Intelligence Division |
| Pattern identification | Detective Bureau notifies Patrol Borough Pattern ID Modules on all alerts |
| Photo books | Required — contain photos of persons previously arrested for burglary, robbery, and grand larceny |

> **Exam Alert:** The ECT (Evidence Collection Team) shall be notified to respond to **ALL** burglary incidents, including those where an arrest is already made at the scene. This is a frequently tested point -- ECT responds even when an arrest has occurred.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The PDS/BRAM Supervisor is responsible for reviewing all burglary/robbery complaints to determine case status (ACTIVE or CLOSED) and ensuring all investigative steps have been taken. Major case prints must be taken for every burglary arrest.
