**1. Who is required to use a paper Activity Log (PD112-145) instead of the digital Activity Log?**

- A) Members on light duty
- B) Auxiliary police officers
- C) Undercover officers
- D) Members above the rank of captain

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Undercover officers.** Per P.G. 212-08, undercovers use the paper Activity Log (PD112-145) and shall NOT use the digital version.

</details>

**2. Entries in the Interrupted Patrol Log are made in whose handwriting?**

- A) The member reporting to the stationhouse
- B) The desk officer's own handwriting
- C) The patrol supervisor on duty
- D) The platoon commander

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) The desk officer's own handwriting.** Per P.G. 212-10, the desk officer makes all entries in the Interrupted Patrol Log in their own handwriting and maintains the log under direct control.

</details>

---

**3. Activity Logs are considered potential what type of material?**

- A) Brady material
- B) Rosario material
- C) Classified material
- D) Privileged material

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Rosario material.** Per P.G. 212-08, Activity Logs are potential Rosario material and may be reviewed by the Department, outside agencies, DAs, and defense attorneys.

</details>

**4. Under the 4th platoon rule, members subpoenaed for next-day court are reassigned to which platoon?**

- A) 1st platoon
- B) 2nd platoon
- C) 3rd platoon (1600x2400)
- D) They remain on the 4th platoon

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 3rd platoon (1600x2400).** Per P.G. 212-03, members on the 4th platoon (1800x0200) who are subpoenaed for next-day court (2nd platoon) are reassigned to the 3rd platoon. If not notified in advance, they are excused at 2400 hours with no OT for the 2400-0200 period.

</details>

**5. Before evacuating transit passengers to the benchwall or roadbed, what must happen first?**

- A) A rescue train must be positioned
- B) Power must be removed
- C) The duty captain must approve
- D) ESU must arrive on scene

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Power must be removed.** Per P.G. 212-16, when evacuating passengers to the benchwall or roadbed, power MUST be removed first. Power is NOT removed for reach trains or rescue trains.

</details>

**6. What distinguishes a "reach train" from a "rescue train"?**

- A) Reach trains use emergency devices; rescue trains do not
- B) Reach trains involve walking through the stalled train; rescue trains involve cross-platform transfer via emergency devices
- C) Reach trains require power removal; rescue trains do not
- D) There is no difference; the terms are interchangeable

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Reach trains involve walking through the stalled train to a train positioned in front or behind; rescue trains are brought alongside for cross-platform transfer using emergency evacuation devices.** Per P.G. 212-16, neither requires power removal.

</details>

**7. The transit radio code 10-75T indicates what?**

- D) Mobile order maintenance

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Transit patrol by a non-Transit member.** Per P.G. 212-23, 10-75T is for transit patrol by non-Transit personnel. 10-75S is station inspection by Transit, and 10-75M is mobile order maintenance.

**13. Before polls open, the scanner machine public counter must read what?**

- A) Any number below 100
- B) The same as the protective counter
- C) Zero (0)
- D) The number of registered voters

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Zero (0).** Per P.G. 212-86, ALL scanner machine public counters must read zero before polls open. If not zero, the desk officer/supervisor must be notified immediately and the scanner shall NOT be used. The protective counter will likely NOT be zero — do not confuse the two.

</details>

**15. Polls close at what time, and what is the rule for persons on line?**

- A) 2000 hours; all persons must leave immediately
- B) 2100 hours; persons on line may vote, no new entries after 2100
- C) 2200 hours; persons on line may vote
- D) 2100 hours; no one may vote after 2100

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**21. Facial recognition technology establishes what level of evidence?**

- A) Probable cause for arrest
- B) Reasonable suspicion for a stop
- C) Investigative lead only — NOT probable cause
- D) Proof beyond reasonable doubt

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Investigative lead only — NOT probable cause.** Per P.G. 212-129, facial recognition does not establish probable cause by itself. A possible match candidate is an investigative lead only.

</details>

**23. VGTOF Handling Code 1 means what?**

- A) Detain — reasonable suspicion
- B) Arrest — warrant exists, approach with caution
- C) Investigation — do not alert individual
- D) Information gathering only

**24. Can a diplomat with full immunity be arrested?**

- A) Yes, for any felony
- B) Yes, but only for violent felonies
- C) No, but may be issued a summons
- D) No, and may not be issued a summons

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) No, but may be issued a summons.** Per P.G. 212-56, diplomats with immunity shall NOT be arrested but MAY be issued a summons. Pending verification, they may be detained if a danger to self or public.

</details>

**25. Can an MOS request a breathalyzer test from a diplomat suspected of DWI?**

- A) No, this would violate diplomatic immunity
- B) Yes, and the diplomat must comply
- C) Yes, the MOS may ask, but the diplomat may refuse
- D) Only with approval from the Intelligence Division

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Yes, the MOS may ask, but the diplomat may refuse.** Per P.G. 212-56, MOS may ASK a diplomat to take a sobriety test (breathalyzer), but the diplomat is entitled to REFUSE. The request and response must be documented in the Activity Log and Unusual Occurrence Report.

</details>
