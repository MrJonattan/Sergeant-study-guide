# Section 212 — Housing Operations

## P.G. 212-26 — Inspection of NYCHA Facilities

### Covered Facilities
- Community centers
- Child care centers
- Maintenance shops
- Health centers
- Senior citizen centers
- Laundry rooms
- Boiler rooms

### Inspection Schedule
- Check each facility on post during the **first AND last hour** of each tour, if possible.
- **Frequently** inspect security during the tour.
- Give special attention to **"Post Condition"** facilities.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisors shall inspect Activity Logs to ensure NYCHA facilities are being inspected by members on post. Supervisors should recommend Departmental Recognition for burglary arrests at NYCHA facilities.

> **Exam Alert:** Inspections are required during **both** the first and last hour of each tour — not just one or the other.

## P.G. 212-28 — Disabled Housing Authority Elevator

### Key Points
- Procedure governs handling of passengers trapped in NYCHA elevators.
- Respond promptly to reports of disabled elevators with trapped occupants.
- Notify appropriate NYCHA maintenance personnel.
- Ensure safety of trapped individuals until professional assistance arrives.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor should respond to ensure proper coordination between NYPD and NYCHA maintenance staff, particularly when occupants include elderly or medically vulnerable individuals.

## P.G. 212-60 — Interior Patrol of Housing Authority Buildings

### Purpose
Covers systematic interior patrols of NYCHA buildings to maintain safety and deter criminal activity.

### Key Procedures
- Conduct thorough interior patrols of NYCHA buildings on post.
- Check stairwells, hallways, rooftops, and common areas.
- Document patrol activity in Activity Log.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors shall review Activity Logs to verify interior patrols are being conducted and documented. Ensure members are varying patrol times and routes to maximize deterrent effect.
