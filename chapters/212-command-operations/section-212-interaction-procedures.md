# Section 212 — Interaction Procedures

## P.G. 212-104 — Interaction with Persons Who Are Deaf or Hard of Hearing

### Legal Foundation
- **Americans with Disabilities Act** (ADA)
- State and local law
- Department policy

All require **effective communication** with persons who are deaf or hard of hearing.

### Primary Consideration
- The type of auxiliary aid **requested by the individual** is the primary consideration.

> **Exam Alert:** The individual's own preference for the type of auxiliary aid is the **primary consideration**. The Department does not unilaterally decide the accommodation.

### Cost
- Must **NOT** charge the person for auxiliary aids or services.

### Qualified Interpreter Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Must interpret | Effectively, accurately, and impartially |
| Family member/child/friend | May **NOT** be qualified — especially in domestic disputes |

> **Exam Alert:** A family member, child, or friend may **not** serve as a qualified interpreter, particularly in domestic dispute situations where impartiality is compromised.

### Communication Resources

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **TRS (Telecommunications Relay Service)** | Dial **711** |
| **TTY number** | (212) 504-4115 for 311 system |
| Sign language interpreter | Contact **Operations Unit** |

### Arrest Procedures
- Arrest proceeds normally with probable cause.
- Auxiliary aids used for communication regarding charges, interrogation, etc.
- Arrestee permitted to use **TRS** for phone calls.
- **Qualified interpreter** provided for communication during processing.

### Pre-Planned Events
- CO prepares a **Typed Letterhead** request to the **Chief of Personnel** when a sign language interpreter is needed for a pre-planned event.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure that communication accommodations are provided promptly and that the individual's preferred method of communication is the primary consideration. For pre-planned events, arrange interpreter services in advance through the Chief of Personnel.
