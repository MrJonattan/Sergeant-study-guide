# Section 212 — Transit Operations

## P.G. 212-16 — Evacuations of NYC Transit Trains

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Reach Train** | Passengers walk through stalled train to reach a train positioned in front or behind |
| **Rescue Train** | Brought alongside; passengers cross via emergency evacuation devices |
| **Emergency Evacuation Devices** | Located at south end of southbound platform near blue light |

### Power Removal Rules

| Evacuation Type | Power Removal Required? |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Reach train | **NO** |
| Rescue train | **NO** |
| Evacuation to benchwall/roadbed | **YES — MUST ensure power is removed first** |

> **Exam Alert:** Power must be removed **only** when evacuating to benchwall or roadbed. Reach trains and rescue trains do **not** require power removal.

### Notifications and Responsibilities
- **MOS:** Notify patrol supervisor/duty captain; report who ordered evacuation and why.
- **Patrol supervisor:** Establish command post; keep dispatcher and duty captain informed.
- **Duty captain:** Contact **RTOCC** (Rapid Transit Operations Command Center); maintain constant communication.
- Typed Letterhead report submitted to Chief, Transit Bureau for all incidents.

## P.G. 212-17 — Critical Situations on NYC Transit

### Types of Critical Situations
- Fires, floods, collisions, derailments
- Persons under trains
- Major service disruptions

### Key Procedures
- Remove power when necessary.
- Post a member in the street to direct emergency units.
- **DO NOT** permit removal of sick/injured firefighter without fire officer consent.
- Remove persons from under trains **EXCEPT** when deceased and possibly a crime victim.

### Train Crew Signal for Police
- Horn/whistle pattern: **LONG-SHORT-LONG-SHORT**

> **Exam Alert:** The train crew signal for police is **LONG-SHORT-LONG-SHORT**. Remember this distinctive pattern.

### Required Reporting Details
Report to duty captain/incident commander:
- Train operator information
- Conductor information
- Line and direction of travel
- Car numbers
- Full incident details

## P.G. 212-19 — Animals on NYC Public Transportation

### General Rule
- Section 1050.9(h)(1) NYCRR: **No animals** unless enclosed in a container.

### Exemptions

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Law enforcement dogs | Permitted without container |
| Service animals | Seeing eye, hearing, physical disability, non-apparent disabilities (epilepsy, heart, lung disease) |
| Animals in training | Permitted |

> **Exam Alert:** Members **CANNOT** require proof of disability or documentation for a service animal. May **only** inquire if the animal is providing disability assistance.

### Stray Dogs on Roadbed
- Request patrol supervisor and ESU.
- **DO NOT** enter roadbed without supervisor permission.
- **DO NOT** chase after the dog (causes it to run).
- Restraining pole shall **not** be waved as a weapon.

### Dead Animal
- Notify radio dispatcher.
- Contact Sanitation Department for removal.

## P.G. 212-21 — Photography on NYC Transit System

### General Rule
- Photography, filming, and video recording are **PERMITTED** on the transit system.
- **Ancillary equipment** (lights, reflectors, tripods) may **NOT** be used.

### Exception
- Press with valid **MOME** (Mayor's Office of Media and Entertainment) ID **may** use ancillary equipment.

### When to Take Action

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Camera alone, no hazard | **No action** |
| Ancillary equipment without press ID | Take action |
| Creating a hazard | Take action |

> **Exam Alert:** Camera alone = no action needed. Only act if ancillary equipment is used without a MOME press ID or if a hazard is being created.

## P.G. 212-22 — Malfunction of Transit Bureau Radio Network

### Dispatch Procedures
- Dispatch re-routes Transit calls to Patrol Services Bureau.
- If a call is not finalized in **30 minutes**, dispatch another unit.
- Prioritize 911 calls from field MOS.

### Command Responsibilities

> **Sergeant Focus:** During radio malfunction:
> - Radio check of strategic areas at **30-minute intervals**
> - Assign officers to **paired patrols** with UHF radios
> - **Suspend** assignments in civilian clothes
> - Members make **hourly signals** to command
> - Strategic Response Group placed on **"Alert"** for Transit use if required

### Critical Limitation
- NYPD radios (other than Transit Bureau) **DO NOT** work underground, except in Manhattan stations equipped with repeaters.

## P.G. 212-23 — Inspection of Rapid Transit Stations on Post

### Radio Code Signals

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **10-75T** | Transit patrol by non-Transit member |
| **10-75S** | Station inspection by Transit member |
| **10-75O** | TOMS |
| **10-75M** | Mobile order maintenance |

### Inspection Requirements
- Confer with railroad clerk at each station about conditions.
- Inspect concessions, toilets, stairways, mezzanines, platforms, and **all** token booths (open and closed).
- Visit each station **at least once per tour**.

### Opposite Sex Toilet Inspection
- Knock and announce **"Police Inspection."**
- **WILL NOT** enter until departure of occupants.
- **UNLESS** informed of criminal activity inside.

> **Exam Alert:** The opposite-sex toilet rule requires knock and announce first. Entry is delayed until occupants depart, unless criminal activity is reported.

### RMP Recorder
- Shall inspect each uncovered station in the sector.

## P.G. 212-24 — Removal of Power in Subway

### Authorization
- **ONLY** in extreme emergencies involving life-threatening situations.
- **Patrol supervisor** determines necessity.

### Factors to Consider
- Time of day (rush vs. non-rush)
- Weather (extreme heat)
- Station type (elevated vs. below ground)
- Train locations in vicinity
- Extent of injuries
- Risk to public

### Emergency Alarm Box

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Spacing | **400-600 feet** apart |
| Location | Beneath **blue lights** |
| Operation | Pull lever **down** as far as possible, then release |
| Effect | Removes power on **ALL tracks** in vicinity |

> **Exam Alert:** Emergency alarm boxes are spaced **400-600 feet** apart under **blue lights**. They remove power on **ALL** tracks in the vicinity, not just one track.

### Critical Warnings
- NYC Transit will **automatically restore** power if not notified immediately via the telephone at the alarm box.
- Officers shall **NOT** personally shut off power **UNLESS** grave risk of **imminent danger** and **no other** safe alternative exists.

### Notification
- Notify Operations Unit of time of request; notify again when power is restored.

### Alternative to Power Removal
- Ride in lead cars through the area to search for suspects.
