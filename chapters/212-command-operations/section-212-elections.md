# Section 212 — Elections

## P.G. 212-86 — Prior to Opening of Polls

### Arrival Time
- Arrive at the poll site at least **1 hour** prior to opening.

### Duties Before Polls Open
1. Deliver election supplies and obtain receipt from Board of Elections members.
2. Inspect **ALL** scanner machine "public counters."
3. Confirm Board of Elections posted no-electioneering signs within **100 feet** of entrance.
4. Check for suspicious packages and Election Law violations.

### Scanner Machine Inspection

| Counter Type | Expected Reading | Action if Not Expected |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Public counter** | Must be **ZERO (0)** | Notify desk officer/supervisor immediately. Scanner **NOT** used if counter is not zero. |
| **Protective counter** | Will likely **NOT** be zero | This is normal — do NOT confuse with public counter |

> **Exam Alert:** The **public counter** must read zero. The **protective counter** will likely NOT be zero — do not mistake one for the other. If the public counter is not zero, the scanner shall NOT be used and the supervisor must be notified immediately.

## P.G. 212-87 — While Polls Are Open

### Election District Staffing

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Full staffing | **4 members** — 2 from each of the top 2 political parties |
| Minimum staffing | **2 members** from different parties |

### Officer Duties
- Remain in poll site unless required for other duty or meal.
- Remain **outside the guardrail** except to perform a specific duty.
- Do **NOT** take part in the election process.

### Persons Permitted Within the Guardrail
- Election inspectors
- Voters
- Authorized Board of Elections representatives
- Attorney General
- Authorized poll watchers
- Persons admitted for the purpose of maintaining order

### Poll Watcher Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Maximum per candidate per ED | **3** poll watchers |
| Inside guardrail per candidate per ED | Only **1** |
| Entry time | May enter before opening (**0500**) |
| Departure | May remain after closing |
| Prohibited actions | Electioneering, tampering, interfering, accompanying voter inside booth |

### Close of Polls

> **Exam Alert:** Polls close at **2100 hours**. Persons already on line at 2100 **may** vote. **NO** new persons may join the line after 2100.

### Person Denied Right to Vote
- Refer to the **Supreme Court** of the county concerned.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure officers understand the guardrail boundaries, do not participate in the election process, and properly handle disputes about voting rights by referring to the county Supreme Court.

## P.G. 212-88 — After Close of Polls

### Post-Closing Duties
- Secure election materials according to Board of Elections procedures.
- Inspect scanner machines after closing.
- Ensure orderly conclusion of voting process.
- Maintain security of poll site until materials are properly transferred.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure all election materials are accounted for and properly secured. Verify that the transition from active voting to post-closing procedures occurs smoothly and without interference.
