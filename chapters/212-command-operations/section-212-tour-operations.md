# Section 212 — Tour Operations

## P.G. 212-01 — Roll Call Formations

*(Updated R.O. 109, effective 10/24/25)*

### Purpose
To inspect, instruct and conduct roll call of uniformed members of the service.

### Timing
- **Five minutes** after the start of tour of duty.

### Patrol Supervisor Duties

1. Assemble platoon in ranks in the sitting or muster room.
2. Make assignments.
3. Inspect the platoon:
   a. Allow uniformed members to reasonably demonstrate that a **protective vest** is being worn.
   b. List members deficient in uniform, equipment or personal appearance.
      - Pay special attention to seasonal/optional uniform items (AG 305-03, AG 305-04).
   c. Reprimand and instruct deficient members **privately**.
   d. Follow up to determine if corrections have been made; take disciplinary action if necessary.
4. Ensure QOL officers and administrative QOL officers are **not** utilized as backfill for other precinct assignments (shooting posts, prisoner transports, hospital posts, patrol supervisor, desk officer, etc.) without authorization of the commanding officer.
5. Ensure QOL officers, administrative QOL officers, and QOL sergeants are not assigned to out-of-precinct details during their regularly scheduled tours without permission of the Bureau Chief concerned, the Chief of Department, or the Police Commissioner.
6. Ensure members are familiar with integrity concerns.
7. Ensure members are aware of active Paid Detail locations and times of assignments at these locations within the precinct.
8. Conduct a **"platoon briefing"** informing uniformed members of:
   a. Information compiled by training sergeant,
   b. Incident critiques,
   c. Updates regarding crime patterns, crime trends, and wanted persons, and
   d. Precinct conditions and tactics for addressing the conditions, etc.
9. Report personally to ranking officer, members absent and reason, if known.
10. Instruct platoon not to congregate in groups around the stationhouse.
11. Direct members of platoon to leave stationhouse for post or assignments at selected intervals.

> **Exam Alert:** The platoon briefing is a **Patrol Supervisor** responsibility. It covers training sergeant info, incident critiques, crime patterns/trends/wanted persons, and precinct conditions/tactics.

### Protective Vest Inspection

> **Exam Alert:** Upon notification that a protective vest inspection will be conducted, the uniformed member must take **appropriate actions** to demonstrate the vest is being worn:
> - Raising, removing, unbuttoning top garment to expose the vest to visual inspection, OR
> - Patting on the front and rear of the vest to produce a sound, OR
> - Any other manner indicating to the patrol supervisor that the vest is being worn

> **Sergeant Focus:** Only authorized/approved protective vests will be worn.

### Lieutenant-Platoon Commander

12. Supervise roll call.
    a. Personally inspect the outgoing platoon to ensure personal appearance meets Department standards.

### Special Operations Lieutenant

13. Ensure QOL officers and admin QOL officers are **not** utilized as backfill without authorization of the Commanding Officer.
14. Ensure QOL officers, admin QOL officers, and QOL sergeants are not assigned to out-of-precinct details without permission of the Bureau Chief, Chief of Department, or Police Commissioner.

### Travel Time Note

> *Sergeants, police officers and detectives required to report in uniform to a location within the geographic boundaries of the precinct to which assigned at the beginning of their tour, shall be given sufficient travel time within the tour.*

## P.G. 212-02 — Meal Period

*(Updated R.O. 6, effective 01/07/26)*

### Purpose
To provide for meal periods, obtain authorization for out of command meal, obtain approval from competent authority for meal period, and report meal location to radio dispatcher.

### Uniformed Member of the Service

1. Take meal in either a **police facility**, **bonafide restaurant**, or **Department vehicle**.
2. Notify radio dispatcher at beginning of meal, of location where meal is to be taken.
   a. If meal is to be taken in a Department facility, notify the desk officer.

> *Members shall commence and end their meal period on assigned post. Travel time to and from a meal location is not authorized.*

### Foot/Train Patrol

3. Obtain meal on assigned post, if possible, and in a place maintained for eating purposes.
4. Make digital **Activity Log** entry prior to leaving post including meal location address, and again upon returning to post.
5. Inform radio dispatcher upon return to post and make digital Activity Log entry.

### RMP Assignment

6. Notify radio dispatcher, by appropriate code signal, prior to commencing assigned meal period and upon return to patrol and provide:
   a. Address when meal taken in restaurant
   b. Nearest intersection when meal taken in RMP
   c. "Stationhouse" when meal taken in command facility
7. Remain in area designated by commanding officer, if meal is taken within command facility.
   a. Be available for assignment by desk officer.
8. Acknowledge calls directed to unit and:
   a. Remain within assigned sector, when practical
   b. Do not park more than one Department vehicle in the same place at the same time
   c. Comply with any request for police service and make digital Activity Log entry.
9. Notify desk officer when deprived of scheduled meal due to police service.

### Desk Officer

10. Take meal, when appropriate, providing another supervisor is assigned to cover desk officer responsibilities and is able to provide continuous police service within the command.
11. Make accommodations, if possible, so that members are not deprived of meal, if their originally scheduled meal is delayed due to police services rendered.
    a. Assign a police officer for relief of RMP operator or recorder, when necessary.
    b. Assign a police officer to provide meal relief for member assigned to fixed post, if available.

### Assigned Meal Relief Officer

12. Remain in view on post at time of relief when assigned to relieve radio motor patrol unit for meal.
    a. Assume all duties of recorder during relief period.

### RMP Operator

13. Transport relieved member directly to meal location.

### Uniformed Member on Meal

14. Notify desk officer if radio motor patrol fails to return within **fifteen minutes** after end of meal period.

> **Exam Alert:** If RMP fails to return within **15 minutes** after end of meal period, the member on meal must notify the desk officer. This is a specific time limit frequently tested.

### Patrol Supervisor

15. Notify radio dispatcher, via **landline**, from meal location when commencing meal period.
    a. Make digital Activity Log entry at beginning of meal (include address of meal location) and upon conclusion of meal period.

### Duty in Civilian Clothes

16. Request permission from immediate supervisor prior to taking meal.
17. Notify immediate supervisor of intended meal location.
    a. If immediate supervisor is unavailable, notify bureau/borough operations desk/Field Operations Desk (FOD) of intended meal location.
18. Document name of supervisor notified and meal location in appropriate Department record (digital Activity Log, movement log, etc.).

### Additional Data — Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Intoxicants** | All on-duty members are **strictly prohibited** from consuming intoxicants in any amount during meal period |
| **Civilian clothes exception** | Members in civilian clothes may be granted permission by bureau chief/counterpart |
| **Meal eligibility** | Must be scheduled for **8 consecutive hours** of duty |
| **8/9-hour tour** | One hour meal period |
| **12-hour tour** | Two, non-consecutive, **45-minute** meal periods |
| **Not during first/last** | Members are not to be assigned meal periods during the **first hour or last hour and one-half** of their tour, except in emergency |
| **3-hour excusal rule** | If a police officer requests excusal of 3 hours or less, they are eligible for a meal period provided the request for partial excusal is made **prior to** start of the scheduled tour |

> **Exam Alert:** Know the meal period rules for different tour lengths: 8/9-hour tour = 1 hour; 12-hour tour = two non-consecutive **45-minute** periods. Meals are NOT assigned during the first hour or last 1.5 hours of the tour.

### Extended Tour Overtime Meal Rules

- Meal period on extended tour overtime may be granted by a supervisor **only** if assigned meal period was denied during the regularly scheduled tour.
- Such meal period may **not** be granted if it interferes with arrest processing or needs of the service.
- Under **no circumstances** will a rescheduled meal on extended tour overtime be assigned during the **last hour of overtime**.
- Once the event necessitating the overtime ends, a member who has not yet taken a meal period is **precluded** from taking any meal thereafter.

### Outside Details — Police Officers

- Police officers assigned to outside details (parades, events, etc.) **should** be granted a one-hour meal period absent exigent circumstances.
- Police officers denied a meal period are entitled to **one hour compensatory time** at straight time.
- This credit is **not** available if meal was denied due to exigent circumstances.
- Detail supervisors **shall** assign meal periods prior to posting the detail.
- If exigent circumstances prevent granting meals, commanding officers of outside details must submit a written communication to the Commanding Officer, Office of Labor Relations detailing the actual exigent circumstances. The Police Commissioner makes the final determination.

### Meal in Adjoining Precinct

- CO of a precinct may designate a portion of an adjoining precinct where members may obtain meals during hours when a suitable eating facility is not available within the precinct.
- Such designation forwarded on **Typed Letterhead** to the appropriate borough commander.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure meal periods are properly scheduled and documented. Key responsibilities include: assigning meal periods prior to posting details, ensuring members are not deprived of meals, and properly documenting any exigent circumstances that prevent granting meals.

## P.G. 212-03 — Expiration of Tour

### Foot Patrol
- Leave post and proceed directly to stationhouse at end of tour.
- Contact desk officer **1 hour prior** to end of tour when on assignment requiring relief.

### RMP Crew
- Proceed to stationhouse, park in relief area.
- Remain until relieved by incoming crew.
- Inform relieving operator of any incomplete assignments or vehicle defects.
- If RMP will **not** be used next tour: operator secures auto and delivers keys to desk officer.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Desk officer shall check CPR System Roll Call to account for all members from previous tour. Conduct **immediate investigation** if any member failed to scan out.

### 4th Platoon Rule

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Platoon | 4th (1800x0200) |
| Trigger | Subpoenaed for next-day court (2nd platoon) |
| Reassignment | 3rd platoon (1600x2400) |
| Not notified in advance | Excused at 2400 hours |
| Overtime | **NO OT** for 2400-0200 period |
| Working beyond 0200 | Entitled to OT compensation |

> **Exam Alert:** The 4th platoon rule reassigns members to the **3rd platoon** (1600x2400), not the 2nd platoon. Members are excused at 2400 if not notified in advance, and there is **no overtime** for the 2400-0200 gap.

## P.G. 212-08 — Activity Logs

### Who Uses Digital vs. Paper

| Format | Who |
|--------|-----|
| **Digital Activity Log** (Department smartphone) | All uniformed MOS below captain rank (except permanent admin/clerical) |
| **Paper Activity Log** (PD112-145) | **Undercovers** — shall NOT use digital |

> **Exam Alert:** Undercovers use the **paper** Activity Log (PD112-145), never the digital version.

### Required Entries
- Day, date, and tour
- Assignment
- Meal time
- Operator and recorder name
- Vehicle inspection (condition, odometer, gas level)
- Chronological activities

### RMP Activity Log Rules
- **Only the recorder** makes chronological entries.
- Operator may note "See Recorder's Activity Log."
- **BOTH** operator and recorder **must** record their own enforcement actions.

### Digital Activity Log Features
- Can capture non-MOS signatures (physician, DA, etc.)

### Rosario Material Warning

> **Exam Alert:** Activity Logs are potential **Rosario material** — they may be reviewed by the Department, outside agencies, DAs, and defense attorneys.

### Restrictions
- **DO NOT** upload official Department arrestee photos to the digital Activity Log (sealed upon termination of criminal action).
- Disclosure or sale of Activity Logs is **prohibited** under NY City Charter sections 2604(b)(4) and 2604(d)(5) — violation carries financial penalties and possible misdemeanor charges.

## P.G. 212-10 — Interrupted Patrol Log

### Purpose
Controls time spent in the stationhouse by platoon members during their tour.

### Procedure
1. Member reports to desk officer with reason for presence in stationhouse.
2. Desk officer makes entries **in own handwriting**:

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Time In | When member arrived |
| Rank | Member's rank |
| Name | Member's name |
| Assignment | Current assignment |
| Reason | Why member is in stationhouse |
| Time Out | When member departed |
| Total Time | Duration in stationhouse |
| Desk Officer | Desk officer initials/signature |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer shall maintain the Interrupted Patrol Log under **direct control** at all times and review it frequently during the tour to ensure member presence is being expedited.

> **Exam Alert:** Entries are made in the **desk officer's own handwriting** — not the member's. Commanding officers shall frequently review this log to determine if stops are necessary and being expedited.
