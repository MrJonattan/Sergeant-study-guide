# Section 212 — Tour Operations

## P.G. 212-03 — Expiration of Tour

### Foot Patrol
- Leave post and proceed directly to stationhouse at end of tour.
- Contact desk officer **1 hour prior** to end of tour when on assignment requiring relief.

### RMP Crew
- Proceed to stationhouse, park in relief area.
- Remain until relieved by incoming crew.
- Inform relieving operator of any incomplete assignments or vehicle defects.
- If RMP will **not** be used next tour: operator secures auto and delivers keys to desk officer.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Desk officer shall check CPR System Roll Call to account for all members from previous tour. Conduct **immediate investigation** if any member failed to scan out.

### 4th Platoon Rule

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Platoon | 4th (1800x0200) |
| Trigger | Subpoenaed for next-day court (2nd platoon) |
| Reassignment | 3rd platoon (1600x2400) |
| Not notified in advance | Excused at 2400 hours |
| Overtime | **NO OT** for 2400-0200 period |
| Working beyond 0200 | Entitled to OT compensation |

> **Exam Alert:** The 4th platoon rule reassigns members to the **3rd platoon** (1600x2400), not the 2nd platoon. Members are excused at 2400 if not notified in advance, and there is **no overtime** for the 2400-0200 gap.

## P.G. 212-08 — Activity Logs

### Who Uses Digital vs. Paper

| Format | Who |
|--------|-----|
| **Digital Activity Log** (Department smartphone) | All uniformed MOS below captain rank (except permanent admin/clerical) |
| **Paper Activity Log** (PD112-145) | **Undercovers** — shall NOT use digital |

> **Exam Alert:** Undercovers use the **paper** Activity Log (PD112-145), never the digital version.

### Required Entries
- Day, date, and tour
- Assignment
- Meal time
- Operator and recorder name
- Vehicle inspection (condition, odometer, gas level)
- Chronological activities

### RMP Activity Log Rules
- **Only the recorder** makes chronological entries.
- Operator may note "See Recorder's Activity Log."
- **BOTH** operator and recorder **must** record their own enforcement actions.

### Digital Activity Log Features
- Can capture non-MOS signatures (physician, DA, etc.)

### Rosario Material Warning

> **Exam Alert:** Activity Logs are potential **Rosario material** — they may be reviewed by the Department, outside agencies, DAs, and defense attorneys.

### Restrictions
- **DO NOT** upload official Department arrestee photos to the digital Activity Log (sealed upon termination of criminal action).
- Disclosure or sale of Activity Logs is **prohibited** under NY City Charter sections 2604(b)(4) and 2604(d)(5) — violation carries financial penalties and possible misdemeanor charges.

## P.G. 212-10 — Interrupted Patrol Log

### Purpose
Controls time spent in the stationhouse by platoon members during their tour.

### Procedure
1. Member reports to desk officer with reason for presence in stationhouse.
2. Desk officer makes entries **in own handwriting**:

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Time In | When member arrived |
| Rank | Member's rank |
| Name | Member's name |
| Assignment | Current assignment |
| Reason | Why member is in stationhouse |
| Time Out | When member departed |
| Total Time | Duration in stationhouse |
| Desk Officer | Desk officer initials/signature |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer shall maintain the Interrupted Patrol Log under **direct control** at all times and review it frequently during the tour to ensure member presence is being expedited.

> **Exam Alert:** Entries are made in the **desk officer's own handwriting** — not the member's. Commanding officers shall frequently review this log to determine if stops are necessary and being expedited.
