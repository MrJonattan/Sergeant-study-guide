# Section 212 — Crime Scene and Investigative Encounters

## P.G. 212-04 — Crime Scene

### Police Line vs. Crime Scene Tape

| Tape Type | Color / Letters | Purpose |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **Police Line Do Not Cross** | White tape with **blue** letters | Establish a "secure area" for police operations (close streets, secure transit facilities, etc.) |
| **Crime Scene Do Not Cross** | Yellow tape with **black** letters | Safeguard a specific area where objects may be evidence; may be **within** the Police Line |

- Both tapes also display: **NYPD Crime Stoppers 1-800-577-TIPS (8744)**
- Crime Scene tape: Only authorized, trained, equipped, actively involved personnel whose presence is **absolutely required** will be permitted — absent exigents involving life or safety
- Deployment of Crime Scene tape for any other reason is **strictly prohibited**

> **Exam Alert:** Know the color distinction: Police Line = white tape/blue letters (secure area). Crime Scene = yellow tape/black letters (evidence area). Crime Scene tape may exist **within** the Police Line perimeter.

### Crime Scene Evidence Cone

- 5-inch-tall orange cone with "NYPD" in black letters
- Placed **next to** small evidence to prevent disturbance (shell casings, bullet fragments, etc.)

> **Exam Alert:** Per PG 219-01, the sergeant's vehicle should contain **5** Crime Scene Evidence Cones ("Five by Five").

### Barrier Tape Duty — Key Distinction

> **PG Conflict:** In PG 212-04 (Crime Scene), it is the **UMOS** duty to "utilize barrier tape as appropriate." In PG 212-05 (Transit Crime Scene), it is the **Patrol Supervisor** who utilizes barrier tape. Know which role applies to each procedure.

### UMOS Responsibilities

- Request through radio dispatcher:
  - Patrol Supervisor
  - PDS / Specialty Unit required
- Detain witnesses and persons with pertinent information
- Secure Crime Scene:
  - Remove unauthorized persons
  - Do not disturb evidence
  - Initially secure a **LARGE** area when uncertain
  - Utilize barrier tape and Crime Scene Evidence Cones as necessary
- Advise Patrol Supervisor and Detectives of witnesses detained and other case info
- Document: Digital Activity Log entries (observations, suspect/witness info, statements)

### Patrol Supervisor Responsibilities

- Assess the scene and determine if CSU services are required
- Call CSU **direct on landline** or if no landline available, through radio dispatcher

> **Exam Alert:** On test day, the correct answer for how the Patrol Supervisor contacts CSU is via **landline** (direct call). Do NOT choose "department-issued cell phone" — the PG specifies landline first, radio dispatcher only if landline unavailable.

- Provide specific details: cross streets, apt #, time/date, crime, weapon, # of victims, hospital
- If Patrol Supervisor cannot respond to scene: detective **on scene** MAY request CSU and other required notifications

> **Exam Alert:** The procedure says the detective may request CSU — it does NOT say the detective requests "Shots Fired." Watch for that distinction on the exam.

- Confer with Crime Scene Unit upon arrival
- Notify Desk Officer of details and request additional assistance if required
- Secure Crime Scene — have "Crime Scene" signs posted

### When to Request Crime Scene (CSU) — The HARBOR Mnemonic

> **Memory Aid — HARBOR:** When Crime Scene Must Respond
> - **H**omicide
> - **A**ggravated assault with dangerous instrument AND victim likely to die
> - **R**ape (forcible)
> - **B**urglary involving forced safe OR circumvented alarm
> - **O**ther crime in which CSU services will assist the investigation
> - **R**obbery OR hijacking with injury caused by firearm

> **Exam Alert:** For burglary, CSU responds when the safe is **forced** OR the alarm is **circumvented**. Compare this carefully with the "Refer to Squad" criteria in PG 207-07 where the safe merely needs to be **involved** and the gun merely **involved**.

### Refer to Squad (207-07) vs. Request Crime Scene (212-04)

| Crime | Refer to Squad (207-07) | Request Crime Scene (212-04) |
|-------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Assault** | SPI | **Likely** & D.I. |
| **Robbery** | Firearm or D.I. **Used**; Victim 60+ | Firearm **Caused Injury** |
| **Burglary** | Safe **Involved**; Gun **Involved**; >$5,000 stolen (Manhattan >$10,000); Person Present | Safe **Forced** OR Circumvented Alarm |

> **Prior Test:** Past exams have mixed up the Squad referral rules from 207-07 with the CSU request rules from 212-04. The key difference: Squad referrals use broader criteria (involved/used), while CSU requests use narrower criteria (forced/caused injury).

### DOA Cross-Reference

> **See Also:** PG 216-04 — In a DOA case, the **detective** notifies CSU, not the patrol supervisor.

## P.G. 212-11 — Investigative Encounters (Stop, Question & Frisk)

*(Updated R.O. 53, effective 06/26/25 — 16 pages)*

### BWC Requirement

> **Exam Alert:** Per PG 212-11, **always activate BWC** if assigned one, no matter what level the stop is. This applies to Level 1, 2, and 3 encounters.

### Three Levels of Encounter

| Element | Level 1 — Request for Information | Level 2 — Common Law Right of Inquiry | Level 3 — Terry Stop |
|---------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Based on** | Objective **credible** reason | **Founded** suspicion | **Reasonable** suspicion of Felony or PL Misdemeanor |
| **ID Self** | Verbally & display shield if practicable | Verbally: Rank, Name, Shield, Command; display shield, absent exigent circumstances | Verbally: Rank, Name, Shield, Command; display shield, absent exigent circumstances |
| **Ask** | Only **related** questions | May ask **accusatory** questions | May ask **accusatory** questions |
| **Detain/Force** | May NOT detain or use force | May NOT detain or use force | **May** detain or use force |
| **Search** | May NOT even ask to search | **May** ask to search | **May** ask to search |
| **Paperwork** | BWC categorization | CLRI - Consent Search Report (PD541-161), RTKBC | Stop Report (PD383-151), RTKBC, Digital Activity Log entry |

> **Exam Alert:** The critical distinction is that at Level 1, you may NOT even **request** consent to search. At Level 2, you MAY ask. At Level 3, you MAY ask and document the request on a **Stop Report** (not a CLRI).

### Definitions (Updated)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Objective Credible Reason** | Based on more than a hunch or a whim; may relate to public safety/service OR law enforcement, but need NOT be based on any indication of criminality |
| **Founded Suspicion** | Some present indication of criminality based on observable conduct or reliable hearsay information |
| **Reasonable Suspicion** | Information known to the member would make an ordinarily prudent and cautious police officer believe a felony or PL misdemeanor has been, is being, or is about to be committed |
| **Frisk** | A carefully limited running of the hands over the outside of a person's clothing for a deadly weapon or instrument readily capable of causing serious physical injury |
| **Search After Frisk** | When the officer places hands inside a pocket or other interior portions of clothing to remove an object that the member felt during a frisk and reasonably suspects is a weapon or dangerous instrument |
| **Protective Measures** | Tactics short of a frisk (e.g., ordering hands out of pockets, stepping away from objects that could be used as weapons, grabbing hands if circumstances suggest weapon) |

### False or Inconsistent Information

- At **any** level of encounter, false or inconsistent information **may** escalate the encounter

### DeBour vs. Terry — Legal Framework

| Case | Court | Levels |
|------|-------|--------|
| **People v. DeBour** | NYS Court of Appeals | Articulates 4 levels: (1) Request for info — no suspicion; (2) CLRI — **founded** suspicion; (3) Forcible stop — **reasonable** suspicion; (4) Arrest — probable cause |
| **Terry v. Ohio** | Federal (Supreme Court) | Provides authority for Level 3: forcibly stop and **possibly** frisk, based on reasonable suspicion |

> **Exam Alert:** DeBour is the **state** case (Court of Appeals) and provides the 4-level framework. Terry is the **federal** case (Supreme Court) and provides Level 3 stop-and-frisk authority. Know which court decided each.

### Level 1 Steps — "Request for Information"

1. Activate BWC, if assigned, for encounters where activation is required per P.G. 212-123.
2. Approach the person if there is an **objective credible reason** to do so.
3. ID self: Verbally AND display shield conspicuously, **if practicable**.
4. Do NOT detain the person, use or threaten the use of force.
5. Do NOT request consent to search.
6. Ask general, non-threatening questions **related to the reason for the approach**.
   - Pointed and accusatory questions are **NOT** permitted.
   - Person may refuse to answer and is free to leave.
7. May inform person free to leave, but NOT required to UNLESS person **specifically asks**.
8. **Should** provide an explanation for the encounter, UNLESS it would impair a criminal investigation.
9. **Protective measures:** In rare cases, may engage protective measures for safety (because of the nature of the approach or the individual's behavior).

### Level 1 — RTKBC and ID Requirements

- Offer **RIGHT TO KNOW BUSINESS CARD (PD142-012)** or **RIGHT TO KNOW BUSINESS CARD - GENERAL (PD142-013)**, as appropriate, per A.G. 304-11.

> **Exam Alert (Updated):** At Level 1, there are specific situations where you are NOT required to proactively identify yourself, explain the reason, or offer an RTKBC:
> - Engaged in undercover activity or operations
> - Exigent circumstances require your immediate action
> - Reasonably expect danger of physical injury, imminent property damage, imminent escape, imminent destruction of evidence
> - Conducting a security search of person entering a public building/facility/event/gathering (including MTA random checks, UNLESS requested by subject)
> - Verifying the identity of a person seeking entrance to a restricted area due to public health, safety, or security concern

### Level 2 Steps — "Common Law Right of Inquiry"

10. Activate BWC, if assigned, per P.G. 212-123.
11. Approach the person if you have a **founded suspicion of criminality**.
12. ID self: Rank, Name, Shield, Command; display shield conspicuously, absent exigent circumstances.
13. Do NOT detain or use force.
14. May ask pointed and accusatory questions.
15. Person may refuse and walk away. If person **runs**, UMOS may **pursue**.
16. Request consent to search (voluntary; elicit clear **"yes" or "no"**):
    a. Ask in a manner that elicits a clear "yes" or "no" response. Example: *"I can only search you, if you consent. Do you understand. May I search you?"*
    b. If a person does not consent, you **cannot** conduct a search.
    c. If seeking consent, you **must** video record the request and the person's response if you have a BWC.
    d. Document request, consent, refusal, and search (if performed) on the **COMMON LAW RIGHT OF INQUIRY - CONSENT SEARCH REPORT (PD541-161)**.
    e. Offer RTKBC in all cases; provide info on how to obtain a copy of the video record.
17. May inform person free to leave (not required unless person specifically asks).
18. May engage protective measures when you have a reasonable concern for your safety.
19. Provide individual with an explanation for the encounter, unless it would impair a criminal investigation.
20. Do NOT offer RTKBC if encounter ends in **arrest or summons**, unless requested.

> **Exam Alert (Updated):** At Level 2, the **CLRI - Consent Search Report** is only prepared for Level 2 requests to search. If you seek consent during a Level 3 Terry Stop, document that request on your **Stop Report**, not a CLRI. The CLRI is NOT prepared when conducting security searches, during exigent circumstances, or when danger of physical injury/imminent escape/evidence destruction exists.

### Level 2 Note — Creating a Situation

> *During a Level 1 or Level 2 encounter, an officer may not create a situation (either by words or actions) where a reasonable person would not feel free to walk away. A person may be detained only if a properly conducted Level 1 or Level 2 encounter yields information to support a reasonable suspicion that the person committed, was committing, or was about to commit a felony or Penal Law misdemeanor.*

### Level 3 Steps — "Terry Stop"

22. Activate BWC, if assigned, per P.G. 212-123.
23. Upon **reasonable suspicion** that the person has committed, is committing, or is about to commit a felony or PL misdemeanor, **stop and detain** the person.
24. Notify the radio dispatcher: location, number of persons being stopped, and whether additional units are needed.
25. ID self: Rank, Name, Shield, Command; display shield conspicuously, absent exigent circumstances.
26. Question the suspect to determine whether there is probable cause to make an arrest.
27. May ask pointed and accusatory questions. **Refusal to answer or produce identification does NOT establish probable cause.**
    a. Offer RTKBC as appropriate.
28. May request consent to search (same rules as Level 2 — elicit clear "yes" or "no", video record if BWC assigned, document on **STOP REPORT (PD383-151)**).
29. Reasonable force may be used to stop a person.
30. May engage protective measures for safety.
31. May frisk if at any point during the encounter you have RS that the person is armed and dangerous.
32. Detention: only as long as necessary to confirm or dispel suspicion. Authority to detain ends when tasks tied to the stop are completed or reasonably should have been completed.
33. Provide individual with an explanation for the encounter, unless it would impair a criminal investigation.
34. Obtain suspect's name, address, and additional information for your digital Activity Log entry regarding the stop.
35. Do NOT transport or move suspect from location UNLESS:
    - Voluntary consent, or
    - Exigency (hostile crowd, threat to safety, hospital show-up, etc.)
36. Release the person immediately after completing the investigation if probable cause to arrest does not exist.
37. Do NOT offer RTKBC if encounter ends in **arrest or summons**, unless requested.

### Level 3 — When RTKBC/ID Not Required

38. Same exceptions as Level 1 (undercover, exigent circumstances, security search, restricted area verification, danger of physical injury/imminent escape/evidence destruction).

### Frisk Rules (Updated)

39. If a police officer develops reasonable suspicion that a person is **armed and dangerous**, the officer may frisk for:
    - A deadly weapon or any instrument, article, or substance readily capable of causing serious physical injury
    - Of a sort not ordinarily carried in public places by law-abiding persons

    RS that a person is armed and dangerous may arise from:
    a. RS that the suspect has committed, is committing, or is about to commit a **violent crime** (assault with deadly weapon, burglary, rape, robbery, etc.)
    b. Observation of something on the person the officer reasonably suspects is a weapon
    c. A statement by the suspect that she/he is armed
    d. Information known that the suspect may be carrying a weapon (victim/witness statements)
40. Purpose of the frisk: ensure **officer safety** — NOT to locate evidence of a crime (such as drugs).
41. **No requirement** to question suspect before conducting a lawful frisk.
42. Conduct the frisk by carefully running your hands down the outside of the person's clothing.
43. If the frisk reveals an object that the member reasonably suspects may be a weapon: search **only** those interior portions of the stopped person's clothing to remove the weapon.
44. **Bag/personal property** — need RS person is armed and dangerous AND bag could contain weapon AND within person's reach:
    - Soft bag: run hands down outside; open ONLY if you feel the contours of what she/he believes is a weapon
    - Rigid and unlocked: open to ensure no weapon
    - Rigid and locked: need **search warrant** or **consent** to search

> **Exam Alert:** Mass transit checkpoint inspections of backpacks/containers for explosive devices follow different rules and are NOT governed by the frisk rules above.

### Requesting Identification Documents

> *At any level, an officer may ask an individual to verbally identify herself/himself or present an identification document. During Level 1 or 2 encounters, the officer must not create a situation where the person does not feel free to leave. Other than the operator of a motor vehicle/motorcycle, members of the public are NOT required to possess or present identification documents. Refusal or inability to produce identification alone will NOT elevate the level of the encounter. Absent probable cause, a person may not be arrested or removed to a Department facility merely because she/he refused to produce identification.*

### Stop Report Documentation (Updated)

47. For all Terry Stops/Level 3 encounters, prepare a **STOP REPORT** using FORMS for **EACH** person stopped.
    a. Complete all applicable captions and follow the directions for each section prior to end of tour.
    b. Check "REFUSED" if the person stopped refused to identify herself/himself.
       - (1) Request the patrol supervisor to respond to verify refusal.
       - (2) Do not detain the person, however, if the investigation is complete and there is no probable cause.
    c. Select all relevant factors that led to the stop if more than one descriptive term applies.
    d. Describe in **plain language** (rather than numeric Penal Law section) the specific felony or PL misdemeanor you suspected.
    e. Describe in your own words, under the "Narrative (Describe the Circumstances That Led to the Stop)" caption, all of the facts and information relied upon to conclude RS existed.
    f. If frisked/searched, describe under the "Narrative (Describe the Circumstances That Led to the Frisk and/or Search)" caption all facts relied upon, the specific area searched, and whether a weapon or other contraband was recovered.
49. When prompted to identify "Reviewing Supervisor" in FORMS, enter the name or tax ID of a supervisor currently performing duty with the platoon/unit and select that supervisor from the dropdown.
    a. Select immediate squad/unit supervisor if assigned and available.
    b. If not available, enter another supervisor performing duty with the squad/unit (patrol supervisor, detail supervisor, etc.).
50. Notify the reviewing supervisor that the STOP REPORT was prepared and submitted to the supervisor's electronic "INBOX" folder in FORMS for review.

### Stop Report — Key Notes

> *The STOP REPORT is NOT prepared for Level 1 and Level 2 encounters, unless the encounter escalates to a Level 3 Terry Stop. The STOP REPORT is NOT prepared when an officer makes a summary arrest for an observed offense/crime or issues a Criminal Court summons or civil summons returnable to OATH for an observed violation, UNLESS the suspect was initially detained for investigation in a Level 3 Terry Stop. The STOP REPORT is NOT prepared for traffic stops based on violations of Vehicle and Traffic Law, unless the suspect is frisked.*

> **Exam Alert:** FORMS will time out after **55 minutes**. UMOS should periodically click "Save" to ensure data is not lost.

51. Record details in digital Activity Log entry:
    a. Date, time and location of stop
    b. Pedigree information (name, DOB, address, telephone number), unless refused, and detailed description
    c. Document refusal to provide pedigree information, if applicable
    d. ICAD number, if applicable
52. Prior to end of tour, submit the STOP REPORT and digital Activity Log printout to the patrol supervisor/unit supervisor for review.
    a. Reviewing supervisor must be at least **1 rank higher** than member submitting the STOP REPORT.

> **Exam Alert (Updated):** Pedigree information is NOT captured electronically and will ONLY be recorded in the member's digital Activity Log. Do NOT put pedigree information into the STOP REPORT narratives.

    d.     e. Complete the "Supervisory Action (Must Complete)" caption. Consider the facts and determine whether:
       - (1) The stop was based upon RS of a felony or PL misdemeanor

       - (3) If searched, whether there was a sufficient basis for the search

Even when the stop was improper, the supervisor approves and documents the issue. If prepared in error (was Level 1 or 2, or arrest/summons not the result of a Level 3), note in "Comments" section.

### Furtive Movements and High Crime Area

- "Furtive movements" or mere presence in "high crime area" alone are **insufficient** for a stop or frisk
- Even when used in combination with other stop factors, the stopping officer must be able to specifically describe the suspicious nature of the "furtive movements" observed
- Must not define "high crime area" too broadly (e.g., an entire precinct or borough)
- A person may not be stopped merely because he or she matches a **generalized description** of a crime suspect (such as an 18-25 year old male of a particular race)
- If a physical description is the **only factor** relied on, it must be more specific than race, age, and gender
- Individuals may not be targeted for stops and frisks because they are members of a racial or ethnic group that appears more frequently in local crime suspect data

> **Exam Alert:** Race may only be considered when the stop is based on a specific and reliable suspect description that includes not just race, age, and gender, but **other identifying characteristics and information**. When a police officer carries out a stop based on reasonable suspicion that a person fits such a description, the officer may consider the race of the suspect, just as the officer may consider height or hair color.

### Additional Data — Binder Maintenance

- Commands other than patrol precincts, PSAs, and transit districts will designate a supervisor to perform desk officer duties regarding STOP REPORTS.
- Photocopies of STOP REPORTS will be sent via Department mail to the precinct of occurrence daily. The precinct places them in their STOP REPORT command binder in sequential order.
- Desk officers/designees in commands other than patrol precincts, PSAs, or transit districts will maintain a standardized STOP REPORT command binder. A corresponding **Stop Report Index** will be printed out daily and maintained in the binder.
- Commanding officers will ensure photocopied STOP REPORTS maintained in the command binder are **removed and filed** in the command by year of occurrence every **January 1st** and quarterly thereafter (**April 1st, July 1st, and October 1st**).

> **Exam Alert (Updated):** STOP REPORT binders are purged quarterly (Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1) and filed by year of occurrence. The Stop Report Index is printed daily.
