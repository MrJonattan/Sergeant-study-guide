# Section 203-10 — Public Contact - Prohibited Conduct

## Source Reference
- **Procedure:** P.G. 203-10
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide1.pdf (A.G. 304-11)
- **Last Revision:** I.O.43 (06/10/21)

## Procedure Summary

This procedure establishes guidelines for uniformed members of the service when interacting with the public during law enforcement activities, including the requirement to provide Right to Know business cards.

## Law Enforcement Activities Requiring Business Card

Uniformed members **shall** offer a pre-printed Right to Know Business Card (PD142-012) upon conclusion of the following law enforcement activities:

1. **Noncustodial questioning** of individuals suspected of criminal activity (Level 2 Encounter)
2. **Stop** where officer has reasonable suspicion and a reasonable person would not feel free to end the encounter (Level 3 Encounter)
3. **Frisk** (Level 3 Encounter)
4. **Search of persons or property**, including vehicles
5. **Roadblock or checkpoint stops** (excluding security checks at sensitive locations)
6. **Home search**
7. **Assigned Detective Only:** First in-person questioning of victims and/or witnesses to a crime

## Requirements

### Member Shall:

1. **Courteously and clearly state** rank, name, shield number, and command, or provide them to anyone who requests
2. **Carry appropriate amount** of pre-printed Right to Know Business Cards (PD142-012)
   - If depleted, carry generic Right to Know Business Cards - General (PD142-013)
3. **Explain reason** for law enforcement activities, unless it would impair investigation or exigent circumstances exist
4. **Offer business card** upon conclusion of activities (except summons/arrest/exigent circumstances)
5. **Enter command** in space provided on front of card
   - **May** enter command phone number or Department cellphone number at discretion
6. **Make digital Activity Log entry** including pertinent information regarding law enforcement activities

> **Exam Alert:** Business cards are NOT required when a summons is issued or an arrest is made.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Uniformed members performing duty in undercover capacity are EXEMPT from providing business cards due to sensitive nature of assignment.

## Exceptions

Business card is **NOT** required when:
- Summons is issued
- Arrest is made
- Exigent circumstances exist (physical resistance, flight, imminent danger of physical injury/damage to property)
- Member is undercover or operationally supporting UC/confidential informant

## Captains and Above

Uniformed members in rank of **Captain and above** and civilian members holding title of **Director and above** will use the **Business Card Executive (PD142-091)** rather than the Right to Know Business Card.

> **Memory Aid — RIGHT TO KNOW:**
> - **R**ank, name, shield, command stated
> - **I**nvestigation reason explained (unless exigent)
> - **G**ive card after encounter
> - **H**ome search, frisk, stop, search, roadblock, detective questioning, noncustodial questioning
> - **T**o Know encounters (Level 2 & 3)
> - **O**nly undercover exempt
> - **K**eep digital Activity Log
