# Section 203-13 — Financial Restrictions - Prohibited Acts

## Source Reference
- **Procedure:** P.G. 203-13
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide1.pdf (A.G. 304-12)
- **Last Revision:** I.O.44 (06/10/21)

## Procedure Summary

This procedure establishes eleven (11) financial restrictions and prohibited acts for members of the service, primarily governed by Chapter 68 of the New York City Charter (Conflicts of Interest).

## Prohibited Financial Acts

### 1. Using Confidential Information
**Shall NOT** use confidential official information to advance financial interest of self or another.

### 2. Conflict of Interest
**Shall NOT** engage in any financial, commercial, or private interest which is directly or indirectly in conflict with official duties.

### 3. Purchasing City Property
**Shall NOT** purchase city-owned real property through negotiated sale, EXCEPT:
- City-owned residential building of six units or less

> **Exam Alert:** Violation subjects member to fine, suspension, or dismissal, AND member may be prosecuted for a misdemeanor.

### 4. Real Estate in Precinct
**Shall NOT** purchase real estate for rental purposes within precinct of assignment.

### 5. Commercial Advertisement
**Shall NOT** authorize use of photograph in uniform or mentioning rank/title/Department membership for commercial advertisement.

### 6. Accepting Testimonial Awards/Gifts
**Shall NOT** accept testimonial award, gift, loan, or thing of value to defray/reimburse any fine or penalty, or reward for police service EXCEPT:
- Award from City of New York Employee's Suggestion Board
- Award of Departmental recognition
- Award to member's family for brave/meritorious act from metropolitan newspaper
- Monetary prize/award from foundations, universities, institutions (after review by Deputy Commissioner Legal Matters AND approval of Police Commissioner)

### 7. Purchasing Custody Property
**Shall NOT** purchase or acquire property of another, without approval of commanding officer, knowing or having reason to know such property was held in custody of Department.

### 8. Loans from Merchants/Residents
**Shall NOT** solicit or accept loans from merchants, firms, or persons doing business located or residing in area of assignment.

### 9. Superior/Subordinate Business Transactions
**Shall NOT** enter into business or financial transaction with another member who is your superior or subordinate where the superior has power to:
- Direct the subordinate's work, OR
- Affect terms and conditions of subordinate's employment

> **Sergeant Focus:** This applies even if superior does NOT have direct supervision but has likelihood of supervising or affecting assignment. Members in same bureau/patrol borough should carefully consider before entering transactions.

### 10. Micropurchases for Personal Benefit
**Shall NOT** use position for benefit of self or associate through micropurchases made on behalf of Department (purchases up to $20,000 without competition).

> **Key Term — Associate:** Spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, sibling; person with business/financial relationship; any firm in which member has present/potential interest.

### 11. Business Dealings with City
**Shall NOT** become interested, directly or indirectly, in any business dealing with the City:

| Prohibition | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| (a) Position in firm dealing with Department | No Department employee may have position in firm known to be engaged in business with Department |
| (b) Position in firm dealing with City | No position unless firm's shares are publicly traded |
| (c) Ownership interest in firm dealing with Department | Prohibited |
| (d) Ownership interest in firm dealing with City | Prohibited unless shares are publicly traded |

> **Exam Alert:** If member has prohibited ownership interest, must disclose to Conflicts of Interest Board within **10 days** of learning.

## Superior/Subordinate Charitable Transactions

**Superior SHALL NOT:**
- Solicit charitable contributions from subordinate
- Offer to sell products of any amount/value to subordinate

**Subordinate MAY:**
- Sell products for profit/charitable purposes to superior
- Solicit donations for charitable purposes from superior
- **IF:** Amount involved is **$25.00 or less**

## Ownership Interest Threshold

| Threshold | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **>5% of firm** OR | Any interest greater than five percent |
| **>$50,000** | Investment equivalent to $50,000 or more |

**Excluded:** Stocks/interests held in pension plan, deferred compensation plan, or mutual fund NOT controlled by member/spouse/unemancipated child.

## Key Definitions

**Firm:** Sole proprietorship, joint venture, partnerships, corporations, any other form of enterprise. Does NOT include public benefit corporations.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Members with questions should contact Deputy Commissioner Legal Matters or Conflicts of Interest Board.

> **Memory Aid — FINANCIAL RESTRICTIONS:**
> - **F**irm dealings with City prohibited
> - **I**nformation confidential (no insider use)

> - **L**oans/transactions with superior/subordinate restricted
