# Section 203 — Key Terms, Forms, and Abbreviations

---

## Core Ethics Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Associate** | Spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, or sibling of a public servant; a person with whom a public servant has a business or other financial relationship; and each firm in which a public servant has a present or potential interest | P.G. 203-13 |
| **Firm** | Sole proprietorship, joint venture, partnerships, corporations, and any other form of enterprise. Does NOT include public benefit corporations or similar entities | P.G. 203-13 |
| **Ownership Interest** | An interest held by a Department employee, the employee's spouse or unemancipated child, that is greater than five percent of the firm OR an investment equivalent to $50,000. Excludes stocks/interests held in pension plan, deferred compensation plan, or mutual fund not controlled by the member | P.G. 203-13 |
| **Micropurchase** | Purchases up to $20,000 that may be made without competition on behalf of the Department | P.G. 203-13 |

## Right to Know Business Cards

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Right to Know Business Card (PD142-012)** | Pre-printed business card with rank, name, shield number, and command; offered upon conclusion of law enforcement activities | P.G. 203-10 |
| **Right to Know Business Card - General (PD142-013)** | Generic card used when pre-printed supply becomes depleted; identification information written legibly by member | P.G. 203-10 |
| **Business Card Executive (PD142-091)** | Used by uniformed members in rank of Captain and above and civilian members holding title of Director and above | P.G. 203-10 |
| **Level 2 Encounter** | Noncustodial questioning of individuals suspected of criminal activity; business card required | P.G. 203-10 |
| **Level 3 Encounter** | Stop where officer has reasonable suspicion and reasonable person would not feel free to end encounter; includes frisk; business card required | P.G. 203-10 |

## Gift Acceptance

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Permitted Tokens** | Plaques, pen and pencil sets, and similar items commonly offered as tokens of appreciation | P.G. 203-16 |
| **Forbidden Gifts** | Cash rewards and personal gifts such as wristwatches; strictly forbidden | P.G. 203-16 |
| **Board of Ethics Rulings** | Department rulings maintained in Command Reference Library; must be reviewed before accepting gifts | P.G. 203-16 |

## Off-Duty Conduct

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Department-Oriented Event** | Parades, funerals, memorial services, and other law enforcement-oriented events; subject to monitoring | P.G. 203-15 |
| **Overhead Command Monitoring** | All Department-oriented events monitored by ranking officers from overhead commands; IAB may monitor in selected cases | P.G. 203-15 |

## Fund Raising

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **IAB Number** | Unique identifier assigned by Internal Affairs Bureau to fund raising event request | P.G. 203-17 |
| **30-Day Accounting** | Specific accounting of funds collected/dispersed must be forwarded to Chief of IAB within 30 days after fund-raiser | P.G. 203-17 |

## Units and Abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Context |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **IAB** | Internal Affairs Bureau | Receives fund raising requests; monitors events; receives corruption reports |
| **Board of Ethics** | Police Department Board of Ethics | Issues rulings on gift acceptance; maintains precedent rulings |
| **CO** | Conflicts of Interest Board | City Charter Chapter 68 body; reviews ownership interest disclosures |
| **DC Legal Matters** | Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters | Contact for conflict of interest questions and waivers |
| **DC Internal Affairs** | Deputy Commissioner, Internal Affairs | Receives reports of members getting 2+ gifts/year |

## Critical Numbers

| Number | What It Represents | Source |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| **$25** | Maximum amount for subordinate charitable sales/solicitations to superior | P.G. 203-13 |
| **$50,000** | Ownership interest threshold (investment equivalent triggering disclosure) | P.G. 203-13 |
| **5%** | Ownership interest threshold (percentage of firm triggering disclosure) | P.G. 203-13 |
| **10 days** | Time to disclose prohibited ownership interest to Conflicts of Interest Board | P.G. 203-13 |
| **30 days** | Deadline for fund raising accounting to IAB; also minimum advance notice for resource requests | P.G. 203-17 |
| **6 units** | Maximum units in city-owned residential building that may be purchased through negotiated sale | P.G. 203-13 |
| **2 gifts** | Threshold requiring report to DC Internal Affairs (2+ gifts/year from any source) | P.G. 203-16 |
| **8 elements** | Required elements in fund raising Typed Letterhead request to Chief of IAB | P.G. 203-17 |
| **20,000** | Micropurchase threshold (purchases without competition) | P.G. 203-13 |

## Sergeant Focus Areas

| Area | Key Point | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Ranking Officer Duty** | Supervisory responsibilities NEVER abrogated; must take affirmative action on corruption | P.G. 203-15 |
| **Gift Pre-Approval** | CO must be notified PRIOR to acceptance of any gift | P.G. 203-16 |
| **Uniform Off-Duty** | Permission required from Police Commissioner/First Deputy/Chief for wearing uniform outside NYC | P.G. 203-15 |
| **Firearms Out of State** | Commissioner/First Deputy/Chief permission required even if state allows out-of-jurisdiction carry | P.G. 203-15 |
