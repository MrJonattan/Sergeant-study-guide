# Section 203-16 — Guidelines for Acceptance of Gifts and Other Compensation by Members of the Service

## Source Reference
- **Procedure:** P.G. 203-16
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide1.pdf (A.G. 304-14)
- **Last Revision:** I.O.41 (06/10/21)

## Procedure Summary

This procedure provides guidance to members relating to acceptance of gifts and other compensation. All exceptions must comply with Chapter 68 of the New York City Charter and Police Department Board of Ethics rulings.

## General Policy

**Members SHALL NOT:**
- Accept any reward, gratuity, gift, or other compensation for any service performed as result of or in conjunction with duties as public servants
- Solicit any gift, gratuity, loan, present, fee, or reward for personal gain

> **Exam Alert:** This policy applies regardless of whether service was performed while on or off duty.

## Permitted Tokens of Appreciation

**May accept** gifts commonly offered as tokens of appreciation:
- Plaques
- Pen and pencil sets
- Similar items

**STRICTLY FORBIDDEN:**
- Cash rewards
- Personal gifts (e.g., wristwatches, etc.)

## Procedure When Offered Gift

### Member of Service:

1. **Comply** with Department regulations relating to financial restrictions and prohibited acts/prohibited interests
2. **Notify commanding officer PRIOR to acceptance** of gift

### Commanding Officer:

3. **Review circumstances** regarding the offering of the gift
   - Review Department Board of Ethics rulings relating to acceptance of gifts
   - Command copies of Board of Ethics rulings must be maintained in Command Reference Library (A.G. 325-18)

## Gift Classification

### If Gift Is Reward/Present for Police Service Rendered in Discharge of Official Duty:

**Commanding Officer Shall:**
4. Prepare report on **Typed Letterhead** to **Police Commissioner** for review and final determination
5. Notify member concerned of determination
6. **Request ruling by Board of Ethics** if circumstances of offering are peculiar in nature and not reflected in current rulings

### If Gift Is Offered by Person/Firm Engaged in Business with City of New York:

**Commanding Officer Shall:**
5. Review **City Charter, Section 2604(B)5** to ensure no conflict of interest
6. Make final determination regarding propriety of accepting gift and notify member concerned
   - Request ruling by Board of Ethics if circumstances are peculiar in nature
7. **Prepare report on Typed Letterhead to Deputy Commissioner Internal Affairs** (through channels) for any member who receives **two or more gifts**, regardless of value or source, within a calendar year

> **Sergeant Focus:** Commanding officers must ensure command copies of Board of Ethics rulings are maintained in Command Reference Library as required by A.G. 325-18.

> **Exam Alert:** If member receives 2+ gifts within calendar year (regardless of value or source), CO must report to Deputy Commissioner Internal Affairs.

> **Memory Aid — GIFTS:**
> - **G**ifts forbidden: cash, wristwatches, personal items
> - **I**n writing to Commissioner (Typed Letterhead)
> - **F**orbidden to solicit (loans, presents, fees, rewards)
> - **T**okens permitted (plaques, pen sets)
> - **S**ource matters (City business = Section 2604(B)5 review)
