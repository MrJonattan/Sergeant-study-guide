# Section 203 — Ethics and Conduct Review Questions

---

## Multiple Choice Questions

**1.** A uniformed member completes a Level 3 frisk of a suspect. The suspect is not arrested and no summons is issued. When must the member provide a Right to Know Business Card?

**A.** Only if the suspect requests it
**B.** Upon conclusion of the law enforcement activity
**C.** Before beginning the frisk
**D.** Business cards are not required for frisks

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-10, uniformed members shall offer a pre-printed Right to Know Business Card upon conclusion of law enforcement activities, including frisks (Level 3 Encounter). The card is NOT required only when a summons is issued, an arrest is made, or exigent circumstances exist.

</details>

---

**2.** Which of the following individuals is authorized to use the Business Card Executive (PD142-091) instead of the Right to Know Business Card?

**A.** A detective conducting first in-person questioning of a witness
**B.** A lieutenant conducting a traffic stop
**C.** A captain at a community event
**D.** Any member in the rank of sergeant and above

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-10, uniformed members in the rank of Captain and above and civilian members holding the title of Director and above will use the Business Card Executive (PD142-091) rather than the Right to Know Business Card.

</details>

---

**3.** A member wishes to purchase a rental property within their precinct of assignment. Is this permitted?

**A.** Yes, with commanding officer approval
**B.** Yes, if the property is city-owned
**C.** No, this is a prohibited financial restriction
**D.** Yes, if the member discloses the purchase to the Conflicts of Interest Board

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-13, members shall NOT purchase real estate for rental purposes within precinct of assignment. This is one of the 11 prohibited financial acts.

</details>

---

**4.** A superior officer wants to sell Girl Scout cookies to a subordinate in their command. What is the maximum amount the subordinate may purchase without violating financial restrictions?

**A.** Any amount, as long as it's charitable
**B.** $50.00
**C.** $25.00
**D.** This is prohibited regardless of amount

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-13, a superior shall NOT solicit charitable contributions from or sell products to a subordinate. However, a subordinate MAY sell products for profit or charitable purposes to a superior, or solicit donations for charitable purposes from a superior, if the amount involved is $25.00 or less.

</details>

**5.** A member discovers they have a prohibited ownership interest in a firm dealing with the Department. When must they disclose this to the Conflicts of Interest Board?

**A.** Within 10 days of learning the interest is prohibited
**B.** Within 30 days
**C.** At the next annual disclosure
**D.** Immediately, before the next tour

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-13, if a member has a prohibited ownership interest, they must disclose the ownership interest to the Conflicts of Interest Board within 10 days of learning that such interest is prohibited.

</details>

**6.** An off-duty uniformed member wishes to wear their uniform to a law enforcement memorial service in another state. Who must grant permission?

**A.** The member's commanding officer
**B.** The borough commander
**C.** The Police Commissioner, First Deputy Commissioner, or Chief of Department
**D.** No permission is required for memorial services

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-15, all off-duty uniformed members who wish to wear their uniform outside New York City must receive permission in advance from the Police Commissioner, First Deputy Commissioner, or Chief of Department.

</details>

**7.** A member is offered a wristwatch as a gift from a citizen in appreciation for police service. How should the member respond?

**A.** Accept the gift and notify the commanding officer
**B.** Accept the gift if it's worth less than $50
**C.** Politely decline; personal gifts like wristwatches are strictly forbidden
**D.** Accept the gift and donate it to charity

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-16, while plaques and pen/pencil sets are permitted as tokens of appreciation, cash rewards and personal gifts such as wristwatches are strictly forbidden.

</details>

**8.** A member receives three gifts from different sources within a calendar year. What action must the commanding officer take?

**A.** No action required; each gift was reviewed individually
**B.** Prepare a report to the Deputy Commissioner, Internal Affairs
**C.** Request a Board of Ethics ruling
**D.** Forward a report to the Chief of Internal Affairs

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-16, the commanding officer shall prepare a report on Typed Letterhead to the Deputy Commissioner, Internal Affairs (through channels) for any member who receives two or more gifts, regardless of value or source, within a calendar year.

</details>

**9.** A fraternal organization is planning a fund raising dinner. When must the Typed Letterhead request be submitted to the Chief of Internal Affairs?

**A.** At least 24 hours before the event
**B.** At least 7 days before the event
**C.** At least 30 days before the event
**D.** Prior to the event (no specific timeline)

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-17, the request must be submitted prior to the fund raising event or posting of advertisements. The 30-day requirement applies to the accounting of funds AFTER the event, not the pre-approval request.

</details>

**10.** Which of the following is NOT a required element of the fund raising Typed Letterhead request to the Chief of Internal Affairs?

**A.** The date, time, and location of the fund-raiser
**B.** Results of inquiries to Intelligence Division and Detective Bureau
**C.** A list of all attending members of the service
**D.** A statement that no illegal gambling, raffles, or 50-50 drawings will take place

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-17, the eight required elements are: (a) date/time/location/organizers/proceeds recipient, (b) Intelligence/Detective Bureau inquiry results, (c) Integrity Monitoring File check, (d) specific manner of fund raising including online sites, (e) no illegal gambling statement, (f) 30-day accounting commitment, (g) no conflict statement, (h) concluding recommendation. A list of attending members is NOT required.

</details>

**11.** A ranking uniformed member observes another member accepting money from a merchant at a fund raising event. What action must the ranking member take?

**A.** Report to the precinct commander the next tour
**B.** Take affirmative action and immediately notify Internal Affairs Bureau
**C.** Document the incident in an Activity Log
**D.** Nothing, as the event was off-duty

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-15, ALL members are required to report corruption or other misconduct. Ranking uniformed members must take affirmative action whenever they observe or become aware of corruption or other misconduct, including immediate notification to the Internal Affairs Bureau. Supervisory responsibilities are never abrogated.

</details>

**12.** Which of the following law enforcement activities does NOT require providing a Right to Know Business Card?

**A.** A home search
**B.** A roadblock checkpoint
**C.** Issuing a summons
**D.** Noncustodial questioning of a suspect

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-10, business cards are NOT required when a summons is issued or an arrest is made. All other listed activities (home search, roadblock/checkpoint, noncustodial questioning) require business card provision.

</details>

**13.** A member wants to accept a monetary prize from a university foundation for a community policing initiative. What is required?

**A.** Commanding officer approval only
**B.** Board of Ethics review only
**C.** Review by Deputy Commissioner Legal Matters AND approval of Police Commissioner
**D.** No approval needed for university awards

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-13, members may accept a monetary prize or award from foundations, universities, institutions, etc., only after review by the Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters AND the approval of the Police Commissioner.

</details>

**14.** What is the ownership interest threshold that triggers Conflicts of Interest Board disclosure requirements?

**A.** Greater than 1% of the firm OR $25,000 investment
**B.** Greater than 5% of the firm OR $50,000 investment
**C.** Greater than 10% of the firm OR $100,000 investment
**D.** Any ownership interest regardless of percentage

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-13, an ownership interest means an interest greater than five percent of the firm OR an investment equivalent to $50,000. This excludes stocks/interests held in pension plans, deferred compensation plans, or mutual funds not controlled by the member.

</details>

**15.** After a fund-raising event, when must the accounting of funds be forwarded to the Chief of Internal Affairs?

**A.** Within 7 days
**B.** Within 15 days
**C.** Within 30 days
**D.** Within 60 days

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Per P.G. 203-17, a specific accounting of funds collected and disbursed must be forwarded within 30 days after the fund-raiser to the Chief of Internal Affairs, including copies of checks, bank deposit slips, or proof of payment for outstanding bills.

</details>
