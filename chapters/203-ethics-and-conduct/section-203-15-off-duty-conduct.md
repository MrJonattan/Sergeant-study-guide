# Section 203-15 / A.G. 304-13 — Off Duty Conduct - Attendance at Parades, Funerals, Memorial Services and Other Department-Oriented or Law Enforcement Events

## Source Reference
- **Procedure:** A.G. 304-13 (cross-referenced as P.G. 203-15)
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide1.pdf
- **Last Revision:** I.O.56 (06/24/21)

## Procedure Summary

This procedure establishes guidelines for members attending parades, funerals, memorial services, and other Department-oriented or law enforcement events, whether on or off duty.

## General Accountability

**All members are strictly accountable** for their conduct at ALL times:
- Whether on or off duty
- Inside or outside New York City
- Conduct bringing discredit to Department is unacceptable
- Conduct violating law is unacceptable
- Disciplinary measures will result from violations

> **Sergeant Focus:** ALL members are required to report corruption or other misconduct whether on or off duty, inside or outside NYC. Ranking officers' supervisory responsibilities are NEVER abrogated.

## Reporting Requirements

### ALL Members Shall:
- Report corruption or other misconduct immediately to **Internal Affairs Bureau**

### Ranking Uniformed Members Must:
- Take **affirmative action** when observing or becoming aware of corruption/misconduct
- **Immediately notify Internal Affairs Bureau**

## On-Duty Members

**Shall:**
- Return to command as soon as event is over
- **NOT** take meal period
- **NOT** be granted lost time until returning to assigned command

## Off-Duty Uniform Members Wearing Uniform

### Outside New York City:
**Shall** receive permission in advance from:
- Police Commissioner, OR
- First Deputy Commissioner, OR
- Chief of Department

### General Requirements:
- Wear uniform **only for duration of event**
- Change to civilian attire **as soon as event is over**

> **Exam Alert:** Under NO circumstances will any member wear uniform off duty or attend parties, dinners, fund raising events, etc., in uniform, without permission of commanding officer or designee. This includes social events AFTER parade, funeral, etc. has ended.

## Alcohol Consumption

**Shall NOT** consume intoxicants when in uniform whether on or off duty (P.G. 203-06).

**Shall NOT,** under any circumstances, allow alcoholic beverages to be brought into:
- Department facilities
- Department vehicles
- Except in performance of duty

## Firearms at Events

**Off-duty members are to be unarmed at their own discretion** when engaged in any activity where it would be advisable NOT to carry a firearm, especially events at which alcoholic beverages are consumed.

**May ONLY carry:**
- Regulation service pistol
- Authorized off-duty pistol/revolver
- Authorized special weapons

> **Exam Alert:** Members out of state for parade, funeral, memorial ceremony, or other Department-oriented/law enforcement-oriented event **may NOT carry any firearms** without permission of Police Commissioner, First Deputy Commissioner, or Chief of Department — EVEN when such event takes place in a state which allows police officers from other jurisdictions to carry their weapons.

## Event Monitoring

**All Department-oriented and law enforcement-oriented events** will be monitored by ranking officers from overhead commands.

**In selected cases** (as directed by Police Commissioner, First Deputy Commissioner, or Chief of Department):
- **Internal Affairs Bureau** will conduct monitoring

> **Memory Aid — OFF DUTY CONDUCT:**
> - **O**bservation required (ranking officers monitor)
> - **F**irearms restricted (Commissioner approval out of state)
> - **F**acilities/vehicles (no alcohol)
> - **D**uty members return immediately (no meal/lost time)
> - **D**iscretion on being unarmed at alcohol events
> - **U**niform permission required (outside NYC)
> - **T**o IAB immediately (report corruption)
> - **Y**our conduct accountable at ALL times
