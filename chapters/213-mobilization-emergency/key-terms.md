# Key Terms — Section 213: Mobilization/Emergency Incidents

## Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| First Amendment Activity | Exercise of rights protected by the 1st Amendment: speech, assembly, petition, press | 213-20 |
| Senior Executive | Deputy chief or higher rank | 213-20 |
| Designee | Captain or above designated by a Senior Executive | 213-20 |
| De-escalation | Reducing intensity of conflict or potential conflict | 213-20 |
| Incident Commander | MOS in charge of the police response at an event or incident | 213-15, 213-20 |
| Nonviolent Participant | Person engaged in lawful First Amendment activity | 213-20 |
| Violent Behavior | Conduct involving force causing or intended to cause physical injury or property damage | 213-20 |
| Protest Liaison | Community Affairs Bureau (CAB) member assigned to communicate with demonstrators | 213-20 |
| Mass Arrest Processing Center (MAPC) | Facility activated to process large numbers of arrests during mass arrest situations | 213-06 |
| Legal Observer | Individual (NLG or NYCLU) present at demonstrations to observe police-protester interactions; granted free access through police lines | 213-20 |
| Handschu Consent Decree | Legal ruling requiring written approval from Intelligence and Counterterrorism Bureau for political activity investigations | 213-20 |
| FINEST Message | Department-wide communication method used to announce changes in citywide security alert levels | 213-18 |
| Traffic Brief | Daily compilation of traffic-related notifications from events affecting traffic flow | 213-17 |
| Unusual Disorder Kit | Standardized kit of supplies maintained by each command for use during unusual disorders | 213-09 |

## Authorization Requirements

|--------|--------------------------|
| Activate MAPC | Chief of Department |

| Political activity investigation (Handschu) | Written approval from Intelligence and Counterterrorism Bureau |
