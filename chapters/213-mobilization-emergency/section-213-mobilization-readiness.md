# Mobilization Readiness and Disorder Preparedness

## Overview

This section covers the foundational procedures for mobilization, emergency response, and preparing for unusual disorders. Sergeants must understand command post operations, rapid mobilization protocols, readiness levels, and the supplies and plans required to handle large-scale incidents. P.G. 213-09 (Unusual Disorder Kits) is the fully extracted procedure here; the remaining procedures are summarized from cross-references.

## P.G. 213-05 — Duties at a Disorder

> **Note:** Full exam-focused content for this procedure is now available in the dedicated file: **section-213-duties-at-disorder.md**. The summary below highlights the most critical points.

### Known Information
- Outlines specific duties of members of service responding to unusual disorders
- Part of the 213-01 through 213-08 series referenced by P.G. 213-09
- Supported by P.G. 213-07 (Unusual Disorder Maps), P.G. 213-08 (Unusual Disorder Plan), and P.G. 213-09 (Unusual Disorder Kits)
- Deployment ratio: 2/3 deployed, 1/3 in reserve; foot posts at 1 Sgt & 8 P.O.s
- Dual Dispatch System: HIGH priority to detail officers, LOW priority to precinct officers
- Ring schedule: every 30 minutes for enforcement units, every 60 minutes for other units
- Two types of mobilizations: Levels 1 & 2 = SRG; Levels 3 & 4 = PCT/TD/PSA
- Prohibited tactics: complacency, breaking formation, unnecessary conversation, congregating

> **Exam Alert:** Sergeants respond to the stationhouse (10-02) first under PG 213-05, form a squad of 8 officers, and report to the mobilization point in a van. While enroute, only the Sergeant and van operator have radios turned on.

> **See Also:** section-213-duties-at-disorder.md — Full procedure with deployment plans, team assignments, lieutenant/sergeant duties, and mobilization level details.

## P.G. 213-09 — Unusual Disorder Kits

### Key Points
- Each command's Commanding Officer is responsible for maintaining an Unusual Disorder Kit
- Kit must contain the following supplies:
  - **75** sector maps
  - **200** index cards
  - **200** Complaint Report Worksheets
  - **200** Aided Report Worksheets
  - **200** Detail Roster sheets
  - **300** tags
  - Staplers, tape, markers, batteries, flashlights
- Kit must be stored in an accessible location known to **all supervisors**
- References procedures P.G. 213-01 through 213-08

### Supervisor Responsibilities
- Ensure kit location is known to all supervisors in the command
- Verify kit contents during semiannual inspections
- Confirm kit is readily accessible and not locked away in an unknown location
- Be prepared to deploy kit contents during mobilization or unusual disorder

> **Exam Alert:** Semiannual inspection of the Unusual Disorder Kit is required in **January** and **July**.

> **Exam Alert:** The kit must contain **75** sector maps, **200** each of index cards/Complaint Report Worksheets/Aided Report Worksheets/Detail Roster sheets, and **300** tags.

> **Sergeant Focus:** All supervisors must know the kit's location. If you are newly assigned to a command, learn the kit location immediately. Ensure inspections occur on schedule in January and July.
