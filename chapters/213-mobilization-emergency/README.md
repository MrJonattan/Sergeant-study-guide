# Chapter 9 — Section 213: Mobilization/Emergency Incidents

## Overview

Section 213 of the NYPD Patrol Guide covers mobilization, emergency response, special events, and First Amendment activities. This chapter is HIGH PRIORITY for the Sergeant Promotional Exam, particularly the procedures governing First Amendment activities (213-06, 213-20) and special events management (213-15). Sergeants must know authorization hierarchies, specific numerical thresholds, processing time limits, and the rights of demonstrators and prisoners.

This study guide covers 17 procedures from Section 213. Six procedures have full extracted text; the remaining 11 are summarized from cross-references.

## Priority Topics for Sergeant Exam

1. **Mass arrest authorization** -- Only Senior Executive (deputy chief+) or designee (captain+) can order mass arrests at First Amendment events (213-20)
2. **MAPC activation and prisoner limits** -- Chief of Department activates; 20 per wagon, 5 per officer, 4 officers per supervisor (213-06)
3. **7 hours 30 minutes processing time limit** for First Amendment violations/misdemeanors (213-06)
4. **Incident Commander hierarchy** -- Precinct CO / Patrol Borough Commander / Chief of Patrol (213-15)
5. **Legal observer identification** -- NLG = green hats, NYCLU = blue hats/vests; free access through police lines (213-20)
6. **Barrier exclusion distances** -- 100 feet on through streets, 25 feet from corners (213-15)
7. **Security alert levels** -- GREEN/BLUE/YELLOW/ORANGE/RED; NYC at ORANGE; changes via FINEST Message (213-18)
8. **Body-worn cameras NOT activated** at demonstrations unless per P.G. 212-123 (213-20)
9. **Handcuff checks at three points** -- entering van, exiting van, regularly at processing (213-20)
10. **De-escalation required** before enforcement action at First Amendment activities (213-20)

## Study Tips

- Start with **section-213-first-amendment.md** -- it contains the highest-yield exam material in this chapter
- Use **key-terms.md** as a quick-reference sheet and self-test on the critical numbers
- Work through all 20 review questions without looking at the answers first, then check yourself
- Pay special attention to authorization levels: who can order what, and at what rank
- Memorize the security alert level colors and order (G-B-Y-O-R)
- The numerical limits in 213-06 (20/5/4, 7:30, 2 hours) are classic exam question material
