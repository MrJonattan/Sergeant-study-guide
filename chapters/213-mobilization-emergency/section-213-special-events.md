# Special Events Management

## Overview

This section covers the planning, execution, and management of special events, including crowd control, traffic management, and command post duties. P.G. 213-15 (Duties and Responsibilities at Special Events) and P.G. 213-17 (Traffic Notifications) are the fully extracted procedures. Sergeants assigned to special events must understand the Incident Commander hierarchy, sector management, barrier placement rules, and notification requirements.

## P.G. 213-15 — Duties and Responsibilities at Special Events

### Key Points

#### Incident Commander Hierarchy
- **Single command event:** Precinct Commanding Officer is Incident Commander
- **Multi-command event:** Patrol Borough Commander is Incident Commander
- **Multi-borough event:** Chief of Patrol is Incident Commander

#### Event Area Management
- Divide event area into **sectors**
- Establish a **command post** at or near the event
- Maintain **detail roster** of all assigned personnel
- Maintain **emergency vehicle access lanes** at all times
- Identify **first aid station** location
- Establish **communications plan** with dedicated radio frequency if available

#### Barrier Placement Rules
- **100 feet** exclusion from parade route on through streets
- **25 feet** on parade route from corners
- Barrier configuration must **NOT unreasonably restrict pedestrian access**

#### Mounted Unit
- Ensure crowd has **sufficient egress avenues**
- Ensure crowd has **reasonable chance to disperse**

#### Post-Event Requirements
- **Critique report** required after the event
- Critique must include **overtime reporting**

### Supervisor Responsibilities
- Brief subordinates on event details, expected crowd size, and potential issues
- Serve as Incident Commander for single-command events (if CO is unavailable, the sergeant may be the senior MOS present)
- Establish and maintain liaison with event organizers
- Ensure barrier placement complies with distance requirements
- Monitor emergency vehicle access lanes for obstructions
- Know first aid station location and communicate it to subordinates
- Maintain communications discipline on assigned frequency

> **Exam Alert:** Incident Commander hierarchy: **Precinct CO** (single command) -> **Patrol Borough Commander** (multi-command) -> **Chief of Patrol** (multi-borough).

> **Exam Alert:** Barrier exclusion distances: **100 feet** from parade route on through streets; **25 feet** from parade route at corners.

> **Exam Alert:** Barriers must **NOT unreasonably restrict pedestrian access** -- this is a specific requirement, not discretionary.

> **Sergeant Focus:** A critique report with overtime reporting is required after every special event. The sergeant must ensure the detail roster is complete and accurate.

## P.G. 213-17 — Notifications Regarding Traffic Related Activities

### Key Points
- **Traffic Management Center (TMC)** must be notified of **all events** affecting traffic
- A daily **"Traffic Brief"** is compiled from these notifications
- **Operations Unit** must be notified for **unscheduled events**
- Notifications must include:
  - Location
  - Expected duration
  - Street closures
  - Alternate routes

### Supervisor Responsibilities
- Ensure timely notification to the Traffic Management Center for any event affecting traffic flow
- For unscheduled events, ensure the Operations Unit is also notified
- Provide complete information: location, duration, closures, and alternate routes

> **Exam Alert:** The **Traffic Management Center** is notified for all events affecting traffic. The **Operations Unit** is notified for **unscheduled** events specifically.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Do not assume someone else has made traffic notifications. Verify that TMC has been contacted, especially for unscheduled events where the Operations Unit must also be informed.
