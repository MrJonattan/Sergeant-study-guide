# First Amendment Activities and Mass Arrest Processing

## Overview

This section is among the most exam-critical in the entire Patrol Guide. It covers the Department's response to First Amendment activities (protests, demonstrations, marches), the tiered response framework, and the mass arrest processing procedure. Sergeants must thoroughly understand the legal protections, de-escalation requirements, authorization hierarchy for enforcement actions, arrest processing rules, and the specific rights of prisoners and legal observers.

---

## P.G. 213-20 — Response to First Amendment Activities

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **First Amendment Activity** | Exercise of rights protected by the 1st Amendment: speech, assembly, petition, press |
| **Senior Executive** | Deputy chief or higher rank |
| **Designee** | Captain or above designated by a Senior Executive |
| **De-escalation** | Reducing intensity of conflict or potential conflict |
| **Incident Commander** | MOS in charge of the police response |
| **Nonviolent Participant** | Person engaged in lawful First Amendment activity |
| **Violent Behavior** | Conduct involving force causing or intended to cause physical injury or property damage |
| **Protest Liaison** | Community Affairs Bureau (CAB) member assigned to communicate with demonstrators |

### Key Points

#### Tiered Response Structure
- **Tier One Response:** A sergeant may serve as Incident Commander
- Escalation to higher tiers involves higher-ranking Incident Commanders

#### Protest Liaisons (Community Affairs Bureau)
- CAB members are assigned as protest liaisons to communicate with demonstrators
- Liaisons **must** wear uniform clearly displaying rank, name, shield, and command
- Liaisons may **NOT** wear civilian attire
- CAB personnel are **NOT** assigned to patrol or enforcement duties except in exceptional circumstances

#### Body-Worn Cameras
- Body-worn cameras are **NOT activated** at demonstrations unless per P.G. 212-123
- This is a specific restriction -- cameras are not turned on simply because a protest is occurring

#### Handschu Consent Decree
- Political activity investigations require **written approval** from the Intelligence and Counterterrorism Bureau
- This is a legal constraint that limits investigative authority at First Amendment events

#### Legal Observers
- **National Lawyers Guild (NLG):** Identified by **bright green hats**
- **NYCLU:** Identified by **blue hats/vests**
- Legal observers are permitted **free access through police lines**

#### Arrests at First Amendment Activities
- Only a **Senior Executive** (deputy chief or higher) or **designee** (captain or above) can order **mass arrests**
- **Individual arrests** for criminal conduct are still permitted by any supervisor
- **De-escalation** attempts are required before enforcement action
- **Warnings** must be given before enforcement (when feasible)
- **Sufficient resources** must be assembled before taking enforcement action

#### Handcuff Checks — Three Required Points
1. **Entering** transport van
2. **Exiting** transport van
3. **Regularly** at arrest processing

#### Transfer of Command
- Transfer of command between boroughs must be documented by **radio transmission** via **Communications Division**

#### Notifications
- **Citywide Events Planning** is available **1000-1800 hours, Monday through Friday**
- Outside those hours, contact the **Operations Unit**

### Supervisor Responsibilities
- At Tier One events, the sergeant serves as Incident Commander
- Ensure de-escalation is attempted before any enforcement action
- Do NOT order mass arrests -- only a Senior Executive or designee has that authority
- Ensure individual arrests for criminal conduct are properly documented
- Verify protest liaisons are in proper uniform with visible identification
- Confirm body-worn cameras are NOT activated unless P.G. 212-123 applies
- Perform handcuff checks at the three required points
- Document any transfer of command via radio through Communications Division
- Know when to contact Citywide Events Planning vs. Operations Unit

> **Exam Alert:** Only a **Senior Executive (deputy chief or higher)** or **designee (captain or above)** can order mass arrests at First Amendment activities. A sergeant CANNOT order mass arrests.

> **Exam Alert:** **NLG** legal observers wear **bright green hats**. **NYCLU** legal observers wear **blue hats/vests**. Both must be permitted **free access through police lines**.

> **Exam Alert:** Body-worn cameras are **NOT activated** at demonstrations unless per P.G. 212-123.

> **Exam Alert:** Handcuff checks are required at **three** points: (1) entering transport van, (2) exiting transport van, (3) regularly at arrest processing.

> **Exam Alert:** Citywide Events Planning hours: **1000-1800, Mon-Fri**. Outside those hours, contact the **Operations Unit**.

> **Exam Alert:** The **Handschu Consent Decree** requires **written approval** from the Intelligence and Counterterrorism Bureau for political activity investigations.

> **Sergeant Focus:** A sergeant can serve as Incident Commander at a Tier One response. However, any escalation beyond Tier One requires a higher-ranking IC. De-escalation is mandatory before enforcement -- this is not optional. Warnings must be given when feasible, and sufficient resources must be assembled before acting.

> **Sergeant Focus:** CAB protest liaisons may NOT wear civilian attire and may NOT be assigned to enforcement duties except in exceptional circumstances. If you see a CAB member in plainclothes at a demonstration, this is a procedure violation.

## P.G. 213-06 — Mass Arrest Processing Procedure

### Key Points

#### Activation
- Mass Arrest Processing Center (MAPC) is activated by the **Chief of Department**
- At First Amendment events, only the **First Amendment Activity Senior Executive or designee** can authorize MAPC activation

#### Prisoner Group Limits
- Groups of **no more than 20 prisoners** per wagon
- Arresting officers process **no more than 5 prisoners** each
- Arrest supervisors assigned **no more than 4 arresting officers**

#### Processing Time Limit
- First Amendment Activity arrest processing: **7 hours 30 minutes** maximum for violations/misdemeanors

#### MAPC Required Provisions
The MAPC must provide:
- Clean water
- Food (if held **more than 2 hours**)
- Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Telephone access
- Medical care
- Restrooms

#### Prisoner Handling
- All prisoners must be **searched before transport**
- **Female prisoners** searched by **female MOS**
- **Juvenile prisoners** separated from adults
- Each prisoner **photographed** at MAPC
- **Property clerk invoices** completed for all property
- **Desk Appearance Tickets (DATs)** issued when appropriate per existing guidelines

#### Documentation
- **Command log** maintained at MAPC
- Arresting officer stays with prisoner group through processing

### Supervisor Responsibilities (Arrest Processing Supervisor)
- Ensure prisoners are fed (food required if held more than 2 hours)
- Ensure medical attention is provided when needed
- Ensure restroom access is available
- Ensure telephone calls are permitted
- Verify female prisoners are searched by female MOS
- Verify juvenile prisoners are separated from adults
- Monitor processing time -- must not exceed 7 hours 30 minutes for First Amendment violations/misdemeanors
- Ensure command log is maintained
- Confirm each prisoner is photographed
- Ensure property clerk invoices are completed for all property

> **Exam Alert:** MAPC is activated by the **Chief of Department**. At First Amendment events, only the **Senior Executive or designee** can authorize MAPC.

> **Exam Alert:** Maximum prisoners per wagon: **20**. Maximum prisoners per arresting officer: **5**. Maximum arresting officers per arrest supervisor: **4**.

> **Exam Alert:** First Amendment arrest processing time limit: **7 hours 30 minutes** for violations/misdemeanors.

> **Exam Alert:** Food must be provided if a prisoner is held **more than 2 hours**.

> **Exam Alert:** Female prisoners must be searched by **female MOS**. Juvenile prisoners must be **separated from adults**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The arrest processing supervisor has direct responsibility for prisoner welfare: food, medical care, restrooms, and telephone access. Failure to provide these is a procedure violation. Track the clock -- 7 hours 30 minutes is the hard ceiling for First Amendment violation/misdemeanor processing.
