# Section 320 — Review Questions

## Practice Questions

**1. An exchange of tour (mutual) must be performed within the same:**
- A. Calendar month
- B. 28-day FLSA cycle
- C. Pay period
- D. Calendar week

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Exchange of tours must be performed in the same twenty-eight day Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) cycle. (A.G. 320-01)

</details>

---

**2. A uniformed member of the service requesting an exchange of tours must submit the EXCHANGE OF TOUR REQUEST to the operations coordinator at least how many days in advance?**
- A. 3 days
- B. 5 days
- C. 7 days
- D. 10 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The request must be submitted at least five days in advance. (A.G. 320-01, step #5)

</details>

---

**3. During an exchange of tours, a uniformed member of the service who becomes ill must report:**
- A. Administrative Sick
- B. Regular Sick, which requires a visit to the Department surgeon
- C. Either Administrative or Regular Sick, at the member's discretion
- D. Line of Duty Sick

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Members are NOT permitted to report Administrative Sick during a mutual and must report Regular Sick, which requires a visit to the Department surgeon. (A.G. 320-01, step #2b)

</details>

---

**4. Which of the following is NOT a requirement for an exchange of tours?**
- A. Members must be the same rank
- B. Members must be assigned to the same command
- C. Members must be below the rank of captain
- D. Members must be assigned to the same platoon

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**D.** Members do not need to be on the same platoon — they must be same rank, same command, below captain, and qualified to perform each other's duties. (A.G. 320-01)

</details>

---

**5. If a uniformed member of the service fails to perform an exchanged tour, the Department may reschedule any same-rank MOS without how much notice?**
- A. 12-hour notice
- B. 24-hour notice
- C. 48-hour notice
- D. 72-hour notice

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The Department may reschedule any uniformed member of the service of the same rank without twenty-four hour notice. (A.G. 320-01, Additional Data)

</details>

---

**6. A member of the service intending to visit an inmate at a correctional facility while off-duty must forward a typed letterhead to the commanding officer at least how many days prior?**
- A. 3 days
- B. 5 days
- C. 7 days
- D. 14 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** At least seven days prior to the intended visit. (A.G. 320-02, step #1)

</details>

---

**7. When a member of the service requests to visit an inmate at a correctional facility, the commanding officer must notify which of the following?**
- A. Patrol Borough Commander and Operations Unit
- B. IAB, Command Center
- C. Chief of Department and Personnel Bureau
- D. Community Affairs Bureau and Legal Bureau

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The commanding officer must notify IAB and the Command Center of details. (A.G. 320-02, step #2)

</details>

---

**8. Reports more than six months old are removed from personal folders on which dates?**
- A. March 1 and September 1
- B. January 1 and July 1
- C. The first Monday of each quarter
- D. The member's anniversary date

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Reports more than six months old are removed on January 1 and July 1 each year. (A.G. 320-03, step #4)

</details>

---

**9. When a member of command is transferred, the personal folder jacket is forwarded to the new command in what manner?**
- A. Via Department mail, unsealed
- B. Via registered mail
- C. In a sealed envelope marked "Confidential"
- D. Hand-delivered by the transferred member

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** The folder jacket is forwarded in a sealed envelope marked "Confidential." (A.G. 320-03, step #7)

</details>

---

**10. The OVERTIME REPORT (PD138-064) is maintained in which location?**
- A. The member's personal folder
- B. A separate Time Record Folder (TRF)
- C. The command's central filing system
- D. The Borough Administrator's office

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The Overtime Report is to be kept in a separate Time Record Folder (TRF), not in the personal folder. (A.G. 320-03, Additional Data)

</details>
