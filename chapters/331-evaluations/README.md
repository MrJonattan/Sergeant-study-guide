# Section 331 — Evaluations

## Source
- **Admin Guide Section:** 331
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide2.pdf (pages 213-229)
- **Priority:** Medium

## Overview

Section 331 governs the performance evaluation process for all uniformed and civilian members of the NYPD. It covers six procedures addressing evaluations by rank — from probationary police officers through deputy chiefs — including the annual evaluation cycle, probationary evaluation schedules, the PERF system for non-investigative personnel, and the Management By Objectives (MBO) system for captains and above. Critical for the Sergeant exam: understanding probationary evaluation timelines, Chronic Absent impact on ratings, reviewer rank requirements, and the PERF scoring scale.

## Priority Study Topics

1. **Probationary evaluation schedules** — PO (10/16/22), Sgt/Lt (4/10), Captain (4/10/16), Investigative (8/15)
2. **PERF scoring scale** — 90-100 Exceptional, 77-89 Exceeds Expectations, 68-76 Meets Standards, 60-67 Needs Improvement, 59 or below Unsatisfactory
3. **Chronic Absent impact** — Category A (consider attendance), Category B (consider Very Low/Unsatisfactory)
4. **Reviewer rank requirements** — below competent or extremely competent: captain or above; Performance Monitoring Program: commanding officer
5. **22nd month recommendation** — Permanent Appointment, Extension of Probation, or Termination of Services
6. **MBO system** — captains through deputy chiefs, five Key Result Areas (KRAs)
7. **Military absence rule** — average of 3 prior evals, minimum satisfactory (3.0)

## Study Tips

- Focus on the **probationary evaluation month numbers** — these are prime exam material
- The **PERF score-to-rating conversion** table is highly testable
- Know the difference between the **Online Evaluation System** (most ranks) and the **PERF System** (non-investigative POs/detectives)
- Understand when the **commanding officer** must serve as reviewer vs. when a **captain or above** is required
- Detective squad supervisor evaluations require **conferral with the precinct CO** — a frequent exam topic
