## P.G. 207-07: Preliminary Investigation of Complaints (Other Than Vice/Narcotics)

*(Updated R.O. 78, effective 12/31/24)*

*7 pages in the Patrol Guide — one of the most critical procedures for supervisors.*

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Active Case** | A case with leads still being pursued or investigative steps remaining |
| **Closed Case** | A case where all reasonable investigative steps have been completed |
| **Unfounded Case** | A case where investigation determines that no offense actually occurred |

### Crime Scene Preservation

**Do NOT disturb evidence at the crime scene.** This is a fundamental responsibility for all responding members and supervisors.

### Patrol Supervisor Notification Requirements

The Patrol Supervisor must **PROMPTLY** report to the following specialized units:

| Unit to Notify | Circumstances |
|----------------|---------------|
| **Major Case Unit** | Bank burglary or robbery |
| **Major Case Unit** | Truck contents valued over **$100,000** |
| **Major Case Unit** | Commercial burglary loss over **$100,000** |
| **Major Case Unit** | Art theft |
| **Joint Violent Crimes Task Force (JVCTF)** | Armed bank robberies |
| **Highway District** | Fatal or serious injury collisions |
| **Special Victims Unit (SVU)** | ALL sex crimes |
| **Special Victims Unit (SVU)** | Child abuse involving victims under 11 |
| **Special Victims Unit (SVU)** | Human trafficking |

> **Exam Alert:** Memorize the **$100,000** threshold for Major Case Unit notifications (truck contents AND commercial burglary).

### When to Refer to Detective Squad

Refer a case to the detective squad when any of the following apply:

| Condition | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Serious physical injury | Any complaint involving serious physical injury to a victim |
| Robbery with firearm | Or robbery where victim is **60 years or older** |
| Burglary with person present | Or burglary loss exceeding **$5,000** (or **$10,000 in Manhattan**) |
| Unique modus operandi | Crime committed with a distinctive or unusual method |
| Repeat victimization | Same victim targeted **within 6 months** |
| Perpetrator may be identified | Leads exist that could identify the suspect |
| Similar crimes in vicinity | Pattern of related criminal activity |
| Police impersonation | Any crime involving impersonation of a police officer |
| Identity theft | Any complaint involving identity theft |
| Felonious assault on retail workers | Assaults on retail employees in the course of their employment |

> **Exam Alert:** The burglary threshold differs by borough: **$5,000** citywide, but **$10,000 in Manhattan**. This Manhattan exception is frequently tested.

## P.G. 207-08: Preliminary Investigation of Vice Related, Narcotics, or Organized Crime Complaints

Vice, narcotics, and organized crime complaints follow a separate investigative track routed through the **Specialty Enforcement Division**. The preliminary investigation procedures differ from standard complaints:

- Members receiving such complaints ensure initial documentation and notify the appropriate specialized unit.
- Patrol supervisors coordinate with vice/narcotics units for proper case handling.
- Exceptions for recording at the precinct of occurrence (pick-up arrests, found drugs) still apply per P.G. 207-01.
- If secondary offense is OC type (Vice, Drugs, etc.), send the specialized unit an "additional copy" of the complaint.

> **See Also:** PG 207-01 -- Found property rules for controlled substances detail when a Pct 61# vs. ISS serial # is used.

## P.G. 207-10: Bias Motivated Incidents

*(Updated R.O. 18, effective 04/04/25)*

*5 pages in the Patrol Guide — high priority for the Sergeant exam.*

### Definition

A **Bias Incident** is any offense or unlawful act motivated in whole or in substantial part by a person's, a group's, or a place's identification with a particular:
- Race
- Religion
- Ethnicity
- Gender
- Age
- Disability
- Sexual orientation (including lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, intersex, asexual+)

As determined by the **Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit**.

> **Exam Alert:** The motivation test is critical. If people argue over a parking spot and use racial slurs, the incident is motivated by the **parking spot** -- NOT by race, etc. The bias must be the **motivating factor**, not just incidental language.

> **Exam Alert:** The 2025 revision explicitly adds **ethnicity** to the protected categories alongside race, religion, gender, age, disability, and sexual orientation. Prior versions used "race" more broadly.

### Disability Defined (for Bias Purposes)

Any physical, medical, mental, or psychological impairment, or a history or record of such impairment:
1. Physical, medical, mental, or psychological impairment refers to:
   - a. Impairment of any system of the body including (but not limited to): neurological, musculoskeletal, special sense organs, respiratory organs, cardiovascular, reproductive, digestive, genito-urinary, hemic and lymphatic, immunological, skin, and endocrine system; OR
   - b. A mental or psychological impairment
2. In the case of alcoholism, drug addiction, or other substance abuse, the term "disability" only applies to a person who:
   - a. Is recovering or has recovered, AND
   - b. Is currently free of such abuse

### Commanding Officer Defined

For the purpose of this procedure, the **Commanding Officer** is the precinct, police service area, or transit district commanding officer within whose jurisdiction the incident occurs.

### Response Chain

The response chain follows a specific escalation path:

```
1. UMOS evaluates condition, takes police action, stabilizes
   ↓
2. UMOS determines if possible bias → Requests PATROL SUPERVISOR to respond
   ↓
3. Patrol Supervisor stabilizes, determines if additional personnel needed
   ↓
4. Patrol Supervisor requests CO/DUTY CAPTAIN to respond if possible bias
   ↓
5. Patrol Supervisor notifies DESK OFFICER of incident
   ↓
6. CO/Duty Captain determines if should be referred to Hate Crimes
   ↓
7. If public official involved → confer with Intelligence Division before classifying
```

> **Exam Alert:** For a possible bias incident reported by **another police agency**, the Patrol Supervisor shall respond. At the request of the Patrol Supervisor OR a Captain or above from the other agency, the CO/Duty Captain (NYPD) will respond and determine whether the occurrence should be designated as non-bias or referred to the Hate Crime Unit as a possible bias incident.

### Commanding Officer / Duty Captain Responsibilities

Upon notification of a potential bias incident, the CO or Duty Captain must:

1. Determine if occurrence is a possible bias incident that **should be referred** to the Hate Crime Unit for further investigation
2. **NOTE:** When considering incidents involving **public officials**, confer with the **Intelligence Division** prior to classifying the occurrence as a possible bias incident

> **Sergeant Focus:** The Intelligence Division consultation for public officials is a new emphasis in the 2025 revision. This is a "shall" requirement, not discretionary.

#### Patrol Supervisor Duties (Possible Bias)

8. Direct that a **COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A)** be prepared and include whether occurrence was determined to be a possible bias incident

#### UMOS Duties (Possible Bias)

9. Take digital photographs on Department issued smartphone for the following, if it is determined to be a possible bias incident:
   - a. Criminal Mischief, 3rd Degree (Section 145.05, Penal Law)
   - b. Criminal Mischief, 4th Degree (Section 145.00, Penal Law)
   - c. Aggravated Harassment, 1st Degree (Section 240.31, Penal Law), and/or
   - d. Aggravated Harassment, 2nd Degree (Section 240.30, Penal Law)
10. Prepare and enter **COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET** into OMNIFORM System
    - a. Upload digital photographs via OMNIFORM Complaints menu and electronically attach to **COMPLAINT REPORT (PD313-152)**

#### Desk Officer (Possible Bias)

11. Ensure digital photographs are uploaded into OMNIFORM System and sign-off **COMPLAINT REPORT**

### If the Occurrence IS Referred to Hate Crime Unit as a Possible Bias Incident

The CO/Duty Captain must:

12. Request additional resources to stabilize the location or defuse the incident (community affairs, crime prevention officers, additional command personnel, training unit, Strategic Response Group, if available, or others, as appropriate)
13. Request detective squad personnel to **respond**
14. Request Evidence Collection Team (ECT) to respond and process scene for evidence
15. **Notify Operations Unit** and obtain possible bias incident **log number**
16. Prepare **UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT (PD370-152)** after conferral with precinct detective commander
    - a. Subject of Report will be "POSSIBLE BIAS INCIDENT-LOG NO. ___."
17. Forward **UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT** to Chief of Detectives and Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit **direct**, and forward additional copies **through channels**
18. Forward duplicate copies of UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT through channels to:
    - a. Deputy Commissioner, Community Affairs Bureau
    - b. Precinct commanding officer
    - c. Police service area/transit district commanding officer, as applicable
19. Direct crime prevention officer, where appropriate, to personally contact and advise complainant concerning actions to take to prevent reoccurrence

> **Exam Alert:** Operations Unit personnel will notify: patrol borough command, Patrol Services Bureau Duty Chief, Housing Bureau or Transit Bureau (as applicable), detective borough commander, Intelligence Division, Commanding Officer Hate Crime Unit, Deputy Commissioner Community Affairs Bureau, and Police Commissioner.

### Detective Squad Member (Possible Bias)

20. Conduct an **immediate** investigation and confer with Hate Crime Unit personnel
21. Forward additional copies of **COMPLAINT REPORT** and **OmniForm Complaint Revision** (if applicable) to Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit **direct within ten days** of incident

> **Exam Alert:** The detective forwards to C.O. Hate Crime Unit DIRECT (not through channels) for possible bias incidents.

### ECT Personnel

22. Respond to scene as directed
23. Process scene for evidence and generate ECT run number
24. Inform initial responding unit to invoice evidence as "Investigatory Evidence"

### CO/Hate Crime Unit (Determination)

25. Evaluate situation and make determination after conferral with Chief of Detectives to:
    - a. Assume complete control of investigation, OR
    - b. Participate jointly with precinct detective squad personnel, OR
    - c. Have precinct detective personnel assume full responsibility for the investigation
26. Visit and personally interview victims of possible bias incident
27. Prepare and forward comprehensive follow-up report on **Typed Letterhead** within ten days of incident, including post-incident actions of all Department units, and the current status of investigation to Chief of Detectives and Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit, through channels

> **Exam Alert:** If the victim does not want the perpetrator arrested and there is appropriate legal justification for the arrest, the Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit, may **direct that the arrest be made**. The primary considerations are the prevention of further violence and the safety of all parties concerned.

### Crime Prevention Officer (Possible Bias)

28. Prepare and forward a report on **Typed Letterhead** within ten days of incident to Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit, indicating action taken concerning incident and whether a security survey was conducted

> **Exam Alert:** If no Crime Prevention Officer is assigned to the command, the **Special Operations Lieutenant** prepares and forwards the report per step 28.

### CO/Hate Crime Unit (Final Determination)

29. Make determination of whether an occurrence is a bias incident or a non-bias incident based upon all factors obtained during investigation, after conferral with the Chief of Detectives
30. Visit and personally interview victims of **confirmed** bias incidents upon completion of investigation
    - a. In appropriate cases, a Hate Crime Unit **supervisor** may be designated to interview victims
31. Prepare report on **Typed Letterhead** indicating determination, and forward through channels to:
    - a. Chief of Patrol
    - b. Chief of Detectives
    - c. Chief of Housing/Chief of Transit, if appropriate
    - d. Patrol borough concerned
    - e. Housing/transit borough, as applicable
    - f. Precinct of occurrence
    - g. Police service area/transit district, as applicable

### When CO/Duty Captain Determines Incident is NOT Bias Motivated

32. Notify detective squad
33. Notify Operations Unit that offense is not a bias incident
    - a. Contact Hate Crime Unit, between **0800 hours and 2400 hours**, seven days a week, for **log number on all non-bias incidents**
34. Direct crime prevention officer to personally contact and advise complainant concerning actions to take to prevent reoccurrence, **if offense was committed at a religious institution or a sensitive location**
35. Prepare an **UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT** and forward to Chief of Detectives and Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit **direct**
    - a. Forward one additional copy of UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT **through channels**

### Precinct Detective (Non-Bias)

36. Conduct appropriate investigation
37. Forward copies of **COMPLAINT REPORT OmniForm Complaint Revision** (if applicable) to Chief of Detectives and Hate Crime Unit, **through channels**, within ten days of incident

### Crime Prevention Officer (Non-Bias)

38. Prepare and forward a report on **Typed Letterhead** within ten days of incident to Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit, if offense was committed at a **religious institution or sensitive location** indicating action taken and whether security survey was conducted

### CO of the Command Concerned (NOT Duty Captain)

- Must **PERSONALLY VISIT** victims of possible bias incident
- Within **10 days** of incident: prepare comprehensive follow-up Typed Letterhead including post-incident actions and current status of investigation
- Forward to Chief of Detectives and Commanding Officer, Hate Crime Unit, **through channels**

### Reporting Timeline

- Reports are due within **TEN (10) DAYS** of the incident for: CO, Crime Prevention Officer, AND Detective

### Bias vs. Non-Bias Comparison Table

| Role | NON-BIAS | POSSIBLE BIAS |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **C.O. sends report to** | Chief of Det + HC (direct) + 1 copy through channels | Chief of Det + HC (direct) + copies through channels |
| **Crime Prevention Officer sends report to** | Only if **religious institution/sensitive location**: C.O., Hate Crime Unit | C.O., Hate Crime Unit |
| **Detective forwards 61/OCR to** | Chief of Detectives AND Hate Crime Unit, **through channels** | C.O., Hate Crime Unit **direct** |
| **C.O. notifies** | Squad + **call Hate Crimes (0800x2400) for Non-Bias Log #** | Squad to **RESPOND** + **call Operations for Bias Log #** |
| **Detective conducts** | An **APPROPRIATE** investigation | An **IMMEDIATE** investigation |

> **Prior Test (Captain's Exam):** When an offense committed against a religious institution is investigated as possible bias but the duty captain determines it is NOT bias motivated, all of the following are true EXCEPT: the precinct detective will forward a copy of the 61 and OCR to C.O., Hate Crimes. -- Correct answer is that this is FALSE because for non-bias, the detective forwards to C.O., Hate Crime Unit AND **Chief of Detectives, through channels** (not just C.O. HCU). The exam takes facts from "non-bias" and "confirmed bias" procedures and mixes them up. Know the addressees for each role's reports.

> **Exam Alert:** Four key testable points: (1) Only the Hate Crime Unit CO determines bias classification, (2) the Precinct CO (NOT Duty Captain) must personally visit victims, (3) the 10-day reporting deadline applies to the CO, Crime Prevention Officer, AND Detective, and (4) for incidents involving public officials, confer with the **Intelligence Division** before classifying as possible bias.

### Penal Law Refresher for Bias Crimes

| Charge | Elements |
|--------|----------|
| **Criminal Mischief 4th** (PL 145.00) | Intentional damage up to $250 (reckless damage of $250 is NOT a crime) |
| **Criminal Mischief 3rd** (PL 145.05) | Intentional damage above $250 up to and including $1,500; above $1,500 is 2nd degree |
| **Aggravated Harassment 2nd** (PL 240.30) | Kick, push, etc. for reasons of race, etc. (usually would be Agg Hass 3, a violation, but done for bias = Misdemeanor) |
| **Aggravated Harassment 1st** (PL 240.31) | Damage a religious place more than $50; burn a cross in public view; draw a swastika or noose; prior Agg Hass 2 (Sub 3) with prior of 1st degree or Agg Hass 2 Sub 3 in last 10 years |

## P.G. 207-29: Investigation of Carjackings

Carjacking investigations involve a coordinated response between patrol, the detective squad, and specialized units. Key supervisor responsibilities include:

- Ensuring immediate notification of the detective squad
- Coordinating a canvass of the area
- Broadcasting vehicle and suspect descriptions
- Ensuring the stolen vehicle alarm is transmitted promptly
- Coordinating with neighboring commands when the crime occurs near precinct boundaries

## P.G. 207-33: Complaints Involving Sex Crimes

*(Updated R.O. 43, effective 06/19/25)*

*4 pages in the Patrol Guide — critical for supervisors.*

### Definition

For purposes of this procedure, the term "sex crime" **shall** include those offenses enumerated in:
- **Article 130** of the New York State Penal Law
- **Article 230** of the New York State Penal Law
- **Article 255** of the New York State Penal Law

### UMOS Response

1. Render reasonable aid and prepare **AIDED REPORT**, if applicable
   - a. Take victim to hospital, if necessary
   - b. Encourage necessary medical attention when victim is reluctant
   - c. Inform the victim that under NYS Law they are entitled to have a **sexual assault advocate** present during the interview
2. Attempt to apprehend perpetrator(s), if present
3. Establish a crime scene, if necessary
   - a. Ensure physical evidence (DNA, fingerprints, discharged shell casings, cell phones, computers, hairs, clothing, masks, etc.) is not contaminated, lost, altered, destroyed, etc.
   - b. Comply with P.G. 218-33, "Processing Sexual Offense Collection Kits in Sex Offense Cases," if applicable
4. Request response of patrol supervisor
5. Notify desk officer, precinct of occurrence

### Patrol Supervisor Duties

6. Respond to location and coordinate patrol response
7. **Promptly report ALL cases** involving a sex crime to **Special Victims Unit** and provide preliminary details
   - a. Notify desk officer, PSA/transit district when sex crime incident occurs on housing/transit jurisdiction, as appropriate
8. Supervise crime scene, if established, pending the arrival of Detective Bureau personnel
   - a. Ensure crime scene is safeguarded
   - b. Ensure potential physical evidence (DNA, fingerprints, discharged shell casings, cell phones, computers, hairs, clothing, masks, etc.) is not contaminated, lost, altered, destroyed, etc.
9. Have **COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A)** prepared, if Special Victims Unit is not responding

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor has dual responsibilities: (1) promptly report to SVU, and (2) safeguard the crime scene pending detective arrival. The supervisor "shall" ensure evidence preservation -- this is mandatory.

### Special Victims Unit Response

10. Notify patrol supervisor, precinct of occurrence and provide investigative unit's estimated time of arrival to incident
11. Notify desk officer, precinct of occurrence and provide investigative unit's estimated time of arrival to incident

### Assigned Investigator (SVU/UMOS) Duties

12. Provide victim with a **written notice** detailing the name, address and telephone number(s) of the nearest **Rape Crisis Center**
    - a. Document referral in the "Details" section of COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET
13. Advise victim of sexual offense(s) as defined in Article 130 and Sections 230.34, 255.25, 255.26, and 255.27 of NYS Penal Law who has relocated, or is planning to relocate, due to safety concerns that they may be eligible to participate in the **New York State Address Confidentiality Program** to shield their addresses
    - a. Refer victim to the NYS Department of State's website for additional information
14. Advise victim of sexual offense(s) as defined in Article 130 and Sections 230.34, 230.34-a, 255.25, 255.26, and 255.27 of NYS Penal Law that they will be provided with the **New York State Sexual Assault Survivor Bill of Rights** and referrals for supportive services when they meet with a member of the Special Victims Division
15. Enter under "Notifications To" section on rear of COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET:
    - a. Name of member notified at SVU, the SVU Squad assigned, investigator assigned and case number, if available
    - b. Include any other investigative unit assigned, investigator assigned and case number, if applicable (e.g., IAB, Crime Scene Unit, etc.)

### Hospital Walk-In Sex Crime Complaints (No Prior Police Contact)

16. **CO, Communications Section:** Ensure all calls received at 911 involving a hospital walk-in complaint of a sex crime are referred by the 911 dispatcher **directly to the Special Victims Unit**
    - a. Uniformed members assigned to patrol will **NOT** be directed to respond to the location and interview the complainant/victim
17. **Special Victims Unit:** Coordinate the response of SVU investigative squad concerned

> **Exam Alert:** The hospital walk-in procedure is a common test question. Remember: 911 sends it directly to SVU, patrol does NOT respond. The CO of Communications Section ensures this routing.

### If Arrest Is Effected by UMOS on Patrol for Any Sex Crime

24. **Arresting Officer:** Promptly report details of arrest directly from the scene to the desk officer, precinct of occurrence, in all circumstances, to facilitate immediate notification to the Special Victims Unit
25. **Desk Officer:** Promptly report effected arrest for any sex crime to Special Victims Unit
    - a. Notify desk officer, PSA/transit district when sex crime incident occurs on housing/transit jurisdiction, as appropriate
26. **SVU:** Notify SVU investigative squad concerned, provide details and coordinate response
27. **Assigned Investigator:** Advise arresting officer and determine if case enhancement is needed
28. **Arresting Officer:** Enter under "Notifications To" section on rear of COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET:
    - a. Name of member notified at SVU, the SVU Squad assigned, investigator assigned and case number, if available
    - b. Include any other investigative unit assigned, investigator assigned and case number, if applicable (e.g., IAB, Crime Scene Unit, etc.)

### SVU as Central Repository

SVU is responsible for investigating sexual offense patterns and the tracking of all sex crimes. SVU will be the **central repository** of information and intelligence regarding complaints involving sex crimes.

> **Exam Alert:** Members of the service must contact SVU if assistance is required regarding the proper classification of complaints involving a sex crime. The member shall request to speak to a **SVU supervisor**. The name of the SVU member who assisted must be placed in the "Details" section of the COMPLAINT REPORT.

### Key Operational Policies

- **"Refer the complaint, not the complainant"** -- Under no circumstances will a sex crime complainant be referred or directed to report to another Department unit or facility
- All members **shall** have a compassionate and non-judgmental attitude and demeanor toward all complainants of sex crimes
- Any complaint involving sex related offenses, including **secondary** offenses that are sex related, requires notification to SVU
- The precinct detective squad will notify SVU **and** Police Impersonation Investigation Unit (PIIU) for complaints involving sex crimes and the impersonation of a law enforcement officer
- SVU will investigate robberies committed by police impersonators which include any sexual assault; PIIU will assist

### Victim Rights Summary

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Sexual Assault Advocate** | Victim must be informed of right to have advocate present during interview; authorized by NYS Law |
| **Address Confidentiality** | Victims who relocate due to safety may participate in NYS Address Confidentiality Program |
| **Sexual Assault Survivor Bill of Rights** | Provided when victim meets with SVU member |
| **Rape Crisis Center Referral** | Written notice with name, address, and phone of nearest center |

## Supervisor Quick Reference — Investigation Thresholds

| Threshold | Triggers |
|-----------|----------|
| **$100,000** | Major Case Unit: truck contents, commercial burglary |
| **$10,000** | Detective squad referral: burglary in **Manhattan** |
| **$5,000** | Detective squad referral: burglary **citywide** (except Manhattan) |
| **60+ years old** | Detective squad referral: robbery victim age |
| **6 months** | Detective squad referral: same victim re-victimized within this period |
| **Under 11** | SVU notification: child abuse victim age |
| **10 days** | Bias incident report deadline |
