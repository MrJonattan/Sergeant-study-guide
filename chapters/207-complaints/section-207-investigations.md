# Section 207 — Investigation Procedures

Covers P.G. 207-07, 207-08, 207-10, 207-29, 207-33

---

## P.G. 207-07: Preliminary Investigation of Complaints (Other Than Vice/Narcotics)

*7 pages in the Patrol Guide — one of the most critical procedures for supervisors.*

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Active Case** | A case with leads still being pursued or investigative steps remaining |
| **Closed Case** | A case where all reasonable investigative steps have been completed |
| **Unfounded Case** | A case where investigation determines that no offense actually occurred |

### Crime Scene Preservation

**Do NOT disturb evidence at the crime scene.** This is a fundamental responsibility for all responding members and supervisors.

### Patrol Supervisor Notification Requirements

The Patrol Supervisor must **PROMPTLY** report to the following specialized units:

| Unit to Notify | Circumstances |
|----------------|---------------|
| **Major Case Unit** | Bank burglary or robbery |
| **Major Case Unit** | Truck contents valued over **$100,000** |
| **Major Case Unit** | Commercial burglary loss over **$100,000** |
| **Major Case Unit** | Art theft |
| **Joint Violent Crimes Task Force (JVCTF)** | Armed bank robberies |
| **Highway District** | Fatal or serious injury collisions |
| **Special Victims Unit (SVU)** | ALL sex crimes |
| **Special Victims Unit (SVU)** | Child abuse involving victims under 11 |
| **Special Victims Unit (SVU)** | Human trafficking |

> **Exam Alert:** Memorize the **$100,000** threshold for Major Case Unit notifications (truck contents AND commercial burglary).

### When to Refer to Detective Squad

Refer a case to the detective squad when any of the following apply:

| Condition | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Serious physical injury | Any complaint involving serious physical injury to a victim |
| Robbery with firearm | Or robbery where victim is **60 years or older** |
| Burglary with person present | Or burglary loss exceeding **$5,000** (or **$10,000 in Manhattan**) |
| Unique modus operandi | Crime committed with a distinctive or unusual method |
| Repeat victimization | Same victim targeted **within 6 months** |
| Perpetrator may be identified | Leads exist that could identify the suspect |
| Similar crimes in vicinity | Pattern of related criminal activity |
| Police impersonation | Any crime involving impersonation of a police officer |
| Identity theft | Any complaint involving identity theft |
| Felonious assault on retail workers | Assaults on retail employees in the course of their employment |

> **Exam Alert:** The burglary threshold differs by borough: **$5,000** citywide, but **$10,000 in Manhattan**. This Manhattan exception is frequently tested.

## P.G. 207-08: Preliminary Investigation of Vice Related, Narcotics, or Organized Crime Complaints

Vice, narcotics, and organized crime complaints follow a separate investigative track routed through the **Specialty Enforcement Division**. The preliminary investigation procedures differ from standard complaints:

- Members receiving such complaints ensure initial documentation and notify the appropriate specialized unit.
- Patrol supervisors coordinate with vice/narcotics units for proper case handling.
- Exceptions for recording at the precinct of occurrence (pick-up arrests, found drugs) still apply per P.G. 207-01.

## P.G. 207-10: Bias Motivated Incidents

*5 pages in the Patrol Guide — high priority for the Sergeant exam.*

### Definition

A **Bias Incident** is any offense or attempted offense motivated in whole or in part by the offender's bias based on:
- Race
- Religion
- Ethnicity
- Gender
- Age
- Disability
- Sexual orientation (including **LGBTQIA+**)

### Response Chain

The response chain follows a specific escalation path:

```
1. MOS evaluates the incident for possible bias motivation
   ↓
2. MOS requests PATROL SUPERVISOR to respond
   ↓
3. Patrol Supervisor requests CO/DUTY CAPTAIN
   ↓
4. CO/Duty Captain notifies OPERATIONS UNIT
   ↓
5. Operations Unit issues a BIAS INCIDENT LOG NUMBER
```

### Commanding Officer / Duty Captain Responsibilities

Upon notification of a potential bias incident, the CO or Duty Captain must:

1. **Request Enhanced Crime Team (ECT)** to respond
2. **Request detective squad** to respond
3. **Prepare an UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT** (PD370-152)

### Who Determines Bias Classification

> **Only the Commanding Officer of the Hate Crime Unit** determines whether an incident IS or IS NOT a bias incident, after conferral with the **Chief of Detectives**.

No other rank or unit makes this final determination.

### Commanding Officer of the Command Concerned

- Must **VISIT** the scene or location
- Must **PERSONALLY INTERVIEW** the victims
- This is a mandatory, non-delegable responsibility

### Reporting Timeline

- Reports are due within **TEN (10) DAYS** of the incident

> **Exam Alert:** Three key testable points: (1) Only the Hate Crime Unit CO determines bias classification, (2) the CO of the command concerned must personally interview victims, and (3) the 10-day reporting deadline.

## P.G. 207-29: Investigation of Carjackings

Carjacking investigations involve a coordinated response between patrol, the detective squad, and specialized units. Key supervisor responsibilities include:

- Ensuring immediate notification of the detective squad
- Coordinating a canvass of the area
- Broadcasting vehicle and suspect descriptions
- Ensuring the stolen vehicle alarm is transmitted promptly
- Coordinating with neighboring commands when the crime occurs near precinct boundaries

## P.G. 207-33: Complaints Involving Sex Crimes

*4 pages in the Patrol Guide — critical for supervisors.*

### Core Requirement

The Patrol Supervisor must **PROMPTLY** report **ALL sex crimes** to the **Special Victims Unit (SVU)**.

### Victim Rights

- The victim is entitled to have a **sexual assault advocate** present during any interview.
- This right must be communicated to the victim.

### Hospital Walk-In Sex Crime Complaints

When a sex crime victim presents at a hospital (walk-in):
- The **911 dispatcher** refers the complaint **DIRECTLY to SVU**.
- **Patrol is NOT directed to respond** to the hospital for these complaints.
- SVU handles hospital walk-in sex crime complaints from initial response onward.

### SVU as Central Repository

SVU serves as the **central repository** for all sex crime information across the Department. All sex crime data is routed through and maintained by SVU.

> **Exam Alert:** The hospital walk-in procedure is a common test question. Remember: 911 sends it directly to SVU, patrol does NOT respond.

## Supervisor Quick Reference — Investigation Thresholds

| Threshold | Triggers |
|-----------|----------|
| **$100,000** | Major Case Unit: truck contents, commercial burglary |
| **$10,000** | Detective squad referral: burglary in **Manhattan** |
| **$5,000** | Detective squad referral: burglary **citywide** (except Manhattan) |
| **60+ years old** | Detective squad referral: robbery victim age |
| **6 months** | Detective squad referral: same victim re-victimized within this period |
| **Under 11** | SVU notification: child abuse victim age |
| **10 days** | Bias incident report deadline |
