# Section 207 — Complaints

## Source
- **Patrol Guide Section:** 207
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide1.pdf
- **Priority:** High

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the procedures for receiving and processing complaints from the public
- Know the classification of complaints and the proper documentation required
- Identify the Patrol Supervisor's role in responding to and investigating complaints
- Recognize procedures for crime scene management and evidence preservation
- Master the notification matrix for specialized units and external agencies
- Understand bias incident response, classification, and reporting timelines
- Know corruption reporting obligations and the civilian complaint process
- Understand missing person procedures and alert system activation criteria

## Sergeant Focus Areas
- **Complaint Recording Jurisdiction:** Mastering where complaints are recorded (precinct of occurrence rules, continuous event exceptions for homicide and Rape 1st Degree, Transit/Housing rules)
- **Investigation Thresholds:** Dollar amounts triggering Major Case Unit ($100K) and detective squad referrals ($5K/$10K Manhattan), victim age (60+), and repeat victimization (6 months)
- **Bias Incident Response:** The full escalation chain, the CO's duty to personally interview victims, 10-day reporting deadline, and that only the Hate Crime Unit CO determines bias classification
- **Corruption Reporting:** Absolute duty to report, Confidential ID Number process, failure to report as misconduct, and the interview sequence when a member is accused
- **Civilian Complaints:** Investigating Supervisor hierarchy (Platoon Commander first), reviewer rank requirement (one rank higher), and immediate Command Log entry for blank form requests
- **Missing Persons:** No minimum time limits, resident precinct recording, anyone can be a complainant, never refer to Missing Persons Squad directly
- **Alert Systems:** Silver Alert activation (Captain+ from Detective Bureau, 24-hour window, under-65 exception), Blue Alert criteria
- **Notification Matrix:** Which situations require Operations Unit notification, which go direct, and the IAB notification pattern (DOH, tax, postal, police impersonation)
- **Sex Crimes:** SVU notification for all sex crimes, victim's right to advocate, hospital walk-ins go directly to SVU without patrol response
