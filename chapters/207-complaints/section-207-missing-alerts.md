# Section 207 — Missing Persons and Alert Systems

Covers P.G. 207-23, 207-31, 207-39

---

## P.G. 207-23: Missing Persons

*7 pages in the Patrol Guide — high priority for the Sergeant exam.*

### No Minimum Time Requirement

There are **NO MINIMUM TIME LIMITS** before accepting a missing person report. A report must be taken immediately upon request.

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most commonly tested points. There is NO waiting period (no "24-hour rule" or any other delay). Accept the report immediately.

### Where Missing Person Reports Are Recorded

Missing person reports are recorded at the missing person's **RESIDENT precinct**.

### Command Responsibility

The **Commanding Officer of the residence precinct** is **ULTIMATELY RESPONSIBLE** for the missing person investigation.

### Missing Persons from Outside NYC

Reports for persons missing from **outside New York City** will **NOT be accepted**. The complainant should be directed to their **local police department**.

### Do Not Refer to Missing Persons Squad

Under **NO circumstances** should the complainant be referred directly to the Missing Persons Squad. The complaint is always taken at the precinct level first.

### Who Can Be a Complainant

The **complainant is NOT limited to family members**. Any of the following may report a person missing:
- Guardian
- School representative
- Hospital administrator
- Roommate
- Any other person with knowledge of the missing person's circumstances

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure desk officers and patrol members do not turn away non-family complainants. Anyone with a legitimate concern may file a missing person report.

## P.G. 207-31: Silver Alert System

*4 pages in the Patrol Guide.*

### Definition — Vulnerable Senior

A **vulnerable senior** for Silver Alert purposes is a person who is:
- **65 years of age or older** with **dementia** (Alzheimer's disease or similar condition)
- Missing under circumstances indicating **imminent danger** to their health or safety

### Who Can Activate a Silver Alert

**ONLY a Captain or above from the Detective Bureau** may activate a Silver Alert.

No lower rank and no unit outside the Detective Bureau has this authority.

### Timing Requirement

A Silver Alert must be issued within **24 HOURS** of the determination that the person qualifies for the alert.

### Tactical Consideration

The most prudent approach is to activate the Silver Alert **AFTER** patrol and field resources have been exhausted. The alert system supplements — rather than replaces — immediate search efforts.

### Under-65 Exception

A Silver Alert can **also be activated for persons under 65** who have dementia, if the **Detective Zone Commanding Officer** reasonably believes activation will be beneficial to the investigation.

> **Exam Alert:** Key testable points: (1) Captain or above from Detective Bureau activates, (2) 24-hour window, (3) under-65 exception exists with Detective Zone CO approval.

## P.G. 207-39: Blue Alert System

### Purpose

The Blue Alert System is activated when a **law enforcement officer** has been:
- **Killed** in the line of duty, OR
- **Seriously injured** in the line of duty, OR
- **Missing** in the line of duty

AND the **suspect has not been apprehended**.

### Activation

The Blue Alert disseminates critical information to the public and law enforcement to assist in locating the suspect. It is reserved for situations where the threat to officer safety is ongoing.

## Supervisor Quick Reference — Missing Persons Decision Tree

```
Person reported missing
├── Is the missing person a NYC resident?
│   ├── NO → Do NOT accept report; direct to local police
│   └── YES → Accept IMMEDIATELY (no time limit)
│       ├── Record at RESIDENT precinct
│       ├── CO of residence precinct is ULTIMATELY responsible
│       └── Do NOT refer complainant to Missing Persons Squad
│
├── Is the complainant a family member?
│   └── Does NOT matter — anyone can be a complainant
│
└── Is this a vulnerable senior (65+ with dementia)?
    ├── YES → Silver Alert eligible
    │   └── Captain+ from Detective Bureau activates within 24 hours
    └── NO, but under-65 with dementia?
        └── Detective Zone CO may authorize Silver Alert if beneficial
```

## Alert Systems Comparison

| Feature | Silver Alert | Blue Alert |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Subject** | Vulnerable senior (65+ with dementia) | Law enforcement officer |
| **Trigger** | Missing under dangerous circumstances | Killed, seriously injured, or missing; suspect at large |
| **Who activates** | Captain+ from Detective Bureau | Department-level activation |
| **Time requirement** | Within 24 hours of determination | Immediate upon qualifying event |
| **Under-65 exception** | Yes, with Detective Zone CO approval | N/A |
