# Section 207 — Complaint Reporting System

Covers P.G. 207-01, 207-02, 207-03, 207-04, 207-05, 207-09, 207-34

---

## P.G. 207-01: Complaint Reporting System

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Complaint** | A report of an alleged criminal offense or other matter requiring police attention |
| **Pick-Up Arrest** | An arrest of a person wanted by another command or agency |
| **Continuous Event** | A crime or series of related crimes spanning multiple locations or jurisdictions |
| **Incident** | A single event or occurrence reported in a complaint |
| **Offense** | A specific criminal charge; up to **10 offenses** may be recorded per incident |

### Where Complaints Are Recorded

The general rule: complaints are recorded in the **PRECINCT OF OCCURRENCE**.

#### Exceptions to Precinct of Occurrence

| Situation | Recorded At |
|-----------|-------------|
| Vice/Narcotics/Organized Crime | **Specialty Enforcement Division** |
| Vice exception: pick-up arrests | Precinct of **arrest** |
| Vice exception: found drugs **IN** building | Precinct of **occurrence** |
| Vice exception: found drugs 2 oz or less **NOT in** building | Precinct of **occurrence** |
| Missing NYC residents | Missing person's **resident** precinct |
| Piers/bulkheads/bodies in navigable waters | Precinct where **pier or bulkhead** is located |
| Lost property | Precinct where complainant **FIRST discovered** the loss |
| Overdue rental vehicles | Precinct where vehicle was **leased/rented** OR where it was **expected to be returned** |

### Continuous Event Rules

- **General rule:** Record at the precinct of the **INITIAL** offense.
- **Homicide exception:** Recorded where the **homicide occurred**, regardless of where the continuous event began.
- **Rape 1st Degree exception:** Recorded where the **rape occurred**, regardless of where the continuous event began.

### Special Bureau Jurisdiction

| Bureau | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Housing Bureau** | Continuous event starting **on-development** remains Housing Bureau jurisdiction |
| **Transit Bureau** | Crime on a **moving train** is recorded at the **NEXT station where the train stops** |

### Forms

| Form | Number | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Complaint Report Worksheet | PD313-152A | Initial recording of complaint information |
| Complaint Report | PD313-152 | Official complaint record |
| Complaint Index Report | — | Indexing and cross-referencing |
| Omniform Complaint Revision | — | Amending/revising existing complaint reports |

## P.G. 207-02: Complaints Not Recorded on Complaint Report

Certain matters brought to the attention of the Department do not warrant a formal Complaint Report. These are handled through alternative documentation methods (e.g., Command Log entries, aided reports, or other administrative records) as appropriate to the nature of the complaint.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure members understand which incidents require a formal Complaint Report and which are properly documented through alternative means. Misdocumentation can affect crime statistics and case tracking.

---

## P.G. 207-03: Voiding Complaint Reports

### When a Report May Be Voided

The **Desk Officer** determines if a complaint should be voided when it is:
- A **duplicate** of an existing report
- Filed at the **wrong precinct**
- For an event occurring **outside NYC**
- An event that **should have been recorded differently**

### Critical Distinction: VOIDED vs. UNFOUNDED

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **VOIDED** | The report **should NOT have been prepared** in the first place (procedural error) |
| **UNFOUNDED** | The report **was properly prepared**, but subsequent investigation reveals that **no offense actually occurred** |

> **Exam Alert:** This distinction is heavily tested. A voided report is a clerical/procedural correction. An unfounded case reflects an investigative finding that no crime took place.

## P.G. 207-04: Reporting of Complaints Occurring in Another Command

When a complaint is reported at a precinct other than the precinct of occurrence:
- The receiving command documents the complaint and ensures the information is transmitted to the **precinct of occurrence** for proper recording.
- The precinct of occurrence retains responsibility for the complaint and any subsequent investigation.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure that complaints taken for another command are promptly and accurately forwarded. Delays can compromise investigations.

---

## P.G. 207-05: Duplicate Copies of Complaint Reports

Procedures govern when and how duplicate copies of complaint reports are prepared and distributed. Duplicate copies ensure that all relevant commands, squads, and units have the information necessary to conduct follow-up investigations or provide support.

---

## P.G. 207-09: Follow-Up Investigations of Complaints Already Recorded

When additional information is obtained on a previously recorded complaint:
- The follow-up investigation is coordinated with the detective squad or specialized unit assigned to the case.
- Updates are documented through the Omniform Complaint Revision process.
- The original complaint report's status (active, closed, unfounded) is updated accordingly.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisors should ensure that officers conducting follow-up canvasses or receiving new information properly route it to the assigned investigative unit rather than creating new complaint reports.

---

## P.G. 207-34: Complaint File

The Complaint File is the centralized repository for all complaint-related documentation within a command. It includes:
- Original complaint reports and worksheets
- Follow-up investigation notes
- Correspondence related to cases
- Status updates and dispositions

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors are responsible for ensuring the complaint file is maintained in an orderly and up-to-date fashion, facilitating case review and audits.

## Supervisor Quick Reference

### Complaint Recording Decision Tree

```
Is it a criminal offense or police matter?
├── NO → Document via Command Log or other appropriate record (207-02)
└── YES → Prepare Complaint Report
    ├── Is it Vice/Narcotics/Organized Crime?
    │   ├── YES → Specialty Enforcement Division (with exceptions)
    │   └── NO → Continue
    ├── Is it a missing NYC resident?
    │   ├── YES → Resident precinct
    │   └── NO → Continue
    ├── Is it a continuous event?
    │   ├── YES → Precinct of INITIAL offense
    │   │   ├── UNLESS homicide → where homicide occurred
    │   │   └── UNLESS Rape 1st → where rape occurred
    │   └── NO → Precinct of OCCURRENCE
    └── Lost property? → Where loss FIRST discovered
```
