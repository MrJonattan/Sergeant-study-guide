# Section 207 — Key Terms, Forms, and Abbreviations

---

## Core Complaint Definitions (P.G. 207-01)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Complaint** | A report of an alleged criminal offense or other matter requiring police attention | P.G. 207-01 |
| **Pick-Up Arrest** | An arrest of a person wanted by another command or agency | P.G. 207-01 |
| **Continuous Event** | A crime or series of related crimes spanning multiple locations or jurisdictions | P.G. 207-01 |
| **Incident** | A single event or occurrence reported in a complaint | P.G. 207-01 |
| **Offense** | A specific criminal charge; up to 10 may be recorded per incident | P.G. 207-01 |

## Case Status Definitions (P.G. 207-07)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Active Case** | A case with leads still being pursued or investigative steps remaining | P.G. 207-07 |
| **Closed Case** | A case where all reasonable investigative steps have been completed | P.G. 207-07 |
| **Unfounded Case** | Investigation determines that no offense actually occurred (report was properly prepared) | P.G. 207-07 |

## Report Status Distinction (P.G. 207-03)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Voided** | The report should NOT have been prepared in the first place (procedural error) | P.G. 207-03 |
| **Unfounded** | The report was properly prepared, but no offense actually occurred (investigative finding) | P.G. 207-03, 207-07 |

## Bias and Discrimination Terms (P.G. 207-10, 207-36)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Bias Incident** | An offense motivated by bias based on race, religion, ethnicity, gender, age, disability, or sexual orientation (including LGBTQIA+) | P.G. 207-10 |
| **Disability** | A physical, mental, or medical impairment that substantially limits major life activities | P.G. 207-36 |

## Alert System Terms (P.G. 207-31, 207-39)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Vulnerable Senior** | A person 65+ with dementia (Alzheimer's or similar), missing under circumstances indicating imminent danger | P.G. 207-31 |
| **Silver Alert** | Alert system for missing vulnerable seniors; may also apply to under-65 with dementia per Detective Zone CO | P.G. 207-31 |
| **Blue Alert** | Alert system activated when a law enforcement officer is killed, seriously injured, or missing in the line of duty and the suspect is at large | P.G. 207-39 |

## Corruption and Misconduct Terms (P.G. 207-21, 207-22)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Corruption/Other Misconduct** | Illegal or improper conduct by a member of the service; all members have an absolute duty to report | P.G. 207-21 |
| **Confidential ID Number** | Number issued to anonymous reporters of corruption; obtaining it satisfies the reporting duty | P.G. 207-21 |

## Civilian Complaint Terms (P.G. 207-27, 207-28)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Investigating Supervisor** | The supervisor who investigates a civilian complaint: Platoon Commander, Special Ops Lt., ICO, or CO/Duty Captain | P.G. 207-28 |
| **Supervisor Reviewing Civilian Complaint** | Must be at least one rank higher than the member receiving the complaint | P.G. 207-28 |

## Immigration-Related Terms (P.G. 207-37)

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **U Visa** | A visa for victims of qualifying criminal activity who have suffered substantial abuse and are helpful to law enforcement | P.G. 207-37 |
| **Qualifying Criminal Activity** | Specified crimes that make a victim eligible for U Visa certification | P.G. 207-37 |
| **Certifying Official** | The Department official authorized to sign U Visa certification forms | P.G. 207-37 |
