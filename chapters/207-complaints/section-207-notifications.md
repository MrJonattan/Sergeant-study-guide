# Section 207 — Notification Requirements

Covers P.G. 207-06, 207-24

---

## P.G. 207-06: Notifications

*3 pages in the Patrol Guide — critical notification matrix for supervisors.*

### Desk Officer Notifications to Operations Unit

The Desk Officer must notify the **Operations Unit** for the following situations:

| Situation | Additional Notification |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Counterfeit U.S. obligations | **Secret Service** |
| DOH employee crime or impersonation | **Inspector General, DOH** + **IAB** |
| Tax official crimes | **Inspector General, Dept. of Finance** + **IAB** |
| Postal property or employee crimes | **Postal Inspector** + **IAB** |
| Contraband weapons | **ATF (Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives)** |
| Product tampering | **New York State Police** |
| Home invasion robbery | **Detective Bureau Wheel** |

### Direct Notifications (No Operations Unit)

| Situation | Notify Directly |
|-----------|----------------|
| Child abuse (victim under 18) | **New York State Child Abuse Register** |
| Police impersonation | **IAB Command Center** |
| Private carting / wholesale markets / gambling boats | **Business Integrity Commission** (via DB Wheel) |

### Specialized Crime Notifications

| Situation | Notify |
|-----------|--------|
| ATM larceny | **Grand Larceny Squad** (via email) |
| Skimmer device / financial information recording device | **Financial Crimes Task Force** |
| Sexual assault of prisoner in custody | **Criminal Justice Bureau** |

> **Exam Alert:** The notification matrix is heavily tested. Focus on which situations require Operations Unit notification versus direct notification, and which require IAB involvement (DOH, tax officials, postal matters all require IAB).

### IAB Notification Pattern

Notice that **three** categories require both Operations Unit AND IAB notification:
1. DOH employee/impersonation
2. Tax official crimes
3. Postal property/employee crimes

Police impersonation goes **directly** to IAB (no Operations Unit).

## P.G. 207-24: Notifications from Caseworkers (Adult Protective Services / Dept. for the Aging)

*(Updated R.O. 104, effective 10/20/25)*

When notifications are received from caseworkers of Adult Protective Services or the Department for the Aging:
- The information is documented and appropriate action is taken
- These notifications often involve vulnerable adults who may be victims of abuse, neglect, or exploitation
- Patrol supervisors ensure that responding officers conduct welfare checks and document findings

> **Sergeant Focus:** These notifications frequently involve elderly or incapacitated persons. Ensure responding members are sensitive to the circumstances and thorough in documentation.
