# Section 221 — Special Weapons and Distraction Devices

## P.G. 221-10: Use and Issuance of Shotguns/Long Guns/Automatic Weapons

**Date Effective:** 08/28/19 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To provide guidelines for use of special weapons and maintain security and control of issuance.

### Definition

**Special Weapons** — Shotguns, long guns, and automatic weapons.

### Special Weapons Record (Three Sections)

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Qualification Section** (20 pages) | Member's rank, name, tax registry number, dates qualified |
| **Issuance Section** | Time out, rank, name, signature, shield number, date qualified, reason, weapon serial number, supervisor authorizing, time returned, verifying supervisor |
| **Maintenance Section** (4 pages) | Date received, serial number, date removed, reason, signatures |

### Key Rules

| Rule |
|------|
| NEVER issue to or allow handling by member whose **range qualifications are not current** |
| Unissued weapons stored in **locked gun cabinet** |
| Command Log entry each tour indicating disposition of all special weapons |
| Load/unload at **safety station** maintained at command |
| Only carry weapons and ammunition **authorized and issued by the Department** |
| Shotgun is **mandated** for one-officer sector patrol vehicles and solo Highway District personnel |

### Solo Unit Shotgun Rules

| Rule |
|------|
| Lock and secure windows, doors; place in locking device when leaving unoccupied vehicle |
| Lock when responding to scene where other members present (unless extraordinary need) |
| Return immediately to precinct if defective |
| If solo RMP converted to solo SP10, summons unit, or other unit — shotgun **immediately returned** |

### Detective Bureau Special Weapons

A **captain or above** may authorize issuance when ALL of the following exist:
1. Tactical plan formulated
2. Potential arrest situation
3. Arrest or search warrant issued
4. History of violence on the part of the perpetrator

Captain or above must **accompany** those members and maintain firearms control. Weapon **unloaded, opened, and encased** while transported.

### ESU/Harbor Unit Special Weapons Uses

| Authorized Situation |
|---------------------|
| Barricaded criminal/hostage taker/EDP with firearm (or reasonable belief) |
| Armed hostage taker threatening life of hostage |
| Searches for armed perpetrators, warrant executions |
| VIP escorts and protection |
| Guarding dangerous criminals (with DOC) |
| Guarding payrolls, narcotics, or weapons transfers |
| Captain or above deems appropriate |
| Spontaneous incident requiring immediate action (last resort) |
| NOT for restraining EDP unless firearm involved |

> **Exam Alert:** Special weapons NEVER issued to member whose **qualifications are not current**. Shotgun is **mandated** for solo sector patrol vehicles.

## P.G. 221-11: Guidelines and Approval Process Pertaining to the Use of Distraction Devices

**Date Effective:** 06/26/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 52 | **Pages:** 2

### Definition

A **distraction device** is a less lethal hand-held apparatus generating startling sound and light energy when deployed into a confined area. Provides short-term tactical cover for high-risk entries.

### Authorization

- Only authorized for use by trained **Emergency Service Unit** members
- Requires direct knowledge and **specific approval** of **Chief of Department** (or designee), absent exigent circumstances

### Approval Process — Search Warrants

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| 1 | Captain or above | Contact **borough executive officer** |
| 2 | Borough XO | Review and confer with **Chief of Department** (or designee) for **final approval** |

### Approval Process — EDP/Perpetrator Search/Exigent Circumstances

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| 1 | Captain or above | Notify **duty inspector** |
| 2 | Duty inspector | Request approval of **citywide duty chief** |
| 3 | Duty chief | Contact **Chief of Department** (or designee) via Operations Unit for **final approval** |

### Exigent Circumstances Exception

Ranking ESU supervisor may request approval directly from **Chief of Special Operations** or **CO, ESU**. Once stabilized, the captain must **immediately notify the Chief of Department** via Operations Unit.

### Post-Incident Reporting

Captain concerned prepares Typed Letterhead report:
- Obtain **Distraction Device Utilization Control Number** from ESU Desk Officer
- Forward through channels to Chief of Department

> **Exam Alert:** Distraction devices require **Chief of Department** approval (or designee). Only used by trained **ESU** members. At every level, **all alternatives must be considered** before recommending approval.
