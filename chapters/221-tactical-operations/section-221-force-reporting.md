# Section 221 — Reporting and Investigation of Force Incidents

## P.G. 221-03: Reporting and Investigation of Force Incident or Injury to Persons During Police Action

**Date Effective:** 07/23/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 64 | **Pages:** 11

### Purpose

To properly record and investigate use of force incidents by MOS/auxiliary members, and/or injuries to persons sustained as a result of police action or while in police custody.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Subject** | Individual who is the target/focus of police action (suspect, perpetrator, prisoner, EDP, disorderly person) |
| **Civilian/Non-Member/Bystander** | Non-employee of Department not intended subject of police action but inadvertently injured |
| **Immediate Supervisor** | Available supervisor NOT involved in incident, from same command, at least **one rank higher** than all involved members; if none available, duty captain determines |
| **Investigating Supervisor** | Level 2: CO/XO/duty captain; Level 3: IAB ranking officer; Level 4: FID ranking officer |
| **Physical Illness/Injury** | Impairment including minor swelling, contusions, lacerations, abrasions |
| **Serious Physical Injury/Illness** | Substantial risk of death, or serious disfigurement, protracted impairment of health, or loss of function of organ/limb |

### Serious Physical Injury Includes

- Broken/fractured bone(s)
- Aneurysm
- Injury requiring **hospital admission**
- Heart attack, stroke
- Other life-threatening illness or injury

### Force Incident Levels

| Level | Qualifying Criteria | Investigating Entity |
|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Level 1** | Hand/foot strikes, forcible take-downs, wrestling/grappling with actively resisting subject, O.C. pepper spray discharge, mesh restraining blanket, CEW in **cartridge mode**, physical injury to subject/civilian/bystander from police action | **Immediate Supervisor** |
| **Level 2** | Intentional striking with any object (baton, vehicle), police canine bite, CEW in **drive stun mode**, prohibited acts (excluding chokehold/prohibited restraint), alleged/suspected excessive force, attempted prisoner suicide, unconsciousness/loss of tooth/stitches/staples | **CO/XO/Duty Captain** + IAB Investigations Unit |
| **Level 3** | Force readily capable of causing death/serious injury (not firearm), alleged/suspected chokehold or prohibited restraint, excessive force with serious physical injury, attempted prisoner suicide with serious injury, serious physical injury from police action | **Internal Affairs Bureau** |
| **Level 4** | ALL firearms discharges by UMOS (intentional, unintentional, self-inflicted), discharge of UMOS firearm by someone else, subject/civilian seriously injured and likely to die or dies in/prior to custody | **Force Investigation Division** |

> **Exam Alert:** The 4 force incident levels are critical. Key distinctions: CEW **cartridge mode = Level 1**, CEW **drive stun = Level 2**. Chokehold/prohibited restraint = **Level 3**. ALL firearms discharges = **Level 4**.

### Immediate Supervisor Duties (All Levels)

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 6 | Review facts and provide details to desk officer |
| 7 | Make prompt assessment — determine force level and appropriate investigation |
| 8 | Establish crime scene for **Level 3 and Level 4** |
| 9 | Obtain and secure **body-worn cameras** from ALL MOS on scene for Level 3/4 |
| 10 | Reserve T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT number from FORMS for Level 1 |
| 11 | Direct involved members to complete T.R.I. INTERACTION REPORT |
| 12 | Question subjects/civilians regarding possible injuries |
| 13 | Take **digital photographs** of visible injuries (and where injury claimed but not visible) for Level 1 |
| 14 | Interview witnesses |
| 15 | Review body-worn camera video for **Level 1 only** |
| 17 | Question involved MOS regarding reason for force and type used |
| 20 | Determine tactical recommendations to improve safety |
| 21 | Complete and electronically sign T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT for Level 1 **prior to end of tour** |

### Body-Worn Camera Viewing Rules

| Level | Who Views |
|-------|-----------|
| Level 1 | Immediate supervisor reviews |
| Level 2 | Guided by investigating supervisor (CO/XO/duty captain) |
| Level 3-4 | Immediate supervisor does **NOT** view; safeguard cameras for IAB/FID |

### Desk Officer Notifications (Level 2, 3, or 4)

| Notification |
|-------------|
| Patrol Borough Command |
| Operations Unit |
| Internal Affairs Bureau Command Center (obtain IAB log number) |
| Precinct detective squad, as appropriate |
| Commanding officer/executive officer, if available |

### Investigating Supervisor Duties (Level 2, 3, 4)

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 28 | Conduct investigation and prepare T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT |
| 29 | Review body-worn camera videos |
| 30 | Interview involved MOS to assess whether force was necessary and reasonable |
| 31 | Ensure photographs taken and uploaded |
| 32 | Report findings and recommendations to IAB Command Center |
| 33 | Review all T.R.I. INTERACTION REPORTS prior to end of tour |

### Command Training Sergeant

- Review all T.R.I. reports for command
- Identify recommendations for creative/innovative approaches to improve safety
- Submit recommendations to precinct CO on Typed Letterhead

### One Report Rule

Only **ONE** T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT per incident, regardless of number of MOS involved or number of subjects/civilians/bystanders.

### NOT Reportable Uses of Force

| Situation |
|-----------|
| Ordering person to lie on ground, guiding to ground in controlled manner, or mere use of equipment (Velcro straps, shield) — **unless injury sustained** |
| Subject with pre-existing illness/injury requiring ER (report per P.G. 210-04 or 216-01 instead) |
| Injuries occurring in custody of **another agency** (e.g., DOC) |
| Department vehicle collisions — **unless** person in custody injured/dies, pursuit injury/death, or apprehension injury/death |

### Key Forms

| Form | Use |
|------|-----|
| **T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT** | One per incident; completed by investigating supervisor |
| **T.R.I. INTERACTION REPORT** | Completed by each involved MOS for every reportable force/injury |
| **PD324-140** (UMOS Report of Firearms Discharge) | Firearms discharges — completed within **48 hours** |

> **Exam Alert:** Immediate supervisor must be at least **one rank higher** than all involved members and NOT involved in the incident.

> **Exam Alert:** FID completes T.R.I. reports for Level 4 incidents. **PD324-140** (Firearms Discharge Report) must be completed within **48 hours**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** For Level 1 incidents, the immediate supervisor is responsible for **all investigative steps**. For Level 2-4, the immediate supervisor is guided by the investigating supervisor. Complete Level 1 T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT **before end of tour**.
