# Section 221 — EDP, MOS Subjected to Force, and Tactical Procedures

## P.G. 221-06: Member of the Service Subjected to Force While Performing Lawful Duty

**Date Effective:** 11/18/21 | **Last Revision:** I.O. 102 | **Pages:** 5

### Purpose

To record and evaluate incidents when MOS is subjected to force while performing lawful duty, on or off duty, or auxiliary members on duty.

### Definition of Force (Against MOS)

Any physical harassment, assault, reckless endangerment, or menacing, including shots fired at member, brandishing weapon at member, and throwing items at member — whether or not injury resulted.

### Immediate Supervisor Duties

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 6 | Respond to scene and conduct preliminary investigation |
| 7 | Notify desk officer |
| 8 | Notify precinct detective squad if outstanding suspects or further investigation required |
| 9 | Ensure **COMPLAINT REPORT** prepared for offense against member and/or additional charge added |
| 10 | Ensure **Line of Duty Injury Report** completed |
| 11 | Perform duties per P.G. 221-03 |
| 12 | Prepare T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT |
| 13 | Instruct involved members to complete T.R.I. INTERACTION REPORT |

### Note on Force Incident Levels

Injuries sustained by MOS are **independent** of force incident levels in P.G. 221-03. Force incident levels do NOT apply to injuries sustained by MOS. Instead, this procedure and A.G. 330-03 (Line of Duty Injury or Death) apply.

### Desk Officer — Charging Requirements

Ensure arresting officer charges defendant with appropriate charges:
- **Resisting Arrest** (Penal Law §205.30)
- **Assault 2nd degree** (Penal Law §120.05, sub.3)
- **Assault on Peace Officer/Police Officer/Fireman/EMS Professional** (Penal Law §120.08)
- **Aggravated Assault Upon Police/Peace Officer** (Penal Law §120.11)

### "Resisting Recidivist"

| Criteria | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Prisoner arrested for above charges **more than once within 5 years** (2nd time in 5 years) | Deemed a **"Resisting Recidivist"** |
| Action required | Detective squad **enhances arrest**, interviews subjects, canvasses for video and witnesses |

### If ADA Refuses to Charge

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| CO/XO/Duty Captain | Confer with desk officer; if unsatisfied, notify **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters** |
| DC Legal Matters availability | Mon-Fri 0700-2300 directly; midnight tours/weekends through Operations Division |

> **Exam Alert:** A "Resisting Recidivist" is a prisoner arrested for charges against MOS **more than once within 5 years**. Requires detective squad enhancement.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Force incident levels (1-4) do NOT apply to MOS injuries. Follow this procedure and A.G. 330-03 instead.

## P.G. 221-13: Mentally Ill or Emotionally Disturbed Persons

**Date Effective:** 10/28/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 113 | **Pages:** 7

### Purpose

To safeguard a mentally ill or emotionally disturbed person who does not voluntarily seek medical assistance.

### Scope

**Primary duty** of all MOS is to **preserve human life**. Safety of all persons is **paramount**. Force may be used when reasonable to prevent serious physical injury or death. When there is time to negotiate, **all the time necessary** will be used.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **EDP** | Person apparently mentally ill or temporarily deranged, conducting themselves in a manner reasonably believed likely to result in serious injury to self or others |
| **Zone of Safety** | Distance maintained between EDP and MOS; greater than effective range of weapon (other than firearm); minimum **20 feet** recommended |
| **Establishing/Maintaining Firearms Control** | UMOS will not discharge firearms except to protect from imminent serious physical injury or death; ensure muzzle and trigger finger discipline |
| **Crossfire** | Unintentional placement of any person in position where they may be injured from firearms discharge |
| **Crossfire Awareness** | Being aware of threat location and surroundings; communicate "crossfire" when aware |

### MOS Initial Response

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Review **"EDP History"** in 911 app on Department smartphone **prior to arrival** |
| 1a | Request **EDP location history** from Communications Section |
| 3 | Transmit **"10-84"** upon arrival |
| 5 | Carry **three-foot polycarbonate shield** with door bag (door rope/wedge), if available |
| 6 | Request: patrol supervisor, ambulance, additional personnel, ESU if necessary |

### Spit Hood Rules

| Rule |
|------|
| Only **ESU personnel** may apply a spit hood |
| ESU member monitors and remains with subject until relieved by EMT (minimum) |
| Only **ESU personnel** or medical professional (EMT minimum) can **remove** |

### Patrol Supervisor Duties

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 11 | Respond to location (even if supervisor from another agency is present) |
| 12 | Transmit **"10-84"** upon arrival |
| 13 | **Establish firearms control** |
| 14 | Verify ESU is responding — may **cancel ESU only** if sergeant or above |
| 23 | Use **CIT-trained** members as contact officers when appropriate |
| 23a | If non-CIT officer has established rapport, allow that officer to continue; CIT supports |
| 24 | Employ less lethal devices when necessary for safety |
| 25 | Direct removal of door rope/wedge if EDP constitutes immediate threat |
| 26 | Request response of CO/duty captain |
| 30 | Take **no additional action** without CO/duty captain authorization if EDP is contained, believed armed/violent, but poses no immediate threat |

### Isolate and Contain — Door Rope/Wedge

| Rule |
|------|
| Use to isolate and contain EDP |
| Secure door with **only one door rope** at a time (girth hitch knot) |
| If another individual is present with EDP, use only in **exigent circumstances** |
| Do NOT remove without **supervisor authorization** or ESU direction (unless exigent) |

### Three Scenarios

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **EDP unarmed, not violent, willing to leave voluntarily** | MOS may take into custody **without supervisor direction** |
| **EDP isolated/contained but will not leave voluntarily** | Request H.N.T., coordinator, T.A.R.U.; comply with P.G. 221-14 (Hostage/Barricaded) |
| **EDP's actions = immediate threat of serious physical injury or death** | Take reasonable measures; deadly force only as **last resort**; attempt de-escalation |

### When EDP Has Been Restrained

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 41 | Remove property dangerous to life or facilitating escape |
| 42 | Remove to hospital in **ambulance** |
| 43 | Ride in body of ambulance with EDP |
| 44 | Transport by RMP only if ambulance unavailable and at **supervisor's direction** |
| 44a | **Under no circumstances** transport EDP to **police facility** |
| 45 | Inform examining physician of less lethal devices used |
| 46 | Unload firearm at safety station when entering **psychiatric ward** |
| 47 | Safeguard EDP at hospital until examined by **psychiatrist** |

### CIT (Crisis Intervention Team) Requirements

- Training sergeants/operations coordinators maintain updated CIT-trained list at desk (weekly)
- Desk officers ensure list maintained at all times
- Check "CIT Trained UMOS on Scene" on AIDED REPORT

### EDP Radio Codes

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 10-97E3 | ESU Assisted in Removing EDP |
| 10-97E4 | Voluntary Surrender to ESU or H.N.T. |
| 10-97E5 | ESU Removal — Less Than Lethal Used |

> **Exam Alert:** **Zone of safety** = minimum **20 feet**. EDP must NEVER be transported to a **police facility**. Only **ESU** may apply or remove a **spit hood**.

> **Exam Alert:** Response of ESU may only be canceled by a **uniformed supervisor in the rank of sergeant or above**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** If EDP is contained and believed armed/violent but poses no immediate threat — take **no additional action** without CO/duty captain authorization. Establish **firearms control** immediately upon arrival. Use CIT-trained members as contact officers.

## P.G. 221-16: Vehicle Checkpoints

**Date Effective:** 06/26/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 53 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To establish criteria for safe, legal, and effective vehicle checkpoints.

### Authorized Purposes

- **DWI check**
- **Vehicle safety check** (including vehicle and driver documentation)
- Emergency exceptions

Vehicle checkpoints for **all other purposes** require prior written approval from **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters**.

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Duration | Should **NOT exceed 2 hours** at one location |
| Stop pattern | **Non-arbitrary** manner (e.g., every third vehicle) — directed by CO |
| Department vehicles | At least **2** Department vehicles required |
| Reflective vests | Required during **hours of darkness** |
| Safety equipment | Turret lights, flares, traffic cones, signs, and/or barriers |
| Supervisor | Must remain at checkpoint for **entire operation** |
| Inclement weather | **Cancel** checkpoint (snow, heavy rain, etc.) |
| VEHICLE REPORT | Complete for **every vehicle stopped**, regardless of enforcement action |

### Member Duties at Checkpoint

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 12 | Identify yourself: rank, name, shield number, command |
| 14 | Explain purpose of checkpoint (unless impairs investigation) |
| 16 | Offer **RIGHT TO KNOW BUSINESS CARD** |

### Permissible Actions

- Request driver's license, registration, insurance card
- Conduct license and VIN check (VIN from **outside** the car only)
- If documentation appropriate, driver must proceed unless **reasonable suspicion** developed

### Prohibited Actions

- Reaching inside car to uncover VIN plate (may **request** motorist uncover it)
- Requesting consent to search without at least **founded suspicion** of criminality (Level 2 Encounter)

> **Exam Alert:** Vehicle checkpoints should NOT exceed **2 hours**. Stops must be **non-arbitrary** (e.g., every 3rd vehicle). At least **2 Department vehicles** required.

## P.G. 221-18: Use of Department Canine Teams

**Date Effective:** 08/28/19 | **Pages:** 3

### Authorized Tactical Uses — Patrol Canines

| Use |
|-----|
| Search buildings for break-in suspects (non-suspects NOT present) |
| Track suspects or missing persons |
| Locate lost/abandoned articles or hidden implements of crime |
| Assist in arrest/prevent escape of person with probable cause for **felony** |
| Protect officers and other persons from injury/death |
| Assist in service of arrest/search warrants |
| Safeguard equipment and designated facilities |
| Perform cadaver searches |
| Locate persons in building collapse / search and rescue |
| Search for felony suspects within transit system, including subway tracks |

### Key Rules

| Rule |
|------|
| Tactical use NOT specifically authorized is **prohibited** without prior approval of **borough commander** |
| Canine teams will **NOT** be used for crowd control or deterrent at **peaceful demonstrations** |
| Use at other than peaceful demonstrations requires prior approval of **Chief of Patrol** |
| Extra-jurisdictional use requires approval of patrol borough commander |
| Patrol supervisor determines if circumstances warrant use; consults with canine handler |

### Department Bloodhounds

- Unique scent discrimination ability
- Used when patrol canine insufficient
- Request through **Operations Unit**
- Determination made by supervisor from Chief of Special Operations or ESU on scene, after consultation with duty captain and Canine Unit member
- Require **good, uncontaminated scent article** — not handled by anyone
- Washed clothes are **not acceptable** as scent articles
- Bloodhounds track only — do NOT backtrack, do NOT defend handler

> **Exam Alert:** Canine teams NOT used for **crowd control at peaceful demonstrations**. Non-authorized tactical use requires **borough commander** approval.

## P.G. 221-21: School Safety Agent — Victim of an Offense While Performing Duty

**Date Effective:** 10/24/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 111 | **Pages:** 3

### Key Points

- School safety agent requests school safety supervisor and special operations lieutenant (or patrol supervisor if unavailable)
- School safety supervisor initiates investigation
- If H.A.R.M. (harassment, assault, reckless endangerment, menacing) — prepare T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT per P.G. 221-06
- School safety agent who is **not a peace officer** shall **never** be directed to effect an arrest

## P.G. 221-22: Traffic Enforcement Agent Victim of an Offense While Performing Duty

**Date Effective:** 10/08/19 | **Pages:** 2

### Key Points

- Patrol supervisor, precinct of occurrence, **must** respond and conduct initial investigation
- Traffic Enforcement District supervisor must respond and assist
- If H.A.R.M. — prepare T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT per P.G. 221-06
- If serious injury or newsworthy — prepare UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT and forward to Chief of Transportation
- All TEA on-duty injuries — Communications Section notifies both duty captain and Traffic Management Center
