## Learning Objectives
- Understand force guidelines, firearm prohibitions, and restraint prohibitions
- Know the 4 force incident levels and corresponding investigating entities
- Master less lethal weapons procedures (O.C. pepper spray, CEW, tear gas)
- Know EDP handling procedures including zone of safety, isolate/contain, spit hood rules
- Understand firearms discharge investigation procedures and temporary assignment rules
- Recognize special weapons issuance requirements and distraction device approval process
- Know vehicle checkpoint rules and canine team authorized uses

## Sergeant Focus Areas

- **Force incident response**: Immediate supervisor must be NOT involved, one rank higher; for Level 1, responsible for all investigative steps; complete T.R.I. before end of tour
- **Duty to intervene**: Must intervene to stop excessive force — failure = serious misconduct up to dismissal
- **Firearms discharge**: Assume command, establish crime scene, inspect/secure firearms, secure BWC, notify desk officer
- **CEW accountability**: Account for all CEWs each tour (Command Log entry with serial numbers); observe function test; carry CEW on patrol if available
- **EDP handling**: Establish firearms control immediately; maintain 20-foot zone of safety; if contained and believed armed but no immediate threat — NO additional action without CO/duty captain; only sergeant+ can cancel ESU
- **De-escalation**: Supervisor assumes command and coordinates de-escalation; consider whether non-compliance is inability vs. deliberate resistance
- **Less lethal prohibitions**: Never use O.C. spray, CEW, or impact weapons on passively resisting subjects; never use CEW on rear-cuffed prisoner
- **Post-force**: Ensure subject positioned for free breathing; inquire about medical needs; obtain medical attention for any injured person
