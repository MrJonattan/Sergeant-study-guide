# Section 221 — Firearms Discharge and Death in Custody

## P.G. 221-04: Firearms Discharge by Uniformed Members of the Service

**Date Effective:** 04/04/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 18 | **Pages:** 7

### Purpose

To record and evaluate incidents in which a UMOS's firearm is discharged by any person.

### Scope Exclusions

Does NOT include discharges during:
- Authorized training session
- Lawful target practice or hunting
- Discharge into a firearms safety station within NYPD, DOC, or H+H facility

### Patrol Supervisor Duties

| Step | Duty |
|------|------|
| 5 | Respond to scene and **assume command** |
| 6 | Assess if involved members have sustained injuries |
| 6a | Direct **seriously injured** members to nearest **trauma center** |
| 6b | Direct members with minor injuries (tinnitus, etc.) to nearest hospital after conferring with CO/duty captain |
| 7 | **Establish crime scene** — all involved vehicles must remain in original positions |
| 8 | Inspect and **secure firearms** of involved MOS for evidence of recent discharge |
| 9 | **Secure body-worn cameras** from all MOS on scene |
| 10 | Notify desk officer |
| 11 | Request immediate response of precinct detective squad |
| 12 | Perform duties of "immediate supervisor" per P.G. 221-03 |

### Desk Officer Notifications (Without Waiting for Details)

| Notification |
|-------------|
| Precinct CO/XO |
| Operations Unit |
| Patrol Borough Command |
| Internal Affairs Bureau Command Center |
| Precinct detective squad |

> **Exam Alert:** If a UMOS discharges a firearm **outside of New York City**, the Operations Unit must be notified within **6 hours** of the discharge. The member must also notify local police authorities immediately.

### Chain of Response

| Who | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Patrol Borough Command | Notify duty captain and duty inspector |
| Operations Unit | Notify **duty chief** and **Force Investigation Division (FID)** |
| CO/XO/Duty Captain | Respond to scene, confer with ranking supervisors, assume command |
| Detective Squad | Respond, assume command of investigation, transition to FID upon arrival |
| FID Supervisor | Respond, confer with detective squad supervisor, **assume command of investigation** |

### FID Investigation Responsibilities

- Complete T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT and INTERACTION REPORTS
- Ensure PD324-140 (Firearms Discharge Report) completed within **48 hours**
- Notify appropriate prosecutorial agency (DA's Office, State AG)
- Confer with prosecutorial agency **before** interviewing involved UMOS
- Supply discharging member with **temporary replacement firearm**
- Transport discharged firearm to Firearms Analysis Section

### Temporary Assignment After Firearms Discharge Causing Death/Injury

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Assigned to | Patrol borough office of assignment (or counterpart) |
| Duration | Minimum **three consecutive scheduled tours** (exclusive of sick time or RDOs) |
| Return to permanent command | Requires prior approval of **First Deputy Commissioner** |

### Post-Incident Member Welfare

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| After initial investigation | CO/XO inquires about member's well-being; offers Counseling Services, Family Assistance, EAU, POPPA |
| Within **24-48 hours** | Follow-up assessment; observe post-trauma reaction; repeat offer of services |
| After 3 working days | Bureau Chief prepares report recommending continuance or discontinuance of temporary assignment |

### CO, FID — Final Report

- Addressed to First Deputy Commissioner
- Includes findings: within/outside guidelines, tactics consistent with guidelines
- Findings categories: Intentional discharge (violation/no violation), Unintentional discharge (violation/no violation)
- 15 copy distribution list (PC, CoD, Chief of Staff, etc.)

### Key Rule — Body Identification

If person is **killed** as direct result of police action, the involved UMOS will **NOT** be assigned to identify the body at the morgue.

> **Exam Alert:** After firearms discharge causing death/injury, member is temporarily assigned for minimum **3 consecutive tours**. Return requires **First Deputy Commissioner** approval.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisor's priorities at firearms discharge: (1) call ambulance/render aid, (2) assume command, (3) establish crime scene, (4) inspect/secure firearms, (5) secure BWC, (6) notify desk officer, (7) request detective squad.

## P.G. 221-05: Person Dies or Sustains Serious Injury and Is Likely to Die in Police Custody or in Connection with Police Action

**Date Effective:** 05/03/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 24 | **Pages:** 5

### Purpose

To establish command responsibility and notification requirements when a person dies or is seriously injured and likely to die in/immediately prior to police custody.

### Definitions

**Police In-Custody Death** — Death/likely to die while in police custody:
- Police force or restraint may be a factor
- Suicide (e.g., in holding cell)
- Accidental (drug overdose, reaction to medicine)
- Apparent natural causes (heart failure, asthma)

**Police Pre-Custody Death** — Death/likely to die immediately prior to or during apprehension:
- Police use of force/restraint (firearms discharge, etc.)
- Suicide (barricaded subject)
- Accidental (falls from building, killed eluding police)
- Apparent natural causes

### Force Investigation Division Responsibility

FID is responsible for **ALL investigations** when a person dies or is likely to die in police custody or immediately prior to custody.

### Notification to Next of Kin

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Made by | Member in rank of **lieutenant or above** |
| Assisted by | Community Affairs Bureau, if appropriate |
| Investigator accompanies | Unless deemed inappropriate by PBC and CO, FID |
| Coordination | Confer with CO, FID **before** contacting next of kin |
| Timing | Within a **reasonable amount of time** |
| Reported to | Office of First Deputy Commissioner and Chief of Department |

### NYS Correction Law Section 47(2)

Report required to NYS Commission of Correction when death occurs while prisoner is:
- Lodged in detention or holding cell
- Being transported to courthouse after being in detention/holding cell
- At hospital after being in detention/holding cell

Report (**SCOC M187**) — faxed within **6 hours** of death; mailed within **10 days**.

> **Exam Alert:** Notification to next of kin must be made by a member in the rank of **lieutenant or above**. FID must confer with before contacting.

## P.G. 221-23: Alcohol Testing for UMOS Involved in Firearms Discharges Resulting in Injury, Death, or Likelihood of Injury or Death

**Date Effective:** 02/16/22 | **Last Revision:** I.O. 13 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To ensure integrity at the scene of police-involved firearms discharges resulting in reasonable possibility of injury or death.

### Procedure

| Step | Who | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| 4 | Duty Captain/Inspector | Inform discharging member(s) they will be subject to **alcohol testing** |
| 5 | | Ensure involved member(s) remain on scene pending FID |
| 10 | FID Zone Commander | Direct alcohol test using **PBT device** in **private setting** |
| 10a | | Test conducted by FID supervisor at least **one rank higher** than discharging member |

### Test Results

| PBT Reading | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Less than **.08** and no indicia of intoxication | **No further testing** required |
| **.08 or greater** and/or indicia of intoxication | Notify Highway IDTU; transport to IDTU facility for **Intoxilyzer** test |

### If Unfit for Duty

- Notify IAB Command Center and obtain log number
- Be guided by A.G. 318-10 (Removal of Firearms from Intoxicated UMOS)

### Key Rules

- Prudent not to ingest alcohol up to **4 hours** before tour
- Undercover members: no more than **2 alcoholic beverages** during tour (absent exigent circumstances)
- Entire Intoxilyzer testing process must be **videotaped**
- Highway District supervisor must be present during all testing phases

> **Exam Alert:** Alcohol testing uses **PBT device** in private setting. Threshold is **.08** — if at or above, Intoxilyzer test at IDTU facility required.
