# Section 221 — Force Guidelines and Use of Force

## P.G. 221-01: Force Guidelines

**Date Effective:** 07/10/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 60 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To provide members of the service with force/restraint and firearm prohibitions.

### Scope

Excessive force will NOT be tolerated. MOS who use excessive force will be subject to Department discipline, up to and including **dismissal**.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **De-Escalation** | Taking action to stabilize a situation and reduce the immediacy of the threat so that more time, options, and/or resources become available; goal is voluntary compliance |
| **Objectively Reasonable Standard** | Reasonableness based on totality of circumstances known by MOS at the time; examined from perspective of a member with similar training and experience |
| **Excessive Force** | Force greater than what a reasonable officer, in the same situation, would use under the circumstances |
| **Prohibited Methods of Restraint** | Sitting, kneeling, or standing on chest or back compressing diaphragm, reducing ability to breathe |
| **Chokehold** | Any pressure to throat, carotid artery, or windpipe which may prevent or hinder breathing or reduce intake of air or blood flow |
| **Mass Casualty Vehicle Ramming Attack** | Subject deliberately rams motor vehicle into crowd or building |

### Firearm Prohibitions — SHALL NOT:

| Prohibition |
|-------------|
| Discharge when doing so will unnecessarily endanger innocent persons |
| Discharge in defense of **property** |
| Discharge to subdue fleeing felon who presents **no threat** of imminent death/serious physical injury |
| Fire **warning shots** |
| Discharge to summon assistance (except emergency when personal safety endangered and no other means) |
| Discharge at or from a **moving vehicle** unless deadly physical force is being used by means **other than** a moving vehicle |
| Discharge at a dog/animal except to protect from **imminent physical injury** and no opportunity to retreat |
| **Cock** a firearm — must fire **double action** at all times |

### Mass Casualty Vehicle Ramming Attack Exception

If a vehicle is being used in a mass casualty ramming attack, UMOS may utilize tactics **otherwise prohibited** within P.G. 221-15 (Vehicle Pursuits) to stop the vehicle if deadly physical force or death is imminent.

### Restraint Prohibitions — SHALL NOT:

| Prohibition |
|-------------|
| Use a prohibited method of restraint |
| Use a **chokehold** |
| Use any level of force to **punish, retaliate, or coerce** statements |
| Use force on **handcuffed/restrained** subjects unless necessary to prevent injury, escape, or overcome active physical resistance or assault |
| Connect or tie rear-cuffed hands to cuffed/restrained ankles or legs |
| Transport a subject **facedown** |
| Use force to prevent a subject from **swallowing** alleged controlled substance, or forcibly attempt to remove substance from mouth or body cavity |

### CEW on Rear-Cuffed Prisoner

A CEW should **never** be used in CARTRIDGE or DRIVE STUN mode on a **rear-cuffed prisoner**.

### Use of Force Review Board

Members subject to investigation for violation of prohibitions may request review. The Board may find that under **exigent or exceptional circumstances**, the prohibited action was justified.

> **Exam Alert:** Firearms must be fired **double action** at all times — cocking is prohibited. Members **SHALL NOT** discharge at or from a **moving vehicle** unless deadly force is used by means OTHER than the moving vehicle.

> **Exam Alert:** **Chokeholds** are prohibited. Prohibited methods of restraint (compressing diaphragm) are prohibited. Force to **punish, retaliate, or coerce** is prohibited.

> **Sergeant Focus:** When a subject ingests narcotics, transport **DIRECTLY** to nearest hospital — do NOT use force to prevent swallowing or attempt to remove from mouth.

## P.G. 221-02: Use of Force

**Date Effective:** 06/26/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 53 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To provide guidelines for MOS regarding the use of force and ensure proper reporting and documentation.

### Additional Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Active Resisting** | Physically evasive movements to defeat control (bracing, tensing, pushing, or verbally signaling intent to resist) |
| **Active Aggression** | Threat or overt act of assault coupled with present ability, reasonably indicating imminent assault or injury |
| **Passive Resistance** | Minimal physical action to prevent lawful duty (standing motionless, going limp) |
| **Resisting Arrest (Penal Law)** | Intentionally preventing or attempting to prevent authorized arrest |

### MOS Procedure When Force Is Necessary

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Take action to protect life and personal safety of **all persons**, including subjects |
| 2 | Utilize **de-escalation** techniques when appropriate and consistent with personal safety |
| 3 | Isolate and contain the subject, if appropriate |
| 4 | **Immediately request a supervisor** to respond |
| 5 | Request additional members, as necessary |
| 6 | Request ESU, if appropriate |
| 7 | Comply with P.G. 221-13 if subject appears to be EDP |

### Supervisor On Scene

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 8 | **Assume command** and coordinate de-escalation |
| 9 | Direct tactics to **minimize possibility of injury** to MOS, subject, or bystanders |

### If De-Escalation Fails (MOS/Supervisor)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 10a | Advise offender they will be charged with resisting arrest |
| 10b | Devise a **tactical plan** with members present to minimize injury |
| 10c | Advise offender that physical force or devices will be used before applying, if appropriate |
| 11 | Apply **no more than reasonable force** necessary to gain control |
| 12 | **Assess situation continually** and adjust force as necessary |

### Prohibited Uses on Passive Resistance

Members should **NOT** use O.C. Pepper Spray, CEW, or impact weapons on persons who are **passively resisting**.

### Duty to Intervene

All MOS **must**, when in a position to do so, **intervene to stop** another MOS or members of other law enforcement agencies from using **excessive force**. Failure to intervene or report excessive force is **serious misconduct** that may result in criminal/civil liability and Department discipline up to **dismissal**.

### Post-Force Requirements

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 13 | Rear-cuff subject (may front-cuff initially, then transition) |
| 14 | Position subject to promote **free breathing** (sit up or turn on side) |
| 15 | Observe closely for injuries, illness, or difficulty breathing |
| 16 | Inquire if subject requires medical attention; document response |
| 17-18 | Ensure immediate medical attention if difficulty breathing or life-threatening symptoms |
| 20 | Notify immediate supervisor of force type, reason, and injuries |
| 21 | Document use of force in digital Activity Log |
| 22 | Complete VEHICLE REPORT if force resulted from vehicle/bike stop |

> **Exam Alert:** MOS must **intervene** to stop excessive force — failure is **serious misconduct** and may result in **dismissal**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisor must **assume command**, coordinate de-escalation, and direct tactics to **minimize injury**. Must consider whether non-compliance is deliberate resistance or an inability to comply (medical condition, mental impairment, language barrier, etc.).
