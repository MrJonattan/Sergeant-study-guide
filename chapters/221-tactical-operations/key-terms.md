# Section 221 — Key Terms and Definitions

## Force Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **De-Escalation** | Taking action to stabilize situation, reduce immediacy of threat; gain voluntary compliance |
| **Objectively Reasonable Standard** | Reasonableness based on totality of circumstances; perspective of similarly trained/experienced member |
| **Excessive Force** | Force greater than what a reasonable officer would use under same circumstances |
| **Active Resisting** | Physically evasive movements (bracing, tensing, pushing, verbal intent to resist) |
| **Active Aggression** | Threat or overt act of assault with present ability, indicating imminent assault/injury |
| **Passive Resistance** | Minimal physical action (standing motionless, going limp) |
| **Prohibited Methods of Restraint** | Sitting, kneeling, standing on chest/back compressing diaphragm |
| **Chokehold** | Any pressure to throat, carotid artery, or windpipe affecting breathing or blood flow |

## Force Incident Levels

| Level | Key Criteria | Investigating Entity |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Level 1** | Hand/foot strikes, take-downs, grappling, O.C. spray, mesh blanket, CEW **cartridge mode**, physical injury from police action | Immediate Supervisor |
| **Level 2** | Striking with object (baton, vehicle), canine bite, CEW **drive stun**, prohibited acts (excl. chokehold), alleged excessive force, attempted prisoner suicide, unconsciousness/lost tooth/stitches | CO/XO/Duty Captain + IAB |
| **Level 3** | Force readily capable of causing death/serious injury (not firearm), chokehold/prohibited restraint, excessive force + serious injury, attempted prisoner suicide + serious injury | Internal Affairs Bureau |
| **Level 4** | ALL firearms discharges, UMOS firearm fired by another, subject seriously injured/likely to die/dies in/prior to custody | Force Investigation Division |

## Immediate Supervisor Definition

Available supervisor NOT involved in the incident, from same command, at least **one rank higher** than all involved members. If none available, **duty captain** determines.

## Injury Classifications

| Term | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Physical Illness/Injury** | Minor swelling, contusions, lacerations, abrasions |
| **Serious Physical Injury/Illness** | Broken/fractured bones, aneurysm, hospital admission required, heart attack, stroke, life-threatening |

## Less Lethal Weapons

| Weapon | Key Parameters |
|--------|---------------|
| **O.C. Pepper Spray** | Two 1-second bursts, minimum 3 feet, aim at eyes; NOT on passive resistance |
| **CEW (TASER 7)** | Cartridge mode = Level 1; Drive stun = Level 2; standard cycle 5 seconds; never on rear-cuffed; support side holster |
| **Tear Gas** | Borough commander approval; ESU only; dry cleaning does NOT remove residue |
| **Distraction Device** | Chief of Department approval; ESU only |

## CEW Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Function Test** | Mandatory test into firearms safety station in test mode under supervisor observation |
| **Laser Warning** | De-escalation — placing laser dot on subject for voluntary compliance |
| **Warning Arc** | Visible/audible arcing of electricity on TASER 7 |
| **Cartridge Mode** | Probes fired for neuromuscular incapacitation (Level 1) |
| **Drive Stun** | Direct/close contact with front electrodes (Level 2) |

## Special Weapons

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Special Weapons** | Shotguns, long guns, automatic weapons |
| **Special Weapons Record** | Three-section log: Qualification, Issuance, Maintenance |

## EDP Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **EDP** | Person apparently mentally ill/temporarily deranged, likely to result in serious injury to self or others |
| **Zone of Safety** | Distance from EDP > effective range of weapon (not firearm); minimum **20 feet** |
| **Crossfire** | Unintentional placement of person in position to be injured by firearms discharge |
| **Crossfire Awareness** | Being aware of threat/surroundings; communicate "crossfire" when aware |
| **CIT** | Crisis Intervention Team — specially trained members as contact officers |

## "Resisting Recidivist"

Prisoner arrested for charges against MOS (resisting arrest, assault 2nd, assault on PO, aggravated assault on PO) **more than once within 5 years**. Requires detective squad enhancement.

## Firearm Prohibitions Summary

| SHALL NOT |
|-----------|
| Discharge to endanger innocent persons |
| Discharge in defense of property |
| Discharge at fleeing felon with no imminent threat |
| Fire warning shots |
| Discharge at/from moving vehicle (unless deadly force by other means) |
| Discharge at animal (unless imminent injury, no retreat) |
| Cock a firearm (double action only) |

## Critical Timeframes

| Item | Timeframe |
|------|-----------|
| PD324-140 (Firearms Discharge Report) | Within **48 hours** |
| Temporary assignment after discharge causing death/injury | Minimum **3 consecutive tours** |
| Post-incident member welfare follow-up | **24-48 hours** |
| Report to NYS Commission of Correction (SCOC M187) | Fax within **6 hours**; mail within **10 days** |
| CEW replacement cartridge | Within **72 hours** |
| CEW unintentional discharge retraining | Within **5 business days** |
| O.C. pepper spray effects | Do not dissipate for **15-30 minutes** |
| Vehicle checkpoint maximum duration | **2 hours** at one location |
| CEW standard cycle | **5 seconds** |
| CEW exposure risk threshold | Beyond **15 seconds** |
| Alcohol — prudent abstinence before tour | **4 hours** |
| Undercover alcohol limit | **2 beverages** per tour |

## Approval Authorities

| Action | Authority Required |
|--------|-------------------|
| Tear gas use | **Patrol Borough Commander** (or designee) |
| Distraction device use | **Chief of Department** (or designee) |
| Return to permanent command after firearms discharge | **First Deputy Commissioner** |
| Non-standard vehicle checkpoint | **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters** (written) |
| Cancel ESU response at EDP | **Sergeant or above** |
| Canine use (non-authorized tactical) | **Borough Commander** |
| Canine at non-peaceful demonstration | **Chief of Patrol** |

## Key Forms

| Form | Used For |
|------|---------|
| **T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT** | One per force incident — regardless of number of MOS/subjects |
| **T.R.I. INTERACTION REPORT** | Each involved MOS completes for every reportable force/injury |
| **PD324-140** (UMOS Report of Firearms Discharge) | All firearms discharges — within 48 hours |
| **SCOC M187** (Report of Inmate Death) | Death in custody — NYS Commission of Correction |
| **PD469-150** (Supervisor's Fitness for Duty Report) | Alcohol testing documentation |
| **VEHICLE CHECKPOINT FORM** | Document checkpoint operations |
| **Special Weapons Record** | Log for issuance/qualification/maintenance of special weapons |

## NYPD Duty to Intervene

All MOS **must** intervene to stop excessive force by MOS or other law enforcement. Failure to intervene or report = **serious misconduct** → criminal/civil liability + Department discipline up to **dismissal**.
