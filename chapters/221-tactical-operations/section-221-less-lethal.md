# Section 221 — Less Lethal Weapons

## P.G. 221-07: Use of Oleoresin Capsicum Pepper Spray Devices

**Date Effective:** 04/19/24 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 23 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To inform UMOS of circumstances for O.C. pepper spray use and to record instances of discharge.

### Scope

O.C. pepper spray constitutes **physical force** under NYS Penal Law. May be used to gain/maintain control of persons who are **actively resisting** or exhibiting **active aggression**, or to prevent physical injury.

### Authorized Uses

| Situation |
|-----------|
| Gain/maintain control of persons **actively resisting** arrest or exhibiting **active aggression** |
| Prevent individuals from physically injuring themselves, MOS, or others |
| Establish physical control of subject attempting to **flee** |
| Establish physical control of an **EDP** |
| Control a **dangerous animal** |

### Application Method

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Position | Hold **upright**, aim at subject's **eyes** |
| Burst pattern | **Two, one-second bursts** |
| Minimum distance | **Three feet** |

### Prohibitions

| SHALL NOT Use On |
|-----------------|
| Persons who **passively resist** |
| If possible, avoid: frail health, young children, women believed pregnant, known respiratory conditions |
| Avoid: indiscriminately over a large area for disorder control (unless specifically trained) |
| Avoid: small contained areas (automobiles, closets) |

### Post-Use Requirements

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 3 | Request **EMS** once situation is under control |
| 4 | Remove subject from contaminated area, expose to fresh air |
| 4a | Determine if subject wearing **contact lenses** (recommend removal ASAP) |
| 5 | Position subject on side or sitting — **never facedown** |
| 6 | Flush contaminated skin with **profuse amounts of water** |
| 7 | Transport to ER if difficulty breathing, severe stress, hyperventilation |
| 7a | Keep **windows of transport vehicle open** |
| 7c | Advise hospital staff that O.C. spray was used |

### Canister Capacity

| Type | Bursts | Replace When |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Stream | ~10 one-second bursts | Half empty (after 5 bursts) |
| Gel | ~8 one-second bursts | Half empty (after 4 bursts) |

### Carrying Requirements

| Duty Status | Carry Location |
|-------------|---------------|
| Uniform | Holster on **non-shooting side** of gun belt |
| Civilian clothes | Holster attached to belt or appropriate manner |
| Undercover | May opt **not** to carry |
| Off duty | May carry (optional) |

### Important Decontamination Notes

- Effects do **not dissipate** for **15-30 minutes**
- Do NOT rub or touch contaminated skin
- Do NOT use salves, creams, ointments, commercial eyewashes, or bandages

> **Exam Alert:** O.C. pepper spray: **two one-second bursts** at minimum **three feet** distance. **Do NOT** use on **passively resisting** subjects.

## P.G. 221-08: Use of Conducted Electrical Weapons (CEW)

**Date Effective:** 12/02/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 124 | **Pages:** 10

### Purpose

To inform UMOS of CEW use circumstances and provide control, accountability, issuance, and investigation procedures.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CEW** | Hand-held less lethal device delivering electrical charge through probes or direct contact |
| **Function Test** | Mandatory test into firearms safety station under supervisor observation; must use **test mode** |
| **Activate** | Arm CEW by releasing safety — laser/LED illuminators turn on |
| **Laser Warning** | De-escalation technique — placing laser dot on subject to gain compliance |
| **Warning Arc** | Activating and discharging TASER 7 to display visible/audible arcing of electricity |
| **Cartridge Mode** | Probes fired at subject for neuromuscular incapacitation — **Level 1 force** |
| **Drive Stun** | Front electrodes in direct contact with subject's body — **Level 2 force** |

### CEW Issuance Priority

| Priority | Assignment |
|----------|-----------|
| 1 | One operable CEW maintained at **desk** |
| 2 | One to each **supervisor** performing patrol duty |
| 3 | One to authorized UMOS in each **RMP** |
| 4 | One to authorized UMOS per **foot post** |
| 5 | Remaining to second authorized UMOS in RMP |

### Carrying Requirements

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| Holster on **support side** (opposite firearm) |
| **Cross-drawing** is prohibited |
| ESU/canine handlers carry in manner approved by CO, Firearms and Tactics |

### Point of Aim

| Target Area | Direction |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Lower center mass** (below chest) | Frontal discharges |
| **Below neck area** | Discharges at back |
| Avoid: head, neck, chest | If possible |
| **Never** aim at groin | |
| **Preferred**: discharge at subject's back | When practical |

### CEW Prohibitions

| SHALL NOT |
|-----------|
| Use on persons who **passively resist** |
| Use as **coercion or punishment** |
| Use on **rear-cuffed prisoner** (cartridge or drive stun) |
| Generally not on children, elderly, obviously pregnant, frail |
| Generally not against subjects on **moving device/vehicle** (fall risk) |
| Generally not where subject may fall from **elevated surface** |
| Not when **combustible gases or flammable liquids** present |
| **Two CEWs simultaneously** on one subject — prohibited |
| **Fleeing** should NOT be **sole** justification |

### Cycle Duration

| Guideline |
|-----------|
| Standard cycle: **5 seconds** — constantly assess |
| Exposure beyond **15 seconds** (multiple applications or continuous) may increase risk of **death or serious injury** |
| All applications must be **independently justifiable** |

### Post-Use Requirements

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Request **EMS** | Anyone who received CEW discharge |
| Medical facility | Anyone struck by probe or had drive stun used on them **must be examined** |
| Probes in skin | Only removed by **medical personnel** |
| Break wire | By hand, ~4-6 inches from probe |
| Spent cartridge | Safeguard and invoice (PROPERTY CLERK INVOICE) |
| Battery | Place in charging dock |
| Notify | Firearms and Tactics Section — obtain **FTS CEW Log Number** |

### Command Accountability

| Duty | Responsible Party |
|------|------------------|
| Account for all CEWs/cartridges each tour | Desk officer/unit supervisor |
| Maintain list of CEW-trained members at desk | Command Training Sergeant |
| Monthly inspection of equipment | Command Operations Coordinator |
| Replacement cartridges within **72 hours** | CO |
| Unintentional discharge retraining within **5 business days** | Command Training Sergeant |

> **Exam Alert:** CEW cartridge mode = **Level 1** force. CEW drive stun = **Level 2** force. Standard cycle = **5 seconds**. Beyond **15 seconds** increases risk of death.

> **Exam Alert:** **All supervisors performing patrol** are to carry a CEW if available. CEW holster on **support side**, opposite firearm. Cross-drawing **prohibited**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure CEWs are accounted for at start of every tour with Command Log entry including serial numbers. Ensure function test is performed into firearms safety station under your observation.

## P.G. 221-09: Use of Tear Gas

**Date Effective:** 06/01/16 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To provide for the use of tear gas.

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Decision authority | **Patrol borough commander** or designee |
| Technical decisions | Ranking member of **Emergency Service Unit** at scene |
| Who may use | Trained **ESU members** only |

### Operational Plan Requirements

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| Establish designated mobilization point |
| Station non-engaged personnel at safe distance |
| Evacuate civilians from affected areas |
| Request: Fire Department company, EMS ambulance, EMS borough supervisor, HazMat Decontamination Trailer, Patrol Wagon |

### Decontamination

- Remove persons to open, **upwind** position
- Eyes open, facing the wind
- Flush face, eyes, contaminated skin with **copious cool water**
- Contaminated clothing: thoroughly aired and washed with soap and water
- **Dry cleaning will NOT remove** tear gas residue

> **Exam Alert:** Tear gas use requires approval of **patrol borough commander** (or designee). Technical decisions made by ranking **ESU** member. Dry cleaning does NOT remove tear gas.
