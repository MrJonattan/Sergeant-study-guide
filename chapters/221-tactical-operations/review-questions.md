# Section 221 — Review Questions

## Multiple Choice

**1. Which of the following is a firearm prohibition under P.G. 221-01?**

- A) Discharging a firearm at a fleeing felon who presents an imminent threat
- B) Firing warning shots
- C) Discharging a firearm to protect from imminent physical injury
- D) Discharging a firearm in self-defense

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Firing warning shots**

Warning shots are prohibited. Members SHALL NOT fire warning shots under any circumstances. (P.G. 221-01)

</details>

---

**2. A CEW discharge in cartridge mode is classified as what level force incident?**

- A) Level 1
- B) Level 2
- C) Level 3
- D) Level 4

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**A) Level 1**

CEW discharge in cartridge mode is a Level 1 force incident. CEW in drive stun mode is Level 2. (P.G. 221-03)

</details>

---

**3. Who investigates Level 3 force incidents?**

- A) Immediate Supervisor
- B) Commanding Officer/Executive Officer
- C) Internal Affairs Bureau
- D) Force Investigation Division

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Internal Affairs Bureau**

Level 3 force incidents (force readily capable of causing death/serious injury, chokehold, excessive force with serious injury) are investigated by the Internal Affairs Bureau. (P.G. 221-03)

</details>

---

**4. The immediate supervisor in a force incident must be:**

- A) Any available supervisor from the same command
- B) A supervisor not involved in the incident, at least one rank higher than all involved members
- C) The desk officer of the precinct of occurrence
- D) The duty captain in all cases

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A supervisor not involved in the incident, at least one rank higher than all involved members**

The immediate supervisor must be available, NOT involved in the incident, from the same command, and at least one rank higher than all involved members. (P.G. 221-03)

</details>

---

**5. After a firearms discharge causing death or injury, the involved UMOS is temporarily assigned for a minimum of:**

- A) One scheduled tour
- B) Three consecutive scheduled tours
- C) Five consecutive scheduled tours
- D) Seven calendar days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Three consecutive scheduled tours**

The involved member is temporarily assigned to the patrol borough office for a minimum of three consecutive scheduled tours (exclusive of sick time or RDOs). (P.G. 221-04)

</details>

---

**6. O.C. pepper spray should be applied using:**

- A) One continuous burst at two feet
- B) Two, one-second bursts at a minimum distance of three feet
- C) Three short bursts at five feet
- D) One long burst at arm's length

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Two, one-second bursts at a minimum distance of three feet**

O.C. pepper spray is held upright, aimed at the subject's eyes, using two one-second bursts at a minimum distance of three feet. (P.G. 221-07)

</details>

**7. What is the recommended minimum "zone of safety" distance from an EDP?**

- A) 10 feet
- B) 15 feet
- C) 20 feet
- D) 25 feet

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 20 feet**

A minimum distance of 20 feet is recommended for the zone of safety. The distance should be greater than the effective range of the weapon (other than a firearm). (P.G. 221-13)

</details>

The PD324-140 must be completed within 48 hours of the incident. (P.G. 221-04)

**9. Who has the authority to approve the use of tear gas?**

- A) Precinct commanding officer
- B) Patrol borough commander or designee
- C) Chief of Department
- D) Emergency Service Unit supervisor

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Patrol borough commander or designee**

The ultimate decision concerning use of tear gas rests with the patrol borough commander or designee. Technical decisions are made by the ranking ESU member. (P.G. 221-09)

</details>

**10. Members of the service have a duty to intervene to stop excessive force. Failure to intervene may result in:**

- A) A verbal reprimand
- B) Command discipline only
- C) Criminal and civil liability and Department discipline up to dismissal
- D) Transfer to another command

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Criminal and civil liability and Department discipline up to dismissal**

Failure to intervene in or report excessive force is serious misconduct that may result in criminal and civil liability and Department discipline up to and including dismissal. (P.G. 221-02)

</details>

**11. A "Resisting Recidivist" is defined as a prisoner arrested for charges against MOS:**

- A) Three or more times in ten years
- B) More than once within five years
- C) Twice within one year
- D) Any repeat offense regardless of timeframe

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) More than once within five years**

A Resisting Recidivist is a prisoner arrested for resisting arrest, assault on PO, or similar charges more than once within five years (i.e., the second time in five years). The detective squad must enhance the arrest. (P.G. 221-06)

</details>

**12. The use of distraction devices requires approval from:**

- A) Patrol borough commander
- B) Duty inspector
- C) Chief of Department or designee
- D) Chief of Patrol

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Chief of Department or designee**

Distraction devices require direct knowledge and specific approval of the Chief of Department (or designee), absent exigent circumstances. (P.G. 221-11)

</details>

**13. Who may apply a spit hood to an EDP?**

- A) Any uniformed member of the service
- B) The patrol supervisor
- C) Only Emergency Service Unit personnel
- D) EMS personnel only

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Only Emergency Service Unit personnel**

Only ESU personnel may apply a spit hood. Only ESU personnel or a medical professional with minimum EMT certification can remove it. (P.G. 221-13)

</details>

**14. CEWs should be carried in a holster on which side?**

- A) Shooting side, same as firearm
- B) Support side, opposite the firearm
- C) Either side at the member's preference
- D) Ankle holster

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Support side, opposite the firearm**

The CEW holster must be attached to the duty belt on the support side, opposite the firearm. Cross-drawing is prohibited. (P.G. 221-08)

</details>

**15. An EDP must NEVER be transported to:**

- A) The nearest hospital
- B) A police facility
- C) A psychiatric ward
- D) An ambulance

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A police facility**

Under no circumstances will an EDP be transported to a police facility. EDPs are removed to a hospital via ambulance (or RMP if ambulance unavailable, at supervisor's direction). (P.G. 221-13)

</details>

**16. At the scene of a firearms discharge, the PBT alcohol test threshold is:**

- A) .04
- B) .05
- C) .08
- D) .10

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) .08**

If the PBT reading is less than .08 and no indicia of intoxication, no further testing is required. At .08 or greater, an Intoxilyzer test at the IDTU facility is required. (P.G. 221-23)

</details>

**17. Which of the following is NOT an authorized use of O.C. pepper spray?**

- A) Gaining control of a person actively resisting arrest
- B) Preventing an individual from injuring themselves
- C) Controlling a subject who is passively resisting
- D) Controlling a dangerous animal

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Controlling a subject who is passively resisting**

O.C. pepper spray, CEW, and impact weapons should NOT be used on persons who are passively resisting. (P.G. 221-07)

</details>

**19. Department canine teams shall NOT be used for:**

- A) Tracking missing persons

**20. When a UMOS discharges a firearm outside of New York City, the Operations Unit must be notified within:**

- A) 1 hour
- B) 6 hours
- C) 12 hours
- D) 24 hours

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 6 hours**

If a firearm is discharged outside of NYC, the member must notify local police authorities immediately and the Operations Unit within 6 hours of discharge. (P.G. 221-04)

</details>

**21. A CEW standard cycle is how many seconds?**

- A) 3 seconds
- B) 5 seconds
- C) 10 seconds
- D) 15 seconds

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 5 seconds**

Members should use a CEW for one standard cycle of five seconds while constantly assessing. Exposure beyond 15 seconds may increase the risk of death or serious injury. (P.G. 221-08)

</details>

**23. Members SHALL NOT discharge firearms at or from a moving vehicle unless:**

- A) The vehicle is being used in a pursuit
- B) The vehicle is stolen
- C) Deadly physical force is being used by means other than a moving vehicle
- D) The vehicle is approaching a checkpoint

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Deadly physical force is being used by means other than a moving vehicle**

Members SHALL NOT discharge firearms at or from a moving vehicle unless deadly physical force is being used against MOS or another person by means OTHER than a moving vehicle. (P.G. 221-01)

</details>

**24. How many T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORTS are prepared per force incident?**

- A) One per involved member
- B) One per subject
- C) Only ONE per incident, regardless of number of MOS or subjects
- D) One per force level

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Only ONE per incident, regardless of number of MOS or subjects**

Only one T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT will be prepared for an incident, regardless of the number of MOS involved or number of subjects/civilians/bystanders involved. (P.G. 221-03)

</details>

**25. For Level 3 and Level 4 force incidents, the immediate supervisor should handle body-worn camera footage by:**

- A) Reviewing all videos and documenting findings
- B) Uploading footage to FORMS T.R.I. application
- C) Safeguarding cameras and NOT viewing — await IAB/FID direction
- D) Forwarding footage to the commanding officer

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Safeguarding cameras and NOT viewing — await IAB/FID direction**

Body-worn camera footage concerning Level 3 and Level 4 force incidents will NOT be viewed by the immediate supervisor. Immediate supervisors will safeguard the cameras and await direction from IAB or FID. (P.G. 221-03)

</details>
