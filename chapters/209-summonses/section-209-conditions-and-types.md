# Section 209 — Conditions of Service and Summons Types

Covers P.G. 209-01, 209-02, 209-03, 209-04

---

## P.G. 209-01: Conditions of Service

### When a Summons Replaces Arrest

A member of the service is authorized to issue a summons or notice of violation **INSTEAD OF** making an arrest when the violator meets **ALL** of the following conditions:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Age** | Violator must be **16 years of age or older** |
| **Capacity** | Violator must be **able to care for self** (not incapacitated by illness, injury, or intoxication) |
| **Offense type** | Must fall within an eligible statute (see below) |

### Discretion for CJRA Violations

For any of the 6 CJRA offenses, a member may judiciously **warn and admonish** the violator instead of issuing a summons, OR issue a summons/arrest for a corresponding crime.

### Eligible Statutes for Summons Issuance

| Statute | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Agriculture & Markets Law | All applicable violations |
| General Business Law | All applicable violations |
| Labor Law | All applicable violations |
| Multiple Dwelling Law | All applicable violations |
| Vehicle and Traffic Law (VTL) | **With exceptions** — see below |
| NYC Administrative Code | All applicable violations |
| NYC Health Code | All applicable violations |
| Penal Law **violations** | **With exceptions** — see below |

### VTL Exceptions — CANNOT Issue Summons, MUST Arrest

| VTL Section | Offense |
|-------------|---------|
| **Section 600** | Leaving the Scene of an Accident |
| **Section 380** | Dangerous Articles (Transportation) |
| **Sections 392 / 392-a** | False Statements |
| **Sections 421 / 422 / 423 / 426** | Stolen Vehicle offenses |
| **Section 1192** | DWI / DWAI |

> **Exam Alert:** The five VTL exception categories are heavily tested. Remember the mnemonic: **L-D-F-S-D** (Leaving scene, Dangerous articles, False statements, Stolen vehicle, DWI). These offenses ALWAYS require arrest, never a summons.

### Penal Law Violation Exceptions — CANNOT Issue Summons

| PL Section | Offense |
|------------|---------|
| **PL 100** | Criminal Solicitation |
| **PL 215.58** | Failure to Respond to Appearance Ticket |
| **PL 240.40** | Public Under Influence of Narcotics or a Drug |

### 16-Year-Olds — Special Rules

| Summons Type | Eligibility |
|--------------|-------------|
| Criminal Court summonses for **ALL violations** | **Yes** — eligible |
| Criminal Court summonses for **VTL misdemeanors** | **Yes** — eligible |
| Criminal Court summonses for **non-VTL misdemeanors** | **No** — not eligible |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must verify that officers correctly distinguish between violations (summons-eligible for 16-year-olds) and non-VTL misdemeanors (not summons-eligible for 16-year-olds). This is a common error in the field.

## P.G. 209-02: Summonses and Notices of Violation Returnable Agencies

> **REVISED:** Effective 03/27/26, R.O. 26. Updated title and expanded agency routing to include bicyclists, e-bike riders, and e-scooter riders under TVB. Added garages and parking lots routing. Clarified "all other summonsable offenses" catch-all.

### Agency Routing Matrix

| Offenses Processed | Returnable To |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **Stopping, Standing and Parking offenses** | NYC Department of Finance, Parking Violations Operations |
| **Traffic infractions other than Stopping, Standing and Parking** (including traffic infractions committed by bicyclists, e-bike riders, and e-scooter riders) | NYS Department of Motor Vehicles, Traffic Violations Bureau (TVB) |
| **Pedestrian offenses and traffic misdemeanors** | NYC Criminal Court |
| **Garages and parking lots** | NYC Criminal Court |
| **All other summonsable offenses**, except violations of OATH and TAB | NYC Criminal Court |
| **Food Vendor Regulations, General Vendor Regulations, Health Code Violations, Public Health Law (Canine Waste), Noise Code Provisions, Sanitation Provisions, Air Code Provisions, Graffiti Law Violations** | Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings (OATH) |

> **Exam Alert:** The 03/27/26 revision now explicitly lists bicyclists, e-bike riders, and e-scooter riders under TVB for traffic infractions. Garages and parking lots are separately listed as returnable to Criminal Court. Know that NYC Criminal Court has concurrent jurisdiction over most VTL traffic infractions (except parking, standing, stopping, and civil-penalty-only offenses).

> **Exam Alert:** For OATH summonses, refer to COMMON OFFENSES RETURNABLE TO THE OFFICE OF ADMINISTRATIVE TRIALS AND HEARINGS (OATH) (PD160-013).

## P.G. 209-03: Civil Summons Returnable to OATH — Criminal Justice Reform Act (CJRA)

> **REVISED:** Effective 06/26/25, R.O. 53. Now 5 pages. Updated title. Expanded valid forms of ID to include NYS Driver Permit and other government photo ID. Added VEHICLE REPORT requirement for all vehicle/bicycle/e-scooter/e-bike stops. Added Messenger role for SUMMONS ENVELOPE RECEIPT delivery. Clarified desk officer I-Card processing (PG 208-23). Clarified that Code 4 takes precedence when combined with other codes.

### Criminal Justice Reform Act of 2016

The CJRA allows certain violations to be issued as **civil summonses** returnable to OATH instead of criminal summonses returnable to Criminal Court.

### The 6 CJRA Violations

| # | Violation | Administrative Code Section |
|---|-----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Open Container** (alcohol) | AC 10-125(b) |
| 2 | **Public Urination** | AC 16-118(6) |
| 3 | **Littering** | AC 16-118(1)(a) |
| 4 | **Spitting** | AC 16-118(1)(b) |
| 5 | **Excessive Noise** (used when not removing a sound reproduction device) | AC 24-218 |
| 6 | **NYC Park Rules** (New York City Park Rules) | Title 56 of the Rules of the City of New York |

> **Exam Alert:** Memorize all 6 CJRA violations. A common exam trick is listing a violation NOT on this list (e.g., disorderly conduct) and asking if it qualifies for CJRA civil summons. Also note: CJRA does NOT apply in the transit system — use TAB (Transit Adjudication Bureau) instead.

> **Exam Alert:** For all 6 CJRA violations, do NOT use the Health Code — use the **NYC Administrative Code**. The exam may present a Health Code section for public urination or littering; the correct source of law is always the Administrative Code for these offenses.

> **Exam Alert:** A Civil Summons Returnable to OATH will **not** be written in the transit system when issuing summonses under the New York City Transit Rules and Regulations.

### Definitions (from revised 209-03)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CRIMINAL JUSTICE REFORM ACT OF 2016** | A series of laws passed by the NYC Council that allows certain violations of the NYC Administrative Code and NYC Park Rules to be eligible for a Civil Summons Returnable to OATH |
| **OATH** | An administrative law court responsible for adjudicating civil summonses |
| **OATH RECIDIVIST** | A person NOT eligible for a Civil Summons Returnable to OATH (see criteria below) |

### OATH Recidivist — NOT Eligible for Civil Summons

A violator is classified as an **OATH Recidivist** and must receive a Criminal Court summons (not OATH civil) if ANY of the following apply:

| # | Condition |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **2 or more felony arrests** in the past **2 years** |
| 2 | **3 or more unanswered Civil Summonses Returnable to OATH** in the past **8 years** |
| 3 | Currently on **parole or probation** |

> **Exam Alert:** The OATH Recidivist criteria are a top exam target. Remember: **2 felonies / 2 years, 3 OATH / 8 years, parole/probation.** Any one condition disqualifies the person from receiving a civil summons.

### Procedure — UMOS Issuing Civil Summons Returnable to OATH

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Inform violator of offense committed (unless physical resistance, flight, or other factors render impractical) |
| 2 | Request violator to show proof of identity and residence |
| 3 | Establish violator's identity and mailing address through observation of valid ID documents |
| 4 | Request name check through Communications Section and/or mobile device |
| 5 | Issue summonses in numerical order per P.G. 209-36 |
| 6 | Use black or blue ink ballpoint pen; block letters; legibly |
| 7 | Enter all available information required by captions on summons, including return date from D.O./counterpart at roll call |
| 8 | Record only **one violation per summons** — separate summons for each additional offense |
| 9 | Enter **"RTR"** and circle at end of details section if violator recorded police activities |
| 10 | When practical, advise violator that instructions and OATH hearing center location are on rear of summons |
| 11 | Request patrol supervisor to respond if legitimate law enforcement reason exists for not issuing OATH |
| 13 | After patrol supervisor verification for Code 3, issue Criminal Court summons per P.G. 209-09 and check "NYPD CODE 3"; complete **VEHICLE REPORT** in FORMS for every vehicle/bicycle/e-scooter/e-bike stop |

### Name Check Results — Actions

| Name Check Result | Action |
|-------------------|--------|
| **Active warrant or I-Card** labeled "Perpetrator - Probable Cause to Arrest" | Remove violator to command |
| **OATH Recidivist** identified | Issue Criminal Court summons; check **NYPD CODE 1** |
| **Criminal Contempt** — violator committed act on MTA/NYC Transit property in violation of court order banning entry | Remove to command; charge Criminal Contempt 2nd Degree as additional charge |

### When to Issue a "C" Summons Instead of an OATH

If a member issues a Criminal Court ("C") summons instead of an OATH for one of the 6 CJRA violations, they must note the **Reason Code** in the appropriate space on the summons.

> **NOTE:** If a violator is stopped for multiple offenses and at least one is a non-CJRA violation answerable in Criminal Court, the summonsing officer will document ALL violations on summonses returnable to Criminal Court. Check **"NYPD CODE 4"** on all summonses. If Code 4 and any other code(s) are applicable, select Code 4.

### NYPD Codes for CJRA Processing

| Code | Meaning | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Code 1** | OATH Recidivist | Person meets recidivist criteria — issue Criminal Court summons |
| **Code 2** | Active warrant or Probable Cause I-card | Person is being returned to issuing court to adjudicate warrant and summons, or being arrested on a separate charge |
| **Code 3** | Law enforcement exception | Officer articulated a legitimate law enforcement reason to issue Criminal Court summons in lieu of OATH for a CJRA violation. Requires **patrol supervisor verification** |
| **Code 4** | Non-CJRA violation or multiple violations | Violation cited is not covered under CJRA, or subject violated multiple rules in which at least one is returnable to Criminal Court (all summonses returnable to Criminal Court). **If Code 4 and any other code(s) are applicable, select Code 4** |

> **Sergeant Focus:** When a Criminal Court summons is issued under **Code 3** (law enforcement exception), a **patrol supervisor MUST respond to the scene** to verify the exception. This is a mandatory supervisory response — failure to respond is a procedural violation. Ensure officer details reason in digital Activity Log.

> **Prior Test:** A UMOS writes a "C" summons instead of an OATH for a CJRA violation. A patrol supervisor must respond — true or false? **WRONG — the supervisor must respond ONLY for Code 3** (law enforcement exception). If the officer used Code 1, 2, or 4, no supervisor response is required. The exam tests whether you know the supervisor response is specific to Code 3, not to every "C" summons.

> **Exam Alert:** PG 209-03 Code 2 covers two scenarios: (1) **Active warrant** — UMOS writes the summons, D.O. directs "an" officer (does not have to be the arresting officer) to return to issuing court; if court is closed, process as online arrest. (2) **I-Card (Probable Cause to Arrest)** — per P.G. 208-23, release violator to responding detective after issuance of summons returnable to Criminal Court; ensure summonsing officer checks "NYPD CODE 2."

> **See Also:** PG 208-23 — Computerized Investigation Card System procedures.

### Generally Accepted Forms of Identification

| # | ID Type |
|---|---------|
| 1 | Valid Photo Driver License |
| 2 | Valid passport |
| 3 | Citizenship or naturalization papers |
| 4 | NYS Non-Driver Identification |
| 5 | NYS Driver Permit |

### CJRA Processing — Desk Officer Role

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 14 | **Question** UMOS regarding the circumstances of the incident and make **Command Log entry** |
| — | Entry includes: (1) Name, address, physical description of violator; (2) Location, time of incident, reason for removal to command; (3) Name, rank, shield # of member who brought violator; (4) Offense(s) charged and serial number(s) of summons(es) served; (5) Time violator entered and departed the command |
| 15 | If violator has **active arrest warrant**: direct arresting officer to issue summons returnable to Criminal Court; direct an officer to return violator to issuing court; ensure "NYPD CODE 2" checked; summonsing officer does not have to be the escorting officer; if court not in session, process as online arrest |
| 16 | Comply with P.G. 208-23 if an active **INVESTIGATION CARD** labeled "Perpetrator - Probable Cause to Arrest" exists: release violator to responding detective after issuance of summons returnable to Criminal Court (unless processed as online arrest); ensure "NYPD CODE 2" checked |
| 17 | If violator has no proper ID, make **every effort** to identify (third party contact, Department computers/devices, digital booking photos, DAS, Mobile ID Device); if violator cannot reasonably be identified, process as an **online arrest** |

> **Exam Alert:** If the violator cannot be identified after every effort (third party contact, Dept computer, booking photo, DAS, Mobile ID Device), process as an **online arrest**.

### CJRA Processing — Operations Coordinator

- (Step 18) Designate an MOS to enter information from Civil Summons Returnable to OATH into the **FORMS** application
- Ensure designated MOS reviews the **"Details of Charge(s)"** section of summons and enters required information into FORMS

### CJRA Processing — Command Clerk

| Step | Task | Details |
|------|------|---------|
| 19 | **Separate and process** | Separate Civil Summons Returnable to OATH by date of service |
| 19a | **WHITE (OATH) copies** | Insert all copies issued on SAME day into OATH envelope addressed to OATH, 66 John Street, 10th Floor, New York, N.Y. 10038; forward to patrol borough office with A.M. mail |
| 19b | **WHITE CARDBOARD (Agency) copy** | File in command by date of service and retain thereat for **two years** |

> **Exam Alert:** The White Cardboard (Agency) copy is retained at the command for **2 years** — not 1 year, not 6 months. This retention period applies to both 209-03 and 209-12 OATH summonses.

### CJRA Processing — Patrol Borough Office Clerk

| Step | Task |
|------|------|
| 20 | Prepare two copies of **SUMMONS ENVELOPE RECEIPT (PD160-011)** |
| 21 | Direct messenger to deliver envelope containing Civil Summons Returnable to OATH to the Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings |

### CJRA Processing — Messenger

| Step | Task |
|------|------|

### Additional Data

- The **desk officer** will make the final determination as to whether the violator's identity, residence, and active warrant and INVESTIGATION CARD history have been ascertained and verified
- Nothing in this procedure precludes officers from judiciously **warning and admonishing** individuals for violations of NYC Administrative Codes, the Rules of the City of New York, or NYC Park Rules or summonsing/arresting violators when a corresponding crime exists

### Code 4 — Multiple Offenses Key Note

> **Exam Alert:** When a traffic incident under PG 209-09 involves multiple offenses, **each separate summons goes to its own court** (TVB, "C" Criminal Court, etc.). If Code 4 applies and one or more is a non-CJRA violation returnable to Criminal Court, **all summonses are returnable to Criminal Court**. If Code 4 and any other code(s) are applicable, select Code 4. Select Code 4 when issuing "C" for Transit Rules (PG 209-39).

> **See Also:** PG 209-09 — Traffic incident procedures where multiple summonses each route to their respective court.

## P.G. 209-04: Return Date and Time

### Return Date Matrix

| Summons Type | Return Date/Time |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Criminal Court** | Per daily **FINEST message** |
| **Traffic Violations Bureau (TVB)** | Per instructions **printed on the summons** |
| **Transit Adjudication Bureau (TAB)** | **28 calendar days** from date of issuance |
| **Parking Violations** | **No date/time entered** by officer — information is pre-printed on the summons |

### TAB Return Date — Special Rules

- If the 28th day falls on a **weekend or holiday**, the return date is the **next business day**
- Count begins from the **date of issuance**, not the date of service

> **Exam Alert:** The 28-day TAB return date is frequently tested. Remember: 28 calendar days, and if it falls on a weekend/holiday, roll forward to the next business day.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **VTL exceptions to summons issuance** — Expect questions listing offenses and asking which require arrest vs. allow summons
2. **OATH Recidivist criteria** — Expect questions presenting a scenario and asking if the person qualifies for CJRA civil summons
3. **NYPD Code 3** — Supervisor verification requirement is a favorite Sergeant exam topic
4. **16-year-old summons eligibility** — Expect questions testing the VTL misdemeanor exception
5. **Returnable agency routing** — Parking vs. traffic infraction vs. pedestrian offense routing
6. **TAB 28-day return date** — Calculation questions including weekend/holiday adjustments

## Sergeant Study Notes

- The **desk officer** is the final authority on identity/residence verification for CJRA processing
- A **patrol supervisor must physically respond** when Code 3 (law enforcement exception) is used — the supervisor must **respond and verify**, and the UMOS details the reason in their Digital Activity Log
- For Code 3, the circumstances must be **limited** and the UMOS must **articulate the reason**
- Supervisors should verify that officers check for OATH Recidivist status BEFORE issuing a civil summons
- The CJRA does NOT apply in the transit system — supervisors must ensure officers use TAB procedures instead
- When a violator cannot care for themselves (illness, injury, intoxication), a summons is NOT appropriate regardless of the offense — the person must be processed differently
