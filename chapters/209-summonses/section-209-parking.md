# Section 209 — Parking Summonses and Related Procedures

Covers P.G. 209-07, 209-08, 209-27

---

## P.G. 209-07: Parking Summonses

### Abandoned and Derelict Vehicle Definitions

| Classification | Definition |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Abandoned Vehicle** | (1) No plates displayed for more than **6 hours**; OR (2) On public street for more than **7 consecutive days**; OR (3) On another person's property for more than **96 hours** |
| **Derelict Vehicle** | Dilapidated, burned, stripped vehicle with **no value except as scrap** |

> **Exam Alert:** Know all three abandoned vehicle timeframes: **6 hours** (no plates), **7 days** (public street), **96 hours** (another's property). Do NOT issue parking summonses to abandoned or derelict vehicles — they require different processing.

### 5 Mandatory Identification Elements

Omission of ANY of these elements results in **DISMISSAL** of the summons:

| # | Element |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **License plate number** |
| 2 | **Plate type** |
| 3 | **Expiration date** |
| 4 | **Make** (manufacturer) |
| 5 | **Model** |

> **Exam Alert:** The 5 mandatory ID elements are one of the most frequently tested topics in Section 209. Omission of even ONE element = automatic dismissal. Remember: **Number, Type, Expiration, Make, Model**.

### Summons Limits and Restrictions

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **One offense per summons** | Each summons covers only one parking violation |
| **Maximum 3 summonses** | No more than **3 summonses** to the same vehicle at the same location per day |
| **Already has 3+** | Do NOT issue additional summonses if vehicle already has **3 or more summonses displayed** on windshield |

### Out-of-State Plates — Plate Cover Violations

- Do NOT ordinarily issue summonses for local plate cover violations (VTL 402) to vehicles with **out-of-state plates**
- **Exception:** Issue summons if the vehicle's **home state also prohibits** plate covers

### Stolen Vehicle Check

Query **NYSPIN** to check if the vehicle is stolen when:
- A prior summons is already on the vehicle
- Damage is consistent with theft
- The vehicle is in an unusual location or observed at an unusual time

### Service of Parking Summons

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Place service copy on **windshield** |
| 2 | Include **orange self-addressed envelope** |
| 3 | In **inclement weather**, fold the summons to protect it |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors should periodically review parking summons issuance to verify all 5 mandatory elements are present. A pattern of dismissals due to missing elements indicates a training deficiency.

## P.G. 209-27: Removal of Ignition Key

### When to Act

- Vehicle is **unattended** with the **key in the ignition** for more than **3 minutes**

### Procedure

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Lock the ignition** and **remove the key** |
| 2 | Prepare **REMOVAL OF IGNITION KEY** form (PD571-121) — **2 copies** |
| 3 | **Attach one copy** to the steering wheel |
| 4 | **Attach the other copy** to the key |

### Disposition

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Violator returns** | Issue summons, return key, **destroy** the form |
| **Not claimed within 48 hours** | Desk officer **invoices key to Property Clerk** |

> **Exam Alert:** The 48-hour window before Property Clerk invoicing and the 3-minute threshold for unattended vehicles are commonly tested timeframes.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer is responsible for tracking unclaimed keys and ensuring timely invoicing to the Property Clerk after 48 hours. Supervisors should include ignition key removals in their review of daily activity.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **Abandoned vehicle timeframes** — 6 hours (no plates), 7 days (public street), 96 hours (another's property)
2. **5 mandatory ID elements** — Any omission = dismissal
3. **3-summons maximum** — Same vehicle, same location, per day
4. **Ignition key timeframes** — 3 minutes unattended, 48 hours to Property Clerk
5. **Out-of-state plate covers** — Generally do not issue unless home state prohibits
6. **NYSPIN check triggers** — Prior summons, theft-consistent damage, unusual location/time
7. **Derelict vs. abandoned** — Know the distinction; neither gets a parking summons

## Sergeant Study Notes

- Train officers on the 5 mandatory elements — dismissals waste enforcement effort
- Review the 3-summons limit to prevent excessive issuance to the same vehicle
- Ensure officers query NYSPIN when indicators of vehicle theft are present
- Monitor ignition key removal logs to verify the 48-hour invoicing deadline
- Verify officers are not issuing parking summonses to abandoned or derelict vehicles
