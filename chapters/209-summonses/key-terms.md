# Section 209 — Key Terms, Forms, and Abbreviations

---

## Core Summons Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Summons** | A written directive ordering a person to appear before a court or administrative tribunal at a specified date and time to answer for a violation or offense | P.G. 209-01 |
| **Civil Summons (OATH)** | A summons issued under the CJRA for one of 6 specified violations, returnable to OATH instead of Criminal Court | P.G. 209-03 |
| **Personal Service** | Direct hand-delivery of a summons to the violator, with verbal notification of the offense charged | P.G. 209-09 |

## CJRA and OATH Terms

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **CJRA (Criminal Justice Reform Act of 2016)** | Legislation allowing 6 specified violations to be processed as civil summonses returnable to OATH rather than criminal summonses returnable to Criminal Court | P.G. 209-03 |
| **OATH (Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings)** | NYC administrative tribunal that adjudicates civil violations including CJRA offenses, vendor violations, health code violations, noise complaints, and environmental offenses | P.G. 209-03, 209-12 |
| **OATH Recidivist** | A person ineligible for CJRA civil summons due to: (1) 2+ felony arrests in past 2 years, (2) 3+ unanswered OATH summonses in past 8 years, OR (3) currently on parole or probation | P.G. 209-03 |
| **ECB (Environmental Control Board)** | Now part of OATH; adjudicates environmental, building, and zoning violations | P.G. 209-20 |

## Vehicle Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Abandoned Vehicle** | A vehicle with: (1) no plates for 6+ hours, (2) on public street for 7+ consecutive days, OR (3) on another's property for 96+ hours | P.G. 209-07 |
| **Derelict Vehicle** | A vehicle that is dilapidated, burned, or stripped, with no value except as scrap metal | P.G. 209-07 |

## Department Vehicle Summons Terms

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Urgent Police Action** | Response to an **emergency**, active **pursuit**, or moving **surveillance** | P.G. 209-30 |
| **Necessary Police Action** | Less than urgent; **enhances safety** of an operation; NOT solely for the member's **convenience** | P.G. 209-30 |
| **Valid Verifiable Defense** | A legitimate, verifiable defense to a summons; safety violations are NOT eligible unless urgent or necessary police action occurred | P.G. 209-30 |
| **Safety Violation** | Specific parking violations (double parking, fire hydrant, sidewalk, bus stop, crosswalk, obstruction of traffic, no standing, snow emergency, no parking-authorized vehicles, no stopping, no parking-taxi stand) requiring urgent/necessary police action defense | P.G. 209-30 |

## License-Related Terms

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Suspended License** | A driver's license that has been temporarily withdrawn by DMV; operator is prohibited from driving | P.G. 209-26 |
| **Revoked License** | A driver's license that has been permanently withdrawn by DMV; operator must reapply | P.G. 209-26 |
| **14-Day Correction Window** | Period within which a violator may claim suspension/revocation was corrected; accept validated license, temporary license, or DMV receipt | P.G. 209-26 |
| **False Personation** | PL 190.23; providing a false identity, particularly relevant during suspended/revoked license stops | P.G. 209-26 |

## DAT vs. Summons Distinction

| Feature | DAT (Desk Appearance Ticket) | Summons |
|---------|------------------------------|---------|
| **Issued after** | Custodial arrest and processing | Field encounter (no arrest) |
| **Requires arrest** | Yes — person is arrested, processed, then released with DAT | No — issued in lieu of arrest |
| **Fingerprinted** | Yes | No |
| **Eligible offenses** | Class E felonies, misdemeanors, violations | Violations, infractions, certain misdemeanors |
| **Reference** | P.G. 208-27 | P.G. 209-01 |
