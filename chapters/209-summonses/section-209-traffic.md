# Section 209 — Traffic Summonses and Personal Service

Covers P.G. 209-09, 209-10, 209-11, 209-15, 209-16, 209-17, 209-18, 209-19

---

## P.G. 209-09: Personal Service of Summonses (TVB/Criminal Court)

### Required Steps for Summons Issuance

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Inform the violator** of the offense committed (unless resistance or flight makes this impractical) |
| 2 | **Request proof of identity and residence** |
| 3 | If **no valid ID** → remove to command for identification |
| 4 | If violator has an **active warrant** → process warrant arrest |
| 5 | **One violation per summons** — issue separate summons for each offense |

### Vehicle Report Requirement

- Complete a **VEHICLE REPORT** in FORMS for **every** vehicle stop (car, e-bike, e-scooter, motor scooter, bicycle, etc.)
- This applies **whether enforcement action is taken or not**

> **Exam Alert:** The Vehicle Report requirement applies to ALL vehicle/bicycle/e-scooter/e-bike stops — not just those resulting in a summons. "RTR" notation is required when the violator exercises their right to record.

### Recording by Violator

- If the violator **records police activities** during the stop, note **"RTR"** (Right to Record) on the summons
- **Circle** at the end of the details section

> **Exam Alert:** "RTR" stands for "Right to Record" — it must be entered on the summons AND circled at the end of the details section. This applies to both traffic stops (209-09) and OATH encounters (209-03, 209-12). Per I.O. 2s.21, if the violator has recorded police activity, enter "RTR."

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must verify that Vehicle Reports are completed for every stop. Missing reports indicate either unreported stops or incomplete documentation — both require corrective action.

## P.G. 209-15: Transit Adjudication Bureau (TAB) Summonses

### TAB Jurisdiction

- Covers violations occurring within the **transit system**
- CJRA civil summonses do **NOT** apply in the transit system — use TAB instead

### Return Date

- **28 calendar days** from date of issuance
- If the 28th day falls on a **weekend or holiday** → next business day

> **Exam Alert:** Remember that CJRA/OATH civil summonses are NOT used in the transit system. Transit violations go through TAB with the 28-day return date.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **Personal service requirements** — Inform of offense, request ID, one violation per summons
2. **Vehicle Report** — Required for ALL vehicle/bicycle/e-scooter/e-bike stops, not just summons issuance
3. **RTR notation** — Required when violator records police activities
4. **No valid ID** — Remove to command; do NOT release without identification
5. **Active warrant during stop** — Process the warrant arrest
6. **TAB vs. CJRA** — Transit system uses TAB, not CJRA/OATH civil summons
7. **28-day TAB return** — Calendar days, weekend/holiday rolls to next business day

## Sergeant Study Notes

- Audit Vehicle Report completion rates to ensure all stops are documented
- Verify officers understand that RTR notation is required, not optional
- When reviewing summons activity, check that each summons contains only one violation
- Ensure officers know the difference between TVB-returnable and Criminal Court-returnable offenses
- Monitor that officers remove violators to the command when no valid ID is presented, rather than releasing them
