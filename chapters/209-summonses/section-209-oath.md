# Section 209 — OATH and Environmental Control Board Procedures

Covers P.G. 209-12, 209-20

---

## P.G. 209-12: OATH Summonses (Non-CJRA)

### OATH Jurisdiction

The Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings (OATH) handles civil enforcement of various city code violations that do not rise to criminal court level. These are **separate from** the 6 CJRA violations covered in P.G. 209-03.

### Non-CJRA OATH Offenses

| Offense Category | Examples |
|------------------|----------|
| **Food/General Vendor violations** | Unlicensed vending, improper vending location |
| **Health Code violations** | Sanitary code infractions |
| **Public Health Law — Canine Waste** | Failure to clean up after a dog |
| **Noise Code violations** | Commercial or construction noise violations |
| **Sanitation violations** | Improper waste disposal, recycling violations |
| **Air Code violations** | Emission and air quality infractions |
| **Graffiti violations** | Possession of graffiti instruments, vandalism |

### OATH vs. CJRA Distinction

| Feature | CJRA Civil Summons (P.G. 209-03) | Non-CJRA OATH Summons (P.G. 209-12) |
|---------|----------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Violations** | 6 specific CJRA violations only | Vendor, health, noise, sanitation, air, graffiti |
| **Recidivist check** | Required (OATH Recidivist criteria) | Not applicable |
| **NYPD Codes** | Codes 1-4 apply | Not applicable |
| **Transit system** | NOT applicable (use TAB) | NOT applicable in transit |
| **Returnable to** | OATH | OATH |

> **Exam Alert:** Do not confuse CJRA civil summonses with non-CJRA OATH summonses. Both are returnable to OATH, but CJRA summonses require recidivist screening and NYPD Code assignment. Non-CJRA OATH summonses do not.

### Processing Requirements

- Proper identification of the violator
- Accurate recording of the offense and applicable code section
- Correct OATH return information
- Service of summons copy to the violator

## P.G. 209-20: Environmental Control Board (ECB) Procedures

Covers enforcement procedures for violations heard by the Environmental Control Board (now part of OATH), including:
- Environmental violations within NYC
- Building code violations
- Zoning violations
- Coordination with city inspection agencies
- Proper ECB summons completion

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors should ensure officers correctly route OATH-eligible offenses to OATH rather than Criminal Court. Misrouting creates unnecessary court appearances and administrative burden.

---

## Common Exam Angles

1. **CJRA vs. non-CJRA OATH** — Both go to OATH, but CJRA requires recidivist screening
2. **OATH offense categories** — Know which violations are OATH-returnable
3. **Routing errors** — Questions may present a scenario where an officer incorrectly routes an OATH violation to Criminal Court
4. **Transit system exclusion** — OATH summonses (both CJRA and non-CJRA) do NOT apply in the transit system

## Sergeant Study Notes

- Train officers on the distinction between CJRA and non-CJRA OATH violations
- Review summons routing to catch misrouted violations
- Ensure officers serving OATH summonses include accurate code sections and offense descriptions
- Verify that non-CJRA OATH summonses are not being subjected to unnecessary recidivist screening
