# Section 209 — Review Questions

### Question 1
**An officer stops a 16-year-old who committed a non-VTL misdemeanor. Can the officer issue a Criminal Court summons?**

A. Yes, 16-year-olds may receive summonses for all offenses
B. Yes, but only with supervisor approval
C. No, 16-year-olds may receive Criminal Court summonses for violations and VTL misdemeanors only
D. No, 16-year-olds are never eligible for summonses

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-01, 16-year-olds may receive Criminal Court summonses for ALL violations and for VTL misdemeanors. Non-VTL misdemeanors are NOT eligible for summons issuance to 16-year-olds.
</details>

### Question 2
**Which of the following VTL offenses may be handled by summons rather than arrest?**

A. VTL 1192 — Driving While Intoxicated
B. VTL 600 — Leaving the Scene of an Accident
C. VTL 511 — Aggravated Unlicensed Operation
D. VTL 392 — False Statements regarding vehicle registration

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-01, VTL 511 (Aggravated Unlicensed Operation) is NOT among the VTL exceptions. The five VTL exception categories requiring arrest are: Section 600 (Leaving Scene), Section 380 (Dangerous Articles), Sections 392/392-a (False Statements), Sections 421/422/423/426 (Stolen Vehicle), and Section 1192 (DWI/DWAI).
</details>

### Question 3
**A person is stopped for public urination and is found to have 2 felony arrests in the past 18 months and 1 unanswered OATH summons. What type of summons should be issued?**

A. OATH civil summons — the person does not meet recidivist criteria
B. Criminal Court summons — the person is an OATH Recidivist
C. No summons — the person must be arrested
D. TAB summons — public urination falls under transit jurisdiction

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-03, an OATH Recidivist is defined as someone with 2 or more felony arrests in the past 2 years, 3 or more unanswered OATH summonses in the past 8 years, OR currently on parole/probation. This person has 2 felony arrests in 18 months (within 2 years), meeting the first criterion. A Criminal Court summons must be issued instead of a CJRA civil summons.
</details>

### Question 4
**An officer issues a Criminal Court summons under NYPD Code 3 (law enforcement exception) for an open container violation. What supervisory action is required?**

A. No supervisory action is required for Code 3
B. The desk officer must approve the Code 3 designation
C. A patrol supervisor must respond to the scene to verify the exception
D. The commanding officer must be notified within 24 hours

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-03, when a Criminal Court summons is issued under Code 3 (law enforcement exception), a patrol supervisor MUST respond to the scene to verify the exception. This is a mandatory supervisory response.
</details>

### Question 5
**An officer is preparing a parking summons and records the license plate number, plate type, make, and model, but forgets to include the expiration date. What is the likely outcome?**

A. The summons will be processed normally with a minor notation
B. The summons will be DISMISSED due to the missing mandatory element
C. The summons will be returned for correction within 30 days
D. The court will obtain the expiration date from DMV records

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-07, there are 5 mandatory identification elements on a parking summons: license plate number, plate type, expiration date, make, and model. Omission of ANY one of these elements results in automatic DISMISSAL of the summons.
</details>

### Question 6
**A vehicle has been parked on a public street without moving for 8 consecutive days. Under the Patrol Guide, this vehicle is classified as:**

A. A derelict vehicle
B. An abandoned vehicle
C. A nuisance vehicle requiring towing
D. A legally parked vehicle unless in a restricted zone

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-07, a vehicle on a public street for more than 7 consecutive days is classified as an abandoned vehicle. A derelict vehicle is one that is dilapidated, burned, or stripped with no value except as scrap. Parking summonses should NOT be issued to abandoned or derelict vehicles.
</details>

### Question 7
**Where are pedestrian violation summonses returnable?**

A. NYS DMV Traffic Violations Bureau
B. NYC Department of Finance
C. NYC Criminal Court
D. Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings (OATH)

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-02, pedestrian offenses are returnable to NYC Criminal Court. The TVB handles non-parking traffic infractions. The Department of Finance handles parking violations. OATH handles CJRA civil summonses and other administrative violations.
</details>

### Question 8
**What is the return date for a summons issued through the Transit Adjudication Bureau (TAB)?**

A. 14 calendar days from date of issuance
B. 21 calendar days from date of issuance
C. 28 calendar days from date of issuance, with weekend/holiday adjustment
D. Per the daily FINEST message

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-04, TAB summonses have a return date of 28 calendar days from the date of issuance. If the 28th day falls on a weekend or holiday, the return date moves to the next business day. Criminal Court summons dates are set per the daily FINEST message.
</details>

### Question 9
**During a car stop, the officer discovers the driver's license has been suspended. The driver claims the suspension was corrected 10 days ago and shows a DMV receipt. What should the officer do?**

A. Confiscate the license and issue a summons — only a validated license is acceptable
B. Accept the DMV receipt as proof of correction within the 14-day window
C. Radio dispatch to verify the DMV receipt before accepting it
D. Arrest the driver for operating with a suspended license

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-26, if a violator claims the suspension/revocation was corrected within the past 14 days, officers should accept a newly validated license, a temporary license, OR a DMV receipt showing correction. A DMV receipt within 10 days falls within the 14-day window.
</details>

### Question 10
**An officer removes the ignition key from an unattended vehicle. The owner does not return. After what period does the desk officer invoice the key to the Property Clerk?**

A. 24 hours
B. 48 hours
C. 72 hours
D. 7 days

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-27, if the ignition key is not claimed within 48 hours, the desk officer invoices it to the Property Clerk. The ignition key is removed only after the vehicle has been unattended with the key in the ignition for more than 3 minutes.
</details>

### Question 11
**A member parked the department vehicle at a fire hydrant while responding to an emergency call. A parking summons was issued. What type of defense applies?**

A. Valid Verifiable Defense — fire hydrant is not a safety violation
B. No defense — department vehicles are not exempt from parking rules
C. Urgent Police Action — response to an emergency qualifies
D. Necessary Police Action — the parking enhanced safety of the operation

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-30, response to an emergency constitutes Urgent Police Action (along with active pursuit and moving surveillance). Fire hydrant parking is a safety violation, which requires proof of urgent or necessary police action. Since the member was responding to an emergency, Urgent Police Action applies.
</details>

### Question 12
**A member parks on the sidewalk to pick up lunch from a restaurant. A summons is issued. Is "Necessary Police Action" a valid defense?**

A. Yes, because the member was in uniform and on duty
B. Yes, because sidewalk parking is not classified as a safety violation
C. No, Necessary Police Action specifically excludes actions taken solely for the member's convenience
D. No, only Urgent Police Action can be used for sidewalk violations

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-30, Necessary Police Action is defined as less than urgent but enhancing safety of an operation — and specifically excludes actions taken solely for the member's convenience. Picking up lunch is a personal convenience, not a police operation.
</details>

### Question 13
**Which of the following is NOT one of the 6 CJRA violations eligible for OATH civil summons?**

A. Open Container — AC 10-125(b)
B. Disorderly Conduct — PL 240.20
C. Public Urination — AC 16-118(6)
D. Excessive Noise — AC 24-218

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-03, Disorderly Conduct (PL 240.20) is NOT one of the 6 CJRA violations. The 6 CJRA violations are: Open Container, Public Urination, Littering, Spitting, Excessive Noise, and NYC Park Rules (Title 56).
</details>

### Question 14
**A tow truck registered in New Jersey enters NYC to pick up a broken-down vehicle and tow it back to a repair shop in New Jersey. The tow truck does not have an NYC tow truck license. Should the officer issue a summons?**

A. Yes, all tow trucks operating in NYC must be licensed under AC 20-496(b)
B. No, out-of-city tow trucks picking up a vehicle for transport outside the city are exempt
C. Yes, but only a warning summons since it is a first offense
D. No, New Jersey tow trucks are covered under interstate commerce exemptions

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 209-28, the NYC tow truck licensing requirement does NOT apply to out-of-city tow trucks that are: (1) passing through NYC, (2) picking up a vehicle to transport outside the city, or (3) dropping off a vehicle that originated from outside the city. This tow truck from NJ picking up a vehicle to tow back to NJ falls under exemption (2).
</details>

### Question 15
**A person stopped for littering has no valid ID. The officer confirms the violation is CJRA-eligible. What is the correct course of action?**

A. Issue the OATH civil summons using the person's verbal identification
B. Release the person with a warning since no ID is available
C. Remove the person to the command for identification
D. Issue a Criminal Court summons instead, since OATH requires valid ID

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 209-03, if a violator has no valid ID, the officer must remove the person to the command for identification. The desk officer makes the FINAL determination on identity and residence verification. The summons type (OATH civil or Criminal Court) is determined after identity is established.
</details>
