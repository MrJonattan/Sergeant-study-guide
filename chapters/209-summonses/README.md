# Section 209 — Summonses

## Source
- **Patrol Guide Section:** 209
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide1.pdf
- **Priority:** Medium
- **Status:** Completed

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the conditions under which a summons may be issued instead of an arrest (age, capacity, eligible offenses)
- Know the VTL and Penal Law exceptions that require arrest rather than summons issuance
- Identify the correct returnable agency for each summons type (Criminal Court, TVB, OATH, TAB, DOF)
- Master CJRA civil summons eligibility, OATH Recidivist criteria, and NYPD Codes 1-4
- Know the 5 mandatory identification elements for parking summonses and the consequences of omission
- Understand abandoned and derelict vehicle definitions and associated timeframes
- Recognize the Patrol Supervisor's responsibilities for Code 3 verification, voided summons review, and summons accountability
- Know the procedures for suspended/revoked license stops, ignition key removal, and department vehicle summonses
- Distinguish between Urgent Police Action, Necessary Police Action, and convenience for department vehicle summons defenses
- Understand TAB return date calculation (28 days) and Criminal Court return date sourcing (FINEST message)

## Sergeant Focus Areas

### Key Supervisor Procedures
- **P.G. 209-01 — Conditions of Service:** Verify officers check age (16+), capacity (able to care for self), and offense eligibility before issuing summonses; ensure VTL exceptions (Leaving Scene, Dangerous Articles, False Statements, Stolen Vehicle, DWI) result in arrest, not summons
- **P.G. 209-03 — CJRA Civil Summons:** Patrol supervisor MUST respond to verify Code 3 (law enforcement exception); desk officer makes FINAL determination on identity/residence; verify OATH Recidivist screening before civil summons issuance
- **P.G. 209-07 — Parking Summonses:** Monitor 5 mandatory ID elements (plate number, type, expiration, make, model) — omission = dismissal; enforce 3-summons max per vehicle per day; ensure no summonses issued to abandoned/derelict vehicles
- **P.G. 209-09 — Personal Service:** Verify Vehicle Reports completed for ALL vehicle/bicycle/e-scooter/e-bike stops; ensure one violation per summons; RTR notation when violator records activity
- **P.G. 209-24 — License Checks:** Mandatory at traffic collisions, car stops, and investigatory situations; do NOT detain solely for license check; Activity Log entry when DMV system is inoperative
- **P.G. 209-26 — Suspended License:** Verify proper confiscation and PD634-152 preparation; accept 14-day correction proof (validated license, temporary license, DMV receipt); park vehicle legally for registered owner
- **P.G. 209-27 — Ignition Key:** 3-minute threshold for removal; PD571-121 in duplicate; 48-hour deadline for Property Clerk invoicing
- **P.G. 209-30 — Dept Vehicle Summons:** Review Summons Plea Forms within 30 days; scrutinize "necessary police action" claims — convenience is NOT a valid defense; know the safety violation list
- **P.G. 209-31 to 209-34 — Voided Summonses:** Supervisory review required for all voided summonses; monitor voiding patterns for training deficiencies or integrity concerns
