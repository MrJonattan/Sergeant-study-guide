# Section 209 — Specialized Summons Procedures

Covers P.G. 209-21, 209-22, 209-23, 209-24, 209-26, 209-28, 209-30, 209-31, 209-32, 209-33, 209-34, 209-35, 209-37, 209-39, 209-40

## P.G. 209-24: Mandatory Driver's License Checks

### When License Checks Are Mandatory

| Situation | License Check Required? |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Traffic collisions** | Yes — mandatory |
| **Car stops** | Yes — mandatory |
| **Other investigatory situations** involving a motor vehicle operator | Yes — mandatory |

### Method

- Use **RMP tablet** as the primary tool
- If no tablet available, use **department smartphone**
- Do **NOT unnecessarily detain** a person solely for the purpose of conducting a license check if no other cause for detention exists

### DMV System Inoperative

- If the DMV computer system is **inoperative**, make an **Activity Log entry** with the motor vehicle operator's information
- Do NOT delay the stop indefinitely waiting for the system

> **Exam Alert:** License checks are mandatory at traffic collisions, car stops, and investigatory situations — but officers may NOT unnecessarily detain a person solely for a license check when no other cause exists.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors should monitor that officers conduct mandatory license checks at every qualifying stop. The Activity Log entry when DMV is inoperative creates the audit trail.

## P.G. 209-26: Suspended or Revoked License

### Confiscation Procedures

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Confiscate the license** |
| 2 | Prepare **SEIZED DRIVER'S LICENSE RECEIPT/REPORT** (PD634-152) |
| 3 | Do **NOT mark or mutilate** the confiscated license |
| 4 | **Park the violator's vehicle** in a legal area until the registered owner arranges removal |

### 14-Day Correction Claim

If the violator claims the suspension/revocation was **corrected within the past 14 days**, accept any of the following:
- **Newly validated license**
- **Temporary license**
- **DMV receipt** showing correction

### False Personation Warning

- Be alert for **False Personation** (PL 190.23) — a violator may provide a false identity to avoid detection of the suspended/revoked status

> **Exam Alert:** The 14-day correction window and the three acceptable forms of proof (validated license, temporary license, DMV receipt) are commonly tested. Also remember: do NOT mark or mutilate the confiscated license.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Verify that officers park the violator's vehicle in a legal area rather than leaving it in traffic. The vehicle remains until the registered owner (not the violator with the suspended license) arranges removal.

## P.G. 209-28: Unlicensed Tow Truck Operators

### Enforcement

- Issue summons for **AC 20-496(b)** returnable to **Criminal Court**

### Exempt Entities

| Exempt Category |
|----------------|
| Government vehicles |
| Vehicle dismantlers |
| Public transit vehicles |
| Bus companies |
| Utility companies |
| TLC-licensed taxi/livery vehicles |
| School buses |
| Vehicle rental companies |

### Out-of-City Tow Trucks — Exemptions

The tow truck licensing requirement does **NOT** apply to out-of-city tow trucks that are:
- **Passing through** NYC
- **Picking up** a vehicle to transport **outside** the city
- **Dropping off** a vehicle that originated from **outside** the city

> **Exam Alert:** Know the three out-of-city exemptions: passing through, picking up for transport outside, dropping off from outside. A tow truck from New Jersey picking up a vehicle in Manhattan to tow it to New Jersey is exempt.

## P.G. 209-30: Summons on Department Vehicle

### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Urgent Police Action** | Response to an **emergency**, active **pursuit**, or moving **surveillance** |
| **Necessary Police Action** | Less than urgent; **enhances safety** of an operation; **NOT** solely for the member's **convenience** |
| **Valid Verifiable Defense** | A legitimate defense to the summons that can be verified through department records |

### Safety Violations

The following are classified as **safety violations** — they are NOT eligible for dismissal unless the vehicle was engaged in **urgent or necessary police action**:

| Safety Violation |
|-----------------|
| Double parking |
| Fire hydrant |
| Sidewalk |
| Bus stop |
| Crosswalk |
| Obstruction of traffic |
| No standing |
| Snow emergency |
| No parking — authorized vehicles |
| No stopping |
| No parking — taxi stand |

### Processing Timeline

- Forward **SUMMONS PLEA FORM** through channels within **30 days** of issuance

> **Exam Alert:** The distinction between urgent police action (emergency, pursuit, surveillance) and necessary police action (enhances safety, NOT for convenience) is heavily tested. Safety violations require proof of urgent OR necessary police action — convenience alone is NEVER a valid defense.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must review Summons Plea Forms for accuracy and ensure they are forwarded within the 30-day deadline. Verify that officers claiming "necessary police action" can articulate how the action enhanced safety — "convenience" is explicitly excluded.

## P.G. 209-40: Speed Camera Violations

Covers procedures related to automated speed camera enforcement, including:
- School zone speed camera operations
- Camera-generated violation processing
- Owner liability provisions
- Adjudication through the Department of Finance
- Fine structure and penalties

> **Exam Alert:** Both red light camera and speed camera violations are civil penalties assessed against the vehicle **owner**, not the operator. No points are added to the driver's license. These are processed through the Department of Finance, not Criminal Court or TVB.

## Common Exam Angles

1. **Mandatory license checks** — Know the three situations (collision, car stop, investigatory)
2. **Suspended license — 14-day correction** — Three acceptable forms of proof
3. **Department vehicle summons** — Urgent vs. necessary police action vs. convenience
4. **Safety violations list** — Must have urgent or necessary police action to defend
5. **30-day Summons Plea Form deadline** — Forwarded through channels
6. **Tow truck exemptions** — 8 exempt categories plus 3 out-of-city exemptions
7. **Voided summonses** — Supervisor authorization required for all types
8. **Red light/speed camera** — Owner liability, no points, Dept of Finance adjudication
9. **False Personation** — Alert when processing suspended/revoked license stops

## Sergeant Study Notes

- Review officers' mandatory license check compliance at every car stop and collision
- Ensure Summons Plea Forms are submitted within 30 days — late submissions may result in default judgments against MOS
- Monitor voided summons patterns for training deficiencies or integrity concerns
- Verify that officers conducting suspended license stops park the vehicle legally and prepare PD634-152
- When reviewing department vehicle summons defenses, scrutinize "necessary police action" claims — the member must show the action enhanced safety, not just convenience
- Ensure officers understand that camera-generated violations are owner liability, civil penalties with no license points
