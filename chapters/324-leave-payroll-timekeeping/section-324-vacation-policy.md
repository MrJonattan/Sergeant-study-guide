# Section 324 — Leave, Payroll & Timekeeping

## A.G. 324-01: Vacation Policy

**Date Effective:** 10/20/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 104 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To establish guidelines for vacation selection, seniority determination, and carry-over for uniformed members of the service.

### Seniority for Vacation Selection

**Police officers and detectives** — based on date of appointment to the rank of police officer:
- Vacation selections are granted according to **seniority in rank** and by squad assignment
- Members with prior service as **police cadets, TEAs, STSA, SSS, or PCT** (appointed off a promotional exam) have seniority over those with the same appointment date
- **Exam numbers** determine seniority for members with the same appointment date (older exam = higher seniority)
- Same exam, same date → **exam list number** determines seniority

**Supervisors** — vacation selections are based on **seniority in rank**. The number of supervisors eligible for vacation at one time is determined by A.G. 324-04, "Ranking Officers Vacations."

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **12% limit** | No more than 12% of police officers/detectives (precinct, PSA, transit district) may take vacation at the same time |
| **Probationary officers** | Cannot take vacation while in training at the Police Academy; after assignment to permanent command, allowed to exceed 12% limit |
| **Carry-over** | Maximum **15 days** of vacation may be carried into the following year, subject to exigencies of the Department |
| **RDO integration** | RDOs immediately before, within, and immediately following vacation are an integral part of vacation; only scheduled work days may be selected |
| **IVD limits** | Maximum **2%** of uniformed patrol command members may take individual vacation days at one time; only **2 IVDs** per year |
| **Holiday restriction** | May not select more than one of: New Year's Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day as an IVD |
| **IVD carry-over** | Maximum **two IVDs** may be carried into the following year, to be used by the last day of February |

### Procedure

1. Vacation selections for police officers and detectives are based on **date of appointment** to the rank of police officer
2. Vacation selections for **supervisors** are based on seniority in rank
3. **12% limit** on vacation at one time for patrol commands
4. Staff members of uniformed patrol commands and those performing special tours select vacation **separately**
5. **Probationary officers** are not permitted to take vacation while at the Police Academy
6. Vacations for members assigned to **other than uniformed patrol commands** are granted at the discretion of the C.O.
7. Members may carry over a maximum of **15 days** of vacation into the following year
8. Members will apply for **vacation lost due to sick leave** without delay upon return to duty
9. RDOs before/after a member's individual vacation day (IVD) are **not** integral to vacation
10. Members shall start vacation at the **beginning of a set of tours** when possible
11. C.O./designee will prepare annual vacation lists **prior to January 1**
12. Two IVDs must be selected within the calendar year

### Digital Leave Requests

Submit a **Digital Leave of Absence Request** via CPR System for annual vacation, IVDs, prior year's vacation, compensatory time, chart days, special military leave, or commander's days — at least **five calendar days** in advance.

> **Exam Alert:** The 12% limitation, 15-day carry-over maximum, and 2% IVD limit are frequently tested numeric thresholds. Also know that probationary officers cannot take vacation at the Academy but can exceed 12% once assigned to permanent command.

> **Memory Aid — VACATION LIMITS:** **12%** of patrol command, **2%** for IVDs, **2** IVDs max per year, **15** days carry-over, **27** days for sergeant/lieutenant ranking officers.

## A.G. 324-02: Working During Vacation

**Date Effective:** 05/08/23 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 21 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To permit full-duty uniformed members below the rank of captain, able to perform patrol duty in uniform, to work up to five continuous days of their annual vacation at the Department's discretion.

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Request form** | VACATION WORK REQUEST (PD106-161) |
| **Deadline** | At least **6 weeks prior**, but no earlier than **8 weeks prior** to desired dates |
| **Duration** | Must be **continuous days** and **regularly scheduled working days** |
| **Compensation** | Days worked are **deducted from vacation time only** — no other leave type permitted |
| **Approval chain** | C.O. → Bureau Chief/Deputy Commissioner → Office of the Chief of Department |
| **IVD interaction** | If working IVDs, notify command at least **2 weeks prior** to approved dates |

### Procedure

**Member Requesting:**
1. Prepare VACATION WORK REQUEST (PD106-161) — select continuous, regularly scheduled working days
2. Submit to commanding officer at permanent command at least 6 weeks prior (no earlier than 8 weeks)

**Commanding Officer:**
3. Review and endorse within **7 days** of receipt
4. Verify member is full duty with adequate days available
5. Forward approved request to bureau chief/deputy commissioner

**Bureau Chief/Deputy Commissioner:**
6. Review and endorse within **7 days**
7. Forward approved request to Office of the Chief of Department

**Office of the Chief of the Department:**
8. Determine command where duty is to be performed based on needs of the Department
9. Notify commanding officer, permanent command at least **2 weeks prior** to approved dates

**Commanding Officer, Permanent Command:**
10. Ensure assignment is recorded on VACATION WORK REQUEST
11. Forward to originating command

> **Exam Alert:** Working during vacation is an exception, not a right — the Department has discretion. The 6-week advance notice and 7-day review windows are key supervisory responsibilities. Only vacation days are deducted — not sick leave or other accrued time.

> **See Also:** A.G. 324-01 (Vacation Policy), A.G. 324-04 (Ranking Officers Vacations)
