# Section 324 — Bereavement Leave

## A.G. 324-06: Bereavement Leave

**Date Effective:** 12/14/22 | **Last Revision:** I.O. 121 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To grant a member of the service (uniformed or civilian) leave of absence with full pay upon the death of specified family/partners.

### Covered Relationships

| Category | Who Qualifies |
|----------|---------------|
| **Immediate Family** | Spouse, natural/foster/step-parent, child, brother/sister, father-in-law, mother-in-law, or relative residing in household |
| **Domestic Partner** | Two persons 18+, neither married or related by blood (NY State marriage rules), who have a close committed personal relationship, live together continuously, registered as domestic partners, and have not terminated domestic partnership within last 6 months |
| **Covered Relative of Domestic Partner** | Parent or child of domestic partner, or relative of domestic partner residing in household |
| **Same-Sex Committed Partner** | Those financially and emotionally interdependent in manner commonly presumed of spouses |
| **Covered Relative of Same-Sex Partner** | Same as domestic partner covered relatives |

### Additional Note for Civilian Members

Civilian members of the service covered under the citywide collective bargaining agreement are entitled to bereavement leave upon the death of a **grandchild**.

### Procedure

**Member:**
1. Prepare **Digital Leave of Absence Request** via CPR System
2. If unable to make personal application, **timekeeper** will prepare request
3. Complete 'Location During Absence' section with country, city/town, and dates when departing city
4. Submit to commanding officer for approval

### Key Rule

If death occurs while a **civilian member** is on vacation, **vacation is not extended** by bereavement leave.

> **Exam Alert:** Know the difference between immediate family, domestic partner, and same-sex committed partner. These distinctions frequently appear on exams. Also note that civilian members have grandchild coverage under their CBA — and vacation is NOT extended by bereavement leave during an existing vacation.

> **Memory Aid — BEREAVEMENT COVERAGE:** Think **I.D.S.** — **I**mmediate family, **D**omestic partner, **S**ame-sex committed partner. Each has covered relatives that follow the same category rules.

> **See Also:** A.G. 324-05 (Authorized Leave)
