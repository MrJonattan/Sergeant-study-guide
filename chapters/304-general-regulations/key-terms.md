# Section 304 — Key Terms and Definitions

## Fitness For Duty

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Fitness For Duty** | Being fit for duty at all times, except when on sick report; not consuming intoxicants to the extent of becoming unfit |
| **Unfit For Duty While Armed** | Disciplinary charge for misconduct involving member found unfit due to alcohol while armed with a firearm |
| **Dismissal Probation** | Period included in negotiated penalties for alcohol-related motor vehicle incidents |

## Counseling Programs

| Program | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **HELPLINE** | Confidential assistance for alcohol-related problems |
| **POPPA** | Police Organization Providing Peer Assistance — joint labor-management, union-operated employee assistance |
| **Counseling Services Unit** | 24/7 assessment and referral; maintains Title 42 Federal confidentiality; not part of disciplinary system |

## Disciplinary Consequences

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Firearm misuse while unfit (alcohol) | **Termination** (exceptional cases: Police Commissioner) |
| Serious physical injury via motor vehicle + alcohol | **Termination** absent exigent circumstances |
| Refusal of chemical testing (VTL §1192) | Charged under A.G. 304-06 "Prohibited Conduct" |
| Found unfit or refuses breath testing during probation | Additional discipline, may include termination |

## Counseling Protections

| Protection | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Confidentiality | Title 42 Federal guidelines |
| Promotions | Participation does NOT jeopardize promotional opportunities |
| Assignments | Current assignments NOT ordinarily changed |
| Discipline | Participation does NOT exempt from discipline for specific misconduct |
| Availability | Services available to members, families, and retirees |
