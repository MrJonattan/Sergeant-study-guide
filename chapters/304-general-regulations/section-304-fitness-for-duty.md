# Section 304 — Fitness For Duty

## A.G. 304-04: Fitness For Duty

**Date Effective:** 04/21/22 | **Last Revision:** I.O. 45 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To establish fitness for duty requirements for all members of the service.

### Core Requirements

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| Be fit for duty at all times, except when on sick report |
| Do not consume intoxicants to the extent that member becomes unfit for duty |
| Members are reminded of absolute responsibility to remain fit for duty while in possession of firearms |

### Firearm Misconduct — Mandatory Termination

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Misuse of firearm while unfit for duty due to excessive alcohol consumption/intoxication | **Termination** from the Department |
| Exceptional cases | Determined by **Police Commissioner** on case-by-case basis |

### "Unfit For Duty While Armed" Charge

| Rule |
|------|
| Any misconduct involving member found unfit for duty due to excessive alcohol consumption while armed with a firearm |
| Results in inclusion of charge **"Unfit For Duty While Armed"** in disciplinary proceedings |
| Strict punitive sanctions imposed upon substantiation — in addition to all other charges |

### Refusal of Chemical Testing

| Scenario | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| UMOS refuses to submit to chemical testing for alleged violation of VTL §1192 (DWI) | Charged with violating A.G. 304-06 "Prohibited Conduct" — engaging in conduct prejudicial to good order, efficiency, or discipline |

### Motor Vehicle Policy

| Scenario | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| UMOS causes **serious physical injury** while operating motor vehicle AND determined unfit for duty due to alcohol | **Terminated**, absent exigent circumstances |
| Any negotiated penalty for operating motor vehicle while unfit due to alcohol | Must include period of **Dismissal Probation** |
| Ordered breath testing during probation — member found unfit or refuses | Additional disciplinary action, may include **termination** |

### Counseling and Assistance Programs

| Program | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **HELPLINE** | Confidential assistance for alcohol-related problems |
| **POPPA** (Police Organization Providing Peer Assistance) | Joint labor-management, union-operated employee assistance program |
| **Counseling Services Unit** | 24 hours/day, 7 days/week; assessment and referrals for treatment |

### Counseling Services Unit Protections

| Protection |
|-----------|
| Maintains strict confidentiality under Title 42 Federal guidelines |
| Does NOT act as part of Department's disciplinary system |
| Participation will NOT jeopardize promotional opportunities |
| Current assignments will NOT ordinarily be changed due to participation |
| Participation does NOT exempt member from disciplinary action for specific acts of misconduct |

> **Exam Alert:** Misuse of firearm while unfit for duty due to alcohol = **mandatory termination** (exceptional cases by Police Commissioner only). Causing serious physical injury while driving + alcohol = **termination** absent exigent circumstances.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors should be familiar with HELPLINE, POPPA, and Counseling Services Unit referral pathways. Early detection and referral is the Department's objective. Participation in counseling does NOT jeopardize promotions.
