# Section 304 — Review Questions

## Multiple Choice

**1. A uniformed member of the service who misuses a firearm while unfit for duty due to excessive alcohol consumption will be:**

- A) Placed on modified assignment
- B) Suspended for 30 days
- C) Terminated from the Department
- D) Given command discipline

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Terminated from the Department**

Any misconduct involving a member's misuse of a firearm while unfit for duty due to excessive consumption of and intoxication from alcohol will result in termination. Exceptional cases are determined by the Police Commissioner on a case-by-case basis. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

---

**2. A UMOS causes serious physical injury to another person while operating a motor vehicle and is determined to be unfit for duty due to alcohol. What is the consequence?**

- A) Dismissal Probation only
- B) Suspension and retraining
- C) Termination, absent exigent circumstances
- D) Modified assignment for 30 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Termination, absent exigent circumstances**

The Department policy states that any UMOS who causes serious physical injury while operating a motor vehicle and is determined unfit for duty due to alcohol consumption will be terminated, absent exigent circumstances. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

---

**3. A UMOS refuses to submit to chemical testing in connection with an alleged violation of VTL §1192 (DWI). The member will be charged with violating:**

- A) A.G. 304-04, "Fitness For Duty"
- B) A.G. 304-06, "Prohibited Conduct"
- C) A.G. 318-10, "Removal of Firearms"
- D) P.G. 221-23, "Alcohol Testing"

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A.G. 304-06, "Prohibited Conduct"**

A UMOS who refuses chemical testing for alleged DWI will be charged with violating A.G. 304-06, "Prohibited Conduct" — engaging in conduct prejudicial to the good order, efficiency, or discipline of the Department. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

**4. The Counseling Services Unit maintains confidentiality standards under:**

- A) New York State Civil Service Law
- B) Title 42 of Federal guidelines governing alcoholism programs
- C) Department Administrative Guide only
- D) HIPAA regulations

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Title 42 of Federal guidelines governing alcoholism programs**

The Counseling Services Unit maintains strict standards of confidentiality as set forth in Title 42 of Federal guidelines governing alcoholism programs. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

**5. A member participating in counseling programs for alcohol-related problems should be aware that:**

- A) Their promotional opportunities will be jeopardized
- B) They will automatically be transferred
- C) Participation will NOT jeopardize promotional opportunities
- D) They will be placed on restricted duty

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Participation will NOT jeopardize promotional opportunities**

Members participating in these programs will not jeopardize their promotional opportunities, and current assignments will not ordinarily be changed. However, participation does not exempt a member from disciplinary action for specific acts of misconduct. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

---

**6. Any negotiated penalty for a UMOS determined to have been operating a motor vehicle while unfit due to alcohol shall include:**

- A) A verbal reprimand
- B) A period of Dismissal Probation
- C) A 10-day suspension
- D) Command discipline only

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A period of Dismissal Probation**

Any negotiated penalty involving a UMOS operating a motor vehicle while unfit due to alcohol shall include a period of Dismissal Probation. The member must also agree to submit to ordered breath testing during the probation period. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

**7. POPPA is best described as:**

- A) A Department disciplinary unit
- B) A joint labor-management, union-operated employee assistance program
- C) A civilian oversight board
- D) An Internal Affairs Bureau division

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A joint labor-management, union-operated employee assistance program**

POPPA (Police Organization Providing Peer Assistance) is a joint labor-management cooperative effort involving the implementation of a union-operated, alternative employee assistance program. Both HELPLINE and POPPA offer confidential services. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>

**8. The Counseling Services Unit operates:**

- A) Monday through Friday, 0900-1700 hours
- B) 24 hours a day, seven days a week
- C) Only during business hours
- D) By appointment only

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 24 hours a day, seven days a week**

The Counseling Services Unit operates 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and will help assess the extent of the problem and make appropriate referrals for treatment. (A.G. 304-04)

</details>
