## P.G. 217-03: Vehicle Collisions — Death/Serious Injury/Critical Injury — Mechanical Defect Involved

**Date Issued:** 08/19/13 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To examine a vehicle involved in a collision which has resulted in death, serious injury and likely to die, or critical injury to ascertain if the vehicle is mechanically defective.

### Key Definition

**CRITICAL INJURY** — A critical injury will be determined based upon the on-scene assessment of Emergency Medical Services (EMS) personnel.

> **Exam Alert:** Critical injury is determined by **EMS on-scene assessment**, not by police or hospital personnel.

### Procedure

When a vehicle has been involved in a collision resulting in death, serious injury/likely to die, or critical injury and either the operator indicates or Highway Unit personnel suspect a mechanical defect:

#### Highway Unit Officer (Steps 1-5)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Prepare **MOTOR VEHICLE COLLISION AND MECHANISM REPORT (PD301-151)** |
| 1a | Prepare WITNESS STATEMENT — VEHICLE COLLISION (PD301-061), if appropriate |
| 2 | Prepare **Early Notification of Fatal Accident (MV-104EN)**, if appropriate, and **fax to NYS DMV within twenty-four (24) hours** |
| 3 | Prepare **Police Report for Fatal Motor Vehicle Accidents (MV-104D)**, if appropriate |
| 3a | Complete a **separate** Police Report for Fatal Motor Vehicle Accidents when more than one person is deceased or more than three vehicles are involved |
| 4 | Forward copies of Early Notification and Police Report to Collision Investigation Squad concerned |
| 5 | Report facts to desk officer, precinct of occurrence, and assigned member of Collision Investigation Squad |

> **Exam Alert:** The Early Notification of Fatal Accident must be faxed to NYS DMV within **24 hours**. A separate MV-104D is needed if more than one person is deceased or more than three vehicles are involved.

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 6-7)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 6 | Summon Department tow truck and have vehicle removed to Department facility |
| 7 | Prepare PROPERTY CLERK INVOICE WORKSHEET (PD521-141A) |

#### Desk Officer, Precinct of Occurrence (Steps 8-10)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 8 | Enter rank, name, and shield number of Highway Unit officer in Command Log |
| 8a | Note in entry if mechanical defect is a possible factor in collision |
| 9 | Make reference to Command Log entry on Police Accident Report (MV104AN) |
| 10 | Have PROPERTY CLERK INVOICE (PD521-141) prepared from WORKSHEET, review and digitally sign |

#### Desk Officer/Supervisor at Department Facility (Steps 11-13)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 11 | **Do not permit removal** of vehicle if required as evidence or until the defect has been corrected |
| 12 | Obtain **signed statement** from person correcting mechanical defect and file in Property Receipt Book |
| 13 | Permit removal only by properly equipped tow truck if defect is not corrected, provided vehicle is not required as evidence |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor at the Department facility must ensure the vehicle is not released prematurely — evidence preservation and defect correction are both gates to release.
