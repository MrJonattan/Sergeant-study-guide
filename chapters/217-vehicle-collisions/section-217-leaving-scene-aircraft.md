# Section 217 — Leaving the Scene, Aircraft Accidents, and Department Property

## P.G. 217-05: Leaving the Scene of a Collision

**Date Issued:** 08/13/19 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To investigate reports of leaving the scene of vehicle collisions.

### Procedure

Upon arrival at a collision scene where the operator has fled without reporting, follow P.G. 217-01 and:

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Step 1)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Prepare **COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A)**, unless the operator who left is apprehended and issued a summons for Leaving the Scene prior to preparation of the worksheet |

> **Exam Alert:** No COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET is needed if the operator is caught and summonsed **before** the worksheet is prepared.

#### If New York Registration Number Is Obtained (Steps 2-4)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2 | Query **NYSPIN through FINEST** to determine if vehicle is reported stolen |
| 2a | **DO NOT** give complainant hard copy |
| 2b | Provide information utilizing **COLLISION INFORMATION EXCHANGE (PD301-157)** |
| 3 | Obtain name and address of registered owner |
| 4 | Give information to complainant |

> **Exam Alert:** Never give the complainant a hard copy of the NYSPIN query. Use the COLLISION INFORMATION EXCHANGE form instead.

#### If Vehicle Registered in Another State (Steps 5-6)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 5 | Query NYSPIN through FINEST to determine identity of registered owner |
| 5a | Request information through an adjoining command if command FINEST is inoperable |
| 6 | Provide complainant with information, if identity is obtained |

### Operational Considerations — Case Disposition

In determining whether to close a case or refer to the detective squad, consider:

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| a. | Extent of damage |
| b. | Whether facts/degree of damage indicate operator **intentionally** left knowing damage was caused |
| c. | Availability of witnesses to testify |
| d. | Timeliness of complaint |
| e. | Whether report is for **prosecution** or **insurance** purposes |
| f. | Other relevant factors |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor should weigh these six factors when deciding disposition. Intent and witness availability are the most critical factors favoring detective referral.

### Property Damage Only — Special Authority

In **property damage only** cases, a summons returnable to the Traffic Violations Bureau may be served **even though the member did not observe the offense** (Vehicle and Traffic Law, **Section 602**).

> **Exam Alert:** VTL Section 602 is an exception to the general rule requiring observation — members can summons for leaving the scene of a property-damage-only collision without witnessing it.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET | PD313-152A |
| COLLISION INFORMATION EXCHANGE | PD301-157 |

## P.G. 217-08: Aircraft Accidents

**Date Issued:** 08/28/19 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To determine the cause of, and record data concerning aircraft accidents.

### Procedure

On arrival at the scene of an aircraft accident, follow P.G. 216-01, "Aided Cases General Procedure," and:

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 1-5)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Request patrol supervisor to respond |
| 2 | Notify the desk officer |
| 3 | **Detain pilot and aircraft** |
| 4 | Safeguard evidence until examined by authorized personnel |
| 5 | Advise the owner or pilot of responsibility to report the accident to the desk officer within **ten (10) hours** — failure to do so is a **misdemeanor** |

> **Exam Alert:** The pilot must report to the desk officer within **10 hours**. Failure is a **misdemeanor**.

#### Desk Officer (Step 6)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 6 | Notify: |
| 6a | Detective squad concerned |
| 6b | Operations Unit |
| 6c | Duty captain |
| 6d | Borough office |

#### Supervising Officer in Command (Step 7)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 7 | Prepare and forward report of unusual occurrence with **two extra copies** for **Chief of Special Operations** |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervising officer must ensure the unusual occurrence report includes the two additional copies for the Chief of Special Operations.

### Additional Data

An authorized investigator (**FAA or Aviation Unit**) determines the cause of the accident, supplies necessary information to the detective, and recommends the release of the pilot and plane when feasible.

> **Exam Alert:** The **FAA or Aviation Unit** — not the NYPD — determines the cause of the aircraft accident.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT | PD370-152 |

## P.G. 217-10: Accidents — Department Property

**Date Issued:** 07/01/20 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To report damage to Department property **other than Department vehicles**.

### Procedure

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Step 1)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Report facts to desk officer and make digital Activity Log entry |

#### Desk Officer (Step 2)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2 | Have **patrol supervisor** investigate facts and prepare report, including: |
| 2a | Date and time of occurrence |
| 2b | Details |
| 2c | Department property involved |
| 2d | Damage to other property |
| 2e | Owner of that property |
| 2f | Action taken |
| 2g | Witnesses |
| 2h | Brief description of injury |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The **patrol supervisor** investigates and prepares the report for Department property damage — this is a direct supervisory responsibility.

#### Patrol Supervisor (Step 3)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 3 | Submit report to command clerk |

#### Command Clerk (Step 4)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 4 | Type report on **Typed Letterhead (four copies)** |
| 4a | Submit to desk officer/counterpart |

#### Desk Officer (Steps 5-6)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 5 | Have patrol supervisor sign report |
| 6 | File one copy and forward three copies as follows: |

### Forwarding Table

| Property | Forward To |
|----------|-----------|
| Booths, buildings | Deputy Commissioner — Management & Budget, Facilities Management Division |
| Horses | Mounted Unit |
| Aircraft, launches | Office of the Chief of Special Operations |

> **Exam Alert:** Know the three-way routing: booths/buildings → Management & Budget; horses → Mounted Unit; aircraft/launches → Chief of Special Operations.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| Typed Letterhead | (4 copies) |
