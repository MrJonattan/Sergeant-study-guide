# Section 217 — Review Questions

## Multiple-Choice Practice Questions

---

### Question 1

According to P.G. 217-03, who determines whether an injury qualifies as a "critical injury" at a vehicle collision scene?

- A) The patrol supervisor
- B) The desk officer after reviewing hospital records
- C) Emergency Medical Services (EMS) personnel based on on-scene assessment
- D) The Highway Unit collision investigator

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C)** Per P.G. 217-03, a critical injury is determined based upon the **on-scene assessment of EMS personnel**.

</details>

---

### Question 2

Under P.G. 217-03, within what timeframe must the Early Notification of Fatal Accident (MV-104EN) be faxed to the NYS Department of Motor Vehicles?

- A) Twelve hours
- B) Twenty-four hours
- C) Forty-eight hours
- D) Before end of tour

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** The Early Notification of Fatal Accident must be faxed to NYS DMV within **twenty-four (24) hours**.

</details>

### Question 3

According to P.G. 217-04, persons involved in a city-involved accident must file claims with which office, and within what timeframe?

- A) Corporation Counsel within 60 days
- B) Comptroller of the City of New York within 90 days
- C) City Law Department within 30 days
- D) Commissioner of Finance within 120 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** Claims against the City must be filed with the **Comptroller of the City of New York** within **ninety (90) days**.

</details>

### Question 4

Under P.G. 217-04, damage to a city hydrant requires notification to which agency or agencies?

- A) Department of Environmental Protection only
- B) FDNY Actions and Claims Unit only
- C) Department of Environmental Protection AND FDNY Actions and Claims Unit
- D) Department of Transportation and FDNY

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C)** Hydrant damage requires dual notification: **DEP and FDNY Actions and Claims Unit**.

</details>

### Question 5

According to P.G. 217-05, under what authority may a uniformed member issue a summons for leaving the scene of a property-damage-only collision without having observed the offense?

- A) Penal Law Section 240
- B) Vehicle and Traffic Law, Section 602
- C) Administrative Code Section 20-518
- D) The member may not issue a summons without observing the offense

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** VTL **Section 602** authorizes issuing a summons for leaving the scene of a property-damage-only collision even without witnessing the offense.

</details>

### Question 6

Per P.G. 217-08, within how many hours must a pilot report an aircraft accident to the desk officer, and what is the consequence of failure?

- A) 6 hours; violation
- B) 10 hours; misdemeanor
- C) 12 hours; misdemeanor
- D) 24 hours; violation

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** The pilot must report within **ten (10) hours**; failure to do so is a **misdemeanor**.

</details>

### Question 7

Under P.G. 217-09, who is authorized to make tow company notifications under the Directed Accident Response Program?

- A) The desk officer at the precinct of occurrence
- B) The patrol supervisor at the scene
- C) The D.A.R.P. operator at the Communications Section
- D) Any uniformed member at the scene

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C)** The D.A.R.P. operator at the **Communications Section** maintains the list and makes notifications on strict rotation. **Precinct commands are NOT authorized** to make notifications.

</details>

---

### Question 8

According to P.G. 217-09, what information about the disabled vehicle must NOT be transmitted over the radio when requesting a D.A.R.P. tow?

- A) Color and body type
- B) Plate number
- C) Make, model, and year
- D) Location of collision

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C)** Members must **NOT transmit the make, model, or year** of the disabled vehicle. Only color, body type, and plate number are transmitted.

</details>

### Question 9

Under P.G. 217-09, if a D.A.R.P. tow company does not arrive within the required time, what should the uniformed member do?

- A) Wait an additional 30 minutes and then leave
- B) Request Communications to cancel and assign the next company in rotation; prepare a REPORT OF VIOLATION
- C) Contact the tow company directly and demand response
- D) Have the desk officer arrange an alternative tow

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** After **30 minutes**, the member requests Communications to **cancel** the initial company, assign the **next in rotation**, and prepares a **REPORT OF VIOLATION (PD672-151)** for the non-responding company.

</details>

### Question 10

According to P.G. 217-09, what is the D.A.R.P. disposition code for a vehicle collision where a D.A.R.P. tow was requested, the member waited 30 minutes, made a second request, and resumed patrol?

- A) 10-99T5
- B) 10-99T7
- C) 10-99T8
- D) 10-99T9

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**D)** Code **10-99T9** indicates: D.A.R.P. requested, waited thirty minutes, made second request, resumed patrol.

</details>

### Question 11

Per P.G. 217-09, which of the following vehicles is EXEMPT from D.A.R.P. towing requirements?

- A) Any vehicle with more than $5,000 in damage
- B) Government vehicles
- C) Vehicles registered out of state
- D) Vehicles less than one year old

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** **Government vehicles** are exempt under Administrative Code Section 20-524(c). Their operators may call their own tow service.

</details>

### Question 12

Under P.G. 217-10, if Department property (other than vehicles) is damaged, who investigates and prepares the report?

- A) The uniformed member who discovered the damage
- B) The detective squad
- C) The patrol supervisor
- D) The desk officer

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C)** The desk officer directs the **patrol supervisor** to investigate the facts and prepare the report.

</details>

### Question 13

According to P.G. 217-14, reference markers on limited access highways are what color and size?

- A) Blue, 12"×10"
- B) Green, 9"×8"
- C) Yellow, 8"×6"
- D) White, 10"×8"

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** Reference markers are **green**, measuring **9 inches × 8 inches**, placed every 1/10 of a mile.

</details>

### Question 14

Per P.G. 217-16, under what circumstances can a chemical test warrant NOT be applied for?

- A) When the collision occurred more than 24 hours ago
- B) When the operator is the only person who suffered serious physical injury
- C) When the operator's BAC is below 0.08
- D) When the operator is a juvenile

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** If the operator is the **only person** with serious physical injury and **no other person** suffers serious injury or death, a chemical test warrant **cannot** be applied for.

</details>

### Question 15

Under P.G. 217-16, if one hour has passed and the assistant district attorney has not contacted the arresting officer, what is the desk officer's next step?

- A) Cancel the chemical test warrant request
- B) Contact the ADA supervisor and Operations Unit; then contact ADA supervisor every 30 minutes
- C) Have the arresting officer proceed without ADA consultation
- D) Request a Department attorney from Legal Bureau

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B)** After one hour, the desk officer contacts the **ADA supervisor and Operations Unit**, then contacts the ADA supervisor **every thirty (30) minutes** thereafter until the ADA is reached.

</details>
