# Section 217 — Vehicle Collisions

## Chapter Overview

Section 217 of the NYPD Patrol Guide governs procedures for **vehicle collisions**, including fatal and serious injury collisions, leaving the scene investigations, the Directed Accident Response Program (D.A.R.P.) for towing, city and NYCHA liability cases, aircraft accidents, and chemical test warrant applications for DWI suspects.

Sergeants must understand towing protocols, notification responsibilities, agency routing for city-involved incidents, and the chemical test warrant process — particularly the critical timeframes and escalation procedures.

## Learning Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Explain the mechanical defect investigation procedures for fatal/serious injury collisions.
2. Define "City Involved" and route reports to the correct city agency.
3. Describe the procedures for investigating leaving-the-scene collisions, including VTL Section 602 authority.
4. Articulate the full D.A.R.P. tow dispatch process, including rotation rules, 30-minute wait, and disposition codes.
5. State the rules for unsolicited tow trucks at collision scenes.
6. Explain aircraft accident procedures, including the pilot's 10-hour reporting obligation.
7. Describe NYCHA-involved accident reporting and notification requirements.
8. Identify the correct reference marker procedures for highway, bridge, and tunnel collisions.
9. Define "Chemical Test Warrant" and explain when it can and cannot be applied for.
10. List the escalation timeline for contacting the ADA in chemical test warrant cases.

## Study Tips

- Memorize all **six D.A.R.P. disposition codes** (10-99T4 through 10-99T9) — a frequent exam topic.
- Know the **agency routing table** for city-involved incidents (P.G. 217-04).
- Understand the **chemical test warrant** restrictions — especially that the warrant CANNOT be applied for if the operator is the only injured person.
- The **30-minute tow wait** rule and the prohibition against precinct commands making tow notifications are highly testable.
- Know the **reference marker** system: green, 9"x8", every 1/10 mile — and that bridge/tunnel markers must come from the Listing book, NOT posted markers.
