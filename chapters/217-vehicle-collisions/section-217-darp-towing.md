# Section 217 — Directed Accident Response Program (D.A.R.P.)

## P.G. 217-09: Directed Accident Response Program (D.A.R.P.)

**Date Effective:** 06/14/22 | **Last Revision:** I.O. 74 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To direct the response of designated tow trucks, through the Communications Section, to the scene of vehicular collisions, for vehicles which the reporting member deems unsafe to be driven, except that an operator of a vehicle with a gross vehicle weight of **fifteen thousand (15,000) pounds or more** may choose their own company.

### Scope

This procedure does **NOT** include collisions occurring in:
- Tunnels
- Bridges
- Arterial highways serviced by franchised towing companies under DOT permits

> **Exam Alert:** Administrative Code Section 20-524(c) **exempts governmental agencies** from D.A.R.P. — government vehicle operators may call their own tow service.

### Key Administrative Code Provisions

| Section | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| 20-518(a)(3) | Participants must maintain business premises under exclusive control, open to public, not shared with other towing companies |
| 20-518(b) | Vehicle removed at police direction must be towed by D.A.R.P. participant and stored at the facility listed on their license |

### Procedure

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 1-3)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Comply with P.G. 217-01, "Vehicle Collisions — General Procedure" |
| 2 | Ascertain if vehicle(s) can be safely driven, or if towing is required using **reasonableness** as a standard |
| 2a | Operators of **government vehicles** or vehicles with GVW of **15,000 lbs or more** may use their own tow company |
| 2b | A uniformed member may authorize removal if owner/operator is **incapacitated, not present, or refuses** to authorize the tow |
| 3 | Notify Communications Section dispatcher when towing service is needed |
| 3a | Include the **color, body type, and plate number(s)** of vehicle(s) requiring a tow |

> **Exam Alert:** Once the uniformed member makes a determination that a tow truck is needed, the **request may NOT be cancelled**.

> **Exam Alert:** **DO NOT TRANSMIT** the make, model, or year of the disabled vehicle(s) involved in a collision.

#### Communications Section Dispatcher (Steps 4-5)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 4 | Inform uniformed member of the name of the towing company responding |
| 5 | Dispatch patrol supervisor |

> **Exam Alert:** The D.A.R.P. operator at Communications maintains the tow company list and makes notifications on a **strict rotation basis**. **PRECINCT COMMANDS ARE NOT AUTHORIZED TO MAKE NOTIFICATIONS.**

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 6-9)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 6 | Allow **only** the tow truck dispatched by Communications to remove vehicle(s), **except:** |
| 6a | Motorist with vehicle GVW of 15,000+ lbs requesting private tow |
| 6b | Arrest evidence vehicles |
| 6c | Vehicles held as investigatory evidence |
| 6d | Vehicles subject to Rotation Tow Program as stolen or abandoned |
| 6e | Government vehicles |
| 7 | Remain on scene for **thirty (30) minutes** after radio dispatcher advises which tow company is responding |
| 8 | Inform motorist(s) of tow company name and supply D.A.R.P. operator telephone number |
| 8a | Instruct motorist to call after 30 minutes if tow has not arrived, prepared with plate number and collision location |

> **Exam Alert:** If the tow company does not arrive within **30 minutes**: (1) request Communications to **cancel** and assign next in rotation, (2) prepare **REPORT OF VIOLATION (PD672-151)** for the cancelled company, (3) advise dispatcher when resuming patrol. Communications enters disposition code **10-99T9**.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 9 | Enter tow company information on Police Accident Report (MV104AN) **only when** the vehicle is towed and the tow company is known |

### Unsolicited Tow Trucks

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 9a | If unsolicited tow truck arrives or is found at scene with probable cause to believe it responded without a specific request: issue summons for **Admin Code Section 20-518(b)(2)** (unsolicited response), prepare REPORT OF VIOLATION |
| 9b | If probable cause of **solicitation** of involved parties: issue **additional** summons for **Admin Code Section 20-515(b)** (soliciting at collision scene). Also applies to persons soliciting from a private vehicle |

> **Exam Alert:** Members of the service **may NOT seize unlicensed tow trucks** for the sole purpose of being unlicensed by the Department of Consumer and Worker Protection.

### Patrol Supervisor (Steps 10-13)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 10 | Respond to location and supervise removal of involved vehicles |
| 11 | Supervise issuance of summonses to tow truck operators |
| 12 | Ensure preparation of REPORT OF VIOLATION when warranted |
| 12a | If witness advises a tow company removed a vehicle **prior to officer's arrival**, have REPORT OF VIOLATION prepared with witness information |
| 13 | Direct reporting officer to enter information regarding vehicle towed without owner/operator's knowledge into FINEST "IMPOUND" screen |
| 13a | Ensure notification is sent to registered owner of vehicle |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor has direct responsibility for supervising vehicle removal, summons issuance, and ensuring the IMPOUND screen is updated for towed vehicles.

### Desk Officer (Steps 14-17)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 14 | If inquiry is made at stationhouse and FINEST "IMPOUND" screen has no information, call D.A.R.P. operator for vehicle location |
| 15 | Review and sign REPORT OF VIOLATION |
| 16 | Assign precinct serial number to REPORT OF VIOLATION |
| 17 | Forward REPORT OF VIOLATION to **Department of Consumer and Worker Protection, Licensing Enforcement Section, 42 Broadway, New York, NY 10004** |

### D.A.R.P. Disposition Codes

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **10-99T4** | Vehicle Collision (No tow required) |
| **10-99T5** | Vehicle Collision (D.A.R.P. tow service) |
| **10-99T6** | Vehicle Collision (Other tow service) |
| **10-99T7** | Vehicle Collision (D.A.R.P. and other tow service) |
| **10-99T8** | Vehicle Collision (Highway-authorized tow service) |
| **10-99T9** | Vehicle Collision (D.A.R.P. requested, waited 30 minutes, made second request, resumed patrol) |

> **Exam Alert:** Memorize all six codes. The only other acceptable code at a collision is **10-90Z** (Gone on Arrival) — used only when vehicle(s) are not at scene and no information is available about removal.

### REPORT OF VIOLATION Entries

| Violation | Reference |
|-----------|-----------|
| Unjustifiable refusal to accept towing assignment | Article XI, subdivision F (DARP) |
| Repeated unjustifiable refusals to accept towing assignments | Article XI, subdivision L (DARP) — list times and dates |

### Tow Authorization Requirements

The dispatched tow truck must possess and present a **"Tow Authorization" form** that includes a **Department of Consumer and Worker Protection license number**. Both tow trucks and operators must be licensed by DCWP.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| Police Accident Report | MV104AN |
| REPORT OF VIOLATION | PD672-151 |
