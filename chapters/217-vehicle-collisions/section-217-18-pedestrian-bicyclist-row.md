# Section 217-18 — Vehicle Collision - Pedestrian/Bicyclist Right of Way Law

## Source Reference
- **Procedure:** P.G. 217-18
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide3.pdf (OCR extracted)
- **Last Revision:** R.O. 54 (06/30/25)

## Procedure Summary

This procedure establishes protocols for investigating vehicular collisions involving pedestrians/bicyclists to determine if a motorist violated **New York City Administrative Code Section 19-190(b)** by failing to yield right of way and causing physical injury, or to issue a summons when no collision occurred but the motorist failed to yield.

## Purpose

a. To record and investigate vehicular collisions involving pedestrians/bicyclists to determine if a motorist violated Administrative Code Section 19-190(b), by failing to yield to a pedestrian/bicyclist who has the right of way and causing physical injury.

b. To issue a summons when a motorist fails to yield the right of way to a pedestrian/bicyclist who has the right of way but no collision occurred.

## Scenario 1: No Physical Injury — Motorist Failed to Yield

**Uniformed Member of the Service Shall:**

1. **Issue a Traffic Violations Bureau (TVB) summons** returnable to TVB when:
   - Probable cause exists that motorist failed to yield right of way
   - No physical injury occurred
   - Offense occurred in officer's presence

2. **Issue a Civil Summons Returnable to OATH** if:
   - Officer did NOT personally observe the incident
   - Probable cause established through reliable witness statement
   - Be guided by P.G. 209-12 for personal service procedures

> **Exam Alert:** Personal observation is required for TVB summons and Criminal Court summons, but NOT for Civil Summons Returnable to OATH — witness statements can establish probable cause for administrative court.

## Scenario 2: Collision with Physical Injury

**Uniformed Member of the Service Shall:**

3. Park radio motor patrol car behind vehicle involved so traffic will not be impeded

4. Ascertain injuries and request ambulance if needed
   - Place IDENTIFICATION TAG (PD317-091) on aided if unconscious and removed to hospital

5. Divert traffic if necessary
   - Use traffic cones and turret lights whenever available

6. Obtain motorist's driver's license, vehicle registration, and insurance identification card

7. **Determine if pedestrian/bicyclist had right of way** at time of collision
   - Be guided by Operational Considerations (pedestrian signals)

8. **Determine if Administrative Code Section 19-190(b) violation occurred** through:
   - Interview of motorist
   - Interview of pedestrian/bicyclist
   - Witness statements
   - Condition of vehicle and pedestrian/bicyclist
   - Collision scene particulars
   - Any other related evidence (document in PAR Details section)

9. **Issue Civil Summons Returnable to OATH** when probable cause establishes violation
   - No violation if collision/injury not result of driver's failure to exercise due care
   - "Due care" = that exercised by reasonably prudent drivers

> **Sergeant Focus:** If violation not personally observed, officer must complete the "Statement of Witness" section on the OATH summons and request witness signature. If witness refuses, write "REFUSED" and document all information in digital Activity Log.

## Scenario 3: Death, Serious Injury, or Critical Injury

**Uniformed Member of the Service Shall:**

10. **Request patrol supervisor to respond** when death, serious injury and likely to die, or critical injury has occurred

**Patrol Supervisor Shall:**

11. Respond and be guided by P.G. 217-02 if required

12. Process the collision per P.G. 217-02

13. **Administer PBT** (if qualified) when serious traffic violation caused serious physical injury
    - If not qualified, request qualified Highway Unit supervisor

**Highway District, Collision Investigation Squad Supervisor May Authorize:**

14. **Arrest for Administrative Code Section 19-190(b)** if investigation warrants

15. **Desk Appearance Ticket** may be issued if motorist qualifies

## Member of Service Involved in Collision

**Uniformed Member of the Service Shall:**

16. **Request patrol supervisor to respond** if a member of service (on or off duty, within five boroughs, in Department or personal vehicle) struck a pedestrian/bicyclist causing any physical injury in violation of Administrative Code Section 19-190(b)

**Desk Officer Shall:**

17. **Immediately notify Internal Affairs Bureau Command Center** upon determining member violated Administrative Code Section 19-190(b)
    - Enter IAB log number in digital Activity Log and Command Log

18. Make notifications per P.G. 208-69 if applicable (Operations Unit, appropriate Department units/agencies)

## Legal Considerations

**Hearsay in Administrative Court:** Civil Summons Returnable to OATH is returnable to administrative court that may only impose civil fines. Generally, hearsay is NOT precluded in administrative courts — unlike Criminal Court which requires personal observation.

**Witness Statement Requirements:** When issuing OATH summons based on witness statement, officer must:
- Indicate in "Details of Charge(s)" section: e.g., "informed by John Smith, whose contact information is known to the NYPD, that he observed..."
- Complete "Statement of Witness" section on reverse of white (OATH) copy
- Request witness signature
- If witness refuses: write "REFUSED" and document in digital Activity Log

## Operational Considerations — Pedestrian Right of Way

**When Pedestrian Signals ARE Present:**

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Steady walking person** | Pedestrians may proceed; traffic MUST yield right of way |
| **Flashing upraised hand** (or with countdown display) | Insufficient time to cross; pedestrians already in roadway should proceed to nearest sidewalk/safety island; traffic MUST yield to pedestrians already crossing |
| **Steady upraised hand** | Vehicle traffic has right of way; pedestrians entering roadway will be at risk; pedestrians already crossing should proceed to nearest sidewalk/safety island |

**When Pedestrian Signals NOT Present:**

- Operators of vehicles and bicycles MUST stop and remain stopped for pedestrian in crosswalk until pedestrian crosses roadway
- **Exception:** If pedestrian suddenly leaves curb and walks/runs into path of vehicle so close that impossible for motorist to yield — no violation

**Green Light Turns:** When motorist has green light and pedestrian crosses directly in front (contrary to pedestrian signal), Traffic Rule 4-03(a)(1)(i) does NOT apply. However, if motorist turns left/right while pedestrian crossing signal indicates crossing is permitted — violation occurs.

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Due Care** | That care which is exercised by reasonably prudent drivers |
| **Administrative Code Section 19-190(b)** | Motorist fails to exercise due care and violates right of way, causing physical injury to pedestrian/bicyclist |
| **Administrative Code Section 19-190(a)** | Motorist fails to yield right of way to pedestrian/bicyclist (no injury required) |
| **OATH** | Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings — administrative court for civil summonses |
| **TVB** | Traffic Violations Bureau — handles traffic summons returnable to state administrative court |
