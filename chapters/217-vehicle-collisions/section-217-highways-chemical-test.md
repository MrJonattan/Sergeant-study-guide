# Section 217 — Highway Reports and Chemical Test Warrants

## P.G. 217-14: Preparation of Police Accident Reports (MV104AN) for Collisions Occurring on Highways, Bridges, and Tunnels

**Date Effective:** 10/24/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 108 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To ensure that the appropriate reference marker numerals/letters are utilized when preparing a Police Accident Report (MV104AN).

### Procedure

Upon notification or observation of a collision on limited access highways, collector-distributor roadways (CDRs), entrance/exit ramps, bridges, and tunnels:

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 1-4)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Comply with P.G. 217-01, "Vehicle Collisions — General Procedure" |
| 2 | Locate the nearest **reference marker** to the collision site (for highways, CDRs, and ramps only) |
| 3 | Utilize the **Limited Access Highway Reference Marker Listing book** (maintained at each command) to determine the proper reference marker |
| 3a | For collisions on **bridges or in tunnels**: use the Listing book — reference markers actually posted on bridges/tunnels near the collision site **will NOT be entered** on Police Accident Reports |
| 3b | Use the Listing book when a reference marker is missing or cannot be seen due to inclement weather |
| 4 | Enter numerals/letters into the **twelve box** caption titled "Reference Marker" on the Police Accident Report |

> **Exam Alert:** For **bridge and tunnel** collisions, always use the **Listing book** — NEVER use markers physically posted at the scene.

#### Command Clerical Staff Member (Steps 5-6)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 5 | Record collision into the On-Line Accident Index |
| 6 | Enter reference marker numerals/letters on the "ADD ACCIDENT" screen under "IF HIGHWAY OR BRIDGE, ENTER REFERENCE MARKER" |

#### Quality of Life Officer (Steps 7-9)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 7 | Review **all** Police Accident Reports for completeness and accuracy |
| 8 | Ensure "Reference Marker" caption is completed when required |
| 9 | Update On-Line Accident Index |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The Quality of Life Officer reviews all Police Accident Reports — supervisors should verify this review is happening consistently.

### Additional Data — Reference Marker Description

- **Size:** 9 inches × 8 inches
- **Color:** Green
- **Placement:** Every **one-tenth (1/10) of a mile** on limited access highways, CDRs, and entrance/exit ramps
- **Caption on report:** Twelve box "Reference Marker" caption on Police Accident Report

> **Exam Alert:** Reference markers are **green**, **9"×8"**, placed every **1/10 mile**.

## P.G. 217-16: Chemical Test Warrant Application

**Date Issued:** 02/04/15 | **Pages:** 5

### Purpose

To apply for a chemical test warrant.

### Key Definition

**CHEMICAL TEST WARRANT** — A Court Order and process directing medical personnel to draw blood from a person suspected of driving while under the influence of alcohol or drugs while causing serious physical injury or death to **another individual**, in order to determine the alcoholic or drug content of the person's blood.

A chemical test warrant will be applied for if a prisoner, arrested for DWI involving a collision resulting in serious physical injury or death to **another individual**:
- Has **refused** to submit to a chemical test, **OR**
- Is **unconscious** and has been under arrest for more than **two (2) hours** without being subjected to a chemical test

> **Exam Alert:** The warrant is for blood draws from DWI suspects who **refuse** testing or are **unconscious for more than 2 hours** without a test.

> **Exam Alert:** If the operator is the **ONLY** person with serious physical injury and no other person suffers serious injury or death, a chemical test warrant **CANNOT** be applied for.

### Procedure

#### Patrol Supervisor (Step 1)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Request Communications Section dispatcher to dispatch **Highway District** personnel to designated testing location or hospital |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor initiates the Highway District request — this is the first step in the chemical test warrant process.

#### Desk Officer, Precinct of Arrest (Steps 2-4)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2 | Contact **Operations Unit** and request notification to District Attorney's Office |
| 3 | Provide Operations Unit with: (a) arresting officer's name, tax registry number, shield, and command; (b) prisoner's pedigree; (c) desk telephone number of testing location |
| 4 | Make Telephone Record entry regarding notification |

#### Operations Unit Member (Steps 5-7)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 5 | Notify appropriate District Attorney's Office |
| 6 | Provide ADA with: arresting officer info, desk telephone of testing location, prisoner's pedigree |
| 7 | Notify desk officer, precinct of arrest, and provide ADA's name and telephone number |

#### Arresting Officer (Step 8)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 8 | Notify desk officer if **one hour** has passed and ADA has not initiated contact at testing location |

> **Exam Alert:** The **1-hour** mark triggers escalation if the ADA has not made contact.

#### Desk Officer (Step 9) — Escalation

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 9 | Contact **ADA supervisor** and Operations Unit if one hour has passed without ADA contact |
| 9a | Record phone call attempt(s) in Telephone Record |
| 9b | Contact ADA supervisor **every thirty (30) minutes** thereafter until ADA is contacted |

> **Exam Alert:** Escalation timeline: 1 hour → contact ADA supervisor; then every 30 minutes until resolved.

#### Arresting Officer (Steps 10-13)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 10 | Inform ADA of details of arrest |
| 11 | Comply with ADA instructions regarding arrest process |
| 12 | Complete all necessary arrest paperwork per P.G. 217-02 and P.G. 208-40 ("Intoxicated or Impaired Driver Arrest") |
| 13 | Complete **CHEMICAL TEST WARRANT APPLICATION (PD374-041)** |

### Articulation Requirements

To apply for the warrant, the member must articulate to the ADA and the judge that there is reasonable cause to believe the defendant:
- (a) Operated a vehicle and during said operation,
- (b) Killed or caused a serious physical injury to **another individual**,
- (c) Was intoxicated or impaired by alcohol or drugs,
- (d) Is under arrest, and
- (e) Has refused a chemical test, or is unconscious and under arrest for more than two hours without a test

#### I.D.T.U. Technician (Steps 14-17)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 14 | Respond to designated testing location or hospital |
| 15 | Prepare and sign **Report of Refusal to Submit to Chemical Test (NYSDMV AA-134)**, when applicable |
| 15a | Have arresting officer sign Report of Refusal |
| 16 | Distribute copies of Report of Refusal as indicated on form |
| 16a | Arresting officer must bring **two copies** for the ADA when the complaint is drawn |
| 17 | Supply arresting officer with chemical test evidence kit |

> **Exam Alert:** If prisoner refuses, maintain the **initial charge** — the arresting officer must articulate circumstances supporting the original DWI charge. After arraignment, the court forwards a **NOTICE OF SUSPENSION and/or NOTICE OF HEARING** for a hearing within **fifteen (15) days** of arraignment.

### Upon Approval of Chemical Test Warrant

#### Desk Officer, Testing Location (Steps 18-19)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 18 | Provide transportation for arresting officer and prisoner to designated hospital |
| 19 | Make Command Log entry: arresting officer name/shield, prisoner's pedigree, Department auto number, time of transport |

#### Arresting Officer (Steps 20-23)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 20 | Upon arrival, inform and submit chemical test warrant and evidence kit to hospital personnel |
| 21 | Notify ADA and Operations Unit if hospital personnel **do not comply** with the warrant |
| 22 | Comply with ADA instructions |
| 23 | Comply with P.G. 218-49, "Immediate Delivery of DNA Evidence to the Police Laboratory and the OCME," upon receiving evidence kit |

#### Desk Officer, Testing Location (Step 24)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 24 | Ensure prisoner is transported to borough court section facility or testing location to complete arrest processing |

### If Chemical Test Warrant Application Is Denied

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 25 | Arresting Officer | Inform desk officer of denial |
| 26 | Arresting Officer | Comply with ADA instructions regarding continuation of arrest processing |
| 27 | Desk Officer | Make Command Log entry regarding denial |
| 28 | Desk Officer | Ensure prisoner transported to borough court section facility |

### Additional Data

- If prisoner is unconscious at hospital, arresting officer requests Communications to dispatch Highway District with chemical test evidence kit. Samples taken by hospital personnel will be witnessed, sealed, and marked by Highway District personnel and delivered to Police Laboratory by arresting officer
- If more than **two hours** elapsed since arrest and unconscious prisoner has not been tested, a warrant **must** be obtained
- **Legal Bureau** available Monday–Friday, **0700–2300 hours**
- **After hours:** contact Operations Unit for Department Attorney notification

> **Exam Alert:** Legal Bureau hours: Mon–Fri, 0700–2300. After hours: Operations Unit.

| Report of Refusal to Submit to Chemical Test | NYSDMV AA-134 |
