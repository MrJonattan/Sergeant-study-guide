# Section 208 — Review Questions

25 multiple-choice questions covering key Sergeant exam topics from Section 208.

---

### Question 1
**A sergeant receives a warrant issued by a city court in Westchester County. The suspect is located in a neighboring county. Can the warrant be executed there?**

A. No, city court warrants can only be executed within the city limits
B. Yes, city/town/village court warrants may be executed in the county of issuance or adjoining county
C. Yes, all warrants have statewide reach
D. No, the warrant must be re-issued by the adjoining county's court

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-01, warrants issued by a city, town, or village court may be executed within the county of issuance OR an adjoining county. Only warrants from NYC Criminal Court, district court, or superior court have statewide authority.
</details>

### Question 2
**An officer observes a traffic infraction in an adjoining county while off-duty. The officer is employed in Kings County. Can the officer make a warrantless arrest?**

A. Yes, any offense in the officer's presence allows arrest
B. Yes, but only if the infraction is also a misdemeanor
C. No, petty offenses require the arrest to be within the geographical area of employment AND the county of occurrence or adjoining county
D. No, off-duty officers cannot make warrantless arrests

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-01, warrantless arrest for a petty offense (violation or traffic infraction) requires the offense be in the officer's presence, within the geographical area of employment, AND within the county of occurrence or adjoining county. An officer in an adjoining county outside their area of employment cannot make this arrest.
</details>

### Question 3
**A misdemeanor suspect flees into a private residence. Under what circumstance may an officer enter without a warrant or consent?**

A. Whenever the officer has probable cause for the misdemeanor
B. Only to prevent imminent injury, destruction of evidence, escape, or under exigent circumstances
C. Never — a warrant is always required for a misdemeanor suspect
D. Only if the suspect is also wanted on a felony warrant

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-01, for a misdemeanor suspect fleeing into a private residence, officers may enter ONLY to prevent imminent injury, prevent destruction of evidence, prevent escape, or under exigent circumstances. For a felony suspect, officers may pursue directly into the residence without these additional requirements.
</details>

### Question 4
**During a search at a police facility, the arrestee's gender is not apparent. How should the searching officer's gender be determined?**

A. The desk officer assigns the searching officer based on the arrestee's biological sex
B. A supervisor makes the determination based on visual assessment
C. The search is assigned to the gender requested by the arrestee, consistent with officer safety and resource availability
D. Two officers of different genders conduct the search together

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-05, if the arrestee's gender is not apparent or the arrestee objects to the assigned gender, the search is conducted by a member of the gender requested by the arrestee, consistent with officer safety and resource availability. Officers must NEVER search a person to determine their gender.
</details>

### Question 5
**Who must authorize a strip search?**

A. The desk officer on duty
B. The arresting officer's immediate supervisor or borough court section supervisor
C. The commanding officer or duty captain
D. Any supervisor of higher rank than the arresting officer

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-05, a strip search requires the knowledge and approval of the arresting officer's IMMEDIATE SUPERVISOR or the borough court section supervisor. The CO/Duty Captain becomes involved only when there is a disagreement between the desk officer and an outside supervisor.
</details>

### Question 6
**The desk officer and an outside supervisor disagree about whether a strip search should be conducted. Who makes the final decision?**

A. The desk officer, as the officer in charge of the command
B. The outside supervisor, as the arresting officer's supervisor
C. The CO or Duty Captain, after being notified by the desk officer
D. The Legal Bureau, after consultation

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-05, when there is a disagreement between the desk officer and an outside supervisor regarding a strip search, the desk officer notifies the CO/Duty Captain, who makes the FINAL decision.
</details>

### Question 7
**How many parts comprise the Miranda warnings as outlined in the Patrol Guide?**

A. Four
B. Five
C. Six
D. Seven

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-09, Miranda warnings consist of SIX parts: (1) right to remain silent, (2) anything said may be used against you, (3) right to attorney before and during questioning, (4) attorney provided if cannot afford one, (5) right to remain silent until consulting attorney, (6) willing to answer questions.
</details>

### Question 8
**A juvenile is arrested and the parent is present. The juvenile agrees to answer questions, but the parent objects. What should the officer do?**

A. Proceed with questioning since the juvenile consented
B. Cease questioning — the parent's objection overrides the juvenile's willingness
C. Continue but limit questions to basic identifying information
D. Request a Legal Bureau consultation before proceeding

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-03, if a parent or guardian objects to questioning, NO questioning is permitted even if the juvenile is willing to answer. The parent's objection is controlling.
</details>

### Question 9
**During custodial interrogation, the suspect states "I think I should talk to a lawyer." What is the proper response?**

A. Continue questioning, as the statement was equivocal
B. Ask the suspect to clarify whether they want an attorney
C. Cease interrogation immediately
D. Advise the suspect of Miranda rights again and continue

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-09, interrogation must cease immediately when a subject indicates they want an attorney or wish to remain silent. Officers should err on the side of cessation when any request for counsel is made.
</details>

### Question 10
**A person is arrested for a Class E felony and presents a valid out-of-state driver's license. Is the person eligible for a DAT?**

A. No, DATs are only for misdemeanors and violations
B. No, out-of-state identification is not accepted for DAT processing
C. Yes, Class E felonies are DAT-eligible, and out-of-state licenses are a satisfactory form of ID
D. Yes, but only if the person also has a local address

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-27, DATs may be issued for Class E felonies, misdemeanors, and violations. Out-of-state driver's licenses are on the list of satisfactory identification that the Department is REQUIRED to accept.
</details>

### Question 11
**A defendant arrested for a misdemeanor has an investigation card on file. Does this preclude issuance of a DAT?**

A. Yes, any prior police contact disqualifies DAT issuance
B. Yes, an investigation card indicates the person is known to police
C. No, an investigation card does NOT preclude DAT issuance
D. No, but the desk officer must obtain supervisor approval

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-27, an investigation card does NOT preclude DAT issuance. Similarly, DNA owed is NOT a disqualifying factor.
</details>

### Question 12
**Which of the following is a DAT disqualifying factor under the "Condition of Defendant" category?**

A. The defendant owes DNA
B. The defendant has an investigation card on file
C. The defendant failed to appear in court within the last 2 years
D. The defendant has a prior misdemeanor conviction

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-81, failure to appear in court within the last 2 YEARS is a disqualifying factor under Category 1 (Condition of Defendant). DNA owed and investigation cards are specifically NOT disqualifying. A prior conviction alone is not a disqualifier.
</details>

### Question 13
**The arresting officer's next regularly scheduled tour is 5 days away. What must happen regarding DA conferral for a DAT case?**

A. The officer confers with the DA upon return in 5 days
B. The conferral is assigned to another officer
C. The desk officer confers with the DA on the officer's behalf
D. The case is automatically upgraded from DAT to continued custody

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-82, if the arresting officer's next regularly scheduled tour is more than 4 DAYS away, the DA conferral must be assigned to another officer.
</details>

### Question 14
**Under the Family Court Act, which of the following relationships qualifies as "family/household"?**

A. Current roommates who share an apartment but are not in an intimate relationship
B. Former coworkers who dated briefly three years ago
C. Persons who have a child in common but were never married
D. Business partners who have known each other for ten years

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-36, the FCA definition of family/household includes persons who have a child in common. Casual acquaintances, business relationships, and mere roommates (without a family-type relationship) do not qualify. Note: the NYPD expanded definition would cover persons living together in a family-type relationship, but a simple roommate arrangement is not a family-type relationship.
</details>

### Question 15
**What additional category does the NYPD expanded definition of "family/household" include beyond the Family Court Act definition?**

A. Persons who attend the same school or workplace
B. Persons currently or formerly living together in a family-type relationship
C. Persons who share a common friend group
D. Persons who live in the same apartment building

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-36, the NYPD expands the FCA definition to include persons currently or formerly living together in a family-type relationship. The FCA itself covers: legally married, formerly married, related by marriage, related by blood, child in common, and intimate relationship.
</details>

### Question 16
**A 17-year-old is arrested for misdemeanor harassment against their former spouse. Does concurrent jurisdiction exist between Criminal Court and Family Court?**

A. Yes, all three conditions are met
B. No, because the offender is under 18 and the charge is not a felony
C. No, because harassment is not a family offense
D. Yes, because the parties were married

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-36, concurrent jurisdiction requires: (1) family offense, (2) FCA relationship, and (3) offender 18+. A 16-17 year old qualifies ONLY if charged with a felony-level family offense. Misdemeanor harassment by a 17-year-old goes to Criminal Court only.
</details>

### Question 17
**At a domestic violence scene, no certified interpreter is available and the victim speaks limited English. A family member offers to interpret. What should the officer do?**

A. Accept the family member as interpreter for the entire investigation
B. Decline — only certified interpreters may be used at any DV scene
C. Use the family member temporarily to stabilize the scene, then verify information with a certified member or Language Line
D. Postpone the investigation until a certified interpreter arrives

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-36, officers should avoid using family members or bystanders as interpreters. However, they may be used temporarily to stabilize the scene. Information must then be verified through a certified member or Language Line.
</details>

### Question 18
**An officer suspects child abuse but decides not to report it because the evidence is inconclusive. What is the legal consequence of willful failure to report suspected child abuse?**

A. No consequence if the evidence is inconclusive
B. Administrative reprimand only
C. Class A misdemeanor plus civil liability
D. Class B misdemeanor

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-36, willful failure to report suspected child abuse constitutes a CLASS A MISDEMEANOR and creates civil liability. The standard is suspected abuse — conclusive evidence is not required before reporting.
</details>

### Question 19
**In a lineup, how many total persons must be present?**

A. At least 4 (suspect plus 3 others)
B. At least 5 (suspect plus 4 others)
C. At least 6 (suspect plus 5 others)
D. At least 8 (suspect plus 7 others)

### Question 21
**The desk officer determines a prisoner is "high risk." What is the required escort protocol?**

A. One MOS assigned during transport only
B. Two MOS assigned during transport to court
C. Two MOS assigned to escort the prisoner at ALL times
D. One MOS with supervisor check-ins every 30 minutes

### Question 22
**A person has been arrested three times for Resisting Arrest in the past 4 years. What classification applies?**

A. No special classification
B. High risk prisoner
C. Resisting recidivist — enhance processing with detective squad
D. Violent felon — transfer to detective squad for processing

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-03, a "Resisting Recidivist" is a person arrested 2 or more times within the past 5 YEARS for Resisting Arrest, Assault 2nd (sub 3), Assault on PO, or Aggravated Assault on PO. Three arrests for Resisting Arrest in 4 years qualifies. Processing is enhanced with the detective squad.
</details>

### Question 23
**A foreign national from a mandatory notification country is arrested. The prisoner states they do NOT want their consulate notified. What should the officer do?**

A. Honor the prisoner's wishes and do not notify
B. Document the refusal and proceed without notification
C. Contact the Intelligence Division Operations Unit — mandatory notification is required regardless of the prisoner's wishes
D. Notify the prisoner's attorney instead

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C.** Per P.G. 208-56, for mandatory notification countries, consular notification is REQUIRED regardless of the prisoner's wishes. The Intelligence Division Operations Unit (available 24 hours) handles mandatory consular notifications.
</details>

### Question 24
**For non-mandatory notification countries, what is the officer's obligation regarding consular notification?**

A. Notify the consulate automatically
B. Inform the prisoner of their right to have their embassy/consulate notified
C. No obligation — consular notification is optional for non-mandatory countries
D. Contact the Intelligence Division for guidance

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-56, for non-mandatory notification countries, the officer must INFORM the prisoner of their right to have their embassy or consulate notified. The prisoner may then choose whether to exercise that right.
</details>

### Question 25
**During DAT processing, the OMNIFORM system shows no NYSID has been returned. How frequently should the officer check for results?**

A. Every 15 minutes
B. Every 30 minutes maximum
C. Every 60 minutes
D. Only once, then proceed without NYSID

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** Per P.G. 208-27, if no NYSID is returned during DAT processing, the officer should check back every 30 MINUTES maximum. The prisoner should not be held indefinitely waiting for NYSID results.
</details>
