# Section 208 — Arrests

## Source
- **Patrol Guide Section:** 208
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide1.pdf
- **Priority:** High
- **Status:** Completed

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the legal standards and departmental procedures for effecting arrests
- Know the processing requirements for different categories of arrests (felony, misdemeanor, violation)
- Identify the Patrol Supervisor's responsibilities during arrest processing
- Recognize specialized arrest procedures including warrant arrests and off-duty arrests
- Master the three levels of search and authorization requirements
- Know DAT eligibility criteria and the three categories of disqualification
- Understand family offense/DV definitions, concurrent jurisdiction, and mandatory arrest provisions
- Know Miranda warning requirements and juvenile interview restrictions
- Understand lineup/showup procedures and identification requirements
- Know consular notification obligations for foreign nationals

## Sergeant Focus Areas

### Key Supervisor Procedures
- **P.G. 208-01 — Law of Arrest:** Warrant scope (statewide vs county), warrantless arrest standards for petty offenses vs crimes, felony vs misdemeanor pursuit into private residences
- **P.G. 208-02 — Removal to Facility:** Supervisor verifies arrest at scene BEFORE removal; designates MOS to document all personnel present and BWC activation
- **P.G. 208-03 — General Processing:** High risk prisoner = TWO MOS escort at ALL times; Resisting Recidivist = 2+ qualifying arrests in 5 years; desk officer observes ALL prisoners; juvenile parent/guardian must be present for Miranda
- **P.G. 208-05 — Search Guidelines:** Three search levels; strip search requires reasonable suspicion + immediate supervisor approval; CO/Duty Captain resolves disagreements; desk officer has FINAL say on religious head covering
- **P.G. 208-09 — Rights in Custody:** Six-part Miranda; cease interrogation on request for attorney or silence; attorney must know prisoner's identity; muster room interview under continuous observation
- **P.G. 208-24 — Lineups/Showups:** Minimum 6 persons; suspect selects position; detective supervisor supervises entire procedure; do NOT advise suspect of right to attorney
- **P.G. 208-27 — DAT General:** Class E felonies, misdemeanors, violations eligible; Department REQUIRED to accept listed ID forms; desk officer makes FINAL determination on identity; never hold DAT-eligible prisoner solely for debriefing
- **P.G. 208-36 — Family Offenses/DV:** FCA vs NYPD expanded definitions; concurrent jurisdiction (18+, FCA relationship, family offense); interview separately; child abuse failure to report = Class A misdemeanor; crime against child = arrest, no DAT
- **P.G. 208-56 — Foreign Nationals:** Mandatory vs non-mandatory notification countries; Intelligence Division Operations Unit handles mandatory notifications
- **P.G. 208-69 — Notifications:** Operations Unit direct for government employees, MOS, food stamps >$500; Intelligence Division for UN employees, foreign armed forces, youth gangs
- **P.G. 208-81 — DAT Disqualifiers:** Three categories: Condition of Defendant (ID, warrant, FTA 2 years), Specific Charges (family offense, sex crime, hate crime), Order of Protection Needed (27 offenses)
- **P.G. 208-82 — DA Conferral:** Same day or next tour; if >4 days, reassign; 1st platoon extends into 2nd platoon
