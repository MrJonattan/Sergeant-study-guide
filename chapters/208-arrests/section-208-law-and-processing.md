# Section 208 — Law of Arrest and General Processing

Covers P.G. 208-01, 208-02, 208-03, 208-09, 208-10, 208-11, 208-12, 208-13, 208-15, 208-16, 208-17

---

## P.G. 208-01: Law of Arrest

### Arrest WITH Warrant

| Warrant Issued By | Scope of Execution |
|---|---|
| **NYC Criminal Court, District Court, or Superior Court** (addressed to MOS/Department) | Arrest **ANYTIME, ANYWHERE** in New York State |
| **City, Town, or Village Court** | Arrest within **county of issuance** or **ADJOINING county** only |

> **Exam Alert:** The key distinction is the issuing court. A NYC Criminal Court warrant has statewide reach; a city/town/village court warrant is limited to the county of issuance and adjoining counties.

### Arrest WITHOUT Warrant

| Situation | Standard Required | Geographical Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| **Any offense** committed in MOS's **presence** | Reasonable cause | Geographical area of employment |
| **Crime** (felony or misdemeanor) — in presence or NOT | Reasonable cause | Geographical area of employment |
| **Petty offense** (violation or traffic infraction) | In MOS's **presence** AND reasonable cause | Within **geographical area of employment** AND **county of occurrence or adjoining county** |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Petty offenses have the most restrictive requirements — the offense must be in the officer's presence, within the geographical area of employment, AND within the county of occurrence or adjoining county. Crimes (felonies and misdemeanors) require only reasonable cause and have no county limitation within the geographical area.

### Close Pursuit

| Offense Type | Pursuit Scope |
|---|---|
| **Petty offenses** | May pursue into **any part of the State** |
| **Crimes** | May pursue **outside the State** |

### Entry into Private Residence

| Suspect Category | Rules for Entry |
|---|---|
| **Felony suspect** fleeing into private residence | May **pursue** into the residence |
| **Misdemeanor suspect** fleeing into private residence | May enter **ONLY** to: prevent **imminent injury**, prevent **destruction of evidence**, prevent **escape**, or under **exigent circumstances**; otherwise need **warrant** or **consent** |

> **Exam Alert:** For misdemeanor suspects, mere flight into a residence is NOT sufficient justification for entry. One of the four specific conditions must exist, or the officer must obtain a warrant or consent.

## P.G. 208-02: Arrests — Removal to Facility

### Supervisor Verification at Scene

- Supervisor **verifies arrest at scene BEFORE removal** to facility
- Immediate supervisor **designates MOS at scene** to obtain names/tax numbers of ALL MOS present
- Designated MOS ascertains **BWC activation** for all MOS at scene

### Preferred Name

- **PREFERRED NAME:** The name an arrestee prefers to be called, based on their **gender identity**
- Officers must ascertain and use the arrestee's preferred name

### Child Present at Arrest

- **Immediately ascertain** if a CHILD IS PRESENT
- Effect arrest **outside child's sight and hearing** if possible
- Do **NOT display a firearm** in front of a child unless necessary for safety

### Medical/Psychological Inquiry

- **Immediately inquire** about medical or psychological treatment
- BWC must be **activated** during this inquiry

### Handcuffing

- Handcuff with **hands behind back** (standard procedure)
- **Pregnant prisoners:** Rear cuff absent **exceptional circumstances** only (i.e., rear cuffing is generally avoided for pregnant prisoners; use rear cuffing only when exceptional circumstances require it)

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor's verification at the scene BEFORE removal is a critical checkpoint. The supervisor must also ensure a member is designated to document all MOS present and BWC activation status.

## P.G. 208-03: Arrests — General Processing

### Immediate Actions Upon Arrest

1. **Field search/frisk** prisoner immediately
2. **Advise Miranda rights BEFORE any questioning**
3. Transport to facility for processing

### Juvenile Arrest Processing

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Notification | Notify **Juvenile Strategies Unit** (24/7) AND **parent/guardian IMMEDIATELY** |
| Miranda | Parent/guardian **must be present** for Miranda warnings |
| Parent objects to questioning | **NO questioning** even if juvenile is willing |

> **Exam Alert:** If a parent or guardian objects to questioning, questioning MUST stop — even if the juvenile wants to answer questions. The parent's objection overrides the juvenile's willingness.

### Desk Officer Responsibilities

| Duty | Details |
|---|---|
| **Escape risk assessment** | Determine escape risk for every prisoner |
| **"High risk" prisoner** | Assign **TWO MOS** to escort prisoner at **ALL times** |
| **Physical/mental condition** | Observe condition of **ALL prisoners** entering command |
| **Prisoner debriefing** | Ensure prisoner is debriefed |
| **Dependent inquiry** | Inquire about dependent adult/child at prisoner's residence |

### Resisting Recidivist

A **"Resisting Recidivist"** is a person arrested for any of the following charges **TWO or more times within the PAST 5 YEARS**:
- Resisting Arrest
- Assault 2nd Degree (subdivision 3)
- Assault on a Police Officer
- Aggravated Assault on a Police Officer

> **Action:** Enhance processing with the **detective squad** when a resisting recidivist is identified.

### Property Removal from Prisoner

Remove the following from the prisoner:
- **Unlawfully carried** items
- **Evidence** of crime
- **Dangerous items:** belts, shoelaces, drawstrings, glass items
- **Prescription drugs** (safeguard and document)

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer must personally observe the physical and mental condition of ALL prisoners entering the command. High-risk prisoners require TWO MOS escort at ALL times — not just during transport.

## P.G. 208-09: Rights of Persons in Custody

### Miranda Warnings — Six Parts

| # | Warning |
|---|---|
| 1 | You have the **right to remain silent** |
| 2 | **Anything you say** may be used against you |
| 3 | You have the right to an **attorney before and during questioning** |
| 4 | An attorney will be **provided if you cannot afford** one |
| 5 | You have the right to **remain silent until you consult** with an attorney |
| 6 | Are you **willing to answer questions**? |

### Cease Interrogation

- **Immediately cease** interrogation if the subject:
  - Requests an **attorney**, OR
  - Expresses a wish to **remain silent**

### Attorney Access

| Situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| Attorney requests interview | Desk Officer permits interview in **muster room** for a **reasonable time** |
| During interview | Assign MOS to keep prisoner and attorney under **continuous observation** |
| Objects passed | Ensure **no objects** are passed between prisoner and attorney |
| Attorney does NOT know prisoner's identity | Attorney is **NOT permitted** to interview the prisoner |

> **Exam Alert:** An attorney who cannot identify the prisoner by name is NOT permitted to conduct an interview. The attorney must know who they are there to see.

## P.G. 208-10: Prisoner Identification and Processing

Covers procedures for proper identification of prisoners during arrest processing, including fingerprinting, photographing, and NYSID verification.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure arresting officers follow identification procedures thoroughly. Misidentification can lead to wrongful release or processing errors.
