## P.G. 208-04: Arrest by Civilian

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **When a civilian may arrest** | For an **OFFENSE** committed **in their presence** |
| **MOS receiving custody** | Must determine if **legal basis** exists for the civilian arrest |
| **If legal basis exists** | Process the arrest; the civilian is the complainant |
| **If NO legal basis** | Do NOT process; advise the civilian of their options |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor must verify that the civilian's arrest meets the legal standard — the offense must have been committed in the civilian's presence. If the legal basis is questionable, consult with Legal Bureau.

## P.G. 208-24: Lineups and Showups

### Lineup Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Minimum participants** | Suspect + at least **5 OTHER persons** = minimum **6 total** |
| **Composition** | Same **sex, race**; approximately same **age, height, physical makeup** |
| **Clothing** | Participants must be **similarly clothed** |
| **Position selection** | Permit the suspect to **SELECT their position** in the lineup |
| **Supervision** | **Detective supervisor** personally supervises the **entire procedure** |

### Showup Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | A **prompt, on-the-scene, singly, one-to-one** display of a suspect to a witness |
| **When used** | Close in time and proximity to the crime |

### Critical Lineup Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Arrest required** | Suspect must be **ARRESTED** before being forced into a lineup (unless voluntary consent) |
| **Known parties** | Lineup is **NOT necessary** when victim and perpetrator are **KNOWN to each other** |
| **Right to attorney** | Do **NOT advise** suspect of right to attorney for lineup |
| **Witness separation** | **Prevent witnesses from speaking** to each other before, during, or after the lineup |

> **Exam Alert:** Two counter-intuitive rules: (1) Do NOT advise the suspect of right to attorney for a lineup, and (2) the suspect SELECTS their own position. Both are frequently tested.

## P.G. 208-56: Foreign Nationals

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Foreign national** | Any person who is **NOT a United States citizen**, regardless of immigration status |

### Notification Requirements

| Category | Action Required |
|---|---|
| **Mandatory notification countries** | Contact **Intelligence Division Operations Unit** (24 hours) — they handle consular notification |
| **Non-mandatory notification countries** | **INFORM the prisoner** of their right to have their embassy/consulate notified |

> **Exam Alert:** For mandatory notification countries, the Department notifies the consulate regardless of the prisoner's wishes. For non-mandatory countries, the prisoner has the RIGHT to notification but can decline. The Intelligence Division Operations Unit handles mandatory notifications.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must determine the citizenship status of all prisoners and ensure proper consular notification. Failure to provide mandatory notification can have diplomatic consequences and may compromise prosecution.

## P.G. 208-84: Early Diversion Initiative (Project Reset)

### Eligibility

| Criterion | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Age** | **18 years or older** |
| **Criminal history** | **First-time offenders** |
| **Charge type** | **Non-violent misdemeanors** |

### Purpose

Project Reset provides an alternative to traditional arrest processing by diverting eligible defendants to community-based programming, reducing the burden on the court system while providing appropriate intervention.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors should ensure that eligible defendants are identified for Project Reset during processing. The program criteria are narrow — age 18+, first-time offender, non-violent misdemeanor only.
