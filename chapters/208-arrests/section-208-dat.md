# Section 208 — Desk Appearance Ticket (DAT) Procedures

Covers P.G. 208-27, 208-81, 208-82

---

## P.G. 208-27: DAT — General Procedure

### What Is a DAT?

A **Desk Appearance Ticket (DAT)** is an appearance ticket issued **in lieu of continued detention** after arrest processing. It directs the defendant to appear in court at a later date.

### Eligible Offenses

| Offense Level | DAT Eligible? |
|---|---|
| **Class E Felonies** | Yes |
| **Misdemeanors** | Yes |
| **Violations** | Yes |
| Class A-D Felonies | No |

### Satisfactory Forms of Identification

The Department is **REQUIRED** to accept the following forms of ID:

| ID Type | Notes |
|---|---|
| NYS Driver's License | — |
| Passport | Any country |
| U.S. Military ID | — |
| Citizenship Papers | — |
| Out-of-State Driver's License | — |
| Resident Alien Card | — |
| Municipal ID | Including **Canadian** municipal ID |
| Public Benefit Card | — |

> **Exam Alert:** The Department is REQUIRED to accept all of the above. The desk officer makes the FINAL determination on identity, but these specific documents MUST be accepted as satisfactory.

### Key ID Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Photo ID requirement** | NO requirement for photo ID if identity is otherwise verifiable (e.g., personal knowledge of the defendant) |
| **Third party contact** | Allow defendant to contact a **third party** to bring identification |
| **Processing delay** | Delay processing for a **reasonable time** to allow ID to arrive |
| **Investigation Card** | An investigation card does **NOT preclude** DAT issuance |
| **DNA owed** | DNA owed is **NOT** a disqualifying factor for DAT |

### OMNIFORM Monitoring

- Monitor OMNIFORM **"DAT Processing"** screen
- If **no NYSID** returned, check back every **30 MINUTES maximum**
- Do not hold prisoner indefinitely waiting for NYSID

### Debriefing and DAT

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Desk Officer ensures prisoner is **debriefed** | Standard requirement |
| DAT-eligible prisoner held solely for debriefing | **NEVER** permitted |
| Exception | Unless there is **reasonable suspicion** of another crime |

> **Sergeant Focus:** A DAT-eligible prisoner should NEVER be held solely for debriefing purposes. The only exception is when there is reasonable suspicion that the prisoner is involved in another crime. Holding a DAT-eligible prisoner without legal basis exposes the Department to liability.

## P.G. 208-81: DAT — Disqualifying Factors

### Three Categories of Disqualification

#### Category 1: CONDITION OF DEFENDANT

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Not properly identified** | Cannot verify identity |
| **Open warrant** | Active warrant exists |
| **Failure to appear** | Failed to appear in court in the last **2 YEARS** |
| **Physical/mental condition** | Must **confer with Legal Bureau** before denying DAT on this basis |

> **Exam Alert:** Physical or mental condition alone does not automatically disqualify. The desk officer must CONFER WITH LEGAL BUREAU before using this as a basis to deny a DAT.

#### Category 2: SPECIFIC CHARGES

| Charge Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Family offense** | Any family offense charge |
| **Sex crime** | Article 130 offenses |
| **Hate crime** | Any bias-motivated offense |
| **Escape/absconding/bail jumping** | Escape from custody, failure to appear |
| **Weapon on school grounds** | Criminal possession of a weapon on school property |
| **License-suspendable crime** | VTL 1192 (DWI), Vehicular Assault, Leaving the Scene of an Accident |
| **A-misdemeanor or felony with harm** | Involving harm to identifiable person/property AND defendant has **pending prosecution** or **unreturned DAT** |

#### Category 3: REASONABLE BELIEF ORDER OF PROTECTION NEEDED

- **Strong possibility of violence** against the victim
- Applies to a list of **27 specific offenses**
- If an Order of Protection may be needed, DAT is not appropriate because the defendant must appear before a judge

> **Sergeant Focus:** The three categories are frequently tested. Remember: Category 1 = who the defendant IS, Category 2 = what the defendant DID, Category 3 = what the defendant MIGHT DO (future danger requiring court protection).

## P.G. 208-82: DAT — Conferral with District Attorney

### Conferral Timeline

| Situation | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Standard | Arresting officer **MUST** confer with DA's office the **SAME DAY** or **NEXT regularly scheduled tour** |
| Next tour > 4 DAYS away | Assign conferral to **another officer** |
| **1st platoon arrest** | Arresting officer **extends tour into 2nd platoon** to confer with DA |

> **Exam Alert:** The 4-day rule is critical. If the arresting officer's next regularly scheduled tour is more than 4 days away, the conferral responsibility must be reassigned. The 1st platoon/2nd platoon extension is also heavily tested — the officer does not wait until a later tour but extends into the next platoon.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must track DAT conferrals to ensure they happen within the required timeframe. A missed conferral can result in case dismissal.
