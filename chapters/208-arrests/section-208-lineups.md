# P.G. 208-24 — Identification Lineups/Showups

> **Date Issued:** 06/23/20 | **Date Effective:** 06/23/20 | **Pages:** 1–3

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Identification Lineup** | Placing a criminal suspect with **at least five other persons** for identification by victim/witnesses |
| **Identification Showup** | Prompt, **on-the-scene** presentation of a suspect **singly** (one-to-one display) for expeditious identification and/or early release of an innocent suspect |

---

## Key Procedures

### Before the Lineup

| Step | Rule |
|------|------|
| Doubt about legality | Resolve by conferring with **patrol supervisor/desk officer** |
| Arrest requirement | Suspect **must be arrested** before forced lineup; voluntary consent does not require arrest |
| When NOT needed | Witness/victim and perpetrator are **known to each other** (relatives, close acquaintances) OR perpetrator caught in the act by an officer |
| Miranda | Give warnings **only if suspect will be interrogated** before, during, or after |
| Inform suspect | Will appear in lineup for identification purposes |
| Right to attorney | Do **NOT** advise suspect of right to attorney for lineup |
| If attorney requested | Inform suspect there is **no right to a lawyer** for lineup |

### Detective Supervisor Responsibilities

The **detective supervisor** (or qualified investigative unit supervisor) must:

1. **Personally supervise** the entire lineup procedure
2. Interview witnesses **separately** before lineup
3. Obtain and record **description of suspect** from each witness
4. Prevent lineup participants from being **seen by witnesses** beforehand
5. Record all details: utterances, actions, witness responses, names/addresses of all present
6. Prepare **diagram with circles** for each position
7. Instruct witness to indicate circle — **not comment** on recognition
8. Mark and **retain diagram** after identification
9. Complete **LINEUP REPORT (PD373-141)**

### Lineup Composition Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Minimum participants | Suspect + **at least 5 others** (minimum 6 total) |
| Multiple suspects | **Separate lineup** for each suspect |
| Fillers | Use **different fillers** for each lineup |
| Participants must be | Same **sex and race** as suspect |
| Physical similarity | Approximately same **age, height, and physical makeup** |
| Clothing | **Similarly clothed**; no distinctive police uniform parts |
| Distinctive clothing | **Cover** unusual/distinctive clothing worn by suspect |

### Minors in Lineups (Under 18 as Non-Suspect Participants)
- Obtain **written permission** from parent/legal guardian on CONSENT FORM (PD 377-030)
- Isolate minors from harmful influences
- Have **precinct youth coordination officer** present if possible
- Prevent photographs from being used for non-evidence purposes

### Attorney at Lineup

| Situation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Attorney present at site | May **observe** lineup from room or any place where attorney cannot be seen |
| Attorney contacts police | Delay lineup for **reasonable time** to permit attorney to appear |
| Reasonable delay factors | Would the delay cause significant inconvenience to witness OR undermine advantages of prompt identification? |
| Attorney talking to witnesses | **NOT permitted** |
| Attorney suggestions | Direct to officer conducting lineup; consider if **reasonable and practical** |
| Attorney interference | **NOT permitted** |

## Sergeant Study Notes

### Key Numbers to Memorize
- **6** = minimum persons in a lineup (suspect + 5 others)
- **1** = showup is a one-to-one display
- **1** = separate lineup per suspect
- Different fillers for each separate lineup

### Common Exam Angles
1. **How many people must be in a lineup?** → At least 6 (suspect + 5 others)
2. **Who supervises the lineup?** → Detective supervisor or qualified investigative unit supervisor
3. **Does the suspect have a right to an attorney at a lineup?** → NO
4. **When is a lineup NOT necessary?** → When victim/perpetrator know each other or caught in the act
5. **Can the attorney observe?** → Yes, but cannot talk to witnesses or interfere
