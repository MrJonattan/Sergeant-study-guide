# Section 208 — Key Terms, Forms, and Abbreviations

---

## Core Arrest Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Arrest** | The taking of a person into custody in order that they may be held to answer for a criminal offense or violation | P.G. 208-01 |
| **Pick-Up Arrest** | An arrest of a person wanted by another command or agency | P.G. 208-17 |
| **Preferred Name** | The name an arrestee prefers to be called, based on their gender identity | P.G. 208-02 |
| **Probable Cause** | Facts and circumstances that would lead a reasonable person to believe a crime has been, is being, or is about to be committed | P.G. 208-01 |
| **Reasonable Suspicion (for strip search)** | Articulable facts supporting a belief that weapons, contraband, or evidence are concealed on the person; required standard for authorizing a strip search | P.G. 208-05 |

## Search Levels

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Frisk / Field Search** | External body examination conducted immediately after apprehension; special attention to waistband, armpit, collar, groin | P.G. 208-05 |
| **Search at Police Facility** | Thorough search by same-gender member with removal of outer garments; NOT the same as a strip search | P.G. 208-05 |
| **Strip Search** | Search requiring removal of undergarments; may NOT be conducted routinely; requires reasonable suspicion and approval of arresting officer's immediate supervisor or borough court section supervisor; same gender, secure area, utmost privacy | P.G. 208-05 |

## Prisoner Classification

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **High Risk Prisoner** | A prisoner determined by the desk officer to be an escape risk; requires **TWO MOS** assigned to escort at **ALL times** | P.G. 208-03 |
| **Resisting Recidivist** | A person arrested for Resisting Arrest, Assault 2nd (sub 3), Assault on PO, or Aggravated Assault on PO, **2 or more times** within the **past 5 years**; processing enhanced with detective squad | P.G. 208-03 |

## DAT-Related Terms

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **DAT (Desk Appearance Ticket)** | An appearance ticket issued in lieu of continued detention for Class E felonies, misdemeanors, and violations; directs defendant to appear in court at a later date | P.G. 208-27 |
| **Satisfactory Form of Identification** | NYS license, passport, US military ID, citizenship papers, out-of-state license, resident alien card, Municipal ID (including Canadian), Public Benefit Card; Department REQUIRED to accept these forms | P.G. 208-27 |

## Miranda Warnings — Six Parts

| # | Warning Text | Source |
|---|-------------|--------|
| 1 | You have the **right to remain silent** | P.G. 208-09 |
| 2 | **Anything you say** may be used against you | P.G. 208-09 |
| 3 | You have the right to an **attorney before and during** questioning | P.G. 208-09 |
| 4 | An attorney will be **provided if you cannot afford** one | P.G. 208-09 |
| 5 | You have the right to **remain silent until you consult** with an attorney | P.G. 208-09 |
| 6 | Are you **willing to answer** questions? | P.G. 208-09 |

## Domestic Violence / Family Offense Terms

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Family/Household (FCA Definition)** | Legally married, formerly married, related by marriage, related by blood, child in common, intimate relationship | P.G. 208-36 |
| **Family/Household (NYPD Expanded)** | FCA definition PLUS persons currently or formerly living together in a family-type relationship | P.G. 208-36 |
| **Family Offense** | Specific offenses committed between family/household members, including harassment, assault, disorderly conduct, stalking, menacing, sexual misconduct, criminal mischief, strangulation, identity theft, grand larceny, coercion, unlawful dissemination of intimate image | P.G. 208-36 |
| **Concurrent Jurisdiction** | When both Criminal Court and Family Court have jurisdiction; requires: (1) family offense, (2) FCA relationship, (3) offender 18+ (or 16-17 charged with felony-level family offense) | P.G. 208-36 |
| **Order of Protection** | A court order directing a person to refrain from certain conduct toward a protected party; violation may be a mandatory arrest | P.G. 208-37 |

## Identification Procedures

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Lineup** | An identification procedure where a suspect is placed among at least **5 other persons** (minimum **6 total**); same sex, race, approximate age/height/build; suspect selects position; detective supervisor supervises | P.G. 208-24 |
| **Showup** | A **prompt, on-the-scene, singly, one-to-one** display of a suspect to a witness; used close in time and proximity to the crime | P.G. 208-24 |

## Foreign National Terms

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Foreign National** | Any person who is **NOT a United States citizen**, regardless of immigration status | P.G. 208-56 |
| **Mandatory Notification Countries** | Countries requiring automatic consular notification upon arrest of their citizen, handled through the Intelligence Division Operations Unit (24 hrs) | P.G. 208-56 |

## Units and Abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Context |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **BWC** | Body-Worn Camera | Must be activated during medical/psychological inquiry and at arrest scene |
| **OLBS** | Online Booking System | Primary arrest processing system |
| **NYSID** | New York State Identification Number | Fingerprint-based identification; check every 30 min max during DAT processing |
| **DA** | District Attorney | Conferral required same day or next tour for DAT cases |
| **FCA** | Family Court Act | Defines family/household relationships for concurrent jurisdiction |
| **SVU** | Special Victims Unit | Notified for sex offenses and child abuse |
| **DOCCS** | Department of Corrections and Community Supervision | Notified for parole violator arrests |
| **TBTA** | Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority | Notified for offenses at TBTA facilities |
| **CUNY** | City University of New York | Notified for offenses at CUNY facilities |
| **DOE** | Department of Education | School Safety Division notified for DOE-related offenses |
| **VTL** | Vehicle and Traffic Law | VTL 1192 (DWI) is a DAT disqualifier |
