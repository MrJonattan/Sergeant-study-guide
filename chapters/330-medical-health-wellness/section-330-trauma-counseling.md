# Section 330 — Trauma Counseling Program

## A.G. 330-07: Trauma Counseling Program

**Date Effective:** 03/24/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 14 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To provide Trauma Counseling Services for members of the service (uniformed and civilian) involved in shooting incidents, disasters, or other violent occurrences resulting in death or injury.

### Scope — Referral Triggers

A member will be referred to the Trauma Counseling Program when involved in:

| Trigger |
|---------|
| Member is **shot or otherwise seriously injured** |
| Member **discharges a weapon** causing injury or death |
| Member causes, accidentally or otherwise, **serious physical injury or death** to another |
| Member directly involved where their **partner was killed or seriously injured** |
| Member directly involved in incidents or serious disasters where **multiple serious injuries and deaths** occurred |

### Trauma Counseling Team Response — Mandatory

The Trauma Counseling Team will respond to **all** incidents involving:
- Member of the service is shot, killed, or seriously injured in performance of duty
- Any incident pursuant to a request from a **captain or above**

### Procedure

| Role | Step | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| Desk Officer | 1 | Notify **Operations Unit** and provide details |
| Operations Unit Supervisor | 2 | Notify **Sick Desk supervisor**, provide details, request Trauma Counseling Team response |
| Sick Desk Supervisor | 3 | Ascertain location for team response and name of contact supervisor at scene |
| Sick Desk Supervisor | 4 | Arrange transportation of Trauma Team |
| Ranking Supervisor at Scene | 5 | Ensure members needing counseling are **available for interview** upon team arrival |
| Ranking Supervisor at Scene | 6 | Provide suitable place for trauma interview |

### Trauma Counseling Team Duties

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 7 | Respond to location and report to designated contact person |
| 8 | Respond to stationhouse of precinct of occurrence or other location to make contact with involved member(s) |
| 9 | Visit place of occurrence if necessary |
| 10 | Notify member they will be contacted for follow-up meeting |

### Follow-Up Schedule

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **48 hours** after event or initial contact | Follow-up meeting |
| **1 to 6 weeks** after incident | Additional counseling/interview sessions |

### Member Duties

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 11 | Cooperate with Trauma Counseling Team and keep appointments |
| 12 | Report to **Behavioral Care and Support Section** or other location **48 hours** after incident or initial contact |
| 12a | Report for additional follow-up meetings as scheduled |

### CO/Duty Captain Duties

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 13 | Cooperate with Trauma Counseling Team and permit access to involved members |
| 14 | Verify that involved members have been referred and contacted in person |
| 15 | Have **UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT (PD370-152)** prepared listing involved members contacted by Trauma Team |
| 16 | Forward copies of UOR, T.R.I. INCIDENT REPORT (if prepared), and other reports to CO, Medical Division |

### Confidentiality

**ALL conversation with the Trauma Counseling Team by members of the service (uniformed and civilian) will be STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL.**

### Additional Data

| Fact |
|------|
| Trauma Counseling Team staffed by Chief Surgeon, Director of Behavioral Care and Support Section, and licensed certified professionals of Medical Division |
| Team members will **NOT interfere** with ongoing investigations |
| Team members will NOT be assigned duties outside their Trauma Counselor functions when responding |
| Services also available for **personal or family tragedies** (violent criminal incidents, death in family, etc.) |
| Does NOT prevent voluntary use of or referral to any Employee Assistance Programs |
| Team members wear authorized nylon windbreaker jackets with **NYPD logo** on front and "N.Y.C. POLICE" on back |

> **Exam Alert:** Follow-up meeting at **48 hours** after event. Additional sessions within **1 to 6 weeks**. All conversations are **strictly confidential**. Trauma Team will NOT interfere with investigations.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure involved members are available for Trauma Team interview. Verify members have been contacted in person. Prepare UOR listing contacted members. Captain or above can request Trauma Team response to any incident.
