# Section 330 — Review Questions

## Multiple Choice

**1. The investigating supervisor for a line of duty injury must sign off on the LOD Injury Report:**

- A) Within 24 hours
- B) Within 48 hours
- C) Prior to completion of tour
- D) By end of the following tour

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Prior to completion of tour**

The investigating supervisor reviews for completeness and accuracy, and signs off electronically prior to completion of tour, absent exceptional circumstances. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

---

**2. For a line of duty injury occurring outside of New York City, the investigating supervisor must be in the rank of:**

- A) Sergeant or above
- B) Lieutenant or above
- C) Captain or above
- D) Inspector or above

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Captain or above**

The investigating supervisor for out-of-City line of duty injuries must be in the rank of captain or above. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

---

**3. The desk officer must notify the Sick Desk supervisor for a LOD injury when:**

- A) The injury occurred during any part of the member's tour
- B) The injury occurred during the first two hours of a tour
- C) The member was treated and released from the hospital
- D) The injury was witnessed by multiple members

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) The injury occurred during the first two hours of a tour**

Sick Desk notification is required when: incident was not specifically witnessed, injury in/near police facility, injury during first 2 hours of tour, or off-duty LOD designation request. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

**4. When a UMOS is treated at a hospital for a LOD injury, which address should be entered in hospital records?**

- A) The member's home address
- B) The precinct of occurrence address
- C) The Medical Division, 1 Lefrak City Plaza address
- D) The member's permanent command address

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The Medical Division, 1 Lefrak City Plaza address**

Only the Medical Division address (1 Lefrak City Plaza, 59-17 Junction Boulevard, Corona, NY 11368) may be entered in hospital records. Member's command, home address, and telephone number will not be disclosed. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

**5. When a UMOS is struck by a bullet on a protective vest and is apparently uninjured:**

- A) No further action is required
- B) The member may continue duty without medical evaluation
- C) The member must be examined by a doctor for blunt body trauma or internal injury
- D) The member is placed on sick report for 24 hours

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The member must be examined by a doctor for blunt body trauma or internal injury**

When an injured member is struck by a bullet on a protective vest and is apparently uninjured, the member must be examined by a doctor to ascertain if a blunt body trauma or internal injury has occurred. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

**6. The Trauma Counseling follow-up meeting should occur:**

- A) 24 hours after the event
- B) 48 hours after the event or initial contact
- C) One week after the event
- D) 30 days after the event

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 48 hours after the event or initial contact**

The member will be contacted for a follow-up meeting 48 hours after the event or initial contact. Additional counseling/interview sessions are scheduled within 1 to 6 weeks after the incident. (A.G. 330-07)

</details>

**9. The correct ratio for the bleach solution used to clean blood or body fluid spills is:**

- A) 1 part bleach to 5 parts water
- B) 1 part bleach to 10 parts water
- C) 1 part bleach to 20 parts water
- D) Undiluted bleach

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 1 part bleach to 10 parts water**

Accidental spills may be cleaned by applying a FRESHLY MIXED solution of 1 part household bleach to 10 parts water. The solution must be freshly mixed for each use and must not be mixed with any solution other than water. (A.G. 330-09)

</details>

**10. When handling hypodermic needles, members should use the:**

- A) Two-handed grip technique
- B) One-handed "scoop" technique
- C) Pliers and container method
- D) Gloved removal technique

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) One-handed "scoop" technique**

Members use a one-handed "scoop" technique, using the needle itself to pick up the container, moving the needle away from themselves. Members should NOT hold the container and move their hand toward the needle. Under no circumstances should a member attempt to remove, dislodge, or bend a needle. (A.G. 330-09)

</details>

**11. When multiple individuals are exposed to an infectious disease at a scene, who coordinates documentation?**

- A) The desk officer
- B) The patrol supervisor at the scene
- C) The first member to arrive
- D) Emergency Medical Services

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) The patrol supervisor at the scene**

If there are numerous individuals involved, the patrol supervisor at the scene will be responsible for the coordination of information and documenting the names of the members of the service involved. (A.G. 330-09)

</details>

**12. Under A.G. 330-09, to avoid unnecessary exposure at a scene, control of an aided victim suspected of having an infectious disease should be assigned to:**

- A) The patrol supervisor
- B) EMS personnel only
- C) An officer who has already had contact with the person
- D) The next available officer

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) An officer who has already had contact with the person**

To avoid unnecessary exposure, control of the aided/collision victim or prisoner will be assigned to an officer at the scene who has already had contact with a person suspected of having an infectious disease. (A.G. 330-09)

</details>

**13. If a LOD designation cannot be made expeditiously, the ranking officer must forward a Typed Letterhead to Medical Division within:**

- A) 30 days
- B) 60 days
- C) 90 days
- D) 120 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 60 days**

When a LOD designation cannot be made expeditiously, a report on Typed Letterhead indicating reasons for the delay must be forwarded to Medical Division within 60 days. Subsequent reports are forwarded for each additional 60-day period. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

---

**14. If an injury comes under the Federal Employees Compensation Act (FECA), the CO must forward documentation to Medical Division within:**

- A) 30 days
- B) 60 days
- C) 90 days
- D) 180 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 90 days**

If the injury comes under FECA, the member's CO will forward items (LOD Injury Report, witness statements, arrest history, criminal court complaint, firearms trace, completed DOL form BEC-721a) to Medical Division, Federal Compensation Liaison Officer, within 90 days of occurrence. (A.G. 330-03)

</details>

---

**15. The bleach solution for cleaning blood spills should be disposed of:**

- A) In any available drain
- B) In a urinal or toilet
- C) In a sink only
- D) In a hazardous waste container

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) In a sink only**

Disposal of bleach or bleach dilutions should be performed only in a sink (not a urinal or toilet, since they sometimes contain chemical deodorizers that may cause toxic gas when mixed with bleach). (A.G. 330-09)

</details>
