# Section 330 — Key Terms and Definitions

## Line of Duty Injury

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Investigating Supervisor** | Sergeant or above from injured member's command; if unavailable, from precinct of occurrence |
| **Out of City LOD** | Investigating supervisor must be **captain or above** |
| **Line of Duty Injury Report** | Prepared via LOD Injury Reporting System (intranet or smartphone) |
| **FECA** | Federal Employees Compensation Act — benefits when Federal statute violated during apprehension |

## Trauma Counseling

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Trauma Counseling Team** | Staffed by Chief Surgeon, Director of Behavioral Care and Support Section, and licensed professionals of Medical Division |
| **Behavioral Care and Support Section** | Location for 48-hour follow-up meeting |
| **POPPA** | Police Organization Providing Peer Assistance — separate from Trauma Counseling |
| **Employee Assistance Programs** | Multiple programs available; Trauma Counseling does not prevent voluntary use of any |

## Infectious Disease/Hazmat

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Infectious Disease** | Diseases transmitted by contact with blood, body fluids, animal saliva, or CNS tissue |
| **Hazardous Material** | Any chemical, biological, or radiological health hazard |
| **Universal Precautions** | Treat all blood and body fluids as infectious for HIV, HBV, and other bloodborne pathogens |
| **Designated Officer** | Individual designated under Ryan White Act (42 U.S.C. 300ff-86) for exposure determinations |

## Key Forms

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **WITNESS STATEMENT - INJURY TO MEMBER (PD429-065)** | Non-Department witness statement for LOD injury |
| **Police Accident Report (MV104AN)** | If vehicle collision involved in LOD injury |
| **AIDED REPORT (PD304-152)** | If no vehicle involved in LOD injury |
| **UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT (PD370-152)** | For LOD death/serious injury; also for Trauma Counseling incidents |
| **SCOC M187** | Report of Inmate Death to NYS Commission of Correction |
| **Biohazard Label** | Affixed to contaminated property, evidence, or waste |

## Key Timeframes

| Item | Timeframe |
|------|-----------|
| LOD Injury Report sign-off by investigating supervisor | **Before end of tour** |
| Trauma Counseling follow-up meeting | **48 hours** after event |
| Additional trauma counseling sessions | **1 to 6 weeks** after incident |
| FECA documentation forwarding | Within **90 days** of occurrence |
| Delayed LOD designation report | Within **60 days** (then every 60 days) |
| Exposure report number | Obtained via LOD Injury Reporting System |

## Sick Desk Notification Triggers (LOD Injury)

| Trigger |
|---------|
| Incident NOT specifically witnessed |
| Injury in or near any **police facility** |
| Injury during **first 2 hours** of tour |
| Off-duty LOD designation request |

## Bleach Solution for Blood/Body Fluid Spills

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Ratio | **1 part bleach : 10 parts water** |
| Preparation | FRESHLY MIXED for each use |
| Do NOT mix with | Any solution other than water |
| Do NOT use on | Uniforms (causes damage) or hands |
| Disposal | In sink only (not urinal/toilet — chemical deodorizers cause toxic gas) |

## Needle Handling

| Rule |
|------|
| One-handed "scoop" technique |
| Move needle AWAY from yourself |
| NEVER remove, dislodge, or bend a needle |
| Cap container if possible; tape if syringe extends |
