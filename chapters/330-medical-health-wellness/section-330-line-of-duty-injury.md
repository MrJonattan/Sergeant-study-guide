# Section 330 — Line of Duty Injury or Death

## A.G. 330-03: Line of Duty Injury or Death

**Date Effective:** 02/03/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 8 | **Pages:** 5

### Purpose

To report and record line of duty injuries and deaths occurring within the City or outside of the City.

### Definition

**INVESTIGATING SUPERVISOR** — A supervisory UMOS in the rank of **sergeant or above** (patrol supervisor, platoon commander, duty captain, etc.) assigned to the injured UMOS's command. If unavailable, a supervisory UMOS within the precinct of occurrence.

### Injured Member Duties

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Request supervisor to respond |
| 2 | Notify desk officer of: (a) circumstances of injury, (b) names/addresses of witnesses |
| 3 | Request witnesses to await arrival of supervisor |
| 4 | Remain at scene unless: (a) hospitalization/medical attention required, (b) further police action necessary |
| 5 | Notify local police authorities and **Operations Unit immediately** if reporting LOD injury from **outside of City** |

### Investigating Supervisor (IS) — Who Qualifies

| Scenario | Minimum Rank |
|----------|-------------|
| Normal LOD (within city) | **Sergeant or above** from member's command |
| If unavailable | Supervisor from precinct of occurrence |
| **Out of city** | **Captain or above** |
| Force against us (P.G. 221-06) | Supervisor uninvolved, AND **1 rank higher** than all involved members |

> **Exam Alert:** The investigating supervisor hierarchy is a frequent test topic. Within the city = Sgt+. Out of city = Capt+. Force incidents = uninvolved AND 1 rank higher.

### Investigating Supervisor Duties

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 6 | Respond to scene and request medical attention if necessary |
| 7 | Interview injured member and investigate circumstances |
| 8 | Interview witnesses and ensure preparation of: (a) WITNESS STATEMENT - INJURY TO MEMBER OF THE DEPARTMENT (PD429-065) for non-Department witnesses, (b) Line of Duty Injury Reporting System witness statement for MOS witnesses |
| 9 | Prepare Police Accident Report (MV104AN) if vehicle collision involved |
| 10 | Direct injured member to prepare AIDED REPORT (PD304-152) if no vehicle involved |
| 11 | Comply with P.G. 221-06 if any force used by UMOS or civilian and UMOS sustains injury |
| 12 | Prepare Line of Duty Injury Report if injured member is incapacitated |

### Investigating Supervisor and the 3 Reports

> **Memory Aid — IS and the 3 Reports:**
> - IS **always** prepares the **PAR** (Police Accident Report)
> - IS **never** prepares the **AIDED** (directs injured MOS to do it; if incapacitated, assigns another member)
> - IS **sometimes** prepares the **LODI** (only if injured member is incapacitated)

### Out of City LOD Injuries

Investigating supervisor must be in the rank of **captain or above**.

### Line of Duty Injury Report

| Rule |
|------|
| Prepared using **Line of Duty Injury Reporting System** on Department intranet or smartphone |
| Include: witness information, documents related to injury (photographs, hospital discharge paperwork) |
| Submitted electronically to investigating supervisor |
| Investigating supervisor reviews for completeness/accuracy and signs off electronically **prior to completion of tour** (absent exceptional circumstances) |
| Include recommendation for approval/disapproval of LOD designation |
| Include findings of review of any available **BWC or other video surveillance** |

### Desk Officer Notifications

> **Memory Aid — D.O. Notification Tiers:**
>
> | Condition | Notify |
> |-----------|--------|
> | MOS not from precinct of occurrence | **MOS's own command** |
> | MOS is DOA or likely | Boro C.O. (not XO), Pct CO/XO/Duty Capt, Operations, **Medical Examiner or Assistant** |
> | MOS requires hospital treatment | **Operations** |
> | Not witnessed AND in/around stationhouse, 1st 2 hours, off-duty | **Sick Desk Boss or Sick Desk** |

| Notify When |
|-------------|
| Injured member's command (if not assigned to precinct of occurrence) |
| Borough commander, CO/XO/duty captain, Operations Unit, Medical Examiner — if member **dies or likely to die** |
| Operations Unit — if member requires **hospital treatment** |
| Sick Desk supervisor when any of these conditions exist: |

### Sick Desk Notification Triggers

| Condition |
|----------|
| Incident causing injury was NOT specifically witnessed |
| Injury occurred within any **police facility** (building, office, etc.) |
| Injury occurred in immediate vicinity of police facility (parking lot, perimeter sidewalk) |
| Injury occurred during **first two hours of a tour** |
| Any request for LOD designation by an **off-duty** UMOS |

> **Exam Alert:** Think about it this way: if none of the Sick Desk trigger conditions exist, the officer is from the precinct, the incident was witnessed, the MOS was on-duty, and it did not happen in/near the stationhouse — the Desk Officer has **no** Sick Desk notification duty.

### CO Duties

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 17 | Review all LOD Injury Reports via Reporting System application |
| 18 | Prepare Typed Letterhead to CO, Medical Division when: (a) injured member is **PPO**, (b) injury occurred while **off-duty**, (c) injury reported **after end of tour** |
| 19 | Prepare Typed Letterhead for LOD death or serious injury/likely to die → forward per P.G. 212-09 "Unusual Occurrence Reports" |
| 20 | Comply with A.G. 330-07, "Trauma Counseling Program" |
| 21 | Forward copies to PPF if LOD death or serious injury/likely to die |

### Hospital Privacy Rule

When treated at hospital for LOD injury, **ONLY** the following address/phone may be entered in hospital records:
- **Medical Division**, 1 Lefrak City Plaza, 59-17 Junction Boulevard, Corona, NY 11368
- Member's command, home address, and telephone number will **NOT** be disclosed unless authorized supervisory officer determines requesting person is entitled

### When Member Is Hospitalized or Dies

Patrol supervisor ensures:
- Firearms, Department property (shield, ID, nameplate), and personal effects are **removed and safeguarded**
- Member struck by bullet on protective vest and apparently uninjured → must be examined by doctor for blunt body trauma or internal injury

> **Exam Alert:** If MOS is DOA, also follow **P.G. 216-04, Dead Body**. For Department vehicle collisions (P.G. 217-06), the patrol supervisor enters the **command address** as operator's address on the PAR.

### Hospital Response — Serious Injury/Death in Line of Duty

Operations Unit notifies the following to respond to hospital:

| Respond | Also Respond |
|---------|-------------|
| Police Commissioner | Commanding Officer, Family Assistance Section |
| First Deputy Commissioner (if PC unavailable) | Lt, Family Assistance Section (borough concerned) |
| Chief of Department | Patrol borough CO |
| Chief of Detectives | CO/duty captain (prepares UOR) |
| Bureau chief of injured/deceased | Detective borough commander |
| Deputy Commissioner, Public Information | Detective assigned to investigate |
| Supervising Chief Surgeon | Crime Scene Unit detective/technician |
| Department Chaplain | Line organization representative |
| Duty inspector (control access to ER) | EAU representative |
| Patrol borough operations commander (if DI unavailable) | POPPA representative |

### Permitted to Speak to Injured/View Deceased (MOS DOA/SILD)

Applies in residence counties too.

**Supervisors (5):**
- PC, 1st Deputy, Chief of Department, Bureau Chief, Patrol Borough C.O. or **representative**

**Investigators (2):**
- Detective assigned to the case, CSU detective/technician

**Fraternal and Religious (2):**
- Union representative, Department Chaplain

**Under Certain Circumstances — Qualified (2):**
- ESU member (if transported by Department ambulance)
- Policewomen's Endowment Association representative (if member identifies as female)

> **Memory Aid — Who Can Speak/View: 5-2-2-2:**
> 5 Supervisors, 2 Investigators, 2 Fraternal/Religious, 2 Conditional

### FECA (Federal Employees Compensation Act)

| Rule |
|------|
| Members may be entitled to medical and financial benefits under FECA if a **Federal statute** was violated and injury occurred while apprehending |
| Investigating supervisor must indicate on LOD Injury Report whether FECA applies |
| If FECA applies → CO forwards items to Medical Division, Federal Compensation Liaison Officer, within **90 days** |

### Delayed LOD Designation

| Rule |
|------|
| If designation cannot be made expeditiously → Typed Letterhead with reasons forwarded to Medical Division within **60 days** |
| Subsequent reports every **60 days** thereafter if necessary |

> **Exam Alert:** Investigating supervisor for out-of-city LOD injuries must be **captain or above**. Sick Desk must be notified when injury is during first **2 hours** of tour, in/near police facility, not witnessed, or off-duty request. Hospital records receive **Medical Division address only** — not member's personal info.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sign off LOD Injury Report electronically **before end of tour**. Include BWC/video review findings. If member dies or likely to die, ensure firearms and Department property are safeguarded. Comply with P.G. 221-06 if any force was used. FECA indicator required on LOD report.
