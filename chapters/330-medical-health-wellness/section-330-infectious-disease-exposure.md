# Section 330 — Exposure to Infectious Diseases or Hazardous Materials

## A.G. 330-09: Exposure of Members of the Service to Infectious Diseases or Hazardous Materials

**Date Effective:** 11/18/21 | **Last Revision:** I.O. 99 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To provide instructions and minimize risk to members of the service (uniformed and civilian) who have contact with, or handle, an animal or person who may have an infectious disease, or who have contact with or handle hazardous materials.

### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Infectious Disease** | Diseases transmitted by contact with infected animal's saliva/CNS tissue, or infected individual's blood or body fluids |
| **Hazardous Material** | Any chemical, biological, or radiological substance which is a health hazard (carcinogens, toxic agents, irritants, corrosives, flammable/combustible liquids, compressed gas, explosives) |
| **Exposure to Infectious Disease** | Airborne transmission or direct contact with individual suspected of having disease; contact with blood/body fluids; human/animal bite; hypodermic needle puncture; contaminated sharp instrument |
| **Exposure to Hazardous Material** | Exposure through inhalation, ingestion, skin contact, or parenteral contact |
| **Universal Precautions** | Approach to infection control: treat all human blood and certain body fluids as if infectious for HIV, Hepatitis B, or other bloodborne pathogens; treat all animal saliva and CNS tissue as if infectious for rabies |
| **Designated Officer** | Individual designated under 42 U.S.C. 300ff-86 to execute provisions of Ryan White Act regarding emergency response employees requesting exposure determination |

### Scene Management Note

To avoid unnecessary exposure, control of the aided/collision victim or prisoner will be assigned to an officer at the scene who has **already had contact** with the person suspected of having an infectious disease. If numerous individuals involved, the **patrol supervisor** coordinates information and documents names of involved MOS.

### Procedure — Upon Exposure

| Role | Step | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| Member | 1 | Comply with guidelines in Additional Data |
| Member | 2 | Notify desk officer, precinct of occurrence |
| Desk Officer | 3 | Obtain **exposure report number** via Line of Duty Injury Reporting System |
| Desk Officer | 4 | Make Command Log entry (include exposure report number) |
| Desk Officer | 5 | Notify **designated Department surgeon** of facts |
| Desk Officer | 6 | Contact member and advise of necessary treatment |
| Member | 7 | Comply with directions of Department surgeon |
| Member | 8 | Make digital Activity Log entry (include exposure report number) |

### When Exposure Involves Actual Injury

- Member transported to hospital for treatment
- Comply with **A.G. 330-03** (Line of Duty Injury or Death)

### Tuberculosis Exposure (No Actual Injury)

- Department offers free Mantoux (PPD) skin testing
- If results positive → notify **Unit Safety Officer** to initiate entry on DOSH 900 log
- Code (e.g., maiden name pseudonym) may substitute for description in column F

### Infectious Disease Guidelines

| Guideline |
|----------|
| Confer with EMS, family, friends, neighbors, doctor, or agency about source individual's medical conditions |
| Wear **disposable gloves** if contact with blood/body fluids possible |
| Heavy-duty rubber gloves for routine capture/seizure of suspected rabid animals |
| **Wash hands** with soap and water (disinfectant soap if possible) after removing gloves |
| Immediately wash unprotected body parts if contact with blood/body fluids or animal bite/scratch |
| Machine wash (hot cycle) or dry clean uniform/civilian clothes that contact blood/body fluids |
| Use **extraordinary care** to avoid accidental needle puncture wounds |
| Affix **Biohazard Label** to any property, evidence, or waste that may be contaminated |

### Accidental Blood/Body Fluid Spills

| Rule |
|------|
| Supply of household bleach maintained at all precinct/PSA/transit district commands, ESU, borough court section facilities |
| Clean spills with **FRESHLY MIXED** solution: **1 part bleach : 10 parts water** |
| Bleach must NOT be mixed with any solution other than water |
| Must be freshly mixed for each use |
| Do NOT use bleach solution to clean **uniforms** (will cause damage) — use routine laundering or dry cleaning |
| Do NOT use bleach to cleanse **hands** |
| Dispose of bleach dilutions in **sink only** (not urinal or toilet — may contain chemical deodorizers causing toxic gas) |

### Hypodermic Needle Handling

| Rule |
|------|
| Place in hypodermic needle/syringe container using **one-handed "scoop" technique** |
| Move needle **away** from yourself (hold syringe, move toward container) |
| Do NOT hold container and move hand toward needle |
| Cap container if possible; if syringe extends beyond container, secure with tape |
| **NEVER** attempt to remove, dislodge, or bend a needle from a hypodermic syringe |

### Ryan White Act — Designated Officer

| Rule |
|------|
| Emergency response employees may request determination of exposure through Department's designated officer at Medical Division, Hazmat Unit |
| If exposure determined → signed written request sent to medical facility where victim was transported |
| Designated officer must **immediately** notify member(s) upon receiving notification from medical facility |
| HIV-related information disclosed to member → **redisclosure prohibited** when not in conformance with state law |

> **Exam Alert:** Bleach solution ratio: **1 part bleach : 10 parts water**, freshly mixed. Hypodermic needles handled with **one-handed "scoop" technique** — NEVER remove, dislodge, or bend. Assign the officer who already had contact to continue handling the person.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Coordinate documentation of all involved MOS names when numerous individuals are exposed. Ensure exposure report number obtained via LOD Injury Reporting System. If actual injury → comply with A.G. 330-03. Know the bleach ratio and needle handling rules for roll call training.
