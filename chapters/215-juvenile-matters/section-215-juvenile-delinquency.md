## P.G. 215-09 — Offense by Juvenile Under 18 (Not JO/AO)

**Purpose:** To process a juvenile who has committed an offense but does not qualify as a Juvenile Offender (JO) or Adolescent Offender (AO). This is the most detailed and exam-critical procedure in Section 215.

### Definitions

> **Exam Alert:** These age definitions are heavily tested. Know them precisely.

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Juvenile Delinquent (JD)** | A person who is at least 12 years of age but less than 18 years of age who commits an act that would be a crime if committed by an adult. For homicide offenses, a child under 12 may also be classified as a JD. |
| **Family/Household** | Includes persons who: (a) are related by blood or marriage; (b) are or were legally married; (c) have a child in common (regardless of marriage); (d) are or were in an intimate relationship; (e) reside or have resided in the same household |

### Procedure — Before Arrest

> **Exam Alert:** The Juvenile Strategies Unit must be contacted BEFORE beginning the arrest process. This is a mandatory step.

**Uniformed Member of the Service:**

1. Contact the **Juvenile Strategies Unit** BEFORE beginning arrest process
2. For robbery designated as a delinquent act: confer with Corporation Counsel regarding the appropriate charge
3. Notify parent/guardian immediately

### Miranda Warnings — Juvenile Specific

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most tested topics in Section 215. The parent/guardian plays a critical role in Miranda.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| When to advise | Before ANY questioning |
| Who must be advised | BOTH the juvenile AND the parent/guardian |
| Form used | MIRANDA WARNINGS FOR JUVENILE INTERROGATIONS (PD244-1413) |
| Parent/guardian presence | Must be present when Miranda warnings are read |
| Parent objects to questioning | **NO questioning permitted** — even if juvenile is willing |
| Parent requests attorney | **NO questioning permitted** — even if juvenile is willing |

> **Exam Alert:** If the parent/guardian objects to questioning OR requests an attorney, questioning MUST STOP — even if the juvenile states willingness to speak. The parent's objection overrides the juvenile's consent.

### Procedure — After Arrest

**Desk Officer:**

- Ensure the REPORT OF SUSPECTED CHILD ABUSE OR MALTREATMENT (PD377-154) is prepared when abuse is suspected
- Ensure JUVENILE REPORT SYSTEM WORKSHEET and COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET are completed
- Ensure AFFIDAVIT OF NOTIFICATION OF RIGHTS (PD244-1413) is completed with parent/guardian present

### Transport Restriction

> **Exam Alert:** A juvenile must NEVER be transported with an adult prisoner.

### Family Court Appearance

> **Exam Alert:** The juvenile must appear in Family Court at **0900 hours on the next business day**.

| Appearance Requirement | Detail |
|------------------------|--------|
| Court | Family Court |
| Time | 0900 hours |
| Day | Next business day |

### Designated Felony

For certain serious offenses designated as felonies (robbery, etc.):
- Confer with Corporation Counsel regarding appropriate charge designation
- The offense may be designated as either a delinquent act or a designated felony

### Release and Recognizance

- Juvenile Delinquent may be released on personal recognizance for designated felonies: appear at 0900 hours on next business day
- Released on recognizance for non-designated felonies: appear at 0900 hours on next business day
- Arresting officer excused from court appearance

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure the desk officer has verified that (1) the Juvenile Strategies Unit was contacted before arrest, (2) parent/guardian was notified immediately, (3) Miranda warnings were administered with parent/guardian present using PD244-1413, and (4) no questioning occurred if the parent objected or requested an attorney.

### Additional Data

## P.G. 215-11 — Arrest of Juvenile Unlawfully in Certain Licensed Premises

**Purpose:** To take a juvenile found in certain licensed premises into protective custody.

### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Juvenile** | An individual actually or apparently under 18 years of age |
| **Licensed Premises** | Public dance halls, cabarets, catering establishments, bars and grills, pool and billiard parlors, bowling alleys, skating rinks, theaters, motion picture theaters |

### Uniformed Member of the Service

1. Ascertain age of the juvenile
2. Determine if presence is unlawful
3. Take juvenile into protective custody if presence in premises is in violation of law and arrest proprietor or person in charge
   - a. If a premise is a **pool/billiard parlor or bowling alley**, issue a **summons in lieu of arrest**
   - b. If proprietor/person in charge is arrested, indicate under "Details" of ON LINE BOOKING SYSTEM ARREST WORKSHEET (PD244-159) a brief statement indicating person in charge permitted or had knowledge of violation and actions that led arresting officer to this conclusion
4. Escort juvenile home, if residence is located within city
   - a. If residence of juvenile is outside city limits, handle as "Child Requiring Shelter" until provision is made for returning juvenile home
5. Inform parents of juvenile of unlawful presence and question concerning reason
6. Advise parent of legal responsibilities
7. Prepare JUVENILE REPORT SYSTEM WORKSHEET (PD377-159A) and COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A), per P.G. 215-08, and include under "Details" section:
   - a. Occupation of parent/guardian
   - b. Statements of parent/guardian
8. Submit WORKSHEETS to desk officer

### Desk Officer

9. Review WORKSHEETS for accuracy and sign
10. Process in normal manner

### Commanding Officer

> **Sergeant Focus:** The CO must forward a report to the Chief of Department for specific premises types.

11. Forward report of action taken to Chief of Department, Investigation Review Section, DIRECT, if premises is:
    - a. Catering establishment
    - b. Cabaret
    - c. Public dance hall

### Forms and Reports

- JUVENILE REPORT SYSTEM WORKSHEET (PD377-159A)
