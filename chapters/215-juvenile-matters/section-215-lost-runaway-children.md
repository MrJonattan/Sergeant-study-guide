## P.G. 215-02 — Lost Child

**Purpose:** To locate relatives or friends of a lost child.

### Uniformed Member of the Service

1. Notify desk officer/counterpart and radio dispatcher
2. Make brief inquiry in vicinity of place where found
3. Bring child to command if relative not located
4. Prepare AIDED REPORT

### Desk Officer

5. Telephone Missing Persons Squad and give description
6. Complete captions on AIDED REPORT and process in normal manner:
   - a. Assign female uniformed member of the service to watch child in the command, if available, or other available uniformed member of the service, when necessary
   - b. Notify youth coordination officer
   - c. If child is not claimed within a reasonable amount of time, notify the Administration for Children's Services (ACS) and request that they send a representative to the stationhouse to provide necessary transportation to shelter (see P.G. 215-03 for ACS borough field office and Emergency Children's Services addresses)
   - d. If ACS is unable to provide transportation, assign a female uniformed member of the service, if available, or other available uniformed members of the service, to escort child to the shelter
   - e. Inform Missing Persons Squad if child is moved to another location

> **Exam Alert:** Step 7 is critical — abuse/neglect screening is mandatory.

7. In cases that could involve abuse, neglect, maltreatment or abandonment, prepare REPORT OF SUSPECTED CHILD ABUSE OR MALTREATMENT (PD377-154) and related forms, as required:
   - a. Notify precinct detective squad if the child is **eleven years of age or older** OR Special Victims Unit if child is **under eleven years of age**
   - b. For allegations made of any sex crime to any child, notify the Special Victims Unit

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure the desk officer completes the full notification chain — Missing Persons Squad in all cases, ACS if child unclaimed, and detective squad/SVU if abuse is suspected.

### Age-Based Notification for Suspected Abuse

| Child's Age | Notify |
|-------------|--------|
| 11 years or older | Precinct detective squad |
| Under 11 years | Special Victims Unit (SVU) |
| Any sex crime allegation (any age) | Special Victims Unit (SVU) |

## P.G. 215-05 — Runaway Children

**Purpose:** To report and process runaway children and arrange for their care.

### Definitions

- **Runaway Child (New York):** A child under 16 years of age who has left home without the consent of a parent or guardian
- **Runaway Child (Interstate Compact):** A child from out of state, handled per the Interstate Compact on Juveniles

### Uniformed Member of the Service

1. Take child into custody
2. Prepare AIDED REPORT
3. Prepare JUVENILE REPORT SYSTEM WORKSHEET

### Desk Officer

> **Exam Alert:** The desk officer must notify Missing Persons Squad in ALL runaway cases — not just when the child is unclaimed.

4. Interview the child
5. Notify Missing Persons Squad in ALL cases
6. Contact parent/guardian
7. Arrange shelter if unable to release child to parent/guardian

> **Sergeant Focus:** Verify that the desk officer has made all required notifications. For runaway children, Missing Persons Squad notification is mandatory regardless of whether the parent has been contacted.

### Forms and Reports

- AIDED REPORT
- JUVENILE REPORT SYSTEM WORKSHEET (PD377-159A)
