## P.G. 215-14 — Child Advocacy Referrals

**Purpose:** To ensure proper notifications and referrals when a child is a victim of abuse or a sex crime.

### Applicability

> **Exam Alert:** Know the two triggering conditions — they have different age thresholds.

| Condition | Trigger |
|-----------|---------|
| Child abuse victim | Child **under 11 years of age** |
| Sex crime victim | Child **under 18 years of age** |

### Notification Chain

> **Exam Alert:** ACS Instant Response Team (IRT) notification is required when a child is removed or an arrest is made.

1. Notify **ACS Instant Response Team (IRT)** when:
   - A child is removed from the home, OR
   - An arrest is made
2. Notify **New York State Central Registry**
3. **Desk Officer** must notify:
   - a. **Special Victims Unit** (first notification)
   - b. Appropriate borough **Child Abuse Squad**

### Notification Summary Table

| Action | Who to Notify |
|--------|--------------|
| Child removed or arrest made | ACS Instant Response Team (IRT) |
| All cases | NY State Central Registry |
| Desk officer notification (1st) | Special Victims Unit (SVU) |
| Desk officer notification (2nd) | Borough Child Abuse Squad |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Verify the desk officer has completed the full notification chain: ACS IRT, State Central Registry, SVU, and the appropriate borough Child Abuse Squad. Ensure the REPORT OF SUSPECTED CHILD ABUSE OR MALTREATMENT (PD377-154) is prepared.

### Forms and Reports

- REPORT OF SUSPECTED CHILD ABUSE OR MALTREATMENT (PD377-154)
- COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A)
- JUVENILE REPORT SYSTEM WORKSHEET (PD377-159A)

### Related Procedures

- Emergency Removals or Investigations and Reporting of Abused, Neglected or Maltreated Children (P.G. 215-03)
- On-Line Juvenile Report System (P.G. 215-08)

## P.G. 215-22 — Requests for Assistance from ACS Child Protection Specialists

**Purpose:** To ensure the safety of all persons present when responding to requests for assistance by Administration for Children's Services (ACS) Child Protection Specialists.

### Scope — When ACS May Request Assistance

Police officers may be requested to assist ACS Child Protection Specialists when:

1. Executing an **Order of Entry** endorsed by a Family Court judge
2. Executing a **Child Removal Order or warrant** endorsed by a Family Court judge
3. Conducting an **emergency removal** pursuant to **Section 1024 Family Court Act**
4. The ACS Child Protection Specialist is concerned for his/her **personal safety** due to suspicion of guns, drugs, or prior or threatened acts of violence against ACS Child Protection Specialists

> **Exam Alert:** ACS CPS may only request assistance when a complaint has been lodged with the State Central Registry.

### ACS CPS Contact Information

ACS Child Protection Specialists carry **cellular telephones** issued by their agency. Police officers may request that the communications section contact the caller to obtain the CPS's exact location.

### Uniformed Member of the Service

1. Respond to the scene
   - a. Give dispatcher **10-84 code** upon arrival
   - b. Locate the ACS employee
2. Confer with ACS Child Protection Specialist at the scene
   - a. Determine specific reason for request
   - b. Obtain copy of Removal/Entry Order(s) or warrant, if applicable

**NOTE:** In an emergency removal situation, the ACS CPS may not have a warrant or Removal Order.

3. **Request patrol supervisor**, if not already present

### Patrol Supervisor

> **Sergeant Focus:** A patrol supervisor MUST respond to ACS CPS assistance calls.

4. Respond and confer with uniformed members of the service and ACS CPS
5. Determine if precinct detective, borough Special Victims or Child Abuse squads have an open case regarding this family, if possible
6. Review Entry/Removal order(s) from ACS, if applicable
   - a. **Confer with Legal Bureau** if question as to validity of the order exists. Be guided by response from Legal Bureau personnel.
7. Determine if additional Department resources are needed

### Uniformed Member of the Service (continued)

8. Ensure the safety of all persons present
9. Aid in carrying out Entry/Removal order(s), if applicable
10. Follow P.G. 215-03 if arrest is required for child abuse or neglect
11. Follow appropriate complaint or arrest procedure for other criminal activities, if appropriate
12. Make an entry in digital Activity Log including:
    - a. ACS Child Protection Specialist's name
    - b. Specific purpose of the request for assistance (see SCOPE statement 1 through 4)
    - c. ACS case number (also known as "State Connections" ID #)

### Patrol Supervisor (continued)

13. Take appropriate action

### Additional Data

> **Exam Alert:** The radio code **10-68A** is specific to ACS CPS assistance requests. This is a commonly tested detail.

- When an ACS CPS appears at a police facility and requests assistance, the **desk officer** will ensure that the communications section is notified in a timely manner and a **10-68A code** is dispatched
- The desk officer will direct the CPS to the scene to await patrol response
- **Radio code 10-68A** = "Request for Assistance to ACS Child Protection Specialists"
- Nothing precludes a police officer from taking police action if evidence of criminal activity exists

### Related Procedures

- Emergency Removals or Investigation and Reporting of Abused, Neglected, or Maltreated Children (P.G. 215-03)
- Family Court Warrant for Child Abuse/Neglect Cases (P.G. 215-04)
- Preliminary Investigation of Complaints (P.G. 207-07)

## P.G. 215-28 — Use of Child Car Seats

**Purpose:** To ensure the proper use of child car seats and booster seats in Department vehicles.

### Age-Based Requirements

> **Exam Alert:** These age thresholds are frequently tested. Know the specific age ranges and seat types.

| Age | Requirement | Seat Position |
|-----|------------|---------------|
| Under 2 years | **Rear-facing** child car seat | Back seat ONLY |
| Under 4 years | Child car seat | Back seat ONLY |
| Ages 4-7 | Booster seat | Back seat ONLY |
| Under 8 years | Some form of child restraint required | — |

> **Exam Alert:** Both child car seats and booster seats must be used in the BACK seat only.

### Desk Officer Responsibilities

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer must ensure MOS are trained in proper child car seat installation and use.

- Ensure uniformed members of the service are **trained** in proper use of child car seats
- Ensure child car seats are **inspected at the start of every tour**

### Training Sergeant

> **Sergeant Focus:** The training sergeant is responsible for ensuring ALL members are trained on child car seat usage.

- Ensure all members of the command are trained in proper child car seat installation and use

### Car Seat Inspection Schedule

| Task | Frequency | Responsible |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Inspect child car seats | Start of every tour | Desk officer ensures |
| Training on car seats | Ongoing | Training sergeant |
