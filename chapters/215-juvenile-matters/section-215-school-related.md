## P.G. 215-17 — Police Actions at DOE Schools

**Purpose:** To establish procedures for police actions at Department of Education (DOE) schools, including arrests, investigations, and student questioning.

### Pre-Arrest Notification

> **Exam Alert:** The School Safety Division Operations Unit must be notified PRIOR to any arrest at a school. A log number must be obtained.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Notify | School Safety Division Operations Unit |
| When | PRIOR to arrest |
| Obtain | Log number |
| Consult | Principal or designee |

### On-Campus vs. Off-Campus Arrests

> **Exam Alert:** Arrests for off-campus incidents should be effected OFF school property whenever possible.

- Arrests of students for **off-campus incidents** should be effected **OFF school property** when possible
- Arrests **on school property** are appropriate only for specific offenses

### Offenses Permitting On-Campus Arrest

The following offenses warrant arrest ON school property:

- Any felony (except certain property crimes below)
- All sex offenses
- Menacing with firearm display
- Other serious offenses involving weapons or violence

> **Sergeant Focus:** Evaluate whether the offense justifies an on-campus arrest. For property crimes that are felonies, consider whether the arrest can be deferred to off-campus.

### Student Questioning

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Parent/guardian | Contact must be **attempted** before questioning |
| Miranda | Required if student is a **suspect** |
| Principal/designee | Must be consulted |
| Student records | Require **court order or subpoena** unless health/safety emergency |

> **Exam Alert:** Student records require a court order or subpoena to access. The only exception is a health or safety emergency.

### Supervisor Responsibilities

> **Sergeant Focus:** When responding to school incidents, ensure:
> - School Safety Division Operations Unit was notified and log number obtained
> - Principal/designee was consulted
> - Parent/guardian contact was attempted before student questioning
> - Miranda warnings given if student is a suspect
> - Off-campus arrest was considered for off-campus incidents
> - Student records were not accessed without proper legal authority

## P.G. 215-18 — Search Protocols for School Safety Agents

**Purpose:** To establish search protocols and limitations for School Safety Agents (SSAs) at DOE schools.

### Authority to Search

> **Exam Alert:** School Safety Agents conduct searches on behalf of the principal/designee — NOT on their own authority (except in emergencies).

| Factor | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Authority source | Principal or designee |
| Standard | Reasonable suspicion |
| Gender requirement | Same-gender search to the maximum extent |
| Strip-searches | **Absolutely prohibited** |
| Emergency exception | Immediate intervention for safety — no principal authorization required |

### Search Standards

- Searches are conducted **only on behalf of the principal or designee** (except emergency)
- The **reasonable suspicion** standard applies
- **Same-gender search** required to the maximum extent possible
- **NO strip-searches** under any circumstances

> **Exam Alert:** Strip-searches of students are absolutely prohibited, regardless of the circumstances.

### Emergency Searches

An **emergency** is defined as a situation requiring immediate intervention for safety. In an emergency:
- The School Safety Agent may act without prior principal/designee authorization
- The search must be limited to what is necessary to address the immediate safety concern

### RIGHT TO KNOW Business Card

- **Not required** at security checkpoints unless specifically requested by the individual
- Must be provided if requested

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure School Safety Agents understand that their search authority derives from the principal/designee, not from independent police authority. Monitor for compliance with the strip-search prohibition and same-gender search requirement.

## P.G. 215-27 — School-Related Threats

**Purpose:** To establish procedures for evaluating and responding to threats directed at schools.

### Pre-Enforcement Consultation

> **Exam Alert:** The School Safety Division Counterterrorism Unit must be consulted BEFORE any enforcement action is taken regarding school-related threats.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Consult | School Safety Division Counterterrorism Unit |
| When | BEFORE enforcement action |
| Central repository | Counterterrorism Unit maintains all school threat records |
| Final determination | Commanding Officer |

### Definition of School-Related Threat

A school-related threat is a **threat with no corroborating evidence**. This distinguishes it from an active threat situation that would require an immediate law enforcement response.

### Processing School Threats

1. Confer with School Safety Division Counterterrorism Unit BEFORE enforcement action
2. The Counterterrorism Unit serves as the **central repository** of all school threats
3. The **Commanding Officer** makes the final determination on the response
4. **Low-level threats** are referred back to the school for disciplinary action

> **Sergeant Focus:** The School Safety Division Counterterrorism Unit must be consulted before taking enforcement action. The CO makes the final call on the threat level and response. Low-level threats should be handled through school discipline, not enforcement.
