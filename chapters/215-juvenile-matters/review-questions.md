## Question 1

Under P.G. 215-09, a Juvenile Delinquent is defined as a person who is:

- A) At least 7 years of age but less than 16 years of age
- B) At least 12 years of age but less than 18 years of age
- C) At least 13 years of age but less than 18 years of age
- D) At least 10 years of age but less than 16 years of age

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) At least 12 years of age but less than 18 years of age**

P.G. 215-09 — A Juvenile Delinquent is a person who is at least 12 but less than 18 years of age who commits an act that would be a crime if committed by an adult. The exception is for homicide offenses, where a child under 12 may also be classified as a JD.

</details>

## Question 2

When processing an arrest of a juvenile under P.G. 215-09, what unit must be contacted BEFORE beginning the arrest process?

- A) Special Victims Unit
- B) Borough Child Abuse Squad
- C) Juvenile Strategies Unit
- D) School Safety Division Operations Unit

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Juvenile Strategies Unit**

P.G. 215-09 — The Juvenile Strategies Unit must be contacted BEFORE beginning the arrest process for a juvenile delinquent. This is a mandatory step that cannot be skipped.

</details>

## Question 3

Under P.G. 215-09, a juvenile's parent/guardian objects to questioning but the juvenile states willingness to speak. What should the officer do?

- A) Proceed with questioning since the juvenile consented
- B) Continue questioning with the parent/guardian removed from the room
- C) Not question the juvenile — the parent's objection overrides the juvenile's consent
- D) Contact Corporation Counsel for guidance before proceeding

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Not question the juvenile — the parent's objection overrides the juvenile's consent**

P.G. 215-09 — If the parent/guardian objects to questioning OR requests an attorney, NO questioning is permitted, even if the juvenile is willing to speak. The parent's objection controls.

</details>

## Question 4

Under P.G. 215-09, Miranda warnings for a juvenile must be administered using which form, and who must be present?

- A) PD244-159, with the juvenile's attorney present
- B) PD244-1413, with the parent/guardian present
- C) PD377-154, with the desk officer present
- D) PD244-1413, with the patrol supervisor present

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) PD244-1413, with the parent/guardian present**

P.G. 215-09 — Miranda warnings must be read using MIRANDA WARNINGS FOR JUVENILE INTERROGATIONS (PD244-1413) with the parent/guardian present. Both the juvenile AND the parent/guardian must be advised of rights.

</details>

Under P.G. 215-17, arrests of students for off-campus incidents should be:

- B) Effected off school property whenever possible
- C) Made only with the written consent of the school principal
- D) Deferred until the student reaches age 18

## Question 7

Under P.G. 215-18, School Safety Agents conduct searches under what authority and legal standard?

- A) Independent police authority; probable cause
- B) On behalf of the principal/designee; reasonable suspicion
- C) Under ACS supervision; preponderance of evidence
- D) As peace officers; probable cause

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) On behalf of the principal/designee; reasonable suspicion**

P.G. 215-18 — School Safety Agents conduct searches on behalf of the principal/designee (except in emergencies) under the reasonable suspicion standard. They do not have independent police search authority.

</details>

## Question 8

Which of the following is TRUE regarding School Safety Agent searches under P.G. 215-18?

- A) Strip-searches are permitted with supervisor approval
- B) Same-gender searches are preferred but not required
- C) Strip-searches are absolutely prohibited under any circumstances
- D) Searches may only be conducted with a court order

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Strip-searches are absolutely prohibited under any circumstances**

P.G. 215-18 — Strip-searches of students are absolutely prohibited regardless of the circumstances. Same-gender searches are required to the maximum extent possible.

</details>

## Question 9

Under P.G. 215-14, when a child under 11 is a victim of abuse, the desk officer must notify:

- A) Precinct detective squad, then ACS
- B) Special Victims Unit, then the appropriate borough Child Abuse Squad
- C) Missing Persons Squad, then SVU
- D) Borough Command, then the precinct detective squad

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Special Victims Unit, then the appropriate borough Child Abuse Squad**

P.G. 215-14 — For a child under 11 who is a victim of abuse, the desk officer must notify the Special Victims Unit first, then the appropriate borough Child Abuse Squad. ACS Instant Response Team must also be notified when a child is removed or an arrest is made.

</details>

## Question 10

Under P.G. 215-02, a lost child who may be a victim of abuse is 13 years old. Who should the desk officer notify regarding the potential abuse?

- A) Special Victims Unit
- B) Precinct detective squad
- C) ACS Instant Response Team only
- D) Missing Persons Squad only

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Precinct detective squad**

P.G. 215-02 — For suspected abuse cases, the desk officer notifies the precinct detective squad if the child is 11 years of age or older, or SVU if under 11. Since this child is 13, the precinct detective squad is the correct notification. However, for any sex crime allegations regardless of age, SVU is notified.

</details>

## Question 12

Under P.G. 215-28, a 3-year-old child being transported in a Department vehicle must be placed in:

- A) A booster seat in the front passenger seat
- B) A rear-facing car seat in the back seat
- C) A child car seat in the back seat
- D) A regular seat belt in the back seat

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) A child car seat in the back seat**

P.G. 215-28 — Children under 4 years of age require a child car seat, and children under 2 require a rear-facing child car seat. A 3-year-old requires a child car seat (not necessarily rear-facing since the child is 2 or older). Both child car seats and booster seats must be placed in the back seat only.

</details>

## Question 13

Under P.G. 215-27, before taking enforcement action regarding a school-related threat, an officer must first confer with:

- A) School Safety Division Operations Unit
- B) Borough Command

- D) The school principal only

## Question 15

Under P.G. 215-22, which of the following is NOT a reason ACS Child Protection Specialists may request police assistance?

- A) Executing an Order of Entry endorsed by a Family Court judge
- B) Conducting an emergency removal pursuant to Section 1024 Family Court Act
- C) Serving a civil summons related to a custody dispute
- D) Concern for personal safety due to suspicion of guns, drugs, or threatened violence

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Serving a civil summons related to a custody dispute**

P.G. 215-22 — ACS CPS may request police assistance for four specific reasons: (1) executing an Order of Entry, (2) executing a Child Removal Order or warrant, (3) conducting an emergency removal under Section 1024 FCA, or (4) personal safety concerns. Civil custody disputes are not among the authorized reasons for police assistance.

</details>
