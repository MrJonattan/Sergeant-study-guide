# Section 215 — Key Terms and Critical Numbers

## Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Juvenile Delinquent (JD)** | A person at least 12 but less than 18 years of age who commits an act that would be a crime if committed by an adult. A child under 12 may qualify for homicide offenses. | P.G. 215-09 |
| **Juvenile Offender (JO)** | A juvenile charged with specific serious felonies who is prosecuted in the criminal justice system rather than Family Court. Age and offense-specific. | P.G. 215-10 |
| **Adolescent Offender (AO)** | A category under Raise the Age legislation for certain offenders aged 16-17 charged with felonies, processed through a Youth Part of Superior Court. | P.G. 215-10 |
| **PINS (Person in Need of Supervision)** | A person under 18 who is truant, incorrigible, ungovernable, or habitually disobedient and beyond the control of a parent/guardian. Processed through Family Court. | General PG |
| **ACS (Administration for Children's Services)** | NYC agency responsible for child welfare, protective services, foster care, and adoption services. | Multiple |
| **IRT (Instant Response Team)** | An ACS team that responds when a child is removed from the home or an arrest is made in a child abuse/neglect case. | P.G. 215-14 |
| **SVU (Special Victims Unit)** | NYPD unit handling sex crimes and crimes against children under 11 years of age. | P.G. 215-02, 215-14 |
| **Corporation Counsel** | NYC's Law Department attorney who represents the city in Family Court proceedings involving juvenile delinquents. Consulted for robbery charges designated as delinquent. | P.G. 215-09 |
| **Family Court** | Court with jurisdiction over juvenile delinquency, child abuse/neglect, PINS, and family offenses. JDs appear at 0900 hours on the next business day. | P.G. 215-09 |
| **Miranda (Juvenile Context)** | Warnings that must be read to BOTH the juvenile AND the parent/guardian using form PD244-1413. Parent/guardian must be present. If parent objects or requests attorney, no questioning is permitted. | P.G. 215-09 |
| **School Safety Agent (SSA)** | Civilian agents who conduct security at DOE schools. Search authority derives from the principal/designee, not independent police authority. | P.G. 215-18 |
| **Reasonable Suspicion** | The legal standard for School Safety Agent searches of students. Lower than probable cause but requires articulable facts. | P.G. 215-18 |
| **Strip-Search Prohibition** | School Safety Agents are absolutely prohibited from conducting strip-searches under any circumstances. | P.G. 215-18 |
| **Licensed Premises** | Public dance halls, cabarets, catering establishments, bars/grills, pool/billiard parlors, bowling alleys, skating rinks, theaters, motion picture theaters. | P.G. 215-11 |
| **State Central Registry** | New York State database of child abuse/maltreatment reports. ACS CPS may only request police assistance when a complaint is lodged here. | P.G. 215-14, 215-22 |
| **10-68A** | Radio code for "Request for Assistance to ACS Child Protection Specialists." | P.G. 215-22 |
| **Juvenile Strategies Unit** | NYPD unit that must be contacted BEFORE beginning the arrest process for a juvenile delinquent. | P.G. 215-09 |
| **School Safety Division Counterterrorism Unit** | Unit that must be consulted BEFORE enforcement action on school-related threats. Serves as central repository of all school threats. | P.G. 215-27 |
| **School Safety Division Operations Unit** | Unit that must be notified PRIOR to any arrest at a DOE school. A log number must be obtained. | P.G. 215-17 |

## Critical Numbers and Ages

| Number/Age | Significance | Source |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| **Under 11** | Abuse cases: notify SVU (not precinct detectives) | P.G. 215-02, 215-14 |
| **11 or older** | Abuse cases: notify precinct detective squad | P.G. 215-02 |
| **Under 12** | Only qualifies as JD for homicide offenses | P.G. 215-09 |
| **12 to 17 (at least 12, less than 18)** | Juvenile Delinquent age range | P.G. 215-09 |
| **Under 16** | Runaway child definition (New York) | P.G. 215-05 |
| **Under 18** | Juvenile definition for licensed premises; sex crime victim threshold for child advocacy | P.G. 215-11, 215-14 |
| **Under 2 years** | Rear-facing child car seat required | P.G. 215-28 |
| **Under 4 years** | Child car seat required | P.G. 215-28 |
| **Ages 4-7** | Booster seat required | P.G. 215-28 |
| **Under 8 years** | Child restraint of some form required | P.G. 215-28 |
| **0900 hours** | Family Court appearance time (next business day) | P.G. 215-09 |
| **10-68A** | Radio code: ACS CPS assistance request | P.G. 215-22 |
| **10-84** | Radio code: arrival at ACS CPS scene | P.G. 215-22 |
| **Section 1024 FCA** | Authorizes emergency removal of children | P.G. 215-22 |
| **MAR BAR MAK + Gun** | Mnemonic for felonies making a 14/15-year-old a Juvenile Offender: Murder/Assault/Rape, Burglary/Arson/Robbery, Manslaughter/Agg Sexual Abuse/Kidnapping, + CPW 2nd within 1000 ft of school | P.G. 215-10 |
| **S.C.A.M.** | Suspected Child Abuse or Maltreatment — the report (and procedure PG 215-03) for investigating and documenting child abuse/neglect cases | P.G. 215-03 |
| **Paper Chase (6 Reports)** | The 6 documents prepared in a child removal case: Complaint Report, DIR, SCAM, Aided Report, Notice of Removal, Affidavit of Service. Only 3 are prepared if no removal (Complaint, DIR, SCAM). | P.G. 215-03 |
| **Constructive Restraint** | Physical control with no restraining device, using minimal force necessary to control movement. Used for a child who is obeying commands. | P.G. 215-13 |
| **Alternative Restraint** | Velcro device used as an alternative to metal handcuffs for possible safety/flight risk and/or non-compliant child. Used only by MOS trained in them; all SSAs carry and are trained. | P.G. 215-13 |
| **Crisis Intervention Team** | School-based team that should be requested when responding to a student in emotional crisis. MOS should inquire about their involvement before engaging. | P.G. 215-13 |
| **Deadly Weapon (9 types)** | 1 loaded weapon (shot can cause S.P.I./D.O.A.), 4 slappers (blackjack, billy, metal knuckles, plastic knuckles), 4 knives (switchblade, pilum ballistic, metal knuckle, dagger). Defined in Penal Law Section 10.00. | P.G. 215-10 |
| **School Grounds** | For CPW 2nd degree JO purposes: inside school property line (elementary through high school) OR outside property line but within 1000 feet in a public place (includes parked vehicle). Penal Law Section 220.00 Sub 14. | P.G. 215-10 |
| **SVU-IRST (Instant Response Tracking Unit)** | SVU unit that accepts law enforcement referrals from State Central Registry, determines appropriate police response, and may prepare complaint reports. | P.G. 215-03 |
| **Safe Horizon** | Organization whose phone number is provided to the non-abusing parent/guardian for child services. | P.G. 215-03 |
| **Juvenile Crime Desk** | Notified when a juvenile is removed to a Department facility. Assists supervisors in determining whether juvenile should be released on JR Worksheet or processed as JD. | P.G. 215-13 |
| **10-90J(1)** | Radio disposition: D.I.R. made, no offense (child abuse "J" code) | P.G. 215-03 |
| **10-92J** | Radio disposition: D.I.R. made, Arrest (child abuse "J" code) | P.G. 215-03 |
| **10-93J** | Radio disposition: D.I.R. made, 61 Prepared (child abuse "J" code) | P.G. 215-03 |
| **10-75F** | Radio disposition: Home visit | P.G. 215-03 |

## Critical Numbers and Ages (Additional)

| Number/Age | Significance | Source |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| **13-year-old JO** | Can ONLY be a JO for Murder 2nd degree, Subs 1 and 2 (Intentional and Deathly Reckless) | P.G. 215-10 |
| **14- and 15-year-old JO** | Full MAR BAR MAK + Gun list applies | P.G. 215-10 |
| **Under 12 (restraint)** | Metal cuffs should NOT be used without prior supervisor approval; NOT handcuffed to fixed objects | P.G. 215-13 |
| **16 and above (restraint)** | Metal handcuffs used when necessary; alternative restraints will NOT be utilized | P.G. 215-13 |
| **1000 feet** | Distance from school that defines "School Grounds" for CPW 2nd degree JO | P.G. 215-10 |
| **24 hours** | Time limit for mailing Notice of Removal (UMOS) and Affidavit (D.O. to Family Court) | P.G. 215-03 |
| **9** | Number of Deadly Weapons defined in Penal Law Section 10.00 | P.G. 215-10 |
