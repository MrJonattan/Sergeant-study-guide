# Section 305 — Review Questions

## Multiple Choice

**1. A UMOS may be unarmed at their own discretion while off duty in all of the following situations EXCEPT:**

- A) On vacation
- B) Engaged in authorized off-duty employment
- C) Walking through a NYC neighborhood
- D) There is a likelihood of consuming alcohol

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Walking through a NYC neighborhood**

Members must be armed at all times when in NYC, unless specific exceptions apply: risk of loss/theft (sporting activities, beach), vacation, authorized off-duty employment, activity where it would be inadvisable to carry, or likelihood of consuming alcohol. Walking through NYC does not qualify. (A.G. 305-07)

</details>

---

**2. Which type of holster is prohibited under A.G. 305-07?**

- A) Shoulder holster
- B) Ankle holster
- C) Belly band holster
- D) Inside-waistband holster

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Belly band holster**

"Belly band" holsters, "clip-on" holsters, and "one size fits all" holsters are prohibited. Shoulder and ankle holsters are permitted if they meet retention requirements. (A.G. 305-07)

</details>

---

**3. Firearms may NOT be stored or left in:**

- A) A locked firearms safe at home
- B) An unattended motor vehicle
- C) The desk officer's safekeeping
- D) A firearms safety station

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) An unattended motor vehicle**

Members shall not store or leave firearms in an unattended motor vehicle, except as authorized (e.g., P.G. 221-10). (A.G. 305-07)

</details>

---

**4. All firearm repairs and modifications must be performed by:**

- A) Any certified gunsmith
- B) The member's commanding officer
- C) Firearms and Tactics Section armorers solely
- D) The member with CO approval

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Firearms and Tactics Section armorers solely**

All repairs and modifications to authorized firearms must be performed solely by Firearms and Tactics Section armorers. The member must pass a qualification course before carrying a modified firearm. (A.G. 305-07)

</details>

---

**5. A report for lost or damaged uniform must be filed within:**

- A) 10 days
- B) 30 days
- C) 60 days
- D) 90 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 30 days**

The report must be prepared on Typed Letterhead addressed to the Deputy Commissioner, Management and Budget, within 30 days from the date of incident. (A.G. 305-05)

</details>

---

**6. Reimbursement for lost or damaged uniform items costing less than $100 should be forwarded to the Deputy Commissioner, Management and Budget:**

- A) Through channels
- B) Direct
- C) Via Internal Affairs Bureau
- D) Through the Chief of Department

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Direct**

Items costing less than $100 are forwarded direct to the Deputy Commissioner, Management and Budget. Items costing $100 or more go through channels. (A.G. 305-05)

</details>

---

**7. The maximum reimbursement amount for a lost or damaged watch is:**

- A) $50.00
- B) $75.00
- C) $100.00
- D) $150.00

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) $75.00**

A maximum of $75.00 has been established as a reasonable amount for watch reimbursement. Members are reminded they must be equipped with a serviceable watch while performing patrol duty. (A.G. 305-05)

</details>

---

**8. A UMOS entering Family Court must be:**

- A) Armed with service pistol only
- B) Unarmed, unless present as arresting officer or on official business
- C) Armed with both service and off-duty weapons
- D) Armed but with weapon concealed

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Unarmed, unless present as arresting officer or on official business**

Members must be unarmed when entering Family Court or Supreme Court Matrimonial Parts, unless involved as arresting officer or on official business in a case. If armed as arresting officer, report to court officer supervisor and sign the Law Enforcement Officer log. (A.G. 305-07)

</details>

---

**9. All settlements for lost or damaged uniform reimbursement are non-transferable and must be executed within:**

- A) 90 days of approval
- B) 120 days of approval
- C) 180 days of approval
- D) One year of approval

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 180 days of approval**

All settlements are non-transferable and MUST be executed within 180 days of approval by the Deputy Commissioner, Management and Budget. (A.G. 305-05)

</details>

---

**10. A UMOS carrying an authorized firearm into a foreign country (not on official business or sporting event) will face:**

- A) A verbal warning
- B) No consequences if firearm is properly secured
- C) Disciplinary action and possibly criminal penalties and/or weapon confiscation
- D) Modified assignment

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Disciplinary action and possibly criminal penalties and/or weapon confiscation**

Members are prohibited from carrying firearms into a foreign country unless on official business or personally participating in an event requiring use of a firearm. Failure to comply will result in disciplinary action and possibly subject the member to criminal penalties and/or weapon confiscation. (A.G. 305-07)

</details>
