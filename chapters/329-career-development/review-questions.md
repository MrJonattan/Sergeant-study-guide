# Section 329 — Review Questions

## Multiple Choice

**1. Terminal leave for a uniformed member with 20 years of service is computed as:**

- A) 30 calendar days
- B) One calendar month per 10 years of service
- C) Two calendar months
- D) 60 calendar days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) One calendar month per 10 years of service**

One calendar month (28, 30, or 31 days depending on the month) of leave is granted for each 10 years of service. Beyond 20 years, 3 days per completed year and 1 day per completed 4 months. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

---

**2. A Tier 2 uniformed member must report to the Police Pension Fund:**

- A) At least 10 calendar days in advance
- B) At least 30 calendar days in advance
- C) Without notice
- D) At least 60 calendar days in advance

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) At least 30 calendar days in advance**

Tier 2 members must report at least 30 calendar days in advance of discontinuance of service. Tier 3 members may report without notice. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

---

**3. A LEOSA Certification Card expires:**

- A) 6 months from date of last range requalification
- B) 12 months from date of last range requalification
- C) 24 months from date of retirement
- D) It does not expire

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 12 months from date of last range requalification**

The LEOSA Certification Card will expire 12 months from the date of the member's last range requalification. The member must have qualified during a regular Department range cycle within the past 12 months. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

---

**4. After commencement of terminal leave, which of the following may occur?**

- A) Member may be granted vacation
- B) Member may be placed on sick report for any illness
- C) No leaves of any kind may be granted
- D) Member may request comp time

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) No leaves of any kind may be granted**

No leaves of any kind may be granted after commencement of terminal leave, nor may the member be placed on sick report, unless such sick report results from action taken in the line of duty while on terminal leave. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

---

**5. Resignation bars reinstatement EXCEPT if approved by:**

- A) The Chief of Department within 6 months
- B) The Police Commissioner within one year
- C) The First Deputy Commissioner within 2 years
- D) The Chief of Personnel within 90 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) The Police Commissioner within one year**

Resignation bars reinstatement except if approved by the Police Commissioner within one year of date of resignation. (A.G. 329-07)

</details>

---

**6. When a UMOS resigns, the RESIGNATION AND EXIT INTERVIEW form must be distributed within:**

- A) 24 hours
- B) 3 business days
- C) 5 business days
- D) 10 business days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 5 business days**

The commanding officer signs and distributes the RESIGNATION AND EXIT INTERVIEW within five business days. (A.G. 329-07)

</details>

**7. A resigning member's firearms must be delivered to:**

- A) Manhattan Property Clerk
- B) Firearms and Tactics Section
- C) Borough Property Clerk's Office only
- D) The member's permanent command

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Borough Property Clerk's Office only**

The resigning member must deliver firearms with PROPERTY CLERK INVOICE and ACQUISITION OR DISPOSITION OF FIREARMS report to the borough Property Clerk's Office ONLY. (A.G. 329-07)

</details>

**8. Tier 2 uniformed members must retire no later than midnight of the eve of their:**

- A) 60th birthday
- B) 62nd birthday
- C) 63rd birthday
- D) 65th birthday

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 63rd birthday**

Tier 2 members are required by law to retire no later than midnight of the eve of their 63rd birthday. Tier 3 members must retire no later than midnight of the eve of their 62nd birthday. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

---

**9. Members on terminal leave are available for duty only during:**

- A) Regular scheduled tours
- B) Emergency situations
- C) Programmatic overtime
- D) Detail assignments

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Emergency situations**

Members on terminal leave, or using accrued time attached to terminal leave, are excluded from excusal limitations and are only available for duty during emergency situations (natural disaster, etc.) or court must-appear notifications. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

**10. A UMOS wishing to retain a Department-issued service firearm (issued after 1/15/13) must obtain a NICS check within how many days prior to separation?**

- A) 15 days
- B) 30 days
- C) 60 days
- D) 90 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 30 days**

Members must obtain a NICS check through a Federal Firearms Licensee (FFL) no more than 30 days prior to separation from the Department. (A.G. 329-06)

</details>

**11. If a UMOS resigns other than in person, who is assigned to open the member's locker?**

- A) The desk officer
- B) A sergeant and clerical member of the service
- C) The commanding officer personally
- D) Internal Affairs Bureau

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A sergeant and clerical member of the service**

The desk officer assigns a sergeant and clerical member of the service to open the member's locker and obtain Department property. (A.G. 329-07)

</details>

**12. Upon learning of a member's resignation, the Operations Unit notifies all of the following EXCEPT:**

- A) Communications Section
- B) Internal Affairs Bureau
- C) Payroll Section
- D) The member's union representative

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**D) The member's union representative**

Operations Unit notifies: Communications Section (FINEST Message), Internal Affairs Bureau, Payroll Section, Police Pension Fund, and Human Resources Division. The union representative is not on this notification list. (A.G. 329-07)

</details>
