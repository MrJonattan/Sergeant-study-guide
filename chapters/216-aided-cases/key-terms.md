# Section 216 — Key Terms, Forms, and Critical Numbers

## Definitions

| Term | Definition | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Aided Case | Any occurrence requiring a person OTHER THAN A PRISONER to receive medical aid or assistance (sick/injured, dead, lost, mentally ill, abandoned child, runaway, homeless) | P.G. 216-01 |
| Routine Sick at Home | Aided at residence who is conscious, identified, no dependent adults/uncared children, no other police service needed — requires only Activity Log entry | P.G. 216-01 |
| Routine Sick in Nursing Home | Uniformed members WILL NOT respond; exceptions: injury, cardiac, D.O.A., etc. | P.G. 216-01 |
| Human Exposure to Rabies | Bite/scratch from rabid or suspected rabid animal, or contamination of open wound (bled within 24 hours) or mucous membranes with infectious material | P.G. 216-09 |
| Communicable Disease | Infectious disease capable of being passed by direct contact with an infected individual or their discharges | P.G. 216-11 |
| Line of Duty (Auxiliary) | After "sign-in" on Auxiliary Police Roll Call (APS #10) until sign-out; NO off-duty designation | P.G. 216-13 |
| Carbon Monoxide | Colorless, odorless gas; symptoms include headache, nausea, vomiting, confusion; severe cases show cherry red skin | P.G. 216-21 |
| AED Utilization | Once AED pads are placed on an aided — even if no shock is administered | P.G. 216-19 |
| CPR Administered | Once a member performs rapid chest compressions or mouth-to-mouth ventilation | P.G. 216-19 |
| QMHP | Qualified Mental Health Professional — licensed psychologist, certified social worker, or registered professional nurse eligible to direct a Section 9.58 removal | P.G. 216-22 |
| Mobile Crisis Outreach Teams | Hospital/community-based teams funded by DOHMH that assess, stabilize, and refer patients | P.G. 216-22 |
| ACT Teams | "Hospitals on wheels" — richly staffed mental health teams licensed by NYS Office of Mental Health; may order involuntary transport | P.G. 216-22 |

## Critical Numbers and Time Limits

| Item | Value | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| Second ambulance call | **20 minutes** after first call if ambulance does not arrive | P.G. 216-01 |
| ME follow-up call | **1 hour** if ME fails to respond | P.G. 216-04 |
| Morgue identification (homicide) | **0900 hours, next day** — officer who discovered body reports to morgue | P.G. 216-04 |
| Reports to Auxiliary Police Section | Within **10 days** | P.G. 216-13 |
| DOHMH Animal Bite Unit hours | **0900-1700, Monday-Friday** | P.G. 216-09 |
| After-hours animal bite notification | **Poison Control Center** | P.G. 216-09 |
| 9-8-8 Hotline availability | **24 hours/day, 7 days/week** in 200+ languages | P.G. 216-01 |
| Rabies wound definition | Open wound that **bled within last 24 hours** | P.G. 216-09 |

## Critical Notification Contacts

| Situation | Notify |
|-----------|--------|
| Poisoned/infected food or denatured alcohol | Operations Unit (via desk officer) |
| Homicide, suspicious death, suicide | Operations Unit, detective squad, Crime Scene Unit (via detective) |
| DOC suspicious death/suicide | Detective squad, CO/duty captain, patrol borough, **IAB** (get log number), Operations Unit |
| Scaffold injury/death | Department of Buildings |
| MTA bus injury/death | NYC Transit |
| Construction site, Parks property, factory/store injury/death | Operations Unit |
| Firefighter killed/injured at fire | DO NOT notify family without **fire officer** permission |
| DOC inmate death | DO NOT notify family unless **Correction supervisor** requests |
| Auxiliary police injury requiring hospital | Operations Unit (promptly) |
| Carbon monoxide alarm (City-owned building) | Department of Buildings (via Communications) |
| Section 9.58 removal validity in question | Legal Bureau |

## Persons Authorized to Disturb a Dead Body

| Letter | Authorized Person |
|--------|-------------------|
| a | Paramedic, E.M.T., or doctor (caution: do not disturb evidence) |
| b | Medical Examiner or assistant |
| c | District Attorney or assistant |
| d | Members of Detective Bureau, detective squad, or Crime Scene Unit |
| e | Highway District member assigned as "collision technician" or "collision investigator" |
| f | ESU member, if body is on train tracks or in subway tunnel |
