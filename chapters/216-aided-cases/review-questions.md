# Section 216 — Review Questions

## Question 1
A uniformed member responds to a residence where a person is conscious, properly identified, and requesting an ambulance for stomach pain. There are no dependent adults or uncared-for children, and no other police service is required. What documentation is needed?

- A) An AIDED REPORT and digital Activity Log entry
- B) Only a digital Activity Log entry
- C) An AIDED REPORT, COMPLAINT REPORT, and Activity Log entry
- D) No documentation is required for routine sick at home

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Only a digital Activity Log entry**

P.G. 216-01 — A "Routine Sick at Home" requires ONLY a digital Activity Log entry when the aided is at a residence AND is conscious and properly identified, no other police service is required, no dependent adults or uncared-for children are present, and no other investigation is needed. All four conditions are met here.
</details>

## Question 2
Which of the following persons is NOT authorized to disturb a dead body at a scene?

- A) A member of the Emergency Service Unit, if the body is on train tracks
- B) A member of Highway District assigned as a collision technician
- C) The patrol supervisor directing the investigation
- D) The Medical Examiner or assistant Medical Examiner

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The patrol supervisor directing the investigation**

P.G. 216-04 — Only six categories of persons may disturb a body: (a) paramedic/EMT/doctor, (b) ME or assistant, (c) DA or assistant, (d) Detective Bureau/squad/Crime Scene Unit, (e) Highway District collision technicians/investigators, (f) ESU on train tracks/subway tunnel. The patrol supervisor is not among them.
</details>

## Question 3
When a uniformed member responds to an aided case involving a suspected drug overdose (cocaine psychosis), what is the proper disposition of the aided?

- A) Bring the aided to the precinct stationhouse for processing
- B) Have the aided brought to an appropriate hospital facility
- C) Release the aided to a responsible family member
- D) Have the aided transported to a police detention facility for evaluation

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Have the aided brought to an appropriate hospital facility**

P.G. 216-01 — The aided is NOT to be brought to a police facility. The patrol supervisor determines if the aided should be removed to the appropriate hospital by RMP or await the ambulance. Drug overdose/cocaine psychosis aided persons go to the hospital, never to a police facility.
</details>

## Question 4
At a psychiatric ward, the attending psychiatrist refers a patient to the hospital emergency room for medical evaluation. What should the uniformed member do regarding their firearm?

- A) Keep the firearm unloaded for the entire duration
- B) Reload at the firearms safety station before leaving the psychiatric area, and unload upon return
- C) Secure the firearm with hospital security
- D) Leave the firearm unloaded and in the holster at all times while in the hospital

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Reload at the firearms safety station before leaving the psychiatric area, and unload upon return**

P.G. 216-07 — If the psychiatrist refers a patient to the hospital ER for medical evaluation and clearance, the member shall reload firearm(s) at the safety station before leaving the psychiatric admitting area, and unload upon return. Interim entries must be made in the Psychiatric Admitting Log.
</details>

## Question 5
A raccoon is observed in a park during mid-afternoon, approaching people in a friendly manner. How should this animal be treated?

- A) As a normal animal; raccoons sometimes adjust their schedules
- B) As potentially rabid until proven otherwise
- C) As a danger only if it displays aggressive behavior
- D) As requiring no police action unless it bites someone

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) As potentially rabid until proven otherwise**

P.G. 216-09 — Raccoons are primarily nocturnal animals; any raccoon seen active during the daytime should be considered rabid until proven otherwise. Any raccoon acting friendly or aggressively should be considered rabid.
</details>

## Question 6
Under what circumstances may a uniformed member discharge a firearm at a dog or other animal?

- A) When the animal has bitten a person
- B) When the animal is suspected of being rabid
- C) To protect themselves or another person from physical injury when there is no other reasonable means to eliminate the threat
- D) When directed to do so by a supervisor

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) To protect themselves or another person from physical injury when there is no other reasonable means to eliminate the threat**

P.G. 216-09 — Members shall not discharge their firearms at a dog or other animal except to protect themselves or another person from physical injury and there is no other reasonable means to eliminate the threat.
</details>

## Question 8
An auxiliary police officer sustains a serious physical injury while on duty. Who acts as the investigating supervisor?

- A) The patrol supervisor, precinct of occurrence
- B) The lieutenant platoon commander, precinct of occurrence
- C) The precinct commanding officer/executive officer/duty captain
- D) The commanding officer of the Auxiliary Police Section

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The precinct commanding officer/executive officer/duty captain**

P.G. 216-13 — The CO/XO/duty captain will be the investigating supervisor if the injured auxiliary police officer sustains a serious physical injury, is likely to die, or dies. The lieutenant platoon commander handles non-serious cases.

## Question 10
An aided case occurs on a NYC Transit train. What is the primary concern regarding service interruption?

- A) Delays may cause commuter complaints
- B) Unnecessary interruption can increase temperatures significantly and may cause panic on crowded trains stopped in tunnels
- C) Service interruptions require MTA authorization
- D) Union regulations prohibit stopping trains for more than 10 minutes

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Unnecessary interruption can increase temperatures significantly and may cause panic on crowded trains stopped in tunnels**

P.G. 216-14 — Unnecessary interruption of train service can inconvenience thousands of passengers and endanger public welfare. In addition, unnecessary interruptions can increase temperatures significantly in trains and stations, and may cause panic on crowded trains stopped in tunnels. Service will be interrupted only when absolutely necessary.
</details>

## Question 14
The Department considers an AED to have been "utilized" when:

- A) A shock is delivered to the patient
- B) The AED is removed from its carrying case
- C) The AED pads are placed on the aided, even if no shock is administered
- D) The AED is turned on and the analysis is completed

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The AED pads are placed on the aided, even if no shock is administered**

P.G. 216-19 — Once an AED is placed on an aided, the Department considers that as utilized, even if a shock is not administered. When the AED pads are placed, the machine performs an analysis and advises what actions should be taken.
</details>

## Question 15
An AED should only be attached to a patient who meets which criteria?

- A) Unconscious adult, not breathing, no pulse
- B) Any unconscious person regardless of age
- C) Any person experiencing chest pain
- D) Unconscious adult or child, not breathing

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**A) Unconscious adult, not breathing, no pulse**

P.G. 216-19 — The AED will only be attached to an unconscious adult patient after it has been confirmed that the patient is not breathing and does not have a pulse. All three criteria must be met.
</details>

## Question 17
During the removal of a TB shelter client, what is the Department's primary responsibility?

- A) Force entry to the premises and remove the patient
- B) Safeguard the patient's property
- C) Assist in the arrest and preserve the peace
- D) Provide transportation to the hospital

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Assist in the arrest and preserve the peace**

P.G. 216-20 — The primary responsibility of the Department during TB shelter removals is to assist in the arrest and preserve the peace. Members are NOT to personally assist in forcing entry, securing premises, or safeguarding property. Those duties are performed by other agencies at DOHMH direction.
</details>

## Question 18
Upon responding to a residential carbon monoxide alarm, what should a uniformed member do?

- A) Enter the location immediately to check for victims
- B) Refrain from entering the location and request the patrol supervisor if alarm is confirmed
- C) Enter the location only if wearing a filter mask
- D) Enter the location after confirming the CO detector is not malfunctioning

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Refrain from entering the location and request the patrol supervisor if alarm is confirmed**

P.G. 216-21 — The uniformed member will refrain from entering the location and request the response of the patrol supervisor if the alarm is confirmed. Only ESU determines whether the location is safe for re-entry.
</details>
