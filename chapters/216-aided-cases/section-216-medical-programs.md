## P.G. 216-19: Public Access Defibrillation Program

**Date Effective:** 11/18/21 | **Revision:** I.O. 104 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To account for and properly document the use of Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) in assigned commands, and to provide for training and certification of uniformed, civilian, and auxiliary members of the service in their use.

### Key Definitions

**AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATOR (AED)** — An electronic device used to defibrillate the heart by applying an electric shock to it.

**UTILIZATION OF AED** — Once an AED is **placed on an aided**, the Department considers that as utilized, **even if a shock is not administered**. When the AED pads are placed on an aided, the machine will perform an analysis and advise what actions should be taken.

> **Exam Alert:** "Utilized" means pads are placed on the patient — a shock does NOT need to be delivered for it to count as utilized.

**CARDIO PULMONARY RESUSCITATION (CPR) ADMINISTERED** — The Department considers CPR administered once a member performs rapid chest compressions or mouth-to-mouth ventilation on an aided.

### AED Kit Contents

> **Exam Alert:** Memorize the kit contents — this is frequently tested.

| Item | Quantity |
|------|----------|
| Operational AED device | 1 |
| Electrode pads with wire leads (sealed packages, each containing two pads) | 2 packages |
| Pocket mask | 1 |
| Gloves | 1 pair |
| Cloth/gauze compress | 1 |
| Razor | 1 |

### Procedure — Desk Officer (Steps 1-6)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Account for **all AED kits** assigned to command at the **beginning of each tour** |
| 2 | Assign an AED at start of each tour to a member trained and certified as AED qualified |
| 3 | Notify radio dispatcher of the unit(s) assigned an AED kit |
| 4 | Make a **Command Log entry** accounting for all AED kits and notification to radio dispatcher |
| 4a | Note assignment of AED kits by their serial numbers, with prefix "AED" (e.g., AED 1234) |
| 5 | Ensure assignment of AED kits are noted on the roll call |
| 6 | Secure AED kits not in use in appropriate location (e.g., radio room, property room) |

> **Sergeant Focus:** AED accountability happens **every tour** — the desk officer must account for all kits, assign them, notify dispatch, and make Command Log entries.

### Member of the Service — Kit Inspection (Steps 7-8)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 7 | Inspect AED kit to ensure all items present |
| 8 | Notify desk officer of any deficiencies/missing equipment; make digital Activity Log entries |

### Upon Arriving at Scene of Cardiac Aided Case (Steps 9-13)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 9 | Advise radio dispatcher of aided's condition and request EMS response |
| 10 | Utilize AED in compliance with Department guidelines/training |
| 10a | AED will **only** be attached to an **unconscious adult patient** after confirming: **not breathing** and **no pulse** |
| 10b | Provide care until relieved by advanced-level emergency medical personnel |
| 11 | Brief responding EMS crew of: (a) condition when found, (b) nature of care provided (rescue breathing, CPR, defibrillation, etc.) |
| 12 | Prepare AIDED REPORT; note in "Details" if AED utilized or CPR administered and results achieved; include AED serial number if utilized |
| 12b | If CPR administered, check appropriate caption on AIDED REPORT |
| 13 | Submit AIDED REPORT immediately to desk officer after returning to command |

> **Exam Alert:** AED is **only** for unconscious adults with **no breathing** and **no pulse**. All three conditions must be confirmed.

### Prisoner AED/CPR Use

> **Exam Alert:** If AED/CPR is administered on a **prisoner** in custody, a **MEDICAL TREATMENT OF PRISONER (PD244-150)** form will be completed **instead of** an AIDED REPORT.

### Desk Officer — Post-Use (Steps 14-15)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 14 | Telephone ESU desk to obtain an **AED/CPR Log number**; enter on AIDED REPORT or MEDICAL TREATMENT OF PRISONER form |
| 14a | Forward copy of completed form to ESU-AED Coordinator via fax and Department mail to ESU, Attention: EMT School |
| 14b | Ensure AED is secured until delivery and is **not reassigned** |
| 15 | Schedule appointment; ensure AED is delivered to ESU-AED Coordinator for downloading of information after each use |

### Training — "BLASTT" Course

> **Exam Alert:** AED training is a one-day course at the **Police Academy** called **"BLASTT"** — Basic Life Support and Trauma Treatment CPR-AED-Naloxone-BTK.

- The commanding officer designates the command's **training sergeant** as the AED liaison
- The command training sergeant schedules members via **T.A.C.T.I.C.S.** (Training Attendance Certification Transcript Integrated Collection System)

### Loss or Theft

If an AED kit or its contents (except cloth/gauze compress and plastic gloves) are lost or stolen, comply with P.G. 219-20, "Loss or Theft of Department Property."

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| AIDED REPORT | (Electronic) |
| MEDICAL TREATMENT OF PRISONER | PD244-150 |

## P.G. 216-20: Handling Shelter Clients with Tuberculosis

**Date Effective:** 11/18/21 | **Revision:** I.O. 99 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To assist the Department of Health and Mental Hygiene in the removal of recalcitrant patients sheltered in Human Resources Administration facilities, who have tuberculosis and are refusing treatment.

### Procedure

#### Operations Unit Personnel (Steps 1-2)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Upon notification by DOHMH that a removal and detention order has been issued: (a) ascertain location of removal, (b) enter in Command Log the name of DOHMH personnel and pedigree information of subject |
| 2 | Notify Communications Section to assign a patrol supervisor, an RMP unit, and ESU to respond |

#### Patrol Supervisor (Steps 3-6)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 3 | Respond to scene and assess the situation |
| 4 | Determine if services of ESU are necessary; cancel ESU if not required |
| 5 | Confirm that the **Removal and Detention Order** is possessed by the DOHMH physician/peace officer at the shelter site |
| 6 | Assign uniformed member to accompany subject and DOHMH physician/peace officer in DOHMH transportation vehicle to the pre-designated hospital |

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor must **confirm the Removal and Detention Order** before proceeding. This is a legal document that authorizes the removal.

### Department's Role — Critical Limitations

> **Exam Alert:** The Department's primary responsibility is to **assist in the arrest and preserve the peace**. Members of the service are:

| Allowed | NOT Allowed |
|---------|-------------|
| Use physical force to restrain subject until hospital delivery (only extent necessary) | Personally assist in **forcing entry** to premises |
| Accompany patient to hospital | **Safeguard property** at location |
| Wear protective equipment | **Secure premises** afterward |

> **Exam Alert:** The Department does NOT force entry, safeguard property, or secure premises — those duties are performed by other agencies at the direction of the DOHMH physician/peace officer.

### Protective Equipment

Responding members will take appropriate precautions including wearing **plastic gloves and facial masks**. Facial masks will be provided at the shelter site by DOHMH.

### Transportation

- DOHMH provides the vehicle
- If HRA cannot provide a vehicle, an ambulance will be requested through Communications
- **Except in extreme cases**, Department vehicles will NOT be used for transportation
- Transport **only** to the designated HHC facility (even if subject is arrested for other conduct)

#### Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 7-10)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 7 | Accompany patient along with DOHMH physician/peace officer to pre-designated hospital |
| 8 | Enter details of removal and transport in digital Activity Log |
| 9 | Ensure DOHMH physician/peace officer name and patient name are recorded in Activity Log and Department forms |
| 10 | Comply with A.G. 330-09, "Exposure of Members of the Service to Infectious Diseases or Hazardous Materials" |

### If Patient Becomes Violent or Barricades

Comply with P.G. 221-13, "Mentally Ill or Emotionally Disturbed Persons" and P.G. 221-14, "Hostage/Barricaded Person(s)," as appropriate.

## P.G. 216-21: Response to Carbon Monoxide Alarms

**Date Issued:** 10/18/16 | **Pages:** 2

### Key Definition

**CARBON MONOXIDE** — A **colorless and odorless** gas that interferes with the delivery of oxygen to the cells of the body.

| Signs and Symptoms |
|--------------------|
| Headache |
| Nausea |
| Vomiting |
| Confusion and disorientation |
| Cherry red skin (severe cases) |

Carbon monoxide is created by incomplete combustion of burning fuels (gasoline, oil, wood, paper).

> **Exam Alert:** CO is **colorless and odorless**. Cherry red skin indicates a **severe** case.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 1-8)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Promptly respond; identify type of alarm (residential, central station monitored, EMS call) |
| 2 | Interview persons with information regarding location of alarm |
| 3 | **Refrain from entering location** |
| 4 | Request response of **patrol supervisor**, if alarm confirmed |
| 5 | Establish frozen area |
| 6 | Render reasonable aid to victims, if present |
| 7 | Request EMS to respond if necessary |
| 8 | Observe affected individuals for signs/symptoms of CO poisoning; direct them to EMS for treatment |

> **Exam Alert:** Members **DO NOT enter** the location. This is critical — CO is invisible and can incapacitate quickly.

### Patrol Supervisor (Steps 9-11)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 9 | Respond to location |
| 10 | Evaluate situation upon arrival |
| 11 | Maintain frozen area and extend if necessary, until hazard is removed |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisors should be guided by P.G. 212-37, "Hazardous Materials," when responding to CO alarms and setting up frozen areas.

### Patrol Supervisor — Continued (Steps 12-16)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 12 | Establish crime scene, if necessary |
| 13 | Request response of **ESU personnel** |
| 14 | Prevent all non-ESU police personnel from entering location |
| 15 | Notify duty captain and precinct desk officer |
| 16 | Direct Communications to notify Department of Buildings if location is City-owned |

### Emergency Service Unit Member (Steps 17-19)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 17 | Respond to location and determine whether CO gases are present |
| 18 | Advise patrol supervisor on appropriate frozen area boundaries |
| 19 | **Determine whether location is safe for re-entry** |

> **Exam Alert:** Only **ESU** determines whether the location is safe for re-entry. Not the patrol supervisor, not FDNY — ESU.

### After ESU Assessment (Steps 20-25)

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 20 | MOS | Remain outside location until ESU deems location habitable |
| 21 | MOS | Assist EMS with removal of aided(s) if location properly vented and ESU advises safe to enter |
| 22 | MOS | Comply with P.G. 216-01, "Aided Cases General Procedure" |
| 23 | P.S. | Direct preparation of AIDED REPORT and COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A), if appropriate |
| 24 | P.S. | Confer with ESU to determine if location is safe for occupants to re-enter prior to allowing re-entry |
| 25 | P.S. | Comply with P.G. 212-37, "Hazardous Materials," if a continuing CO threat exists |

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| AIDED REPORT | (Electronic) |
| COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET | PD313-152A |

## P.G. 216-22: Mobile Crisis Outreach Teams and Assertive Community Treatment Teams

**Date Effective:** 06/11/24 | **Revision:** R.O. 39 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To safeguard and remove mentally ill or emotionally disturbed persons at the direction of Mobile Crisis Outreach or Assertive Community Treatment Teams pursuant to **section 9.58 of the New York State Mental Hygiene Law**.

### Key Definitions

**MOBILE CRISIS OUTREACH TEAMS** — Independently operated by local hospitals or community-based mental health organizations; usually funded by DOHMH; may be attached to NYS-licensed "comprehensive emergency programs." Goal: assess, stabilize, and rapidly refer patients to longer-term providers.

**ASSERTIVE COMMUNITY TREATMENT TEAMS (ACT)** — Similar to Mobile Crisis Outreach Teams but are "hospitals on wheels," more richly staffed by mental health workers. Provide a wide range of ongoing services. May order involuntary transport of patients to hospital ERs. Receive contract funding from DOHMH and are licensed by the NYS Office of Mental Health.

**QUALIFIED MENTAL HEALTH PROFESSIONAL (QMHP)** — Members of a Mobile Crisis Outreach or ACT Team who are:

| QMHP Eligible Professionals |
|------------------------------|
| Licensed psychologists |
| Certified social workers |
| Registered professional nurses |

> **Exam Alert:** Only these three types of professionals qualify as QMHPs eligible to direct a removal under Section 9.58.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Comply with P.G. 221-13, "Mentally Ill or Emotionally Disturbed Persons," as required |
| 2 | Request the physician or QMHP on scene to present **section 9.58 identification card** along with signed and completed **OMH 482 form** (Mobile Crisis Outreach Team Authorization for Transport) |
| 2a | Record pertinent information in digital Activity Log and AIDED REPORT, including identity of physician/QMHP ordering removal and length of time aided was in police custody |
| 3 | Inform communications dispatcher that the removal is involuntary, when appropriate |
| 4 | Prepare AIDED REPORT and include: |
| 4a | **Upload photographs** of section 9.58 identification card and OMH 482 form to AIDED REPORT |
| 4b | Note that individual was removed pursuant to section 9.58 of NYS Mental Hygiene Law |
| 4c | Name of QMHP who directed the removal and the mobile crisis team name |

> **Exam Alert:** Two documents must be presented: (1) **Section 9.58 ID card** and (2) **OMH 482 form**. Both must be photographed and uploaded to the AIDED REPORT.

### AIDED REPORT Coding

To accurately record section 9.58 removals, select:
- **'Emotionally Disturbed'** in the 'Aided Person' section
- **'Mobile Crisis Team Removal? — Yes'** in 'Additional Information' section
- **'Involuntary Removal? — Yes'** in 'Additional Information' section

### Additional Data

**Transportation:**
- Officer will request an ambulance to provide transportation
- Officer **rides in the body of the ambulance** with the patient

**Frisk Authority:**
- Officer may frisk individual's person for weapons when transporting an EDP to the hospital
- If individual has a bag or similar item, officer may only frisk the bag for a weapon or safety concern **if the bag is in a grabbable area**
- If the officer feels what appears to be a weapon during the frisk, the officer may search the bag for the weapon

**Hospital Responsibility:**
- The mobile crisis team member ordering the removal is responsible to communicate details to hospital staff and provide required documentation
- Once individual is removed to hospital and registered as a patient, uniformed member presence is **no longer required** unless the patient is exhibiting violent behavior

**Property:**
- Mobile Crisis Outreach or ACT Teams ordering the removal will safeguard all personal property (other than contraband) removed from the patient

**Legal Concerns:**
- Contact the **Legal Bureau** if the validity of a removal is in question

> **Exam Alert:** If the validity of a Section 9.58 removal is questioned, contact the **Legal Bureau** — not IAB, not the district attorney.
