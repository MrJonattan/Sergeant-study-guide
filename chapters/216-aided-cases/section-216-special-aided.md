# Section 216 — Special Aided Cases

## P.G. 216-07: Firearm Safety Stations at Psychiatric Wards and Admitting Areas

**Date Effective:** 07/01/20 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To utilize firearms safety stations, when available, for the safe unloading and reloading of firearms at psychiatric wards or psychiatric admitting areas of city hospitals.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Escort mentally ill or emotionally disturbed person to designated patient waiting area |
| 2 | Sign in and complete entries under captions in **Psychiatric Admitting Log** maintained by hospital security police |
| 3 | Proceed to designated firearms safety station, when available, and **unload firearm(s)** |
| 3a | Replace unloaded firearm(s) in holster and put loose ammunition, and/or magazine containing ammunition, **into pocket** |
| 4 | Confer with psychiatric admitting staff and provide necessary information |
| 5 | Safeguard patient at hospital until examined by a psychiatrist |
| 6 | Inform psychiatrist of circumstances which brought patient into police custody |
| 7 | Enter details in digital Activity Log and prepare AIDED REPORT (indicate name of psychiatrist and admission number) |
| 8 | Proceed to designated firearms safety station and **reload firearm(s)** prior to leaving hospital facility |
| 9 | Complete "time-out" entry in Psychiatric Admitting Log |
| 10 | Submit AIDED REPORT to desk officer |

> **Exam Alert:** If the psychiatrist **refers a patient to the hospital emergency room** for medical evaluation and clearance, the member shall **reload** firearm(s) at the safety station before leaving the psychiatric area, and **unload upon return**.

> **Exam Alert:** Interim entries must be made in "Remarks" column of the Psychiatric Admitting Log for each unload/reload cycle.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Patrol supervisors/integrity control officers shall comply with unloading procedures and make a **visit entry** in the Psychiatric Admitting Log when supervising subordinates.

### Relieving Member

If safeguarding extends beyond expiration of tour, the relieving uniformed member will:
- Comply with sign-in and firearms unloading procedures
- Inform psychiatrist of circumstances regarding aided

### DOC Forensic Psychiatry

When a mentally ill or emotionally disturbed prisoner is escorted to a Department of Correction Forensic Psychiatry Ward, comply with the regulations of the Department of Correction regarding unloading and safeguarding firearms.

## P.G. 216-08: Persons Overcome by Gas, Drugs, or Other Poisonous Substances

**Date Effective:** 07/01/20 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To secure special assistance and obtain samples of poisonous substances in appropriate cases.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Call an ambulance |
| 2 | Request Emergency Service Unit, if oxygen required |
| 3 | Give ambulance attendant information regarding the nature of the poisoning |
| 4 | Permit doctor or attendant to take the substance, prescription, or container, **if not required as evidence** |
| 4a | If evidence, give **sample** to doctor/attendant and deliver remainder to desk officer or detective |
| 5 | Notify and make samples available to Poison Control Center of the Health Department, if required |
| 6 | Obtain a receipt for such materials |
| 7 | Make digital Activity Log entry of facts and time notifications were made |
| 8 | Prepare AIDED REPORT |
| 9 | Prepare COMPLAINT REPORT WORKSHEET (PD313-152A), if necessary |

### Desk Officer Notification

The desk officer will notify the **Operations Unit** in:
- Poisoned or infected food cases
- Wood or denatured alcohol poisoning cases

> **Exam Alert:** The **Operations Unit** (not Poison Control) is notified for poisoned food and denatured alcohol cases.

### Additional Guidelines for Contaminated Food/Drug Samples

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Spoiled/contaminated food from restaurants | Refer to Dept. of Health, Food Poisoning Investigation Unit |
| Person treated at hospital for poisoning (sample available) | Take sample to DOH Laboratories, 455 First Avenue, Manhattan, with REQUEST FOR LABORATORY EXAMINATION REPORT (PD521-168) |
| Pattern of several separate poisoning incidents | Notify Poison Control Center; comply with instructions received |
| Mass removal of potentially dangerous product | Handled case by case; **NEVER** remove items from retail establishment to stationhouse without consultation with city/state/federal agencies via Operations Unit |
| Evidence with criminal sanctions | Deliver to Police Laboratory with REQUEST FOR LABORATORY EXAMINATION attached |
| Non-evidence materials | Invoice and forward to Property Clerk |
| Citizen-delivered substance (no public health/criminal allegation) | **Do NOT accept**; refer to private laboratories for civil cases |

> **Exam Alert:** Mass product removal is handled case by case. Under **no circumstances** will items be removed from retail shelves and brought to a stationhouse before consultation with agencies via the Operations Unit.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| AIDED REPORT | (Electronic) |
| COMPLAINT REPORT | PD313-152 |
| REQUEST FOR LABORATORY EXAMINATION REPORT | PD521-168 |

## P.G. 216-09: Animal Bites

**Date Effective:** 11/18/21 | **Revision:** I.O. 110 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To notify the NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene of human exposure to a rabid animal, an animal suspected of being rabid, and/or an animal that has bitten, scratched, or abraded a human being and to provide for observation of the animal involved.

### Key Definition

**HUMAN EXPOSURE TO RABIES** — A bite or a scratch from a rabid animal, an animal suspected of being rabid, or contamination of an open or fresh wound (having bled within the last twenty-four hours) or mucous membranes (eyes, nose, mouth) with infectious material (saliva, nervous tissue) from a rabid or suspected rabid animal.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Render reasonable aid and prepare AIDED REPORT, if applicable |
| 2 | Comply with P.G. 219-17, "Use of Patrol Kits" |
| 3 | Advise person to thoroughly **wash affected area with soap and water** and seek medical attention as quickly as possible (even if not serious) |
| 4 | Ascertain owner of animal; obtain description and attempt to locate for examination |
| 4b | If animal is located, comply with P.G. 214-33, "Care and Disposition of Animals" |
| 5 | Prepare **DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT (PD311-152)** in ALL instances involving an animal bite and/or dangerous animal; include ICAD Incident number |
| 5a | If the animal is gone upon arrival, a DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT **must still be prepared**, regardless of whether a person was injured |
| 6 | Deliver DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT and submit AIDED REPORT to desk officer |

> **Exam Alert:** The DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT must be prepared in **ALL instances** — even if the animal is gone and no person was injured.

### Desk Officer (Steps 7-13)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 7-8 | Review and approve AIDED REPORT; process per P.G. 216-02 |
| 9 | Review and sign DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT; distribute as delineated |
| 10 | Notify DOHMH by telephone of animal bite/exposure |
| 11 | Forward DOHMH copy of report via Mail and Distribution Unit |
| 12 | Notify DOHMH if animal/owner is located after report forwarded; ensure supplementary info recorded on AIDED REPORT in FORMS |
| 13 | File command copy of all DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORTS |

### DOHMH Contact Hours

| Time | Contact |
|------|---------|
| 0900-1700, Monday-Friday | Dept. of Health and Mental Hygiene, **Animal Bite Unit** |
| After hours | **Poison Control Center** of DOHMH |

> **Exam Alert:** After-hours animal bite notifications go to **Poison Control**, not the Animal Bite Unit.

### Critical Additional Data

**Raccoons:** Raccoons are primarily **nocturnal** animals; any raccoon seen active during the **daytime** should be considered rabid until proven otherwise. Any raccoon acting friendly or aggressively should be considered rabid.

> **Exam Alert:** Daytime raccoon = presumed rabid.

**Firearms and Animals:** Uniformed members shall **not discharge their firearms** at a dog or other animal **except** to protect themselves or another person from physical injury and there is **no other reasonable means** to eliminate the threat.

**Snakebite:** Requires notification to the Poison Control Center of DOHMH. Enter notification on AIDED REPORT.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| AIDED REPORT | (Electronic) |
| DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT | PD311-152 |

## P.G. 216-10: Taxicab Drivers

**Date Effective:** 08/01/13 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To report certain aided cases involving taxicab drivers to the NYC Taxi and Limousine Commission.

### Procedure

When a taxicab is involved and the driver has epilepsy, vertigo, heart disease, or other condition making him/her unfit to operate a taxi:

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | MOS | Follow normal aided case procedures |
| 2 | MOS | Obtain driver's credentials and give **RECEIPT FOR CREDENTIALS (PD616-011)** |
| 3 | Desk Officer | Forward credentials and facts on **REPORT OF VIOLATION (PD672-151)** to NYC Taxi and Limousine Commission |
| 4 | Desk Officer | Note on REPORT OF VIOLATION if unable to obtain credentials |

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|

| REPORT OF VIOLATION | PD672-151 |

## P.G. 216-11: Communicable Disease

**Date Effective:** 11/18/21 | **Revision:** I.O. 109 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To assist in the orderly removal of a person with a communicable disease.

### Definition

**COMMUNICABLE DISEASE** — An infectious disease capable of being passed to another by direct contact with an infected individual or his/her discharges.

### Procedure

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Desk Officer | Assign a uniformed member to assist in the removal of patient |
| 2 | MOS | Comply with A.G. 330-09, "Exposure of Members of the Service to Infectious Diseases or Hazardous Materials" |
| 3 | MOS | Accompany patient to the hospital when requested by attendant or physician |

### Confidentiality Rules

> **Exam Alert:** Information concerning a prisoner's or victim's communicable disease status must be kept **CONFIDENTIAL**.

- Generally should **NOT** be released to: the public, the media, the person's family and friends, or other prisoners
- Any release may only be made with the **written consent of the Deputy Commissioner — Legal Matters**

> **Exam Alert:** Only the **Deputy Commissioner — Legal Matters** can authorize release of communicable disease information (with written consent).

### Prisoner Segregation

- Prisoners who **volunteer** their positive status indicating the need for segregation or medical treatment should be placed in **isolation cells**, if available
- This information may be recorded on a Prisoner Movement Slip or on form **MEDICAL TREATMENT OF PRISONER (PD244-150)** to alert Corrections, DA's offices, or courts

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| MEDICAL TREATMENT OF PRISONER | PD244-150 |

## P.G. 216-12: School Crossing Guard (SCG) Injured Line of Duty

### Purpose

To report and investigate injuries to School Crossing Guards sustained in the line of duty.

### Procedure

#### Uniformed Member of the Service
- Request **Patrol Supervisor** to respond

#### Patrol Supervisor
- Interview School Crossing Guard (get hospital, EMS, and doctor info if removed)
- Interview witnesses and have them make a **Witness Statement**
- Document:
  - **Employee's Notice of Injury (Law Department Form WCD 23)** — have SCG prepare in own handwriting, if possible, **2 copies**
  - Submit to **C.O. of Precinct of occurrence** (breaks the chain of command)
  - Have the UMOS prepare either an **Aided Report** or the **PAR**, if vehicle involved
- Notify:
  - Report circumstances to the Desk Officer
  - If SCG brought to hospital, notify Ops promptly
  - A **2nd notification** is required before final reports are prepared to update condition

> **Exam Alert:** No LODI Report for Crossing Guards — it is a WCD 23 "Employee Notice of Injury" instead per P.G. 216-12.

> **Exam Alert:** The WCD 23 is submitted to the **C.O. of the Precinct of occurrence** — this breaks the chain of command (not through the desk officer) per P.G. 216-12.

#### Desk Officer
- Document:
  - Command Log entry regarding circumstances and result of investigation
  - Report on Typed Letterhead (49) — attach a COPY of the Command Log entry and deliver to C.O.
- Notify:
  - Employee Resources Section (ERS), Civilian Employee Services Unit — **NEXT BUSINESS DAY**
  - Operations — **WITHIN 24 HOURS**

> **Exam Alert — Timely Reporting:** Desk Officer calls Operations within **24 hours**. C.O. directs "Supervisor's Report of Injury" within **48 hours** per P.G. 216-12.

#### Commanding Officer
- Forward 2 copies of (WCD 23, 49, and Witness Statement) to ERS, Attn: Civilian Employee Unit
- Crossing Guard is **Not Paid** — keep Guard "on payroll, WITHOUT PAY" until they return and cause the "Election of Rate of Change Against Annual and/or Sick Leave Balances" form to be prepared
- Notify ERS, Civilian Employee Unit when Guard returns to duty, goes sick again on this injury, and when returns again from that last sick
- Document:
  - Direct "Supervisor's Report of Injury" gets made within **48 hours**
  - Direct "Report to Employee Benefits Unit" be prepared
- C.O. directs that 3 reports get made (doesn't specifically say by whom):
  1. Supervisor's Report of Injury — **WITHIN 48 HOURS**
  2. Report to Employee Benefits Unit
  3. Election of Rate of Change Against Annual and/or Sick Leave Balances

#### Forms and Reports Summary

| Who | Form/Report |
|-----|-------------|
| UMOS | Aided Report or PAR |
| School Crossing Guard | Employee's Notice of Injury (WCD 23) — given to C.O. |
| Desk Officer | Typed Letterhead (given to C.O.) |
| Desk Officer | Command Log entry (given to C.O.) |
| Witness | WITNESS STATEMENT — Injury To Member Of Department |
