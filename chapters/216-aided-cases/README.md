# Section 216 — Aided Cases

## Chapter Overview

Section 216 of the NYPD Patrol Guide governs all **aided cases** — incidents where a person (other than a prisoner) requires medical aid or assistance. These procedures cover situations ranging from routine sick-at-home calls to dead body investigations, psychiatric admissions, defibrillator use, and involuntary removals under Mental Hygiene Law.

Sergeants must understand when to respond, what notifications are required, how to supervise aided scenes, and the specific rules that govern specialized scenarios such as Department of Correction deaths, carbon monoxide alarms, and Mobile Crisis Team removals.

## Learning Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Define "aided case" and distinguish it from routine sick-at-home and nursing home calls.
2. Identify the correct steps for handling a dead human body, including Medical Examiner notifications and body identification at the morgue.
3. Explain firearm safety station procedures at psychiatric wards.
4. Describe the patrol supervisor's role in drug overdose, carbon monoxide, and EDP-related aided cases.
5. State the rules for animal bite reporting and when firearms may be discharged at an animal.
6. List the notifications required for aided cases involving DOC facilities, firefighters, and MTA incidents.
7. Explain the AED program, including kit contents, utilization definition, and documentation requirements.
8. Describe the procedures for TB shelter client removals and the Department's limited role.
9. Articulate the requirements for Mobile Crisis Team / ACT Team removals under Section 9.58 of Mental Hygiene Law.
10. Identify key forms, reports, and their associated procedure numbers.

## Study Tips

- Pay close attention to **who may disturb a dead body** (P.G. 216-04) — a frequent exam topic.
- Memorize the **AED kit contents** and the definition of "utilized" (P.G. 216-19).
- Understand the difference between **988 hotline** referrals and EDP handling (P.G. 216-01).
- Know when uniformed members **will NOT** respond (nursing homes, DOC facilities).
- The **investigating supervisor hierarchy** for auxiliary police injuries (P.G. 216-13) is testable.
