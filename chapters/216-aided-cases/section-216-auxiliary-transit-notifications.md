# Section 216 — Auxiliary Police, Transit, Notifications, and DOC Deaths

## P.G. 216-13: Line of Duty Injury or Death — Auxiliary Police Officers

**Date Effective:** 02/03/25 | **Revision:** R.O. 8 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To report and record line of duty injuries and deaths of auxiliary police officers.

### Key Definitions

**LINE OF DUTY** — An incident that occurred while a member was on duty. Auxiliary police officers are considered **ON DUTY** after they "sign-in" on the **Auxiliary Police Roll Call (APS #10)** until they sign out at the end of their tour.

> **Exam Alert:** Line of duty designation will **NOT** be given for any off-duty incidents. There is **no off-duty designation** for auxiliary police.

**INVESTIGATING SUPERVISOR** — Determined by a hierarchy based on severity:

| Priority | Investigating Supervisor | When |
|----------|------------------------|------|
| 1st | Lieutenant platoon commander, precinct of occurrence | If available |
| 2nd | Patrol supervisor, precinct of occurrence | If lieutenant platoon commander unavailable |
| 3rd | Precinct CO/XO/duty captain | If injured auxiliary sustains **serious physical injury**, is **likely to die**, or **dies** |

> **Exam Alert:** The investigating supervisor hierarchy escalates based on injury severity. CO/XO/duty captain handles the most serious cases.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service (Steps 1-3)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Request patrol supervisor to respond |
| 2 | Notify desk officer of: (a) circumstances of injury, and (b) request witnesses to await arrival of supervisor and precinct auxiliary coordinator (if on duty) |
| 3 | Remain at scene with auxiliary police officer unless: (a) hospitalization/medical attention required, and/or (b) further police action necessary |

**Note:** If injury results from a reportable use of force incident, comply with P.G. 221-03 and P.G. 221-06.

### Patrol Supervisor (Steps 4-7)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 4 | Respond to scene |
| 5 | Interview auxiliary police officer; if removed to hospital, obtain name of hospital, attendant, and doctor |
| 6 | Have auxiliary police officer prepare, **in own handwriting** if possible, two copies of **Employee's Notice of Injury (Law Department Form WCD 23)** |
| 6a | If member unable, patrol supervisor will prepare and submit report to CO, precinct of occurrence |
| 7 | Request witnesses to await arrival of investigating supervisor, if necessary |

> **Exam Alert:** The Employee's Notice of Injury must be prepared in the **auxiliary officer's own handwriting** when possible. Two copies are required.

### Patrol Supervisor — Continued (Steps 8-11)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 8 | Obtain and safeguard shield, identification card, portable radio, and personal property of auxiliary police officer, if hospitalized |
| 9 | Report circumstances to desk officer |
| 10 | Prepare Police Accident Report (MV104AN), if vehicle collision involved |
| 11 | Have AIDED REPORT prepared, if vehicle not involved |

### Desk Officer (Steps 12-17)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 12 | Notify investigating supervisor as indicated in definitions |
| 13 | Make Command Log entry |
| 14 | Notify **Operations Unit** and supervisor at **Auxiliary Police Section** |
| 14a | Notify member's command, if not assigned to precinct of occurrence |
| 15 | Notify precinct CO/XO/duty captain and Medical Examiner, if member dies or is likely to die |
| 16 | Notify borough commander, if member dies |
| 17 | Enter results of investigation in Command Log, indicating whether injury was sustained in line of duty |

### Investigating Supervisor (Steps 18-23)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 18 | Interview injured auxiliary police officer and investigate circumstances |
| 19 | Notify Operations Unit promptly if injured member requires hospital treatment |
| 19a | Additional notification to Operations Unit required before final reports to update status |
| 20 | Make initial report to desk officer without delay |
| 21 | Prepare THREAT, RESISTANCE OR INJURY (T.R.I.) INCIDENT REPORT, as appropriate |
| 22 | Interview witnesses; request they prepare and sign **WITNESS STATEMENT — INJURY TO MEMBER OF THE DEPARTMENT (PD429-065)** |
| 23 | Prepare report on Typed Letterhead or UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT (PD370-152); forward to precinct commander |

### CO, Auxiliary Police Section (Steps 24-25)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 24 | Designate supervisory member to respond and assist precinct CO/XO/duty captain if injured auxiliary dies or is likely to die |
| 25 | Prepare T.R.I. INTERACTION REPORT per P.G. 221-06 for each auxiliary member injured as a result of force |

### Auxiliary Police Coordinator (Step 26)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 26 | Ensure all required reports and forms are forwarded to Auxiliary Police Section within **ten (10) days**, including copies of UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORTS |

> **Exam Alert:** Reports must reach the Auxiliary Police Section within **10 days**.

### CO/XO/Duty Captain (Steps 27-28)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 27 | Prepare report on Typed Letterhead or UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE REPORT for LOD death or serious injury/likely to die; forward per P.G. 212-09 (exclude copy for Police Pension Fund) |
| 28 | Forward two copies of Employee's Notice of Injury and WITNESS STATEMENT to CO, Auxiliary Police Section |

### Additional Data

In LOD cases where an auxiliary police officer dies or is likely to die, the command auxiliary police coordinator where the member is assigned shall maintain liaison with the Patrol Borough Auxiliary Police Section. In the absence of the coordinator, the precinct CO will designate an alternate.

## P.G. 216-14: Aided Cases on New York City Transit Trains

**Date Issued:** 10/18/16 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To provide procedures for handling aided cases occurring on trains.

### Procedure — Uniformed Member of the Service

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Ascertain condition of aided (unconscious, pregnant, epileptic, intoxicated, bleeding, etc.) |
| 2 | Transmit information immediately via radio dispatcher |
| 3 | **Remove aided from train**, if feasible, unless not advisable due to nature of illness/injury |
| 3a | Seek assistance of train crew or passengers, if necessary |
| 4 | Keep radio dispatcher informed of pertinent developments |
| 5 | **Avoid unnecessary interruptions of train service** |
| 6 | Notify passengers of delay in service and alternate routes, when possible |
| 7 | Prepare AIDED REPORT |

> **Exam Alert:** The primary goal is to **remove the aided from the train** when feasible. If not feasible, the radio dispatcher and **NYC Transit Command Center** will coordinate moving the train to a location with adequate assistance.

> **Exam Alert:** Unnecessary interruption of train service can **increase temperatures significantly** in trains/stations and may **cause panic** on crowded trains stopped in tunnels. Service will be interrupted **only when absolutely necessary**.

### Radio Dispatcher (Steps 8-12)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 8 | Obtain all relevant information concerning aided |
| 9 | Provide all relevant information to field units responding |
| 10 | Dispatch sufficient personnel and assistance promptly |
| 11 | Notify other city agencies as necessary |
| 12 | Notify Operations Unit for serious incidents |

## P.G. 216-15: Notifications

**Date Issued:** 10/18/16 | **Pages:** 2

### Purpose

To ensure that relatives or friends are notified, where necessary, in aided and collision cases.

### Procedure — Desk Officer

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Direct notifications be made **by telephone**, if possible |

> **Exam Alert:** Make notifications **tactfully and in person**, if possible, when the aided/collision victim is **deceased or seriously injured and likely to die**. Efforts should be made to have a relative, friend, or neighbor present.

> **Sergeant Focus:** In death or likely-to-die cases, the desk officer will ensure a notification is made in a timely manner and **document the time, date, and name** of the member making notification on the AIDED REPORT or Police Accident Report.

### Notification Assignments (Step 2)

| Scenario | Who Makes Notification |
|----------|----------------------|
| Relatives/friends within precinct of occurrence | Member assigned to precinct |
| Relatives/friends outside precinct but within NYC | Member assigned to **resident precinct** |
| Relatives/friends outside NYC | **Inter-City Correspondence Unit** |
| Firefighter killed/injured at fire | **DO NOT** notify without permission of fire officer in charge |
| Inmate who dies in DOC facility | **DO NOT** notify unless requested by Correction supervisor in charge |
| Homicide victim | **Assigned detective** makes notification |

> **Exam Alert:** Three "DO NOT" rules: (1) No firefighter family notification without fire officer permission, (2) No DOC inmate family notification unless Correction requests it, (3) Homicide notification is always the detective's responsibility.

### Special Notifications (Step 3)

| Scenario | Desk Officer Notifies |
|----------|----------------------|
| Aided killed/injured on **MTA bus** | NYC Transit |
| Aided killed/injured on **scaffold** | Department of Buildings |
| Aided killed/injured at **construction site** | Operations Unit |
| Aided killed/injured on **Parks Department property** | Operations Unit |
| Aided killed/injured in **factory or store** | Operations Unit |

> **Exam Alert:** MTA bus goes to NYC Transit, scaffold goes to Buildings, everything else (construction, Parks, factory/store) goes to **Operations Unit**.

### Documentation (Steps 4-10)

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 4 | MOS | Record notification details on AIDED REPORT or MV104AN |
| 4a | | Under "Details," enter rank/name of fire officer or DOC supervisor, as applicable |
| 4b | | Indicate which agency will make notifications |
| 5 | MOS | If notification cannot be made, electronically save AIDED REPORT and notify desk officer |
| 6 | D.O. | Review and sign AIDED REPORT; process per P.G. 216-02 |
| 7 | D.O. | Print and retain duplicate copy at desk if notifications not yet made; mark "Supplementary" |
| 8 | D.O. | Enter notification into FORMS once obtained; file "Supplementary" AIDED REPORT |
| 9 | D.O. | Enter detective's name on AIDED REPORT for homicide victim |
| 10 | D.O. | Ensure appropriate notifications are made once aided info is inputted into FORMS |

**Note:** In all other cases, notifications will be made **even though other authorities** (hospital, etc.) indicate they have already made the notification.

## P.G. 216-16: Suspicious Death or Suicide in Department of Correction Facility

**Date Issued:** 10/18/16 | **Pages:** 1

### Purpose

To investigate suspicious deaths or suicides in Correction Department facilities.

### Procedure — Desk Officer

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Notify: |
| 1a | Precinct detective squad (if unavailable, notify detective borough command) |
| 1b | Commanding officer/duty captain |
| 1c | Patrol borough |
| 1d | **Internal Affairs Bureau** (obtain log number) |
| 1e | Operations Unit |

> **Exam Alert:** IAB must be notified for DOC suspicious deaths/suicides — and you must **obtain a log number**.

> **Exam Alert:** Uniformed members of the service assigned to duty in uniform **WILL NOT** respond to Correction facility.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2 | Direct preparation of AIDED REPORT; include under "Details" name/rank of DOC supervisor present and identity of person making death pronouncement |
| 3 | Have COMPLAINT REPORT (PD313-152) prepared; include IAB log number |
| 4 | Verify that Medical Examiner's office has been notified |
| 4a | Enter time of notification, identity of person receiving call, and name of ME on AIDED REPORT |
| 4b | Make follow-up call if ME does not respond within **one hour** |
| 4c | Obtain ME case number for entry on AIDED REPORT |
| 5 | Record all calls to ME in Telephone Record |
| 6 | Notifications **WILL NOT** be made to relatives/friends of an inmate who dies in DOC facility, unless requested by Correction supervisor in charge |
| 6a | Indicate on AIDED REPORT that DOC will make notification, where applicable |
| 7 | Request permission of Medical Examiner prior to moving body |

> **Exam Alert:** Two key "WILL NOT" rules for DOC deaths: (1) Uniformed members will NOT respond to the facility, and (2) Notifications will NOT be made to family unless DOC requests it.

### Key Forms

| Form | Number |
|------|--------|
| AIDED REPORT | (Electronic) |
| COMPLAINT REPORT | PD313-152 |
