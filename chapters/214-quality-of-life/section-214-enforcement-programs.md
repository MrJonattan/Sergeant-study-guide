# Enforcement Programs — Narcotics, Graffiti, and Establishments

## Overview

This section covers three distinct enforcement programs that target quality-of-life degradation at the community level: narcotics eviction, graffiti vandalism rewards, and multi-agency operations against problem establishments. Sergeants must understand the interagency coordination requirements, documentation standards, and the limits of police authority in each program.

---

## P.G. 214-02 — Narcotics Eviction Program

### Key Points

- Cooperative effort between NYPD and the District Attorney's Office to evict drug dealers through **civil court** proceedings
- All narcotics arrests and seizures inside locations are reviewed by the DA's Office for eviction potential
- Controlled substances must be maintained at the Police Lab or Property Clerk for a **minimum of three months**
- The DA determines whether facts substantiate a civil court action

### Evidence Standards

- **Direct evidence:** observations of sale, inculpatory statements
- **Circumstantial evidence:** 1/8 ounce or more of narcotics, cutting agents, scales, packaging materials
- **Personal use amounts do NOT support eviction**
- Activity outside homes/stores (stairwells, hallways, street) will **not** support action unless linked to interior premises

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- CO/XO/Special Ops Lt: review precinct Complaint Reports to determine if a narcotics operation is indicated
- Special Ops Lt: package information and forward to the respective county DA's Narcotics Eviction Program Coordinator
- Ensure uniformed MOS prepare the precinct COMPLAINT REPORT (PD313-152)

> **Exam Alert:** Controlled substances must be maintained at the Police Lab/Property Clerk for a minimum of **three months**. Personal use amounts do NOT support eviction proceedings.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The Special Ops Lt is responsible for packaging and forwarding information to the DA's Narcotics Eviction Program Coordinator. The CO/XO/Special Ops Lt must review all narcotics arrest reports for eviction potential.

## P.G. 214-03 — Graffiti Reward Program

### Key Points

- Admin Code 10-117.2 authorizes a reward of up to **$500** for information leading to the arrest of graffiti vandals
- MOS must photograph graffiti using a Department digital camera and upload via **"Graffiti Photo Upload" in OMNIFORM**
- Immediately notify the **Citywide Vandals Task Force (CVTF)** for prisoner debriefing
- Obtain a log number from CVTF and enter it in the "Notifications" section of the Complaint Report Worksheet

### Reward Eligibility

- **No law enforcement officers** eligible for rewards
- **No city employees** eligible for rewards
- Reward board is chaired by the XO, Office of Chief of Department, plus a NYC Police Foundation representative

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure MOS properly photograph and upload graffiti evidence through OMNIFORM
- Verify CVTF notification and log number entry on the Complaint Report Worksheet

> **Exam Alert:** The graffiti reward is up to **$500**. No law enforcement officers or city employees are eligible. Photos are uploaded via "Graffiti Photo Upload" in OMNIFORM.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Ensure MOS obtain the CVTF log number and enter it in the "Notifications" section of the Complaint Report Worksheet.

## P.G. 214-39 — C.U.R.E. Operations

### Key Points

- **C.U.R.E.** = Coordinating a United Resolution with Establishments
- Multi-agency operation targeting establishments causing community complaints
- Overseen by the **Mayor's Office of Nightlife**
- Coordinated by the **Legal Bureau's Civil Enforcement Unit**
- Must be approved by the **Chief of Patrol** with notice to the Mayor's Office of Nightlife
- Applies to any commercial establishment licensed or required to be licensed by the State or City

### Selection Criteria

Eligible bases for selection include:
- Complaint reports, summonses, arrests, community complaints
- 911 calls, 311 requests, general disorder, business violations
- Lack of cooperation with authorities
- Aided cases from intoxication or overdose
- Documented pattern of problems

### What is NOT Sufficient Grounds

- Refusal to attend nightlife meetings
- Property crimes beyond the establishment's control
- Crimes where security acted properly

### C.U.R.E. Notice and Timeline

- **C.U.R.E. NOTICE (PD635-121)** must be served at least **30 days** prior to the operation
- Prior notice is NOT required when it would compromise an active investigation or pose a health/safety risk
- CO determines within **30 days** of the NOTICE whether to: conduct the operation, do additional monitoring, or take no further action
- Chief of Patrol approves or disapproves through written endorsement

### Mediation Alternative

- Mayor's Office of Nightlife offers the **M.E.N.D. Program** (free mediation)
- The Department's role during C.U.R.E. operations is to assure safety of all involved

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- CO must make a determination within 30 days of serving the C.U.R.E. NOTICE
- Ensure the C.U.R.E. NOTICE (PD635-121) is properly served at least 30 days before operations
- Understand the distinction between valid and insufficient selection criteria

> **Exam Alert:** The C.U.R.E. NOTICE (PD635-121) must be served at least **30 days** prior to the operation. The CO must determine a course of action within **30 days** of the notice. The Chief of Patrol must approve the operation.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Refusal to attend nightlife meetings is NOT sufficient grounds for a C.U.R.E. operation. Know the three possible outcomes after the 30-day notice period: conduct operation, additional monitoring, or no further action.
