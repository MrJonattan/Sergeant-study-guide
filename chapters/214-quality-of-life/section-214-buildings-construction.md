# Buildings and Construction — Sites, Vacant Buildings, Stop Work Orders, Security, Repairs, and DOB Warrants

## Overview

These six procedures address police responsibilities related to construction sites, vacant buildings, stop work orders, securing commercial premises, repairing damage from warrant entries, and assisting the Department of Buildings. A common theme is that MOS authority is limited — police preserve peace and address safety hazards but do not enforce civil penalties or assist in property damage.

---

## P.G. 214-16 — Construction Sites

### Key Points

- MOS: request a **supervisor** to respond
- Supervisor investigates and directs referral to another department if safety is not involved
- MOS shall **NOT** enforce Admin Code violations that carry only a civil penalty
- Emergency during non-working hours: notify the **telephone switchboard** for notification to departments concerned

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Respond to the scene and investigate
- Determine if a safety hazard exists or if the matter is a civil penalty issue
- Direct referral to the appropriate city department when safety is not involved

> **Exam Alert:** MOS shall **NOT** enforce Admin Code violations with only a civil penalty at construction sites. For emergencies during non-working hours, notify the **telephone switchboard**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor must respond, investigate, and make the determination about whether the condition involves safety or is solely a civil penalty matter.

## P.G. 214-17 — Vacant Buildings

### Key Points

- MOS: examine the building, notify supervisor if a hazard exists, make an Activity Log entry
- Supervisor: determine the degree of hazard, direct placement of barriers or assign officers, call **ESU** if required
- Switchboard operator: enter condition on the **HIGHWAY CONDITION RECORD** and telephone the Department of Buildings
- CO: periodically review the vacant house list and HIGHWAY CONDITION RECORD

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Determine the degree of hazard at the vacant building
- Direct barriers or post officers as needed
- Request ESU when structural or access hazards require specialized response
- CO must periodically review the vacant house list and HIGHWAY CONDITION RECORD

> **Exam Alert:** The switchboard operator enters the condition on the **HIGHWAY CONDITION RECORD** and notifies the Department of Buildings by telephone. The CO periodically reviews both the vacant house list and the HIGHWAY CONDITION RECORD.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Determine the degree of hazard and decide whether barriers, posted officers, or ESU response is appropriate.

## P.G. 214-18 — Stop Work Orders

### Key Points

- MOS assist the **Department of Buildings** representative in enforcing stop work orders
- Role is to preserve peace while a "Request to Appear" notice is served
- The enforcement action itself is taken by the DOB representative

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure MOS understand their role is limited to preserving peace
- Confirm DOB representative is present and leading the enforcement action

> **Exam Alert:** MOS role at stop work order enforcement is to **preserve peace** while the DOB representative serves the "Request to Appear" notice.

## P.G. 214-19 — Chains/Padlocks to Secure Commercial Premises

### Key Points

- Used for **front breaks** at commercial premises
- The **patrol supervisor** determines if the premise can be secured with a padlock and chain
- **ESU** responds, secures the premise, and attaches a **NOTIFICATION TO OWNER (MISC. 3930)**
- ESU delivers the key to the **desk officer**
- Desk officer safeguards the key and delivers it to an assigned member when the owner arrives

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Patrol supervisor makes the initial determination about whether the premise can be secured
- Ensure ESU is requested to respond, secure the premise, and attach the notification
- Verify the desk officer receives and safeguards the key

> **Exam Alert:** **ESU** secures the premises and attaches the NOTIFICATION TO OWNER (MISC. 3930). The **patrol supervisor** makes the determination to secure. ESU delivers the key to the **desk officer**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor is the decision-maker on whether to secure the premises. The chain of custody for the key runs from ESU to the desk officer to the assigned member when the owner appears.

## P.G. 214-20 — Repair of Entrances Damaged During Forced Warrant Entry

### Key Points

- Applies when the **wrong premise** is entered during warrant execution
- Supervisor: ask the owner/tenant their preference — **self-repair or Department repair**
- CO/Duty Captain: assess the damage and notify the **Operations Unit** for the Building Maintenance Section
- MOS assigned to **secure the location until repairs are complete**
- Building Maintenance Section reports quarterly cost to the **Deputy Commissioner, Management and Budget**

### NYCHA Developments

- Repairs at NYCHA developments are handled by **NYCHA maintenance staff**
- PSA supervisor notifies NYCHA maintenance

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ask the owner/tenant whether they prefer self-repair or Department repair
- CO/Duty Captain assesses damage and contacts Operations Unit
- Assign MOS to secure the location until repairs are completed

> **Exam Alert:** This procedure applies only when the **wrong premise** is entered. The owner/tenant chooses between self-repair or Department repair. NYCHA repairs are handled by **NYCHA maintenance staff**. Building Maintenance Section reports costs **quarterly** to the Deputy Commissioner, Management and Budget.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor's first action is to ask the owner/tenant their preference for repair. An MOS must be assigned to secure the location until repairs are complete.

## P.G. 214-37 — DOB Access Warrants

### Key Points

- An Access Warrant is a **Court Order** allowing a DOB inspector to access premises for a safety inspection
- Desk officer: review the warrant — must be **signed by a Supreme Court judge** and **not expired**
- MOS role: **preserve peace and prevent crime ONLY**
- MOS will **NOT** assist in breaking a door or damaging property
- If access is not granted: **DOB reschedules** (not a police responsibility)

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Desk officer verifies the warrant is properly signed by a Supreme Court judge and is current
- Ensure MOS understand their role is limited to peacekeeping
- Confirm MOS do not assist in forced entry or property damage

> **Exam Alert:** The Access Warrant must be signed by a **Supreme Court judge** and must not be expired. MOS will **NOT** assist in breaking doors or damaging property. If access is denied, **DOB reschedules** — it is not a police responsibility to force entry.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer is responsible for reviewing the warrant for proper judicial signature and expiration. Police authority is strictly limited to preserving peace — DOB handles all enforcement and rescheduling.
