# Vehicles and Snow — Emergency Regulations, Snow Removal, Derelict Vehicles, Expired Plates, and Parkway Removal

## Overview

These five procedures cover vehicle-related enforcement and snow emergency operations. Sergeants must know the specific timeframes for snow removal summonses, the process for handling derelict vehicles and expired registration plates, and the authority for removing vehicles from parkways and highways. Pay close attention to the 5-day timelines and the role of the Stolen Property Inquiry Unit.

---

## P.G. 214-26 — Snow Emergency Regulations

### Key Points

- Triggered by a **FINEST message** from the **DOT Commissioner**
- Summonses issued for:
  - **Traffic Reg 4-12(k)(1):** Abandoning or parking illegally on a snow street
  - **Traffic Reg 4-12(k)(2):** Operating without snow tires or chains and becoming stuck
- If a vehicle is **not immobilized**: warn the operator and direct them to leave the snow street at the nearest exit

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Monitor for FINEST messages activating snow emergency regulations
- Ensure MOS issue summonses for violations on designated snow streets
- Direct MOS to warn and redirect non-immobilized vehicles rather than immediately summonsing

> **Exam Alert:** Snow emergencies are triggered by a **FINEST message** from the **DOT Commissioner**. Vehicles not immobilized receive a warning and are directed to leave at the **nearest exit** — not an automatic summons.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The activation mechanism is the FINEST message. For vehicles that can still move, the response is a warning and direction to leave, not immediate enforcement.

## P.G. 214-27 — Snow Removal

### Key Points

- Summons issued for **Admin Code 16-123** if snow is not removed **four hours** after snow stops falling
- MOS must prevent the throwing of snow into cleared roadways

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure MOS understand the 4-hour threshold before summons issuance
- Monitor for snow being thrown into cleared roadways

> **Exam Alert:** The snow removal deadline is **four hours** after snow stops falling (Admin Code 16-123). Throwing snow into cleared roadways is also a violation.

## P.G. 214-29 — Derelict Vehicles with Registration Plates

### Key Points

- **Derelict** = so dilapidated, burned, or stripped as to have no value except scrap
- Derelict vehicles are only taken into custody when required as **evidence**
- The **Department of Sanitation** removes all derelict vehicles not required as evidence
- Notify the **Stolen Property Inquiry Unit** in ALL cases

### Registration Plate Removal

| Plate Type | Form |
|-----------|------|
| New York plates | REGISTRATION PLATE REMOVAL NOTICE (PD571-120) |
| Out-of-state plates | EXPIRED REGISTRATION PLATE REMOVAL PROGRAM (PD571-124) |

### Owner Notification and Timeline

- Owner notified to pick up plates within **5 days**
- If unclaimed after 5 days: forward plates and typed letterhead to DMV
- Desk officer: assign a check **5 days later**, then notify Department of Sanitation if the vehicle is still present

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure the Stolen Property Inquiry Unit is notified in all cases
- Desk officer assigns the 5-day follow-up check
- Desk officer notifies Department of Sanitation if the vehicle remains after the follow-up

> **Exam Alert:** Notify the **Stolen Property Inquiry Unit** in ALL derelict vehicle cases. Owner has **5 days** to pick up plates. NY plates use **PD571-120**; out-of-state plates use **PD571-124**. Department of **Sanitation** (not police) removes derelict vehicles.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer must assign the 5-day follow-up check and then contact the Department of Sanitation if the vehicle remains. Derelict vehicles are only taken into police custody when needed as evidence.

## P.G. 214-30 — Removal of Expired Registration Plates

### Key Points

- Applies to plates expired **more than 2 months**
- Issue a summons for unregistered vehicle
- Remove plates and follow the same process as P.G. 214-29
- **Occupied vehicle** with expired plates: remove to stationhouse, comply with **P.G. 218-12**

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure MOS verify plates are expired more than 2 months before taking action
- Direct proper handling of occupied vehicles with expired plates per P.G. 218-12

> **Exam Alert:** The threshold is plates expired **more than 2 months**. Occupied vehicles with expired plates are removed to the stationhouse under **P.G. 218-12**. The plate removal process follows the same steps as P.G. 214-29.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The 2-month expiration threshold is the trigger. Know the distinction between unoccupied vehicles (remove plates on scene) and occupied vehicles (remove to stationhouse per P.G. 218-12).

## P.G. 214-31 — Removal of Vehicles from Parkways/Highways/Expressways

### Key Points

- **Admin Code 20-511(q)** requires removal of vehicles from highways
- Advise the owner that the tow car operator is entitled to charge towing and storage fees
- Owner must appear at the precinct with the **registration certificate** for a written release
- MOS signs an **"Authorization to Tow"** if the owner refuses or the vehicle is unattended
- Notify the **Stolen Property Inquiry Unit** immediately

### Highway District Vehicles

- Vehicles with **"protective bumper guards"** may push disabled vehicles to the nearest safe parking area
- May **NOT** push vehicles to repair shops or private property

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure the Stolen Property Inquiry Unit is notified immediately
- Verify the owner is advised of towing/storage fee liability
- Confirm MOS sign the "Authorization to Tow" when the owner refuses or is absent

> **Exam Alert:** The owner must present the **registration certificate** at the precinct for a written release. Highway District vehicles with **protective bumper guards** may push to the nearest safe parking — NOT to repair shops or private property. Notify the **Stolen Property Inquiry Unit** immediately.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Two key notifications: the owner about towing fees, and the Stolen Property Inquiry Unit about the vehicle. Highway vehicles can push to safe parking only — never to repair shops or private property.
