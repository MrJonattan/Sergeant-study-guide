# Key Terms — Section 214: Quality of Life Matters

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| C.U.R.E. | Coordinating a United Resolution with Establishments — multi-agency operation targeting problem commercial establishments |
| M.E.N.D. Program | Free mediation offered by the Mayor's Office of Nightlife as an alternative to C.U.R.E. operations |
| Chronic 311 Condition | 5 or more 311 requests in a 28-day period for the same condition at the same location |
| Chronic Abuser Alarm | Location with 3 or more unnecessary/unfounded alarms in a 3-month period |
| Dangerous Animal | An animal that menaces, threatens, attacks, or bites a person |
| Derelict Vehicle | A vehicle so dilapidated, burned, or stripped as to have no value except scrap |
| Door Service | Method of serving alarm notice by placing in mailbox or under door when no qualified person is available |
| Deferred Service | Method where CPO serves alarm notice the next business day when service cannot be made at the scene |
| Qualified Person (alarm service) | Owner, corporate officer, manager, supervisor, or adult employee/resident |
| Unlawful Eviction | Eviction through force, denial of essential services, removing possessions, changing locks, or interfering with comfort |
| Access Warrant | Court Order signed by a Supreme Court judge allowing DOB to inspect premises |
| OMAP | Designated liaison between NYPD and NYC 311 |
| CVTF | Citywide Vandals Task Force — handles graffiti vandal debriefing |
