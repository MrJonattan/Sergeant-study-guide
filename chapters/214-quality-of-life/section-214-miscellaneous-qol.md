# Miscellaneous Quality of Life — TLC Padlock, Signs, Obscenity, Refrigerators, Animals, and 311

## Overview

This section covers six diverse quality-of-life procedures. The most heavily tested topics here are care and disposition of animals (214-33) and 311 service requests (214-35). Sergeants must know the specific notification requirements, timeframes, and the limits of police authority in each area.

---

## P.G. 214-09 — TLC Padlock Enforcement

### Key Points

- MOS accompany TLC agents to padlock unlicensed livery base locations
- MOS accompany TLC for both the **initial sealing** AND the **follow-up padlocking** (10 days later)
- MOS function: **preserve peace** and ensure TLC representatives are not prevented from performing duties
- Arresting officers and complainants are provided **by TLC**
- Desk officer: assign **daily inspections** of padlocked locations

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Desk officer assigns daily inspections of padlocked locations
- Ensure MOS understand their role is peacekeeping only — TLC provides arresting officers and complainants

> **Exam Alert:** MOS accompany TLC for both the initial sealing AND the follow-up padlocking **10 days later**. TLC provides the arresting officers and complainants. The desk officer assigns **daily inspections** of padlocked locations.

## P.G. 214-10 — Unlawful Posting of Signs

### Key Points

- Summons for **Admin Code 10-119** (violation, not misdemeanor)
- Applies to posting on: gutter, lamppost, telephone pole, tree
- Remove the sign if possible and place it in a trash basket

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure MOS issue proper summons under Admin Code 10-119
- Direct removal of signs when feasible

> **Exam Alert:** Unlawful sign posting is a **violation** under Admin Code 10-119. Signs posted on gutters, lampposts, telephone poles, or trees are subject to summons.

## P.G. 214-11 — Obscenity/Offensive Material

### Key Points

- Governed by **PL Article 235** (Obscenity) and **PL 245.11** (Public Display of Offensive Sexual Material)
- MOS: do **NOT** take enforcement action — **immediately notify the patrol supervisor**
- Patrol supervisor: respond, determine if material meets criteria, make an Activity Log entry, and **consult Legal Bureau**
- **NO enforcement** without specific direction of the Legal Bureau

### Critical Legal Distinctions

| Offense | Classification | Enforcement |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| PL Article 235 (Obscenity) | Requires **judicial determination** that the item is obscene | No enforcement without Legal Bureau direction |
| PL 245.11 (Public Display) | **Class A misdemeanor** | Summary enforcement allowed under appropriate circumstances |

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Respond to the scene and evaluate whether the material meets legal criteria
- Consult the Legal Bureau before any enforcement action on obscenity
- Make an Activity Log entry documenting the determination
- Understand that PL 245.11 (Public Display) allows summary enforcement, while PL 235 (Obscenity) requires judicial determination

> **Exam Alert:** MOS do NOT take enforcement action — they notify the **patrol supervisor** immediately. No obscenity enforcement without **Legal Bureau** direction. Obscenity (PL 235) requires a **judicial determination**. Public Display (PL 245.11) is a **Class A misdemeanor** with summary enforcement authority.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The patrol supervisor is the decision-maker. Always consult Legal Bureau for obscenity matters. Know the distinction: PL 235 requires judicial determination; PL 245.11 allows summary enforcement.

## P.G. 214-32 — Discarded Refrigerators/Freezers

### Key Points

- Purpose: **prevent injury to children**
- If owner is known: direct the owner to remove the door or latch; **arrest if owner refuses**
- **ESU** responds to remove the door or latch if MOS are unable to do so

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Direct MOS to contact the owner if known
- Request ESU if MOS cannot remove the door/latch
- Authorize arrest if the owner refuses to comply

> **Exam Alert:** The purpose is to prevent injury to **children**. If the owner refuses to remove the door/latch, the owner is subject to **arrest**. Request **ESU** if MOS cannot remove the door/latch themselves.

## P.G. 214-33 — Care and Disposition of Animals

### Key Points

- **Dangerous animal** = menaces, threatens, attacks, or bites a person
- **DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT (PD311-152)** prepared in **every instance** — even if the animal is gone
- An animal is **NEVER** placed in the trunk of a Department vehicle
- Process offender for **DAT** if eligible

### Dangerous Dog Categories (4)

1. Unprovoked approach or menacing behavior
2. Known propensity for dangerous behavior
3. Bites without provocation
4. Used for fighting purposes

### Animal Care & Control (AC&C) Response

| Time | Resource |
|------|----------|
| Mon-Fri 0800-2000 | AC&C Rescue Units |
| 24/7 | Brooklyn AC&C Control Center |
| After hours | Contact Operations Division for on-call AC&C member |
| AC&C response >15-30 minutes | Request ESU |

### Use of Force

- Only to prevent harm or injury
- **Firearm** used ONLY to protect from **imminent physical injury** with **no opportunity to retreat**

### Desk Officer Notifications

- Notify the **Department of Health and Mental Hygiene** by phone for:
  - Rabid animal
  - Suspected rabid animal
  - Animal that bit, scratched, or abraded a human

### Agriculture & Markets Law Citations

| Section | Offense | Classification |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| 351 | Animal Fighting | Felony |
| 353 | Torture/Cruelty | Misdemeanor |
| 353-a | Aggravated Cruelty | Felony |
| 355 | Abandonment | Misdemeanor |
| 360 | Poisoning (horses, mules, cattle) | Felony |

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure the DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT (PD311-152) is prepared in every instance
- Desk officer must notify the Dept of Health and Mental Hygiene by phone for rabid, suspected rabid, or bite/scratch/abrasion cases
- Ensure animals are never placed in the trunk of a Department vehicle
- Evaluate use of force — firearm only when imminent physical injury and no retreat opportunity

> **Exam Alert:** The DANGEROUS ANIMAL/BITE REPORT (PD311-152) is prepared in **every instance**, even if the animal is gone. Animals are **NEVER** placed in the trunk of a Department vehicle. Firearms are used ONLY for **imminent physical injury** with **no opportunity to retreat**. Request ESU if AC&C response exceeds **15-30 minutes**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The desk officer must notify the Dept of Health and Mental Hygiene by phone for three situations: rabid animal, suspected rabid animal, or any animal that bit/scratched/abraded a human. Know all four dangerous dog categories and the Agriculture & Markets Law sections.

## P.G. 214-35 — Processing 311 Service Requests

### Key Points

- **CHRONIC 311 CONDITION:** **5 or more** requests in a **28-day period** for the same condition at the same location
- **5 or more** calls within an **8-hour period**: prioritize for **immediate response**
- MOS: notify Communications Section with **10-42 code** upon receipt, specify complaint type
- Desk officer: manually refresh the 311 system **at least every hour**

### Daily Review and Logging

- **Second Platoon Desk Officer** reviews the 311 system at **0800 daily** for the previous 24 hours
- Command Log entries required at the **beginning AND end** of each tour with the number of pending assignments

### Switchboard Operator

- Inform callers to call **311 directly** in the future

### Special Ops Lt Responsibilities

- Review deferred requests
- Design and implement response plans
- Conduct a **monthly audit** of 311 callers
- Provide a **weekly update** of the 28-day chronic conditions list posted at the desk

### CO Responsibilities

- Direct the quality of life sergeant to make **daily callbacks**
- Minimum **2 attempts** per caller

### 311 Assignment Types

Abandoned Vehicle, Animal Abuse, Blocked Driveway, Drug Activity, Encampment, Illegal Fireworks, Mass Gathering, Noise (6 types), Panhandling, Squeegee, Vending

### Administrative

- **OMAP** = designated liaison to NYC 311
- Improperly routed requests: reassign via the 311 system
- Referrals to other agencies: disposition **10-95** "Refer to Another Agency"

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Second Platoon Desk Officer: review 311 system at 0800 daily
- Desk officer: refresh 311 system at least every hour
- Command Log: entries at beginning and end of each tour
- Special Ops Lt: monthly audit, weekly chronic conditions update, review deferred requests
- CO: direct daily callbacks with minimum 2 attempts per caller

> **Exam Alert:** CHRONIC condition = **5+ requests** in **28 days**. Immediate response trigger = **5+ calls** in **8 hours**. Second Platoon Desk Officer reviews at **0800 daily**. Desk officer refreshes **at least every hour**. Special Ops Lt: **monthly** audit, **weekly** chronic conditions update. CO directs **daily** callbacks with minimum **2 attempts** per caller.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Multiple supervisory layers with different frequencies: hourly (desk officer refresh), daily (0800 review, callbacks), weekly (chronic conditions list), monthly (311 caller audit). The 10-42 code is used to notify Communications Section. Disposition 10-95 for referrals to other agencies.
