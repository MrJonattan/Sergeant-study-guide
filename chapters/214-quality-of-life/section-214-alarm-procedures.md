# Alarm Procedures — Chronic Abuser, Vehicle Alarm Summons, and Deactivation

## Overview

Alarm-related procedures are among the most testable quality-of-life topics for the sergeant exam. This section covers the chronic abuser alarm system for building alarms, OATH summons for vehicle alarms, and the deactivation process. Sergeants must know the specific timeframes, service methods, and the critical distinction between building and vehicle alarm authority.

---

## P.G. 214-08 — Chronic Abuser Alarm Procedures

### Key Points

- Purpose: reduce police response when **3 or more alarms** in a **3-month period** are unnecessary or unfounded
- **NOTICE OF UNNECESSARY ALARM (PD310-120)** served to a qualified person
- **No NOTICE served** if a crime was actually committed at the premises
- **10-11** = Alarm Condition signal
- **10-90N** = Notice Served disposition

### Qualified Person for Service

A qualified person includes: owner, corporate officer, manager, supervisor, or adult employee/resident.

### Service Methods

| Method | When Used |
|--------|-----------|
| Personal service | Served to qualified person on scene |
| Door service | No qualified person available — place in mailbox or under door |
| Deferred service | Cannot serve at scene — CPO serves next business day |

### Crime Prevention Officer (CPO) Duties

- Maintains master file of alarm notices
- Enters notices daily into the Alarm Board Notification System Database
- Reviews "Abuser Alarm Location Notification" (forwarded daily by IT Services Division)
- Reviews with CO to determine which locations to terminate

### Termination and Reinstatement

- **TERMINATION NOTICE (PD310-121)** served only when directed by the **precinct CO**
- Reinstatement: CO reviews the request and may require an alarm system or premises security survey
- Minimum **48-hour delay** before reinstatement takes effect

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- CO directs the issuance of TERMINATION NOTICES (PD310-121)
- CO reviews reinstatement requests and may require a security survey
- CO works with CPO to determine which locations warrant termination

> **Exam Alert:** The trigger is **3 or more** unnecessary/unfounded alarms in a **3-month period**. TERMINATION NOTICE (PD310-121) is served only when directed by the **precinct CO**. Reinstatement requires a minimum **48-hour delay**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Know the three service methods: personal, door service, and deferred (CPO serves next business day). No NOTICE is served if a crime was actually committed at the premises.

## P.G. 214-24 — OATH Summons for Vehicle Alarms

### Key Points

- Vehicle alarm still audible after **3 minutes**: take action
- Take all reasonable steps to disconnect **without damaging** the vehicle
- Time of violation = **end** of the 3-minute observation period
- Affix the **white cardboard (agency) copy** to the vehicle
- Issuance is **NOT mandatory** — based on discretion and/or supervisor direction
- Alarm that resets and sounds repeatedly = **one continuous action** (one summons)

### OATH Codes and Penalties

| Admin Code | Description | OATH Code | Mail-In | Maximum |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 24-238(a) | Improper Audible Burglar Alarm | N68 | $280 | $2,100 |
| 24-238(b) | Audible Status Indicator | N71 | $280 | $2,100 |

### Towing Authority

- If unable to disconnect the alarm: place vehicle in the **Rotation Tow Program**

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- May direct MOS to issue OATH summons for vehicle alarms
- Understand that issuance is discretionary, not mandatory
- Ensure MOS observe the full 3-minute period before taking action

> **Exam Alert:** The observation period is **3 minutes**. Time of violation = **end** of the observation period. Mail-in fine is **$280**, maximum penalty is **$2,100**. An alarm that resets and re-sounds is **one continuous action**.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Issuance is NOT mandatory. A repeated alarm cycle counts as one continuous action, not multiple violations. The white cardboard (agency) copy is affixed to the vehicle.

## P.G. 214-25 — Deactivation of Motor Vehicle Alarms

### Key Points

- Vehicle owners are required to display the **local precinct phone number** inside the vehicle
- Alarms should auto-deactivate within **10 minutes**
- MOS may attempt to disconnect within the first **10 minutes**
- Do **NOT** intentionally damage the vehicle
- Request **ESU** if difficulty entering the engine or passenger compartment
- If personal notification of the owner is not made: leave a **handwritten notification under the windshield wiper**

### Building Alarms — Critical Distinction

- MOS are **NOT permitted** to deactivate **building alarms**
- May issue a summons if a building alarm continues for **15 or more minutes**

### Supervisor Responsibilities

- Ensure MOS do not damage vehicles during deactivation attempts
- Direct ESU response when MOS cannot access the engine/passenger compartment
- Know the distinction: MOS CAN deactivate vehicle alarms but CANNOT deactivate building alarms

> **Exam Alert:** Vehicle alarms should auto-deactivate within **10 minutes**. MOS are **NOT permitted** to deactivate building alarms. Building alarm summons may be issued after **15 minutes**. Request **ESU** for difficult vehicle alarm access.

> **Sergeant Focus:** The critical distinction is vehicle vs. building: MOS may deactivate vehicle alarms but are NOT permitted to deactivate building alarms. For building alarms, a summons may be issued after 15 minutes of continuous sounding.
