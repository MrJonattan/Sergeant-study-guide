# Chapter 10 — Section 214: Quality of Life Matters

## Overview

Section 214 covers 26 Patrol Guide procedures related to quality of life enforcement, from alarm response and evictions to animal control and 311 service requests. This is **medium priority** material for the sergeant promotional exam. The procedures emphasize the supervisory role in interagency coordination, the limits of police authority in civil matters, and specific numeric thresholds that are frequently tested.

## Priority Topics for Sergeant Exam

1. **311 Service Requests (214-35)** — Multiple supervisory frequencies (hourly, daily, weekly, monthly), chronic condition thresholds
2. **Unlawful Evictions (214-12)** — 30-day occupancy threshold, borough-specific return dates, NO DAT, not fingerprintable
3. **Chronic Abuser Alarm (214-08)** — 3 alarms in 3 months, three service methods, 48-hour reinstatement delay
4. **Vehicle Alarms (214-24/25)** — 3-minute observation, vehicle vs. building alarm authority, $280/$2,100 fines
5. **Care of Animals (214-33)** — Bite report in every instance, firearm use criteria, Dept of Health notifications
6. **C.U.R.E. Operations (214-39)** — 30-day notice, insufficient grounds, Chief of Patrol approval
7. **Evictions/Civil Process (214-13)** — Police role limited to peacekeeping, no property on sidewalk, no door breaking
8. **Obscenity (214-11)** — Judicial determination vs. summary enforcement distinction

## Study Tips

- Focus on numeric thresholds and timeframes — these are the most common exam question targets in Section 214
- Learn the distinction between police authority and other agency authority (DOB, TLC, city marshals) — many procedures limit MOS to peacekeeping only
- Know which supervisor level is responsible for each action (desk officer, patrol supervisor, Special Ops Lt, CO)
- Pay attention to form numbers — the exam may reference specific PD forms by number
- The vehicle alarm vs. building alarm distinction (can deactivate vehicles, cannot deactivate buildings) is a classic exam question
