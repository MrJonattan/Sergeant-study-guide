# Section 220 — Command Element

## P.G. 220-05: CIMS Command Element

**Date Effective:** 08/01/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 66 | **Pages:** 15

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Command Element in CIMS.

### Command Element Responsibilities (45 Steps — Key Highlights)

| Step | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| 1 | Assume overall incident management responsibility |
| 2 | Obtain briefing; assess situation; notify Desk Officer, Patrol Borough, Operations Unit, Communications |
| 3 | Ensure incident safety; intervene in/suspend unsafe operations |
| 5 | Establish priorities: **Life Safety (#1)**, Site Management, Investigative, Recovery/Restoration |
| 7 | Utilize "Management by Objectives" — determine Incident Objectives, Strategies, Tactical Direction |
| 12 | Select location and establish **Incident Command Post (ICP)** |
| 13 | Manage incident **from the ICP** |
| 14 | Establish appropriate organizational structure |
| 27 | **Manage Life Safety Operations** — immediate #1 priority; intervene in/suspend unsafe activities |
| 28 | **Manage Site Management Operations** — perimeters, Emergency Credentialing |
| 29 | **Manage Investigative Operations** — establish Intel/Investigations Section when considerable component |
| 30 | **Manage Recovery/Restoration Operations** — final mitigation, return to non-emergency |
| 31 | Designate Deputy or Deputies when necessary |
| 32 | Ensure responding agencies do not allocate unnecessary/inappropriate resources |
| 33 | Ensure planning meetings scheduled and managed |
| 34 | Formulate/approve/authorize one single **Incident Action Plan** (verbal or written) |
| 35 | Approve requests/orders for additional resources |
| 36 | Approve requests/orders for release and demobilization of resources |
| 37 | Authorize release of information to news media / public |
| 38 | Ensure all records, forms, logs, reports properly prepared and maintained |
| 39 | Continually monitor performance, analyze data, evaluate implementation of IAP |
| 40 | Ensure appropriate Incident Facilities established at appropriate locations |
| 41 | Ensure Transfer of Command performed in organized manner |
| 42 | Ensure compliance with Span of Control, Chain of Command, Unity of Command |
| 43 | Order demobilization when appropriate |
| 44 | Comply with demobilization procedures and Demobilization Plan |
| 45 | Ensure all "After-Action" briefings, reports, meetings, critiques completed |

### Life Safety Operations (Step 27 — Critical)

- The **immediate** #1 priority
- Tactical activities taken to preserve and protect public safety including:
  - (1) Required to stabilize the incident
  - (2) Implemented in response to imminent hazards or threats to life
- Life Safety Operations **do not include personnel safety measures** (those are the Safety Officer's responsibility)
- Command Element may intervene in and/or suspend Life Safety Operations to prevent, stop, and/or correct hazardous, unhealthy, or unsafe activities

> **Exam Alert:** The PG explicitly states Life Safety Operations do **NOT** include personnel safety measures. Personnel safety is the Safety Officer's role. Life Safety is about protecting the public. *(PG 220-05, step 27c)*

### Investigative Operations (Step 29 — New Detail)

When there is a considerable investigative/intelligence component, the Command Element should establish an Intelligence/Investigations Section. The I/I function may be organized at three different levels:

| Significance | Organization |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Considerable** | Separate General Staff Section (I/I Section) |
| **Not considerable** (unusual) | Branch or Group within the **Operations Section** |
| **Minimal** (extremely unusual) | Command Staff position or Planning Section/Situation Unit function |

> **Exam Alert:** The I/I function can be placed at three different organizational levels depending on significance. This is commonly tested — the default is a General Staff Section, but it may be downgraded. *(PG 220-05, step 29a)*

### Incident Management Priorities (In Order)

| Priority | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **#1 — Life Safety** | Immediate priority; supersedes all other operations (absent extraordinary circumstances) |
| #2 — Site Management | Perimeter control, traffic/crowd control, security, Emergency Credentialing |
| #3 — Investigative Operations | May run concurrently with Life Safety (with Command Element approval) |
| #4 — Recovery/Restoration | Final mitigation; return to non-emergency; Coordinating Agency facilitates/supports |

> **Memory Aid — LSIR:** **L**ife Safety → **S**ite Management → **I**nvestigative → **R**ecovery/Restoration. The four incident management priorities in order. *(PG 220-05)*

### Incident Objectives Must Be

| Quality | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Attainable** | Considering existing limitations and constraints |
| **Measurable** | To determine if being achieved effectively |
| **Adaptable/Flexible** | To provide for alternative strategies |

### Incident Facility Location Requirements (Step 40)

Applies to ICP and all Incident Facilities. Locations will be chosen that:

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| Are a reasonable and appropriate distance from the incident |
| Provide for adequate safety, health, security and force protection |
| Provide easy and expeditious access and egress |
| Are sufficiently large enough and will allow for expansion |
| Will permit continuous operations |
| Provide adequate work space and utility / communications services |
| Generally within the **Site Access Control / Outer Perimeter** |

### ICP Notifications

1. Desk Officer, Precinct of occurrence
2. Patrol Borough Office
3. Communications Section dispatcher/supervisor
4. Operations Unit
5. NYPD Emergency Operations Center (if activated)
6. Other appropriate NYPD units
7. Other appropriate agencies/organizations

### One ICP Rule

- For **every incident** except nuclear weapon detonation, there is **only ONE single ICP**
- Nuclear weapon incidents require **borough-centric ICPs**

### Command Element Structures

| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Single Command** | One Primary Agency designates one Incident Commander with overall responsibility |
| **Unified Command** | Multiple Primary Agencies each designate an Agency Incident Commander; all co-locate at one ICP; jointly set objectives and create one IAP |

> **Exam Alert:** In **Single Command**, the IC picks **their own people** to staff ALL positions below them. In **Unified Command**, the Co-ICs jointly pick most positions but each agency picks its own Safety Officer and Operations Chief. *(PG 220-04, 220-05)*

> **Exam Alert:** Two possible exceptions to NYPD controlling Single Command staff:
> - **Public Information Officer** — Mayor's Press Office has authority to step in and name the PIO
> - **Liaison Officer** — NYCEM has authority to step in and designate the Liaison Officer
> In both cases, NYPD should still try to make NYPD personnel the PIO/Liaison (preferably DCPI for PIO), or at minimum the **assistant** to whoever is designated. *(PG 220-06, 220-08)*

> **See Also:** PG 220-06 — Public Information Officer designation rules including Mayor's Press Office authority.

> **See Also:** PG 220-08 — Liaison Officer designation rules including NYCEM authority.

### Span of Control

| Context | Recommended Ratio |
|---------|-------------------|
| General | **1:3 to 1:7** |
| Law enforcement operations | **1:8 to 1:10** |

### Unity of Command

Every subordinate has **only one** designated supervisor.

### Chain of Command

Direction may only be given through the established Chain of Command (absent emergency circumstances).

### Deputy (Step 31)

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Qualifications | Same as the principal (knowledge, skills, experience) |
| Must be capable of | Assuming the principal's position |
| May be from | A different agency/jurisdiction |
| Role (flexible) | "Eyes and ears," make notifications, function as relief, assigned specific tasks |

The Deputy's role is flexible and may include:
1. Collect incident related information, monitor performance, evaluate operations/resources — be the "eyes and ears" of the principal
2. Make important notifications (e.g., Bureau Chief, Patrol/Detective Borough Commander, Operations Unit, Emergency Operations Center if activated, Area Command if activated)
3. Function as a relief
4. Be assigned specific tasks as requested by the principal

**Cross-agency Deputies:** A Deputy may be from a different Agency/Jurisdiction that:
- Currently has, or may have, significant resources involved in the incident management effort; or
- Currently is, or may be, significantly affected by the incident

> **Exam Alert:** Designating a Deputy from a different Agency/Jurisdiction may **significantly increase inter-agency coordination and cooperation**. *(PG 220-05, step 31d)*

### Transfer of Command (Step 41)

- Should be **face-to-face** if possible
- Incoming Command Element must receive **comprehensive briefing** including all essential information
- Transfer of Command briefing should be **recorded and/or displayed**, if possible
- Identify a **specific time** for Transfer of Command to occur
- When possible, Transfer of Command should occur at the **start of a new Operational Period**
- Notifications must be made to: Primary Agency(ies), Supporting Agency/Agency Representatives, Coordinating Agency, Command Staff, General Staff, all incident management supervisory personnel, Communications Section dispatcher/supervisor, and other appropriate personnel

### Media and Public Information (Step 37)

- Authorize release of information to news media and/or public
- Determine recommended health, safety, and security protective measures to communicate (e.g., evacuate, shelter in place, change clothes, wash)
- When appropriate, disseminate **Emergency Public Information** to the media and public
- For large, complex, newsworthy, multi-agency incidents: release info via **joint-Agency press conference(s)**
- When necessary, utilize a **Joint Information System** and establish a **Joint Information Center (JIC)** staffed by public affairs personnel from all Primary and Supporting Agencies
- In extremely unusual circumstances, multiple JICs may be established
- When appropriate, collaborate with Office of the Deputy Commissioner, Public Information, Mayor's Press Office, and NYCEM to activate a JIS and establish JIC(s)

### Demobilization (Steps 43-45)

- Command Element orders demobilization when appropriate
- Order demobilization of some or all incident resources, operations, organizational elements, facilities, sites, and other appropriate functions/activities
- Comply with appropriate demobilization procedures and the Demobilization Plan (if applicable)
- Ensure all required "After-Action" briefings, reports, meetings, critiques are completed

> **Exam Alert:** **Life Safety is the immediate #1 priority** — it supersedes all other incident management operations (absent extraordinary circumstances).

> **Exam Alert:** CIMS Span of Control: general = **1:3 to 1:7**; law enforcement = **1:8 to 1:10**.

> **Exam Alert:** There is only **ONE Incident Command Post** per incident (except nuclear weapon incidents).

> **Exam Alert:** In Unified Command, Agency Incident Commanders are at **equal authority** regardless of agency rank.

> **Sergeant Focus:** A Sergeant arriving first at a multi-agency incident may serve as the initial Command Element/Incident Commander until relieved through Transfer of Command by a higher-ranking officer.

> **Exam Alert:** When minimal finance/admin tasks are required (e.g., only managing overtime), the Finance/Admin Section Chief position may **not** need to be a full General Staff Section. It could instead be one specialist working for the Planning Section Chief. *(PG 220-12, step 1a)*
