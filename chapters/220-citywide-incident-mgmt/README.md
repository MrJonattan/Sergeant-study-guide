# Section 220 — Citywide Incident Management

## Source
- **Patrol Guide Section:** 220
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide4.pdf (OCR)
- **Priority:** High

## Learning Objectives
- Understand CIMS (Citywide Incident Management System) — NYC's adaptation of NIMS/ICS
- Know Single Command vs Unified Command structures and the Primary Agency Matrix
- Identify Command Staff (PIO, Safety, Liaison) and General Staff (Operations, Planning, Logistics, Finance/Admin, I/I) roles
- Understand incident facilities (ICP, Operations Post, I/I Post, Staging Area) and their co-location rules
- Know resource categories (Single Resource, Resource Team, Special Response Team) and statuses (Assigned, Available, Out-of-Service)
- Recognize the CIMS incident sequence and Transfer of Command options
- Understand IMT activation authority and CBRN/HazMat command rules

## Sergeant Focus Areas

- **Transfer of Command**: An arriving sergeant has four options — assuming command is NOT mandatory; may elect to make no change if the current CE is managing effectively
- **Span of Control**: Law enforcement ratio is 1:8 to 1:10 (wider than general 1:3 to 1:7)
- **Squad composition**: 1 Sergeant + 8 Police Officers — the sergeant leads the basic CIMS tactical unit
- **Life Safety priority**: Always #1 — supersedes all other operations including investigation
- **Check-In**: Resources must check in BEFORE assignment to Operations Section
- **Safety Officer authority**: May directly stop unsafe activities when life is in imminent danger without chain of command approval
- **CBRN command**: NYPD assumes Single Command when crime/terrorism is involved; Unified Command otherwise
- **Clear text communications**: No 10-codes at CIMS incidents — use clear text and CIMS terminology
