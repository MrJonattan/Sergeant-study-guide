# Section 220 — Command Staff

## P.G. 220-06: CIMS Public Information Officer

**Date Effective:** 08/01/13 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Public Information Officer in CIMS.

### Key Responsibilities

| Duty |
|------|
| Manage public information activities (media inquiries, information development) |
| Obtain **approval from Command Element** before releasing information |
| Formulate/disseminate unified, consistent, concise, accurate official information |
| Conduct media briefings; distribute written media releases |
| Release Emergency Public Information, warnings, and protective measures |
| Establish/supervise media placement areas (sufficient space, no interference with operations) |
| Monitor media and forward useful information to Command Element/Planning/Intelligence |

### Designation Rules

| Structure | Designated By |
|-----------|--------------|
| Single Command | Incident Commander (but **Mayor's Press Office** has authority and may designate) |
| Unified Command | Agency Incident Commanders jointly (but **Mayor's Press Office** has authority and may designate) |

### Joint Information Center (JIC)

- Established when necessary for large, complex, multi-agency incidents
- Staffed by public affairs personnel from all Primary and Supporting Agencies
- JIC spokesperson designated by **Mayor's Press Office**
- In extremely unusual circumstances, multiple JICs may be established

> **Exam Alert:** The **Mayor's Press Office** has authority to designate the Public Information Officer and the JIC spokesperson.

## P.G. 220-07: CIMS Safety Officer

**Date Effective:** 08/01/13 | **Pages:** 7

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Safety Officer in CIMS.

### Key Responsibilities

| Duty |
|------|
| Perform Incident Safety Analysis |
| Advise Command Element on safety, health, force protection, security |
| Develop/implement safety measures for personnel, vehicles, equipment, facilities |
| Implement PPE program |
| Monitor operations for compliance with Health and Safety Plan |
| **Immediately stop hazardous/unsafe activities** using chain of command |
| **Exercise emergency authority** to directly stop if life/safety in imminent danger |
| Review/approve the Medical Plan |

### Safety Officer Function — Legal Requirement

The Safety Officer function is **required by law** at a hazardous materials incident.

### Emergency Authority

The Safety Officer may **exercise emergency authority to directly prevent, stop, and/or correct** hazardous, unhealthy, or unsafe activities when life or safety of incident personnel is in **imminent danger** — then subsequently notify the Command Element.

### Designation Rules

| Structure | Designation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Single Command (non-CBRN) | Incident Commander designates **one** Safety Officer |
| Unified Command | Each Agency Incident Commander may designate an Agency Safety Officer (or jointly designate one) |
| Single Command (CBRN/HazMat) | NYPD, FDNY, DEP, DOHMH each designate an Agency Safety Officer (or jointly designate one) |

> **Exam Alert:** Safety Officer can **exercise emergency authority** to directly stop unsafe activities when life is in **imminent danger** — without waiting for chain of command approval.

> **Exam Alert:** Safety Officer function is **required by law** at hazardous materials incidents.

## P.G. 220-08: CIMS Liaison Officer

**Date Effective:** 08/01/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 66 | **Pages:** 4

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Liaison Officer in CIMS.

### Key Responsibilities

| Duty |
|------|
| Serve as primary contact for Supporting Agencies |
| Coordinate with Supporting Agency/Agency Representatives |
| Ensure inter-agency coordination and cooperation |
| Facilitate communication between agencies |

### Designation Rules

| Structure | Designated By |
|-----------|--------------|
| Single Command | Incident Commander (but **NYCEM** has authority and may designate) |
| Unified Command | Agency Incident Commanders jointly (but **NYCEM** has authority and may designate) |

> **Exam Alert:** **NYCEM** (NYC Emergency Management) has authority to designate the Liaison Officer.

> **Exam Alert:** Command Staff positions are: **Public Information Officer** (Mayor's Press Office authority), **Safety Officer**, **Liaison Officer** (NYCEM authority).
