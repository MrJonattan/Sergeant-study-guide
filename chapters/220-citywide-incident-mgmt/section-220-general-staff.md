# Section 220 — General Staff Sections

## P.G. 220-09: CIMS Operations Section Chief

**Date Effective:** 08/01/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 66 | **Pages:** 14

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Operations Section chief in CIMS.

### Key Responsibilities

| Duty |
|------|
| Direct and coordinate **all tactical operations** at the incident |
| Implement tactical portion of the Incident Action Plan |
| Establish Operations Section Post |
| Manage/supervise Sectors, Groups, Branches |
| Maintain accountability for all resources assigned to Operations Section |

### Subordinate Elements

| Element | Supervised By | Managed By |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Branch** | Branch Director | Major functional or geographic areas |
| **Sector** | Sector Supervisor | Geographic area of operations |
| **Group** | Group Supervisor | Functional area of operations |

### Operations Section Post

- Separate from the ICP (generally NOT co-located)
- Located within the Site Access Control / Outer Perimeter
- Where Operations Section chief and staff direct tactical operations

### Unified Operations Section

Activated when:
- Unified Command — Command Element is managing the incident
- Single Command at a CBRN/HazMat incident

Each Primary Agency designates an **Agency Operations Section chief** based on Core Competencies.

> **Exam Alert:** The Operations Section directs **all tactical operations**. The Operations Section Post is generally **NOT co-located** with the ICP.

## P.G. 220-10: CIMS Planning Section Chief

**Date Effective:** 08/01/13 | **Pages:** 9

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Planning Section chief in CIMS.

### Key Responsibilities

| Duty |
|------|
| Collect, evaluate, and disseminate incident information |
| Prepare/distribute the **Incident Action Plan** |
| Prepare the **Demobilization Plan** |
| Track resource status |
| Conduct planning meetings |
| Provide technical specialists |

### Subordinate Units

| Unit | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Resources Unit** | Track resource status; perform Check-In function |
| **Situation Unit** | Collect/analyze situation data; prepare situation displays |
| **Demobilization Unit** | Plan/execute orderly demobilization |
| **Documentation Unit** | Maintain incident files and records |

> **Exam Alert:** The **Planning Section** prepares the Incident Action Plan and the Demobilization Plan. The **Resources Unit** tracks resource status and performs the Check-In function.

## P.G. 220-11: CIMS Logistics Section Chief

**Date Effective:** 08/01/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 66 | **Pages:** 9

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Logistics Section chief in CIMS.

### Key Responsibilities

| Duty |
|------|
| Provide **all service and support** for the incident |
| Manage communications infrastructure |
| Manage food, water, supplies, equipment |
| Manage facilities and transportation |
| Manage medical support for incident personnel (not victims) |

### Subordinate Structure

| Branch | Units |
|--------|-------|
| **Service Branch** | Communications Unit, Medical Unit, Food Unit |
| **Support Branch** | Supply Unit, Facilities Unit, Ground Support Unit |

> **Exam Alert:** Logistics provides **service and support** (NOT tactical operations). Medical Unit provides care for **incident personnel**, not victims.

## P.G. 220-13: CIMS Intelligence/Investigations Section Chief

**Date Effective:** 02/18/15 | **Pages:** 7

### Purpose

To outline the duties of the Intelligence/Investigations Section chief in CIMS.

### Key Concept

When there is a **considerable investigative or intelligence component**, the Command Element should establish an Intelligence/Investigations Section as part of the General Staff. In unusual situations where it's not considerable, it may be organized as a Branch or Group within Operations, or even a Command Staff position.

### Subordinate Groups

| Group | Function |
|-------|----------|
| **Intelligence Group** | Intelligence collection and analysis |
| **Surveillance and Evidence Collection Group** | Surveillance operations, evidence collection |
| **Forensic Group** | Forensic analysis and processing |
| **Missing/Unidentified Persons Group** | Track missing or unidentified persons |
| **Investigative Support Group** | Support investigative operations |

### Intelligence/Investigations Section Post

- Generally **NOT co-located** with the ICP or Operations Section Post
- Located at a **secure location** within reasonable distance

> **Exam Alert:** The Intelligence/Investigations Section may be organized as a **General Staff Section** (considerable component), an **Operations Branch/Group** (unusual), or a **Command Staff position/Planning function** (extremely unusual).
