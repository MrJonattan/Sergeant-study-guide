# Section 211-07 — Prevention of Court Appearance on Scheduled Day Off

## Purpose

To prevent members from being required to appear in court on their scheduled days off, and to establish procedures when such conflicts arise.

---

## Member's Responsibility — At Court

When a judge sets an adjournment date that conflicts with the member's scheduled time off:

1. **Inform the judge** of the conflict
2. **Request another date**

If the rescheduled date **still conflicts**:

3. Make a **digital Activity Log entry** with:
   - Judge name
   - ADA name
   - Appearance date
   - Adjournment date
   - Court and part

4. **Inform the ACU supervisor**, who requests a new date per the member's work schedule

> **Exam Alert:** The member must first attempt to resolve the conflict directly with the judge before involving ACU.

---

## ACU Supervisor Actions

If the ACU supervisor is **unable to obtain a new date**:

- Signs the member's **digital Activity Log**
- Enters the information into the **CPR System**

---

## Command-Level Responsibilities

### Roll Call Clerk

> **Sergeant Focus:** When a member's court appearance is scheduled on an RDO:

1. Make a **diary entry**
2. Inform the **Operations Coordinator**

### Operations Coordinator

- Contacts the **ADA** to ascertain necessity of appearance
- Attempts to **reschedule** if possible

### Integrity Control Officer

- Checks records relating to **court appearances on RDOs**

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sergeants should be aware that the Integrity Control Officer monitors RDO court appearances for patterns that may indicate integrity issues.

---

## Scheduling Members Appearing on RDO

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Start time | **0900 hours** or as appropriate |
| Tour length | Consistent with **normal chart** |

---

## Excusal After Court

- Member returning from court **may** be excused upon request **if exigencies permit**
- Member enters **"Requested Excusal"** on the **OVERTIME REPORT (PD138-064)**

> **Exam Alert:** Excusal is discretionary ("may be excused... if exigencies permit"), not automatic. This is a "may" vs. "shall" distinction.

---

## Cancellation — Critical Rule

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most important rules in Section 211.

A voicemail, email, or message left attempting to cancel is **NOT sufficient**.

The requesting agency must **EXPLICITLY CONCUR** with the cancellation.

**If the agency does not concur, the member MUST appear as scheduled.**

| Method | Sufficient? |
|--------|-------------|
| Voicemail left with agency | **NO** |
| Email sent to agency | **NO** |
| Message left with agency | **NO** |
| Agency explicitly agrees to cancel | **YES** |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must ensure members understand that unilateral cancellation attempts do not relieve them of the obligation to appear. Only explicit concurrence from the requesting agency cancels the appearance.
