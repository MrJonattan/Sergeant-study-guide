# Review Questions — Section 211: Court & Agency Appearances

---

### Question 1
According to P.G. 211-05, how many times daily must the roll call clerk access the CPR System to check for court appearance notifications?

- A) Once, at the beginning of the business day
- B) Twice — beginning and end of the business day
- C) Three times — beginning, midpoint, and 30 minutes before close
- D) Four times — at regular intervals throughout the day

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)**
The roll call clerk must access the CPR System 3 times daily: (1) at the beginning of the business day, (2) at the midpoint of roll call office hours, and (3) 30 minutes before the close of roll call office hours.
**Reference: P.G. 211-05**
</details>

---

### Question 2
Which of the following is a valid reason to cancel a court appearance under P.G. 211-05?

- A) Member has a personal appointment
- B) Member is sick, after conferral with the district surgeon
- C) Member left a voicemail with the DA's office requesting cancellation
- D) Member's supervisor determines the appearance is unnecessary

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
Cancellation is accepted ONLY if the member is: sick (after conferral with district surgeon), on annual vacation selection, military leave, bereavement leave, or terminal leave. A voicemail is never sufficient — the requesting agency must explicitly concur.
**Reference: P.G. 211-05**
</details>

---

### Question 3
A "short date notification" under P.G. 211-05 is defined as:

- A) A notification for a member to appear within 72 hours
- B) A notification for a member to appear the next day, or a notification delivered on Friday for a Monday appearance
- C) A notification received less than one week before the appearance date
- D) Any notification that bypasses the normal CPR System process

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
A short date notification is a notification for a member to appear the next day, or a notification delivered on Friday for a Monday appearance. It is time-sensitive and triggers immediate email notifications to the member, roll call personnel, operations coordinator, and integrity control officer.
**Reference: P.G. 211-05**
</details>

---

### Question 4
Under the 4th Platoon Rule in P.G. 211-05, a member normally assigned to the 4th platoon (1800x0200) who is subpoenaed to appear in court the next day shall be:

- A) Excused from the court appearance
- B) Placed on overtime for the court appearance
- C) Reassigned to the 3rd platoon (1600x2400)
- D) Reassigned to the 1st platoon (0800x1600)

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)**
Members normally assigned to 4th platoon (1800x0200) who are subpoenaed or directed to appear the next day are reassigned to the 3rd platoon (1600x2400). Members not notified in advance who are already on the 4th platoon are excused at 2400 hours, with no overtime authorized for the 2400-0200 period.
**Reference: P.G. 211-05**
</details>

---

### Question 5
Under the criminal court telephone alert system (P.G. 211-06), a member on alert shall not be given a meal period before what time?

- A) 1100 hours
- B) 1200 hours
- C) 1300 hours
- D) 1400 hours

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)**
Under the criminal court telephone alert, NO meal period is authorized before 1300 hours. The member must remain available for a call to court.
**Reference: P.G. 211-06**
</details>

---

### Question 6
If a member on criminal court telephone alert is not directed to report to court by 1300 hours, what happens?

- A) The member is placed on standby until end of tour
- B) The alert is NOT activated for that day
- C) The member is assigned to administrative duties
- D) The member is directed to contact ACU for further instructions

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
If a member on telephone alert is not directed to court by 1300 hours, the alert is NOT activated for that day. The 1300 hours cutoff is absolute.
**Reference: P.G. 211-06**
</details>

### Question 7
Under P.G. 211-06, the ACU supervisor must notify the commanding officer IN WRITING when:

- A) A member requests a schedule change due to court
- B) A member fails to appear when called OR appears in court without being called
- C) A member's court case is adjourned
- D) A member is placed on telephone alert for three consecutive days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
The ACU supervisor must notify the CO in writing when: (a) a member fails to appear when called to court, OR (b) a member appears in court without being called. Both scenarios trigger mandatory written notification.
**Reference: P.G. 211-06**
</details>

### Question 8
When a member intends to testify before a governmental agency about Department operations (P.G. 211-09), written notification must be forwarded to the Police Commissioner no later than:

- A) 3 business days before the appearance
- B) 5 business days before the appearance
- C) 7 calendar days before the appearance
- D) 10 business days before the appearance

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
Written notification must be forwarded no later than 5 business days before the testimony. If unable to meet this deadline, the member must immediately notify the Office of the Police Commissioner or Legal Bureau by telephone.
**Reference: P.G. 211-09**
</details>

---

### Question 9
Under P.G. 211-09, the written request to the Police Commissioner for appearances before governmental agencies is prepared in how many copies?

- A) Duplicate (2 copies)
- B) Triplicate (3 copies)
- C) Quadruplicate (4 copies)
- D) A single original only

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
The written request is prepared in triplicate: the original goes direct to the Police Commissioner, one copy goes through channels to the Police Commissioner, and one copy goes direct to the Deputy Commissioner, Public Information.
**Reference: P.G. 211-09**
</details>

---

### Question 10
A member who wishes to voluntarily testify before a private organization about Department policies must obtain:

- A) Verbal approval from their commanding officer
- B) Prior written approval from the Police Commissioner
- C) Approval from the Legal Bureau only
- D) No prior approval, but must file a report afterward

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
Voluntary appearances require PRIOR WRITTEN APPROVAL from the Police Commissioner. This is distinct from subpoenaed appearances, which require notification but not prior approval (since the member is legally compelled to appear).
**Reference: P.G. 211-09**
</details>

---

### Question 11
At the Traffic Violations Bureau, which form is NOT used to record officer court attendance?

- A) Officer Time Slip
- B) Activity Log
- C) Court Attendance Record (PD468-141)
- D) Officer's Notes copy of summons

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)**
The Court Attendance Record (PD468-141) is NOT used for TVB appearances. Instead, the computerized Officer Time Slip is used to record TVB hearing attendance and dispositions.
**Reference: P.G. 211-11**
</details>

---

### Question 12
Under P.G. 211-11, the training sergeant's monthly review of TVB dispositions involves selecting how many members for potential retraining?

- A) 5 members with the most dismissals
- B) 10 members with the highest percentage of dismissals
- C) All members with any dismissals
- D) 15 members with the most court appearances

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
The training sergeant monthly selects 10 members with the highest percentage of dismissals for failure to establish prima facie case or Not Guilty due to unclear evidence. These members are retrained and/or subject to corrective action.
**Reference: P.G. 211-11**
</details>

---

### Question 13
Under the Freedom of Information Law (FOIL), the Department must respond to a records request within:

- A) 3 business days
- B) 5 business days
- C) 10 business days
- D) 30 calendar days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)**
FOIL requires a response within 5 business days. The response must either: (1) make the record available, (2) deny the request in writing, or (3) acknowledge receipt with an approximate date of availability.
**Reference: P.G. 211-17**
</details>

---

### Question 14
A subpoena issued by a court in another state (not New York) is:

- A) Always valid and must be complied with immediately
- B) Valid only if served within New York City
- C) NOT valid unless presented to and endorsed by a New York State judge
- D) Valid only for criminal matters, not civil

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)**
Out-of-state subpoenas (criminal or civil) are NOT valid unless presented to and endorsed by a New York State judge. However, the Department should make every reasonable effort to assist other jurisdictions, with permission obtained through channels.
**Reference: P.G. 211-16**
</details>

---

### Question 15
Under P.G. 211-20, if a member receives advice from a supervisor or ADA that contradicts the Department attorney's prior advice, the member MUST:

- A) Follow the supervisor's or ADA's advice as the most recent guidance
- B) Use personal judgment to determine the best course of action
- C) Follow the Department attorney's advice and inform the other party of the prior conferral
- D) Request a meeting between the Department attorney and the supervisor/ADA before taking action

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)**
The member MUST inform the individual giving contrary advice of the prior conferral with the Department attorney. If there is a suggestion to disregard the Department attorney's advice, the member or supervisor MUST notify the Department attorney and MUST FOLLOW THE DEPARTMENT ATTORNEY'S ADVICE. Disregarding Department attorney advice may result in disciplinary action and civil liability.
**Reference: P.G. 211-20**
</details>
