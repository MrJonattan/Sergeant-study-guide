# Section 211-11 — Traffic Violations Bureau Appearances / Computerized Time Slips

## Purpose

To record and monitor TVB hearing appearances.

---

## Member's Procedure at TVB

1. Bring **"Officer's Notes" copy** of the summons
2. Bring **Activity Log entry/printout** for each summons
3. Report **immediately** to the **Police Sign-In Room supervisor**

---

## TVB Supervisor Responsibility

> **Sergeant Focus:** Have the member logged into the computer system **IMMEDIATELY**.

> **Exam Alert:** If the member's presence is **not entered** in the system, judges may **dismiss cases for non-appearance**. Prompt computer entry is critical.

---

## After the Hearing

1. Return **immediately** to the Police Sign-In Room
2. Receive the **"Officer Time Slip"** (computerized)
3. Notify command **desk officer by telephone** immediately upon dismissal

> **Exam Alert:** The **Court Attendance Record (PD468-141)** is **NOT used** for TVB appearances. The Officer Time Slip replaces it.

| Form | Used at TVB? |
|------|-------------|
| Officer Time Slip (computerized) | **YES** |
| Court Attendance Record (PD468-141) | **NO** |

---

## Command-Level Processing

### Desk Officer

> **Sergeant Focus:**

- Reviews time slips for **accuracy**
- Notes **time officer returns** to command

### Clerical Member

- Files time slips **chronologically** in a **separate folder**

### Integrity Control Officer

- Reviews time slips **frequently** for integrity concerns

---

## Training Sergeant — Monthly Review

> **Sergeant Focus:** This is a direct sergeant responsibility and a likely exam topic.

The Training Sergeant reviews the **Disposition and Code columns** for negative patterns.

### Monthly Selection Process

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Frequency | **Monthly** |
| Selection | **10 members** with the highest percentage of dismissals |
| Criteria | Failure to establish prima facie case **OR** Not Guilty due to unclear evidence |
| Action | Members **retrained** and/or subject to **corrective action** |

> **Exam Alert:** The number is **10 members** selected monthly based on **highest percentage** of dismissals — not a fixed number of dismissals, but the highest percentage.

---

## TVB Disposition Codes

### Hearing Dispositions

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **N** | Not Guilty |
| **G** | Guilty |
| **W** | Guilty with Appearance (plea, no officer effect) |
| **D** | Dismissing Case |
| **P** | Paid Online |

### Dismissal Reason Codes

> **Exam Alert:** Know the difference between dismissal codes (why the case was dismissed) and disposition codes (hearing outcome).

| Code | Meaning (Not Guilty) | Meaning (Dismissals) |
|------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **1** | Evidence not clear/convincing | Insured |
| **2** | Justification | Licensed |
| **3** | — | Registered |
| **4** | — | Equipment docs presented |
| **5** | — | Officer No-Show |
| **6** | — | No Prima Facie case |
| **7** | — | Cannot identify motorist |
| **8** | — | No recollection |
| **9** | — | Non-Owner / No Knowledge |

> **Sergeant Focus:** Codes **5** (Officer No-Show), **6** (No Prima Facie case), **7** (Cannot identify motorist), and **8** (No recollection) are the codes most likely to trigger training sergeant review and corrective action. These reflect officer performance issues.
