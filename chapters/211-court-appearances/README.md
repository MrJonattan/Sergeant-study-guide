# Section 211 — Court & Agency Appearances

## Source

- **Patrol Guide Section:** 211
- **Source PDF:** public-pguide2.pdf
- **Priority:** Medium

## Overview

This chapter covers the procedures governing member appearances at courts, governmental agencies, and private organizations, including the systems used to monitor and control those appearances, subpoena processing, records requests, and conferral with Department attorneys.

---

## Learning Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Explain the role of the CPR System in monitoring court and agency appearances
2. Describe the roll call clerk's responsibilities for accessing the CPR System (3 times daily)
3. Identify valid reasons for cancelling a court appearance and the explicit concurrence requirement
4. Apply the 4th Platoon Rule for members with next-day court appearances
5. Describe the criminal and civil court telephone alert procedures, including the 1300 hours cutoff
6. Explain the procedure for preventing court appearances on scheduled days off
7. Identify when and how to notify the Police Commissioner of governmental/private organization appearances
8. Distinguish between voluntary and subpoenaed appearances and their approval requirements
9. Describe TVB appearance procedures and the training sergeant's monthly review process
10. Explain FOIL requirements including the 5 business day response deadline
11. Distinguish the validity of subpoenas from NYC courts, NYS courts, Federal courts, and out-of-state courts
12. Apply the Brady obligation to ADA/ACC document requests
13. Describe the procedure for conferring with a Department attorney and the contrary advice rule

## Sergeant Focus Areas

- Tracking and approving subordinates' court appearance schedules via CPR System
- Ensuring timely notification and proper documentation for all court-related absences
- Roll call clerk supervision (3 daily CPR checks)
- Mediating court appearance conflicts through ACU
- Training sergeant monthly TVB dismissal review (10 members)
- Ensuring compliance with the explicit concurrence cancellation rule
- Following Department attorney advice over contrary supervisor/ADA guidance
- FOIL response compliance within 5 business days
- Brady obligation awareness for document production
