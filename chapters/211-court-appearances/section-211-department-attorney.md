# Section 211-20 — Conferral with Department Attorney

## Purpose

To provide members with formal legal advice in situations arising from the performance of duty.

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Member Presents Facts

Member presents the facts of the situation to a **supervisory officer**.

### Step 2 — Supervisor Evaluates

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor determines if conferral with a Department attorney is necessary. This is a judgment call that falls directly on the sergeant.

### Step 3 — Contact Department Attorney

| Hours | Contact Method |
|-------|---------------|
| **0700 to 2300, Monday through Friday** | In person or by telephone |

> **Exam Alert:** The hours **0700-2300, Monday through Friday** are the standard contact hours for Department attorneys.

---

## Urgent Cases Outside Standard Hours

When an urgent case arises outside of 0700-2300 Monday through Friday:

1. Contact the **CO/duty captain** for guidance
2. When **absolutely necessary**: a **captain or above** may contact:
   - **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters**, or
   - **CO, Legal Bureau**
   - Contact made **through the Operations Unit**

> **Exam Alert:** Only a **captain or above** can authorize after-hours contact with DC Legal Matters or CO Legal Bureau. A sergeant cannot make this contact directly.

---

## Member Compliance

Member **shall comply** with attorney directions.

---

## Command Log Entry

> **Sergeant Focus:** A Command Log entry is required with the following information:

| Required Entry | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| Date and time | Of the conferral |
| Member requesting | Rank, name, and command |
| Captain or above name | If after-hours contact |
| Attorney name and title | Who provided the advice |
| Legal question details | The issue presented |
| Legal opinion details | The advice received |
| Actions taken | Steps taken based on advice |

---

## Contrary Advice — Critical Rule

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most important rules in Section 211. It is tested frequently.

If, after conferral with a Department attorney, the member receives **CONTRARY advice** from a supervisor or ADA:

### Member's Obligation

1. **MUST inform** that individual of the prior conferral with the Department attorney

### If Suggestion to Disregard Department Attorney Advice

2. The member or supervisor **MUST notify** the Department attorney
3. **MUST FOLLOW THE DEPARTMENT ATTORNEY'S ADVICE**

> **Exam Alert:** The hierarchy is clear: **Department attorney advice overrides** contrary advice from supervisors or ADAs. There is no exception.

| Source of Advice | Authority |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Department attorney | **Highest — must be followed** |
| Supervisor giving contrary advice | Must be informed of prior conferral; Department attorney prevails |
| ADA giving contrary advice | Must be informed of prior conferral; Department attorney prevails |

---

## Consequences of Disregarding Department Attorney Advice

Disregarding Department attorney advice may result in:

- **Disciplinary action**
- **Civil liability**

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sergeants must understand that they cannot override Department attorney advice. If a sergeant believes the attorney's advice is incorrect, the proper course is to notify the Department attorney of the conflicting opinion — not to direct the member to disregard the attorney's guidance. Following this protocol protects both the member and the supervisor from disciplinary action and civil liability.
