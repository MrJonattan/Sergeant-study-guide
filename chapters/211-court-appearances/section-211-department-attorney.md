# Section 211-20 — Conferral with Department Attorney

## Purpose

To provide members with formal legal advice in situations arising from the performance of duty.

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Member Presents Facts

Member presents the facts of the situation to a **supervisory officer**.

### Step 2 — Supervisor Evaluates

> **Sergeant Focus:** The supervisor determines if conferral with a Department attorney is necessary. This is a judgment call that falls directly on the sergeant.

### Step 3 — Contact Department Attorney

| Hours | Contact Method |
|-------|---------------|
| **0700 to 2300, Monday through Friday** | In person or by telephone |

> **Exam Alert:** The hours **0700-2300, Monday through Friday** are the standard contact hours for Department attorneys.

---

## Urgent Cases Outside Standard Hours

When an urgent case arises outside of 0700-2300 Monday through Friday:

1. Contact the **CO/duty captain** for guidance
2. When **absolutely necessary**: a **captain or above** may contact:
   - **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters**, or
   - **CO, Legal Bureau**
   - Contact made **through the Operations Unit**

> **Exam Alert:** Only a **captain or above** can authorize after-hours contact with DC Legal Matters or CO Legal Bureau. A sergeant cannot make this contact directly.

---

## Member Compliance

Member **shall comply** with attorney directions.

---

## Command Log Entry

> **Sergeant Focus:** A Command Log entry is required with the following information:

| Required Entry | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| Date and time | Of the conferral |
| Member requesting | Rank, name, and command |
| Captain or above name | If after-hours contact |
| Attorney name and title | Who provided the advice |
| Legal question details | The issue presented |
| Legal opinion details | The advice received |
| Actions taken | Steps taken based on advice |

---

## Contrary Advice — Critical Rule

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most important rules in Section 211. It is tested frequently.

If, after conferral with a Department attorney, the member receives **CONTRARY advice** from a supervisor or ADA:

### Member's Obligation

1. **MUST inform** that individual of the prior conferral with the Department attorney

### If Suggestion to Disregard Department Attorney Advice

2. The member or supervisor **MUST notify** the Department attorney
3. **MUST FOLLOW THE DEPARTMENT ATTORNEY'S ADVICE**

> **Exam Alert:** The hierarchy is clear: **Department attorney advice overrides** contrary advice from supervisors or ADAs. There is no exception.

| Source of Advice | Authority |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Department attorney | **Highest — must be followed** |
| Supervisor giving contrary advice | Must be informed of prior conferral; Department attorney prevails |
| ADA giving contrary advice | Must be informed of prior conferral; Department attorney prevails |

---

## Consequences of Disregarding Department Attorney Advice

Disregarding Department attorney advice may result in:

- **Disciplinary action**
- **Civil liability**

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sergeants must understand that they cannot override Department attorney advice. If a sergeant believes the attorney's advice is incorrect, the proper course is to notify the Department attorney of the conflicting opinion — not to direct the member to disregard the attorney's guidance. Following this protocol protects both the member and the supervisor from disciplinary action and civil liability.

## P.G. 211-21: Obtaining Assistance of Corporation Counsel (NEW)

> **New Procedure:** 01/28/26 | R.O. 12 | 4 pages

### Purpose

To ensure that legal representation is provided to a member of the service (uniformed or civilian) who is served with a summons and complaint or who otherwise becomes aware that he/she is a **defendant in a civil lawsuit** arising from an alleged act or omission that occurred in the performance of duty.

> **Exam Alert:** Timeliness is essential. Failure to complete and forward **REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE (PD411-160)** in a timely manner may result in a **default judgment** being entered against the member named as a defendant.

### When Summons and Complaint is Personally Served at Command

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Desk Officer/Supervisor | **Accept service** of summons and complaint for member assigned to command |
| 2 | Desk Officer/Supervisor | Make **Command Log entry** including date and time of service at command |
| 3 | Desk Officer/Supervisor | **Notify member concerned immediately** |
| 4 | Desk Officer/Supervisor | **Deliver papers** to member |
| 4a | | If member unavailable: give papers to **commanding officer** without delay |
| 5 | Desk Officer/Supervisor | Make **Command Log entry** including date and time member actually received summons and complaint |

> **Note:** Any summons and complaint served at a satellite location (precinct detective squad, Medical Division command, Warrant Division command, etc.) will be accepted and processed per this procedure — as long as there are no police officer safety concerns. If safety concerns exist (narcotics, vice enforcement, undercover officers), refer the server and papers to the overhead command.

> **Note:** A summons and complaint WILL NOT be accepted for a member who is **no longer assigned** to the command on the date of service. The process server will be directed to the member's new command.

### When Summons and Complaint is Served by Mail

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 6 | Operations Coordinator | **Attach mailing envelope** to the summons and complaint |
| 7 | Operations Coordinator | Make **Command Log entry** indicating receipt via US Mail or other carrier |
| 7a | | Include date and time of service at command |
| 8 | Operations Coordinator | **Notify member concerned immediately** |
| 9 | Operations Coordinator | **Deliver papers** to member |
| 9a | | If member unavailable: give papers to **commanding officer** without delay |
| 10 | Operations Coordinator | Make **Command Log entry** including date and time member actually received summons and complaint |
| 11 | Operations Coordinator | Mail summons and complaint **back to sender** if member is no longer assigned to command or has retired |
| 11a | | Forward letter on **OFFICIAL LETTERHEAD (PD158-151)** indicating member's present command or retired status |

> **Note:** If commanding officer is unavailable, give papers to **executive officer**, who will assume responsibility for completing the CO's steps.

### Acknowledgement of Service / Waiver of Service

Service may be made by sending 2 copies of the summons and complaint along with an "Acknowledgement of Service" or "Waiver of Service" by mail. The operations coordinator attaches the mailing envelope.

| Decision | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| Member **signs** the acknowledgement/waiver | No process server will be sent to member's home/command |
| Member **does not** sign | A process server may be sent; costs may be assessed against the member |
| If member signs | A copy goes to **Legal Bureau** with envelope, summons/complaint, and **REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE (PD411-160)** |

### Member Concerned — REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE (PD411-160)

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 12 | Member | Prepare **"Part A"** of REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE (PD411-160) |
| 13 | Member | Retain **"PINK" copy** and a copy of the summons and complaint for personal records |
| 14 | Member | Deliver **"BLUE" and "WHITE" copies** of REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE with original summons/complaint (if served) and envelope (if by mail) to **current commanding officer immediately** |

> **Exam Alert:** The form has 3 copies: **PINK** (member retains), **BLUE** (filed at command), **WHITE** (forwarded DIRECT to Legal Bureau). Know the distribution.

### Commanding Officer — Part B and Distribution

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 15 | CO | Prepare **"Part B"** of REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE |
| 15a | CO | Contact **IAB Records Unit** to determine if an investigation was conducted; provide name and tax registry of individual contacted |
| 15b | CO | Contact **Department Advocate's Office** to determine if charges/specs exist; provide name and tax registry of individual contacted |
| 15c | CO | Determine whether recommendation should be made for Corporation Counsel to represent the member based on information available |
| 16 | CO | Distribute as follows: |
| 16a | | **WHITE copy** with original summons/complaint and envelope — DIRECT to Legal Bureau, Room 1406, One Police Plaza, Mon-Fri 0900-1700 |
| 16b | | **BLUE copy** with duplicated copies of summons/complaint — FILED at the command |
| 16c | | Copy of the fully executed REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE — to the **member concerned** |

> **Exam Alert:** The WHITE copy goes **DIRECT** to Legal Bureau (not through channels). The BLUE copy is filed at the command. The CO must check both IAB Records Unit and Department Advocate's Office before making a representation recommendation.

### Legal Bureau — Review and Forward

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 17 | CO, Legal Bureau | Review all documents and forward completed REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE to **Corporation Counsel** |
| 18 | CO, Legal Bureau | Contact Corporation Counsel on a **quarterly basis** to determine names of all members and cases for which REQUESTS FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE have been **declined** during the preceding **90-day period** |

### Member Follow-Up

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 19 | Member | Contact Legal Bureau **10 business days** after CO has forwarded the REQUEST to ensure it has been received |
| 19a | | Notify commanding officer if REQUEST has NOT been received by Legal Bureau |
| 20 | Member | Contact **private counsel and/or union** to obtain legal representation when Corporation Counsel has **declined** representation |

> **Exam Alert:** The member must follow up with Legal Bureau after **10 business days**. If Corporation Counsel declines, the member must obtain private counsel or union representation.

> **Note:** All sections of the REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE must be completed in full. Failure to complete the form will result in it being returned and will **delay** a representation determination by Corporation Counsel.

### Corporation Counsel Representation — General Municipal Law Section 50-k

A member served with a summons and complaint or who becomes aware of being a defendant in a civil lawsuit arising from an act or omission in the **performance of duty** will be defended by the Corporation Counsel and the City will pay any resulting judgment, **IF** it is determined under General Municipal Law Section 50-k that at the time of occurrence, the member was acting in the **proper discharge of duty**.

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Notice of representation | Forwarded to member by Corporation Counsel |
| Member's obligation | Must **cooperate and assist** Corporation Counsel in defending the action |
| Failure to cooperate | May result in **withdrawal of representation** |

### Pro Se Complaints

The Legal Bureau will attempt to identify individual members named as defendants in **"pro se" complaints** (complaints filed by individuals without an attorney).

| Member Status | Filing Deadline |
|---------------|----------------|
| Current employee | Legal Bureau files within **60 calendar days** (Waiver of Service Executed, Waiver of Service Unexecuted if declined, or member cannot be identified) |
| Former member | Legal Bureau files within **90 calendar days** |
| Former member who authorized Department to accept service | Within **30 calendar days** |

### Notice of Claim Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Required before tort action | A **"Notice of Claim"** is required before commencing a tort action against the City of New York |
| Service on NYPD | Considered **IMPROPER** service — may provide basis for dismissal |
| Where to direct | Process server should be directed to the **Office of the Comptroller** |
| Mailed to Department | Return to sender |

> **Exam Alert:** A **Notice of Claim** served on the Police Department is **IMPROPER** — it should be directed to the Office of the Comptroller. Any Notice of Claim mailed to the Department should be returned to sender. A REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE should NOT be prepared for a subpoena — comply with P.G. 211-15 or 211-16 instead.

### Process Server — Active Members

When serving process upon active members, a process server will respond to the command where the individual is assigned or to the parent command if the assignment is confidential. Service at the command need not be made directly on the member named as defendant.

> **Sergeant Focus:** Sergeants must ensure that civil lawsuit papers are handled promptly — failure to forward the REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE in time can result in a default judgment against the member. The CO must check both IAB Records Unit and Department Advocate's Office before completing Part B. The WHITE copy goes DIRECT to Legal Bureau, not through channels. Follow up after 10 business days.

## Common Exam Angles — 211-20 and 211-21

1. **Department attorney hours** — 0700-2300, Monday through Friday
2. **After-hours contact** — Only a captain or above may contact DC Legal Matters or CO Legal Bureau, through the Operations Unit
3. **Contrary advice** — Department attorney advice overrides supervisors and ADAs; hierarchy is clear and non-negotiable
4. **Disregarding Department attorney advice** — May result in disciplinary action and civil liability
5. **Command Log entry** — Required for all conferrals; 7 required elements (date/time, member requesting, captain if after-hours, attorney name/title, legal question, legal opinion, actions taken)
6. **REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE (PD411-160)** — 3 copies: PINK (member), BLUE (command file), WHITE (DIRECT to Legal Bureau)
7. **Part A vs. Part B** — Member prepares Part A; CO prepares Part B after contacting IAB Records Unit and Department Advocate's Office
8. **10 business day follow-up** — Member must contact Legal Bureau to confirm receipt of REQUEST
9. **Default judgment risk** — Failure to timely forward REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE may result in default judgment against the member
10. **Notice of Claim** — Service on NYPD is IMPROPER; direct to Office of the Comptroller
11. **Pro se complaint deadlines** — 60 days (current employee), 90 days (former member), 30 days (former member who authorized service)
12. **Corporation Counsel representation** — Under General Municipal Law Section 50-k; member must cooperate or face withdrawal of representation
13. **Quarterly check** — CO Legal Bureau contacts Corporation Counsel quarterly for declined cases in preceding 90 days
14. **Mail service** — Operations Coordinator attaches mailing envelope; Command Log entry for receipt
15. **Acknowledgement/Waiver of Service** — Member may sign to avoid process server; if signed, copy goes to Legal Bureau with REQUEST

## Sergeant Study Notes — 211-20 and 211-21

- The **Department attorney's advice overrides** all other advice — including from supervisors and ADAs; sergeants cannot override this
- If a sergeant receives contrary advice from an ADA after a Department attorney conferral, the sergeant must **inform the ADA** of the prior conferral and **follow the Department attorney's advice**
- The **Command Log entry** for conferral with a Department attorney must include all 7 required elements — this documentation protects both the member and the supervisor
- For **civil lawsuits**, the REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE must be forwarded **immediately** — timeliness is critical to avoid default judgments
- The **CO must contact both IAB Records Unit and Department Advocate's Office** before completing Part B — this is a mandatory step, not discretionary
- The **WHITE copy** goes **DIRECT** to Legal Bureau, Room 1406, One Police Plaza — NOT through channels
- Members must follow up with Legal Bureau after **10 business days** — if the REQUEST has not been received, notify the commanding officer
- A **Notice of Claim** served on the NYPD is improper — do NOT accept it; direct the process server to the Office of the Comptroller
- A REQUEST FOR LEGAL ASSISTANCE should **NOT** be prepared for a subpoena — subpoenas are handled under P.G. 211-15 or 211-16
- **Pro se complaints** have specific filing deadlines that vary by member status (60/90/30 days) — the Legal Bureau handles identification and filing
- If **Corporation Counsel declines** representation, the member must obtain private counsel or union representation — the sergeant should ensure the member is aware of this obligation
