# Section 211-15 through 211-18 — Subpoena Processing and Records Requests

## P.G. 211-15 — Processing Subpoenas for Police Department Records and Testimony

> **Updated:** 12/31/24 | R.O. 78 | 6 pages

### Purpose

To ensure proper compliance by the Police Department and its members with lawful court mandates and subpoenas requiring attendance at court or production of Department records.

### Scope

The unit that is the repository of a given Department record has the responsibility to respond to a subpoena demanding the record's production. A command will **NOT** forward a subpoena to the Legal Bureau, Central Records Division, or other units within the Department when the requested record(s) is/are present in the subpoenaed command.

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Subpoena** | Generic legal term for any device compelling appearance at a specific time/place for testimony or production of documents |
| **Subpoena ad testificandum** | Compels a person to **appear and give testimony** |
| **Subpoena duces tecum** | Compels the **production of documents or evidence** |
| **Command Liaison Officer (CLO)** | Every commanding officer/counterpart — designated as the official liaison for implementation/compliance with this procedure; may delegate tasks but remains responsible |
| **Subpoena Record Book** | Permanent bound record book maintained at each command with **12 captions** (see below) |
| **Command Subpoena File** | Chronological file of photocopies of subpoenas and ADA/ACC records requests |

### Subpoena Record Book — 12 Captions

| Caption | Content |
|---------|---------|
| a. | Number (serialized, beginning with 001 each year) |
| b. | Date and time received |
| c. | Type of subpoena (duces tecum or ad testificandum) |
| d. | Name of member concerned |
| e. | Brief description of records requested |
| f. | Return date |
| g. | Issuing court |
| h. | Title of case (e.g., Smith vs. Jones) |
| i. | Party issuing subpoena (plaintiff, defendant, DA, or judge) |
| j. | Fee receipt issued (indicate "No fee" or receipt number) |
| k. | Date records forwarded |
| l. | Date court receipt received |

> **Exam Alert:** The Subpoena Record Book has **12 captions** (a through l). Know especially caption j (fee receipt) and caption l (date court receipt received) — these track compliance documentation.

### Critical Rule — No Forwarding Without Permission

> **Under NO circumstances** will a subpoena or subpoena duces tecum served on or received at a command be forwarded to the Subpoena Litigation Unit **without the express permission** of the Supervisor, Subpoena Litigation Unit.

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most important rules in 211-15. Commands do NOT forward subpoenas to the Subpoena Litigation Unit without permission. This applies to both the subpoena itself AND original records.

### Procedure — Subpoena for Personal Appearance Only (Ad Testificandum)

Upon receiving a subpoena that only requires personal appearance/testimony:

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | CLO | Record receipt of subpoena in **Subpoena Record Book**; ensure all captions completed |
| 2 | CLO | Issue **FEE RECEIPT (PD122-017)** per P.G. 212-82 if fee is tendered |
| 3 | CLO | Photocopy subpoena; deliver **ORIGINAL** to member concerned |
| 3a | | Note Command Serial Number, Fee Receipt number, date/time of delivery on reverse of photocopy; member signs photocopy |
| 4 | CLO | Direct member to comply with subpoena |
| 5 | CLO | File photocopy in **Command Subpoena File** |
| 6 | CLO | Notify roll call clerk; ensure **Command Diary** entry is made |
| 6a | | Comply with notification procedures to Appearance Control Unit |
| 7 | CLO | Ensure member has appeared in court as required |

### "So-Ordered" Requirement — Subpoena Duces Tecum

> **Exam Alert:** This is a CRITICAL distinction frequently tested. A subpoena duces tecum directed to the Police Department **MUST** be "so-ordered" by a judge. If not so-ordered, Department records will **NOT** be produced.

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Subpoena duces tecum is **"so-ordered"** | Comply — produce the requested documents |
| Subpoena duces tecum is **NOT "so-ordered"** | Member complies with personal appearance only; Department records **will NOT be produced** |
| If issuing attorney's contact info is available | CLO contacts attorney and advises that unless "so-ordered," records will not be produced; no further action required |

> **Exam Alert:** "So-ordered" means the attorney who desires the documents has obtained **judicial approval** for the subpoena duces tecum. The judge indicates this by signing or initialing at a caption entitled "so-ordered."

### Procedure — Subpoena Duces Tecum (Documents Only, No Personal Appearance)

| Step | Role | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| 8 | CLO | Ensure subpoena duces tecum has been endorsed by judge as **"so-ordered"** |
| 8a | | If NOT so-ordered: make Subpoena Record Book entries, indicate "Date of Compliance" that subpoena was not so-ordered, file copy in rear of Command Subpoena File |
| 8b | | If attorney contact available and not so-ordered: advise that records will not be produced unless so-ordered; no further action |
| 9 | CLO | Complete all applicable captions in Subpoena Record Book |
| 10 | CLO | Gather **photocopies** of all documents requested in subpoena maintained at command (including Activity Log entries, if applicable) |
| 11 | CLO | Prepare and sign **"Certification"** on **OFFICIAL LETTERHEAD (PD158-151)** |
| 11a | | If photocopies contain **redactions**: use Certification letter Number 1 (Appendix "A") |
| 11b | | If photocopies contain **NO redactions**: use Certification letter Number 2 (Appendix "B") |
| 11c | | If records **DO NOT EXIST** at command or any other command/unit: use Certification letter Number 3 (Appendix "C") |
| 12 | CLO | Prepare **RECEIPT FOR SUBPOENAED DEPARTMENT RECORDS** on **OFFICIAL LETTERHEAD (PD158-151)** (Appendix "D") |
| 13 | CLO | Forward copies of records, copy of subpoena, certification, and receipt to **clerk of court** via U.S. Mail or Department mail |
| 13a | | Indicate on outside of envelope that it contains records requested via subpoena |
| 13b | | Include index, calendar, docket or indictment number as available |
| 14 | CLO | Upon receiving court receipt: attach to filed subpoena copy; note in Subpoena Record Book under "Date Court Receipt Received" |

> **Exam Alert:** There are **3 certification letter variants**: (1) with redactions, (2) without redactions, (3) records do not exist. The CLO may delegate the authority to prepare and sign the certification to properly trained members within the command.

### Redaction Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| When to redact | When subpoenaed records contain information **unrelated** to the facts of the case |
| Method | Block out or delete so unrelated information cannot be discerned |
| **NO redactions** to Legal Bureau, Corporation Counsel, or DA | Redaction rules do NOT apply to material forwarded to these entities |
| Before forwarding to **anyone outside PD** (other than ADA/ACC/law enforcement) | Redact name, address, telephone number, and any data identifying a **victim or witness** |

> **Exam Alert:** The redaction rule has two layers: (1) redact unrelated information from all subpoenaed records, and (2) additionally redact victim/witness identifying information before forwarding to parties outside the PD (other than ADA/ACC/law enforcement). NO redactions apply to material sent to Legal Bureau, Corporation Counsel, or DA.

### Non-Trial Testimony — Consultation Rule

Members receiving a **subpoena ad testificandum** requiring testimony at **other than a trial proceeding** are advised:
- Do **NOT** answer any question without first consulting with the ADA/ACC assigned to the case
- If no ADA/ACC is assigned or the member is unsure, consult the **Subpoena Litigation Unit**

### Notifications to Subpoena Litigation Unit — Immediate Telephone Call Required

Make immediate telephone notification to the Subpoena Litigation Unit if any command receives a subpoena for:

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| a. | **Confidential records** (personnel folders, documents relating to confidential investigations) |
| b. | Records relating to cases of **notoriety or publicity** |
| c. | Records relating to **sensitive or unusual cases and corruption** cases |

> Also notify if there is **any doubt** about whether subpoenaed records should be released. Record notification in **Telephone Record**. Instructions from Subpoena Litigation Unit will be **strictly complied with**.

### Notifications in Unusual Cases

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Member is witness for **defendant** in criminal case, or for plaintiff where **City of New York is defendant** | Notify ADA or ACC of this fact **prior to start** of hearing or trial |
| Subpoena to appear at **parole revocation hearing** | Notify DA's Office of the appropriate county; note notification in **Command Diary** |

### Records Forwarding Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Original records | Under **NO circumstances** will original records be removed from a Department facility without prior approval of a Department attorney or the commanding officer |
| Photocopies ONLY | Only photocopies will be forwarded |
| Records not at receiving command | Forward subpoena to command having custody of the record |
| Multiple-item subpoena | Receiving command complies with items it has; forwards photocopy of subpoena to command with remaining items |
| Records to attorneys (non-ADA/ACC) | **NEVER** forward records directly to an attorney without prior consultation with Subpoena Litigation Unit |

### ADA/ACC Requests — No Subpoena Required

ADAs and ACCs are **NOT required** to issue a subpoena for production of Police Department records. No member will require or request a subpoena for records from ADAs/ACCs. Such document requests are handled per **P.G. 211-18**.

### Transferred Members

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Member transferred from command | Process server advised of new command; directed to serve there |
| Member retired | Process server informed; service will **not** be accepted |
| Subpoena received by non-personal delivery for transferred member | Forward subpoena to member's current command |
| Non-personal delivery for retired member | Contact issuing attorney to inform of retirement |

### Subpoenas Concerning Personal Matters

A subpoena served on a member in their capacity as a **private individual** (e.g., civil litigation, witness to off-duty events) may be served at command. If accepted by someone other than the named party (e.g., desk officer), that person must ensure the subpoena is actually received by the named member. Fee tendered goes to the member. Court appearance is on the member's **off-duty time**. Questions go to Subpoena Litigation Unit.

## P.G. 211-16 — Subpoenas Issued by Courts Outside New York City

### Purpose

To determine whether an out-of-NYC subpoena requires a response and the nature of that response.

---

### NYC Court Subpoenas

- **Valid** — must comply per applicable procedures

### Fee Receipt

- **Fee Receipt (PD122-017)** issued per P.G. 212-79

---

### New York State Court Subpoenas (Outside NYC)

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **No personal appearance** required | Comply per **P.G. 211-15** |
| **Personal appearance** required | Consult **Legal Bureau Subpoena Litigation Unit** + comply with **P.G. 212-83** (Leaving City on Police Business) |

---

### Federal Court Subpoenas

#### Criminal Cases

> **Exam Alert:** Federal criminal subpoenas have broader reach than civil subpoenas.

- **Valid from any Federal Court in the United States**

| Requirement | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Documents only | Comply per **P.G. 211-15** |
| Personal appearance | Notify **Subpoena Litigation Unit** + report to **Police Commissioner** |

**Report to PC format:**
- On **Typed Letterhead**, in **duplicate**
- Include: court name/location, type of proceeding, person concerned, date of receipt
- If PC not notified prior: include reason
- **Original** — direct to PC
- **Duplicate** — through channels

#### Civil Cases

> **Exam Alert:** Federal civil subpoenas have **limited validity** compared to criminal subpoenas. This is a frequently tested distinction.

A Federal civil subpoena is valid **ONLY** when:

| Condition | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **1** | Served **within the court's district** |
| **2** | Served **within 100 miles** of the hearing place |
| **3** | Issued by a Federal Court **in NYS** and served **in NYS** |
| **4** | Endorsed by a judge for **extraordinary service** |

---

### Out-of-State Subpoenas (Other States)

> **Exam Alert:** This is a critical distinction for the exam.

Criminal or civil subpoenas from other states are **NOT VALID** unless presented to and **endorsed by a New York State judge**.

**However:**
- The Department should make **every reasonable effort** to assist other jurisdictions
- Permission to appear obtained **through channels**
- Comply with **leaving city procedures**

> **Exam Alert:** "Not valid" does not mean "ignore." The Department still cooperates — the member just needs proper authorization through channels.

---

### When in Doubt

**Consult the Legal Bureau Subpoena Litigation Unit.**

## P.G. 211-17 — Processing Legal Bureau Requests for Department Records Including FOIL

### Purpose

To ensure prompt response to lawful subpoenas and FOIL requests.

---

### FOIL — Freedom of Information Law

> **Exam Alert:** FOIL provisions are heavily tested. Know the presumption, timeline, and fee.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Statute | **NYS Public Officers Law Sections 84-89** |
| Presumption | All government records are **PRESUMED available** for public inspection |
| Exemptions | **Narrowly drawn** — burden on agency to justify denial |

---

### Key Personnel

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Subpoena Litigation Unit** | One Police Plaza, **Room 110C** |
| **FOIL Records Access Officer** | Supervisor in charge of FOIL Unit within Subpoena Litigation Unit |
| **FOIL Appeals Officer** | **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters** |
| **Command Liaison Officer** | Every CO — designated official liaison for tracking records requests |

---

### FOIL Response Timeline

> **Exam Alert:** The **5 business day** deadline is a critical number.

Within **5 BUSINESS DAYS**, the Department must:

| Option | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **1** | Make record available |
| **2** | Deny in writing |
| **3** | Acknowledge receipt with **approximate date** of availability |

---

### Key Rules for Records Handling

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Subpoenas served at commands | **NOT forwarded** to Subpoena Litigation Unit **without permission** |
| Photocopies | Must be **complete, clear, legible**, without alteration |
| Confidentiality concerns | Forward report on **Typed Letterhead** to Supervisor, Subpoena Litigation Unit |
| Original records | Under **NO circumstances** forward to Subpoena Litigation Unit **without permission** |

> **Exam Alert:** Two things require permission: (1) forwarding subpoenas served at commands, and (2) forwarding original records. Never forward originals without permission.

---

### FOIL Denial Appeals

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Time limit | **30 days** to appeal |
| Appeals to | **Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters** |

---

### FOIL Photocopy Fee

> **Exam Alert:** A commonly tested number.

**25 cents per page**

## P.G. 211-18 — Processing Requests for Department Documents from ADAs and Assistant Corporation Counsels

### Purpose

To ensure prompt compliance with ADA/ACC document requests.

---

### Brady Obligation

> **Exam Alert:** This is one of the most critical legal concepts in this section.

**Failure to turn over reports results in REVERSAL OF CONVICTION** — even if the prosecutor was unaware of the report's existence.

Courts have held: **Possession by NYPD = possession by the prosecutor.**

> **Sergeant Focus:** Supervisors must understand that ANY Department document relevant to a case is considered to be in the prosecutor's possession. Failure to produce it is a Brady violation that can overturn a conviction.

---

### Request Requirements

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Subpoena required? | **NO** — ADA/ACC request does NOT require an accompanying subpoena |
| Format | Request on **DA/Corp Counsel letterhead** |

### Required Information on Request

- Case name
- Attorney name and phone number
- Arrest date
- Precinct of arrest
- Arresting officer name
- Docket number

---

### Recording

- Record in **Subpoena Record Book** under:
  - **"D.A. Request"** or
  - **"Corporation Counsel Request"**

---

### Doubt About Propriety

- Consult **Legal Bureau Subpoena Litigation Unit**

---

### Summary Arrests — Special Requirement

> **Sergeant Focus:** For summary arrests, the arresting/assigned officer shall:

1. Secure **photocopies of ALL** Department forms
2. Secure photocopies of ALL **reports**
3. Secure photocopies of ALL **personal notes**
4. Secure photocopies of ALL **scrap-paper notes**
5. Make all photocopies **available to ADA/ACC at the complaint room**

> **Exam Alert:** Note that "scrap-paper notes" are specifically included. ALL notes, regardless of format, must be produced. This reinforces the Brady obligation.

---

## Common Exam Angles

1. **"So-ordered" requirement** — Subpoena duces tecum directed to the Police Department MUST be "so-ordered" by a judge; if not, Department records will NOT be produced
2. **Subpoena Record Book — 12 captions** — Know all 12 (number, date/time received, type, member name, description, return date, issuing court, case title, party issuing, fee receipt, date forwarded, date court receipt received)
3. **No forwarding without permission** — Subpoenas and original records NEVER forwarded to Subpoena Litigation Unit without express permission
4. **3 certification letters** — (1) with redactions, (2) without redactions, (3) records do not exist
5. **Redaction rules** — Redact unrelated info; additionally redact victim/witness identifiers before forwarding outside PD; NO redactions to Legal Bureau/Corporation Counsel/DA
6. **Non-trial testimony** — Do NOT answer questions without first consulting ADA/ACC or Subpoena Litigation Unit
7. **Immediate SLU notification** — Required for confidential records, notoriety/publicity cases, sensitive/unusual/corruption cases, or any doubt
8. **CLO is the commanding officer** — Every CO is designated as Command Liaison Officer; may delegate but retains responsibility
9. **Original records** — NEVER removed from Department facility without approval of Department attorney or commanding officer; photocopies ONLY
10. **ADA/ACC requests — no subpoena needed** — ADAs and ACCs do NOT need a subpoena for Department records (P.G. 211-18)
11. **Brady obligation** — NYPD possession = prosecutor's possession; failure to produce = reversal of conviction
12. **FOIL — 5 business days** — Must make record available, deny in writing, or acknowledge receipt
13. **FOIL appeal** — 30 days to appeal to DC Legal Matters
14. **FOIL photocopy fee** — 25 cents per page
15. **Summary arrest notes** — ALL notes including scrap-paper notes must be photocopied and made available to ADA/ACC
16. **Federal criminal subpoenas** — Valid from any Federal Court in US; if personal appearance required, report to PC on Typed Letterhead in duplicate
17. **Federal civil subpoenas** — Valid ONLY when: within court's district, within 100 miles, issued by NYS Federal Court and served in NYS, or endorsed for extraordinary service
18. **Out-of-state subpoenas** — NOT valid unless endorsed by a NYS judge; Department still cooperates through channels
19. **Subpoena concerning personal matters** — Member appears on off-duty time; fee goes to the member
20. **Transferred/retired members** — Transferred: subpoena forwarded to new command; Retired: service not accepted

## Sergeant Study Notes

- The **Command Liaison Officer** is the commanding officer — sergeants should understand that the CO bears ultimate responsibility for subpoena compliance, though tasks may be delegated
- The **"so-ordered" requirement** for subpoena duces tecum is the most critical rule in 211-15 — if not so-ordered, documents are not produced regardless of what the attorney demands
- **Subpoena Record Book** must be maintained as a permanent bound record with all 12 captions — sergeants should verify entries are complete
- **NEVER forward original records** outside the facility without Department attorney or CO approval — photocopies ONLY
- **Redaction** has two layers: (1) unrelated information is redacted from all subpoenaed records, (2) victim/witness identifying data is additionally redacted before forwarding outside PD — but NO redaction applies to material going to Legal Bureau, Corporation Counsel, or DA
- For **non-trial testimony**, members must consult with the assigned ADA/ACC before answering any questions — if unsure who is assigned, contact the Subpoena Litigation Unit
- The **Subpoena Litigation Unit** must be immediately notified by telephone for confidential records, high-profile cases, and sensitive/corruption matters — record notification in the Telephone Record
- **Brady obligation** means every Department document relevant to a case is considered in the prosecutor's possession — even scrap-paper notes
- **FOIL requests** require response within **5 business days** — appeals go to the Deputy Commissioner, Legal Matters within 30 days
- **ADA/ACC requests** do NOT require a subpoena — the Department will not request or require ADAs/ACCs to serve subpoenas for records
- For unusual cases: if a member is testifying for the **defendant** or the City is a defendant, notify the ADA/ACC **before** the hearing or trial starts
- The **3 certification letter variants** (with redactions, without redactions, records do not exist) ensure proper documentation of compliance — the CLO may delegate signing authority to trained members
