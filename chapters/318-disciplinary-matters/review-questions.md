# Section 318 — Review Questions

## Multiple Choice

**1. Upon observing a UMOS who appears unfit for duty due to intoxication, the supervisory member must prepare the SUPERVISOR'S FITNESS FOR DUTY REPORT (PD469-150):**

- A) Within 24 hours
- B) By end of tour
- C) Immediately
- D) Within 48 hours

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Immediately**

The supervisory member must prepare the PD469-150 immediately based upon observations of the member. The CO/XO/duty captain must also prepare their own PD469-150 immediately. (A.G. 318-10)

</details>

---

**2. When a UMOS is determined to be intoxicated, the CO/XO/duty captain should:**

- A) Issue a verbal warning and send member home
- B) Remove firearms, place on modified assignment or suspend, and advise of counseling
- C) Notify Internal Affairs Bureau only
- D) Contact the Police Commissioner directly

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Remove firearms, place on modified assignment or suspend, and advise of counseling**

The CO/XO/duty captain removes firearms (per A.G. 318-13), places member on modified assignment or suspends, advises of Counseling Service Programs, and mandates supervisory officer contact to Counseling Services Unit. (A.G. 318-10)

</details>

---

**3. The Typed Letterhead report for an intoxicated UMOS must be prepared in how many copies and forwarded to how many recipients?**

- A) Five copies, five recipients
- B) Six copies, six recipients
- C) Eight copies, eight recipients
- D) Ten copies, ten recipients

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Eight copies, eight recipients**

Eight copies are prepared and forwarded to: First Deputy Commissioner (original, DIRECT + through channels), Chief of Department, Chief of Personnel, Chief of Internal Affairs, Deputy Commissioner Trials, Department Advocate's Office, and member's CO — all DIRECT. (A.G. 318-10)

</details>

**4. Prior to questioning a member who is the subject of an official investigation, the interrogating officer must provide:**

- A) CHARGES AND SPECIFICATIONS (PD468-121)
- B) NOTIFICATION OF ALLEGATION(S) AND EMPLOYEE'S RIGHTS (PD339-121)
- C) SUPERVISOR'S FITNESS FOR DUTY REPORT (PD469-150)
- D) Miranda Warning Card

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) NOTIFICATION OF ALLEGATION(S) AND EMPLOYEE'S RIGHTS (PD339-121)**

Prior to questioning, the interrogating officer must provide the member a completed PD339-121 for review and endorsement, allowing reasonable time for review. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**5. A member who is the subject of an official investigation has the right to view which recording prior to questioning?**

- A) Any CCTV footage from the precinct
- B) Their own Body-Worn Camera recording related to the incident
- C) All BWC recordings from all officers at the scene
- D) No recordings may be viewed

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Their own Body-Worn Camera recording related to the incident**

The member will be permitted to view their own BWC recording related to the incident(s) under investigation prior to questioning at a time and place deemed appropriate by the supervisor in charge of the investigation. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**6. A UMOS who refuses to answer questions specifically directed and narrowly related to official duties will be:**

- A) Given a verbal reprimand
- B) Placed on modified assignment
- C) Subject to suspension from duty
- D) Charged with insubordination only

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Subject to suspension from duty**

Members must answer questions specifically directed and narrowly related to official duties. Refusal shall result in suspension from duty. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**7. Under the Garrity warning, a member's statements during an official investigation:**

- A) Can be used in both criminal proceedings and departmental charges
- B) Cannot be used in criminal proceedings but CAN be used in departmental charges
- C) Cannot be used in either criminal proceedings or departmental charges
- D) Can be used in criminal proceedings but NOT departmental charges

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Cannot be used in criminal proceedings but CAN be used in departmental charges**

If the member answers, neither their statements nor any information gained by reason of such statements can be used against them in subsequent criminal proceedings. However, the statements may be used against them in relation to subsequent departmental charges. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**8. An interrogation conducted after CHARGES AND SPECIFICATIONS have been served must be completed at least how many days prior to the Department trial date?**

- A) 5 days
- B) 10 days
- C) 15 days
- D) 20 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 10 days**

An interrogation conducted after service of charges must be completed at least 10 days prior to the date of Department trial, except as directed by the Deputy Commissioner - Trials. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**9. The determination of what constitutes a "reasonable time" for a member to obtain counsel before interrogation is made by:**

- A) The member's commanding officer
- B) The desk officer
- C) The captain (or above) in charge of the investigation
- D) The member's union representative

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The captain (or above) in charge of the investigation**

In all cases, the determination as to what is a reasonable time will be made by the captain (or above) in charge of the investigation, considering the nature of the investigation, timeliness, and stage of the investigation. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**10. A supervisory officer is mandated to contact the Counseling Services Unit when a member is:**

- A) Placed on modified assignment, suspended, or has firearms removed due to unfitness
- B) Under criminal investigation only
- C) Transferred to another command
- D) Issued command discipline

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**A) Placed on modified assignment, suspended, or has firearms removed due to unfitness**

A supervisory officer is mandated in all cases to contact the Counseling Services Unit on behalf of a member who is placed on modified assignment, suspended, or has their firearms removed due to being unfit for duty. (A.G. 318-10)

</details>

**11. Which of the following is a prohibited interrogation tactic?**

- A) Recording the interrogation mechanically
- B) Having a line organization representative present
- C) Promises of reward for answering questions
- D) Scheduling interrogation during business hours

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Promises of reward for answering questions**

Prohibited tactics include: "off the record" questions, offensive language or threats (transfer, dismissal, disciplinary punishment), and promises of reward for answering questions. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**12. Questions and answers from an official investigation interrogation may be released outside the Department only with written approval of:**

- A) The Police Commissioner
- B) The Chief of Department
- C) The Deputy Commissioner - Legal Matters
- D) The Chief of Internal Affairs

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The Deputy Commissioner - Legal Matters**

Questions and answers resulting from interrogation are confidential and not to be revealed or released outside the Department without prior written approval of the Deputy Commissioner - Legal Matters. If a subpoena duces tecum is received, contact the Legal Bureau immediately. (A.G. 318-11)

</details>

**13. The maximum penalty for a Schedule "A" Command Discipline is:**

- A) 3 days
- B) 5 days
- C) 10 days
- D) 20 days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 5 days**

The three types of CDs have different maximums: "A" = 5 days, "B" = 10 days, "C" = 20 days. "C" CDs are issued only by the Department Advocate. (A.G. 318-01)

</details>

**14. When a CD results from an IAB investigation and the CO wants to change the recommended disciplinary action, the CO must obtain conferral and approval from:**

- A) The investigating entity
- B) The Deputy Commissioner, Department Advocate
- C) The Chief of IAB
- D) The Patrol Borough Adjutant

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) The Deputy Commissioner, Department Advocate**

When a CD comes from IAB: to change **findings**, you need approval of the **investigating entity**. To change **discipline**, you need approval of the **DC Department Advocate**. The exam often switches these two. (A.G. 318-02)

</details>

**15. The CD Review Panel may increase the penalty proposed by the CO to not more than:**

- A) The same penalty proposed
- B) One and a half times the proposed penalty
- C) Double the proposed penalty
- D) Triple the proposed penalty

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Double the proposed penalty**

The CD Review Panel may approve, reduce, or increase the penalty — but the increase cannot exceed double what the CO proposed. (A.G. 318-02)

</details>

**16. A Uniformed Sergeant must suspend an MOS for all of the following EXCEPT:**

- A) Arrest for a misdemeanor
- B) Refusal to take a Dole Test
- C) Being impaired (BAC under .08)
- D) AWOL for 5 consecutive tours

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Being impaired (BAC under .08)**

The mandatory suspension grounds (A DOG CAR) include DWI (VTL 1192 subs 2-4a) but NOT impairment below .08. By its absence from the list, impaired is not a must-suspend. (A.G. 318-05)

</details>

**17. A member must elect one of the three CD options within what timeframe of receiving the CO/XO's decision?**

- A) 3 calendar days
- B) 3 working days
- C) 5 working days
- D) 5 calendar days

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) 3 working days**

The member must notify the CO in writing on the CD of the option elected within 3 **working** days of receiving the CO/XO's decision. When the AG does not specify "working," it means calendar days. (A.G. 318-02)

</details>

**18. Records and dispositions of substantiated Schedule "B" CDs may be sealed after:**

- A) 1 year from issuance with no subsequent discipline
- B) 3 years from issuance with no subsequent "B" or Charges
- C) 3 years from disposition, upon written request, with no subsequent "B" or Charges
- D) They are automatically sealed after 3 years

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 3 years from disposition, upon written request, with no subsequent "B" or Charges**

Sealing of "B" CDs is NOT automatic — the member must request in writing via a 49 to their CO on the 3rd anniversary of the date of disposition. If no subsequent "B" CDs or Charges during those 3 years, the CO expunges from the command folder. (A.G. 318-12/318-14)

</details>

**19. When an MOS is arrested outside the city, who designates the Captain or above to conduct the investigation?**

- A) The Patrol Borough CO
- B) The Duty Captain
- C) The Chief (of Department)
- D) The MOS's Commanding Officer

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The Chief (of Department)**

When arrested outside the city, there is no "Duty Captain" — the Chief designates a Captain or above to conduct the investigation. Inside the city, the Borough CO assigns the Ranking Officer. (A.G. 318-09)

</details>

**20. Loss of a Department-issued smartphone results in which type of Command Discipline?**

- A) Schedule "A" CD
- B) Schedule "B" CD
- C) Schedule "C" CD
- D) Charges and Specifications

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**A) Schedule "A" CD**

Loss of an ID Card, Summons, Summons Book, or Department-issued smartphone are all Schedule "A" CDs. Loss of shield, Activity Log, prisoner (failure to safeguard), or other Department property (except the "A" items) are Schedule "B" CDs. (A.G. 318-01)

</details>
