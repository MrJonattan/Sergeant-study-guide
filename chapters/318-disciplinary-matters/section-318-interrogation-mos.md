# Section 318 — Interrogation of Members of the Service

## A.G. 318-11: Interrogation of Members of the Service

**Date Effective:** 01/01/25 | **Last Revision:** R.O. 2 | **Pages:** 3

### Purpose

To protect the rights of the member of the service (uniformed or civilian) in an official Department investigation.

### Pre-Questioning Requirements

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Provide member completed **NOTIFICATION OF ALLEGATION(S) AND EMPLOYEE'S RIGHTS (PD339-121)** for review and endorsement |
| 1a | Allow **reasonable amount of time** for review prior to endorsement |
| 2 | Permit member to obtain **counsel** if: (a) serious violation alleged, OR (b) sufficient justification presented even if violation is minor |

### Member Rights During Investigation

| Right |
|-------|
| Reasonable period of time to obtain and confer with counsel prior to questioning |
| Permitted to view their own **Body-Worn Camera recording** related to incident(s) under investigation prior to questioning |
| Representative of department **line organization** present at all times during interrogation |

### Interrogation Scheduling

| Rule |
|------|
| Routine, non-critical matters: schedule during **business hours** on a day when member is scheduled to work |
| Emerging investigations: conduct after all preliminary steps, conferrals completed, and member afforded reasonable time for counsel |
| Determination of "reasonable time" made by **captain (or above)** in charge of investigation |

### Interrogating Officer Must Inform Member Of

| Information |
|------------|
| Rank, name, and command of person in charge of investigation |
| Rank, name, and command of interrogating officer |
| Identity of all persons present |
| Whether member is **subject or witness** in the investigation |
| Nature of accusation |
| Identities of witnesses/complainants (address need not be revealed) — except confidential source or field associate (unless they witnessed the incident) |
| Information concerning all allegations |
| Department's policy regarding **false, misleading, and inaccurate statements** (A.G. 304-10) |

### Prohibited Interrogation Tactics

| SHALL NOT |
|-----------|
| "Off the record" questions |
| Offensive language or threats (transfer, dismissal, or other disciplinary punishment) |
| Promises of reward for answering questions |

### Interrogation Rules

| Rule |
|------|
| Must be **recorded** (mechanically or by Department stenographer) |
| Department Advocate determines if transcript required in non-criminal/minor violation cases |
| Regulate duration with breaks for **meal, personal necessity, telephone call** |
| Record all recesses |
| Assign member to **2nd Platoon** if possible (CO of member) |

### Charges and Specifications Timing

| Rule |
|------|
| Interrogations may be conducted before or after CHARGES AND SPECIFICATIONS (PD468-121) served |
| Post-service interrogation must be completed at least **10 days** prior to Department trial date |
| Exception: as directed by Deputy Commissioner - Trials |

### Tape/Transcript Requirements (Department Advocate)

| Item | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Copy of tape (interrogation before charges) | No later than **20 days** after service of charges |
| Copy of tape (interrogation after charges) | No later than **5 days** after interrogation |
| Transcript (if prepared) | By **1000 hours** on trial date, in all cases |

### Member Obligations

| Duty |
|------|
| Answer questions **specifically directed and narrowly related** to official duties |
| Refusal = **suspension from duty** |
| Submit OVERTIME REPORT (PD138-064) if lost time accrues |

### Criminal Investigation Warning (Garrity Warning)

When member is under arrest, subject of criminal investigation, or criminal charges may result, the following warning must be given:

**Key points of the warning:**
- Member is being questioned as part of official investigation
- Entitled to all rights under NY State law, State Constitution, and US Constitution
- Right not to be compelled to incriminate yourself
- Right to legal counsel at each stage
- If member refuses to testify on questions related to official duties → subject to **departmental charges → possible dismissal**
- If member answers → statements cannot be used in **criminal proceedings** but CAN be used in **departmental charges**

### Confidentiality

| Rule |
|------|
| Questions and answers from interrogation are **confidential** |
| Not to be revealed or released outside Department without **written approval of Deputy Commissioner - Legal Matters** |
| If subpoena duces tecum received → contact **Legal Bureau** immediately |

### Return to Full Duty

Members (uniformed and civilian) whose actions result in **death or serious physical injury** of a member of the public may only return to full-duty status pending a Department **administrative review**.

> **Exam Alert:** Member must be provided **PD339-121** before questioning. Counsel permitted for serious violations. Refusal to answer questions related to official duties = **suspension**. Statements can be used for **departmental charges** but NOT **criminal proceedings** (Garrity protection).

> **Sergeant Focus:** Know the 8 items the interrogating officer must inform the member about. No "off the record" questions, threats, or reward promises. Interrogation must be recorded. Post-charge interrogation must be completed **10 days** before trial. Assign member to **2nd Platoon**.

> **See Also:** AG 318-02 — Issuance of CDs (the CD interview is informal and non-adversarial with no minutes recorded, unlike a formal interrogation under AG 318-11 which must be recorded).
