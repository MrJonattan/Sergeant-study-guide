# Section 318-29 / A.G. 318-29 — Conducting Ordered Breath Testing of Uniformed Members of the Service for the Presence of Alcohol

## Source Reference
- **Procedure:** A.G. 318-29
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide1.pdf (OCR extracted)
- **Last Revision:** R.O. 24 (05/03/24)

## Procedure Summary

This procedure establishes protocols for conducting ordered breath testing of uniformed members who have agreed to submit to such testing as part of a negotiated disciplinary penalty. Testing may occur **on-duty or off-duty** and includes **unannounced home visits**.

## Scope

Applies to uniformed members who have signed a **Negotiated Settlement** agreeing to submit to ordered breath testing during a probationary period or other agreed-upon time period pursuant to A.G. 304-04 (Fitness for Duty).

## Key Threshold Values

| BAC Level | Significance |
|-----------|--------------|
| **< .02** | No further action if no indicia of intoxication |
| **≥ .02** | Highway I.D.T.U. notified for Intoxilyzer test |
| **≥ .04** | Presumption of **unfit for duty** |

> **Exam Alert:** A blood alcohol level of **.04 or greater** on the Intoxilyzer creates a **presumption of lack of fitness for duty**.

## Subject Member Agreement

Members subject to ordered breath testing have signed a Negotiated Settlement containing:

> *"I agree to submit to and to make myself available, on or off duty, for ordered breath testing for the presence of alcohol during a probationary period or other agreed upon time period pursuant to A.G. 304-04, 'Fitness for Duty.' I understand that this will entail unannounced visits to my personal residence. In addition, I am fully aware that if said testing of my blood alcohol level is .04 or more that I shall be presumed unfit for duty. I also understand that should I be found unfit for duty or should I refuse such testing I will be subject to further disciplinary action up to and including summary termination."*

## Commanding Officer, Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit Responsibilities

1. **Maintain listing** of all uniformed members subject to ordered breath testing (including duty charts, vacation selections, home phone, cellular, email)

2. **Schedule periodic testing** during each quarterly period:
   - Q1: January–March
   - Q2: April–June
   - Q3: July–September
   - Q4: October–December
   - **At least one test per year must be during off-duty hours**
   - Member may be tested more than once per quarter

3. **Ensure all supervisors** assigned to Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit and Medical Division's Absence Control & Investigations Unit are **trained by Highway District** in PBT device operation

4. **Arrange for supervisor** to conduct breath test using PBT device when member is scheduled

5. **Maintain database** tracking results of all on-duty and off-duty ordered breath tests

6. **Submit quarterly report** to Chief of Personnel listing all subject members, testing dates, on/off-duty status, and results

> **Sergeant Focus:** The probationary period is **extended** by any period the member is on suspension, modified assignment, restricted duty, limited duty, sick leave, leave of absence, or annual leave. Performance Analysis Unit must be contacted to ascertain exact end date.

## On-Duty Ordered Breath Testing

### Designated Supervisor Shall:

7. **Conduct test at Medical Division or Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit** at date/time when subject member is regularly scheduled to work

8. **Notify commanding officer/desk officer** at subject member's command to have member **transported by higher-ranking member** to Medical Division or Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit expeditiously

9. **Respond to Medical Division or Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit** on the date/time member was notified to appear
   - **Document all times** relating to testing process

10. **Advise subject member** they may be tested by multiple means (PBT device and Intoxilyzer)

11. **Direct member to prepare Ordered Breath Test Questionnaire form**
    - Form retained in member's file at Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit
    - Some information may be medical records under ADA — handle **confidentially**

12. **Conduct breath test using PBT device** in private setting
    - **Observe member for at least 20 minutes** prior to test to ensure no activities that may skew results (eating, drinking, smoking, vomiting, regurgitating, belching)

13. **If PBT reading < .02 and no indicia of intoxication** → No further action required

14. **If PBT reading < .02 but member appears intoxicated** (related indicia present) or under influence of prohibited substance:
    - Confer with Commanding Officer, Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit
    - Notify Office of Personnel Bureau for authorization to test using **Intoxilyzer** OR conduct **drug screening test for cause** per P.G. 332-06

15. **If PBT reading ≥ .02:**
    - **Immediately notify Highway I.D.T.U.** to respond to Medical Division
    - I.D.T.U. technician conducts Intoxilyzer test at Medical Division facility
    - **Entire Intoxilyzer process videotaped** by Highway District member
    - If PBT ≥ .02, Intoxilyzer reading ≥ .02: copy of videotape provided to supervisor for evidentiary safeguarding

16. **I.D.T.U. technician responsibilities:**
    - Use **Ordered Breath Test Instruction Sheet** to interview member
    - Use **Intoxilyzer Operational Checklist** to conduct test
    - **Observe member 20 minutes** prior to test
    - Videotape entire process including reading of results

17. **Supervisor evaluates unfitness for duty:**
    - Consider Intoxilyzer reading **along with related indicia of intoxication**
    - Use **.04 or greater** as threshold for presumption of unfitness
    - If member apparently unfit: notify Commanding Officer, Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit → notify Office of Personnel Bureau
    - Be guided by **A.G. 318-10** (Removal of Firearms from Intoxicated UMOS)
    - **Notify IAB Command Center** and obtain log number if member determined unfit

## Off-Duty Ordered Breath Testing

### Home Visit Procedures

**Testing Hours:** 0900–2100 hours, including weekends

**Note:** Subject members tested off-duty are entitled to **overtime compensation**.

### Designated Supervisor Shall:

15. **Ascertain if member is on sick report:**
    - If NOT on sick report → proceed to member's residence
    - If on sick report → **do not conduct test** until member no longer on sick report

16. **Respond to member's residence** with at least one other supervisor/member from Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit or Medical Division's Absence Control & Investigations Unit
    - **If member does not answer door:** Leave **Notification Card** under door with instructions to contact supervisor immediately upon receipt
    - **Attempt alternative contact means** if necessary
    - **Attempts to evade contact may result in disciplinary action up to termination**

17. **Once contact made:**
    - Request member's identification card to confirm identity
    - Direct member to **remain at current location** pending arrival
    - Member **neither requested nor permitted to drive** until testing completed and fitness determined

18. **Proceed to conduct testing** in private setting (member's residence, Department auto) in dignified, respectful fashion
    - Solicit member's input on where they feel most comfortable
    - **Document all times** relating to testing process

19. **Advise member** they may be tested by multiple means (PBT and Intoxilyzer)

20. **Direct member to prepare Ordered Breath Test Questionnaire form** (handled confidentially per ADA)

21. **Conduct breath test using PBT device**
    - **Observe member 20 minutes** prior to test

22. **If PBT reading < .02 and no indicia of intoxication** → No further action

23. **If PBT reading < .02 but member appears intoxicated:**
    - Confer with Commanding Officer, Personnel Bureau Investigations Unit
    - Notify Office of Personnel Bureau for Intoxilyzer authorization OR drug screening test per A.G. 332-06

24. **If PBT reading ≥ .02:**
    - Transport member **expeditiously to Medical Division's Lefrak City facility**
    - Notify Highway I.D.T.U. immediately to respond and conduct Intoxilyzer test
    - I.D.T.U. technician conducts test (videotaped)
    - Supervisor evaluates unfitness using .04 threshold

25. **After all testing and administrative matters completed:**
    - **Ensure member is transported back to residence**

## Indicia of Intoxication

Supervisors use **sight, hearing, and smell** to obtain evidence of alcohol intoxication:

### Sight
- Bloodshot eyes
- Flushed face
- Soiled, mussed, disarrayed clothing
- Fumbling (dropping paperwork, keys, etc.)
- Presence of alcohol containers
- Physical coordination issues (swaying, staggering, unsteady, falling, wobbling, sagging knees, using wall/furniture as prop)
- Unusual actions (hiccupping, belching, vomiting, fighting, sleepy, urinating)

### Hearing
- Slurred speech
- Admission of drinking
- Inconsistent responses
- Incoherent speech
- Abusive language, profanity
- Antagonistic behavior
- Unusual statements

### Smell
- Odor of alcohol on breath
- "Cover up" odors (breath spray, mints)
- Open alcoholic beverages
- Unusual odors

|------|-----------|

| **I.D.T.U.** | Impaired Driving Testing Training Unit — Highway District unit that conducts Intoxilyzer tests |
| **Indicia of Intoxication** | Observable signs of intoxication detected through sight, hearing, and smell |
| **Negotiated Settlement** | Disciplinary agreement in which member agrees to submit to ordered breath testing |

## Critical Numbers and Timeframes

| Number | Context |
|--------|---------|
| **.02** | PBT threshold — triggers Intoxilyzer test if ≥ |
| **.04** | Intoxilyzer threshold — presumption of unfitness for duty if ≥ |
| **20 minutes** | Mandatory observation period before PBT/Intoxilyzer test |
