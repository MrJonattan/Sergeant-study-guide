## Questions by Procedure

### P.G. 212-57 — Service of Family Court/Supreme Court Orders of Protection (3 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- Fax hours to Sheriff's Office (Mon-Thu 0300-2300, Fri 0500-2300)
- Requirement to accept orders from public
- SH-0609 form completion
- Order of Protection Log entries

**Exam Points:**
- Accepting ≠ serving
- NYC Sheriff's Office is the process server during operating hours
- When Sheriff is not operating, NYPD serves until Sheriff resumes

### P.G. 214-34 — Police Action in Premises Where Alcoholic Beverages Are Sold (3 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- When to notify Detective Bureau Operations Desk (unlicensed sales, after hours)
- Patrol supervisor must respond to scene
- PD310-150 report requirement
- Additional copy for Consumer/Worker Protection (cabaret, dance hall, catering, bowling alley)

**Exam Points:**
- Patrol supervisor response is mandatory
- Additional report copy for specific establishment types

### P.G. 208-14 — Arrest by Uniformed Member Outside City (4 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- First action: comply with local agency procedures
- Operations Unit notification requirements
- Duty captain response (required for residence counties)
- Report chain (arresting officer → C.O. → Chief of Department)

**Exam Points:**
- Applies when NOT specifically assigned or ordered
- Operations Unit decides for incidents outside residence counties
- Detailed report to Chief of Department prepared by arresting officer's C.O.

### P.G. 218-26 — Processing Found Property (4 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- $10+ threshold for reporting/depositing
- Subway found property → NYC Transit Lost Property Office
- Taxi found property → 8 repository commands by patrol borough
- Receipt forms (PD521-141 at command, PD542-012 on patrol)

**Exam Points:**
- Subway exception: non-currency, non-contraband goes to Transit Lost Property
- Taxi exception: 8 designated repository commands
- Different receipts for command vs. patrol delivery
