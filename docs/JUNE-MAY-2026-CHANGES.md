## 🔴 CRITICAL CHANGES (June 2026)

### P.G. 202-18: Desk Officer (Updated June 2, 2026)

**NEW Requirements:**

1. **CPR System "Court Appearances" Query** (NEW Step 8)
   - Desk officer must query CPR System "Court Appearances" to ensure MOS required to appear in court have been properly notified and are attending

2. **Rundown to Communications Dispatcher** (NEW Step 9)
   - Upon completion of roll call, provide communications dispatcher with complete "Rundown" of command's resources as listed in CPR System Roll Call
   - Notify communications dispatcher throughout tour with changes as they occur (resources reassigned, additional resources added, etc.)

**Exam Impact:** These are NEW steps added to the Desk Officer procedure. Questions may test:
- When to provide rundown (after roll call)
- What system to query for court appearances (CPR System)
- When to notify dispatcher of changes (throughout tour as they occur)

### P.G. 202-19: Patrol Supervisor (Updated June 2, 2026)

**NEW Requirements:**

1. **Salesforce Application** (NEW Step 10)
   - Use Salesforce Application to assist in supervising and conducting quality of life operations

2. **Automatic Vehicle Location (AVL) in DAS** (NEW Step 11)
   - Use AVL feature in Domain Awareness System (DAS) to monitor location of RMP units during tour
   - Purpose: Enhance safety of MOS and ensure proper delivery of police services
   - If AVL not functioning: Notify desk officer
   - Do NOT remove Department vehicle from service for inoperable AVL unless directed by IAB or Fleet

**Exam Impact:** These are NEW technologies Patrol Supervisors must use. Questions may test:
- What system monitors RMP locations (DAS/AVL)
- Who to notify if AVL fails (desk officer)
- Who can order vehicle removal for AVL issues (IAB or Fleet only)

### P.G. 212-125: Crime Victim Assistance Program (CVAP) (Updated June 4, 2026)

**NEW PROCEDURE** - This appears to be a completely new or significantly revised procedure.

**Key Points:**

1. **CVAP Definition**
   - Managed by Mayor's Office of Criminal Justice (MOCJ) + Safe Horizon
   - Places trained victim advocates in Department facilities Citywide

2. **Desk Officer Responsibilities**
   - Make Command Log entry when CVAP advocate:
     - Present for duty
     - Leaves or returns to precinct/PSA for any reason
     - End of tour

3. **Crime Analysis Officer/Designee Responsibilities**
   - Review Complaint Reports and NYS Domestic Incident Reports in FORMS at beginning of tour
   - Print reports EXCEPT:
     - Sex crimes
     - Crimes where victim is under 18
     - Cross complaints
     - Unfounded domestic incidents
     - Reports not involving crime victim (lost property, found drugs/firearms, deceased with no criminality)
   - Deliver printed reports to CVAP advocate daily

4. **Domestic Violence Supervisor Responsibilities**
   - When possible, facilitate CVAP advocate accompanying DV investigator on home visits
   - EXCEPT at locations where:
     - Open Complaint Report exists
     - Wanted perpetrator may be present

5. **Operations Coordinator Responsibilities**
   - Direct CVAP advocate to wear ballistic vest during visits with DV investigators
   - Vests issued from command's reserve supply
   - Ensure proper log entries and vest returned before end of tour

**Exam Impact:** This is NEW content. Questions may test:
- Who manages CVAP (MOCJ + Safe Horizon)
- Which reports NOT to give CVAP advocate (sex crimes, under 18, cross complaints, unfounded DV, non-crime)
- When CVAP advocate wears ballistic vest (during home visits with DV investigators)
- Who directs ballistic vest use (Operations Coordinator)
